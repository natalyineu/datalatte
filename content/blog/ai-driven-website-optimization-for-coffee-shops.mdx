## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I really need a website if I have 10,000 Instagram followers?**

Instagram is rented land. If the algorithm changes tomorrow, if your account gets hacked, if Meta decides to charge for reach (they're already testing it) — you lose access to those 10,000 people. Your website is the one place you control. Also: 71% of customers prefer to visit a website before deciding on a business. Instagram posts don't show up in Google search. Your website does.

**Q: How much should I actually spend on this stuff?**

For a single-location small business, you can get a functional, optimized website for $1,500–$3,000 if you work with someone who knows what they're doing (and doesn't upsell you on features you don't need). Monthly tools: Google Ads ($300–$1,500 depending on your market), Mailchimp (free for under 2,000 contacts), Square or Booksy for booking (free to $50/month). Total ongoing: $300–$1,500/month. If you're spending more than that and not seeing results, you're paying for the wrong things.

**Q: AI sounds expensive. Is it actually worth it for a single coffee shop location?**

The "AI-driven optimization" in the title doesn't mean you need to buy a $10,000 AI platform. It means using free or cheap tools: Google's PageSpeed Insights (AI-powered recommendations for speed), ChatGPT to draft email copy and test headlines, Google Ads smart bidding (which is AI). The most expensive AI tool I've recommended to a small business client was $29/month for a copywriting assistant. The cheapest was a free Google Analytics 4 property. AI for small business is mostly about working smarter, not spending more.

**Q: How long before I see results?**

If you fix the top two conversion killers (mobile speed and unclear calls to action), you'll see changes within 2–4 weeks — especially if you're running ads. SEO improvements take 3–6 months. Email list building takes 6–12 months to become a significant revenue channel. The fastest ROI is fixing what's already broken. That's measurable in weeks, not months.

**Q: What if I'm a barber shop, not a coffee shop? Does this still apply?**

Yes. Every local service business has the same core problem: people search online, land on a website, and leave without taking action. Whether you're selling haircuts, coffee, pet grooming, or fitness classes, the mechanics are identical. You need a fast site. You need clear pricing. You need a simple way to book or order. You need social proof. The tools (Square, Booksy, Mailchimp, Google Ads) work the same way. The only thing that changes is the offer.

**Q: Can I do this myself or do I need to hire someone?**

You can do the technical fixes (image compression, adding a booking button, updating your Google Business Profile) yourself in a weekend. What you can't do yourself is the diagnosis — knowing which fix will actually move the needle. That's where experience matters. I've seen owners spend 20 hours tweaking a homepage headline when their real problem was a 7-second load time on mobile. A 30-minute audit from someone who's done this before would have saved them 19.5 hours and $2,000 in lost revenue.
