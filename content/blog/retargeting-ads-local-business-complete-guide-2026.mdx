## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do retargeting ads work for local businesses?
Retargeting ads use cookies or user data to show targeted ads to people who visited your website but didn’t book an appointment or make a purchase. For example, if 97% of your site visitors leave without booking, these ads remind them of your services and encourage them to return.

### Are retargeting ads cost-effective for small businesses?
Yes, with an average ROI of 20%, retargeting campaigns often outperform other ads. They focus on users already familiar with your business, reducing wasted spend compared to broad campaigns.

### How can I target customers who abandoned their cart?
Use pixel tracking or UTM parameters to identify cart abandoners (40% average rate). Then, serve them personalized ads with discounts or reminders to complete their purchase.

### How long should a retargeting campaign run?
Most campaigns run 30–60 days, depending on user behavior. Analyze performance weekly and adjust targeting or creatives if engagement drops below 15% of monthly ad spend.

## Step 5: Segment Your Retargeting Audiences for Better Results

Not all lost customers are the same. A first-time visitor who browsed your menu for 30 seconds is different from a repeat customer who hasn’t booked in three months. Segment your retargeting lists based on behavior to serve the right message at the right time.

- **Website visitors who viewed a service page but didn’t book** → Show them a “Still thinking about us?” ad with a limited-time offer (e.g., 15% off their first haircut at your salon).
- **Cart abandoners** → Remind them exactly what they left behind. For a fitness studio, that might be a “Reserve your spot now – class filling fast!” ad with a countdown timer.
- **Past customers who haven’t returned in 60+ days** → Use a “We miss you!” campaign with a loyalty discount. Coffee shops can offer a free pastry with any drink purchase to re-engage lapsed regulars.

Segmentation boosts click-through rates by up to 50% compared to blanket retargeting. Start with just two or three segments – you can always refine later.

## Step 6: Use Frequency Caps to Avoid Ad Fatigue

Retargeting works because it keeps your business top-of-mind – but too many ads can backfire. If a potential customer sees the same “Book now!” ad ten times in a day, they’ll feel stalked rather than welcomed. Set a frequency cap of 3–5 impressions per person per week across all platforms.

For example, a pet groomer in Toronto found that capping Facebook ads at 4 impressions per week reduced negative feedback by 60% while maintaining a 22% conversion rate. Test different caps for your business: start with 3, then increase to 5 if engagement stays high. Monitor your ad relevance score (Facebook) or quality score (Google) – a drop signals it’s time to refresh creative or lower frequency.

## Step 7: Leverage Seasonal Offers and Urgency

Local businesses thrive on timely, location-based promotions. Tie your retargeting ads to upcoming events, holidays, or local weather patterns to create urgency.

- **Hair salon before Valentine’s Day** → “Book your Valentine’s Day look – only 3 slots left this week!”
- **Coffee shop during a cold snap** → “Warm up with our new peppermint mocha. Order ahead and skip the line.”
- **Fitness studio at the start of a new month** → “New month, new you – first session free if you book by Friday.”

Urgency works because it taps into the fear of missing out (FOMO). Use countdown timers in your ad copy or offer codes that expire in 72 hours. A UK-based yoga studio increased retargeting conversions by 35% simply by adding “Ends Sunday” to their ad headlines.
