## Set Up WhatsApp Business in 4 Steps  
1. **Download the app** and register with the phone number you already use for customer calls.  
2. **Build a complete business profile** – add your physical address, opening hours, a short tagline, and a clickable link to your online booking page.  
3. **Enable free core tools** – **Labels** let you tag contacts (e.g., “Regular – Salon A” vs. “First‑timer”), **Away Messages** auto‑reply when you’re closed, and **Quick Replies** store answers to common questions (e.g., “Our price list is $45 for a haircut”).  
4. **Set up a verification badge** (optional but adds trust) and create a **Broadcast List** for promotions; the first 100 contacts are free, then it’s $15 / month.  

A coffee shop in Austin uses labels to separate “regulars” from “first‑timers.” When a regular texts “Can I order 2 lattes?” the barista sees the label instantly and prepares the order without asking for details, cutting prep time by **15 seconds** per order.  

<Callout type="example"> The "Pet Paws" groomer in Sydney set up an away message: "We’re closed today. Text us anytime—we reply within 2 hours." Bookings from WhatsApp jumped **30%** in 2 weeks.</Callout>

## Automate First Responses Without Feeling Robot  
Use the **Quick Replies** feature to save phrases like:  
- "Your 3 PM haircut is confirmed!"  
- "Our hours are 7 AM–7 PM daily."  
- "Download the booking app here [link]."  

A yoga studio in Toronto automates responses for "class times" but adds a personal note for cancellations: "We’re sad to see you go—let us know if you need help finding another class."  

<Callout type="tip"> **Mix automation and humanity**: Use 1–2 automated replies for FAQs, but always follow up with a voice message or photo for cancellations or urgent requests.</Callout>

## Turn Messages Into Bookings (No App Needed)  
Create a **click-to-book** flow. For example:  
1. Add a WhatsApp number to your Google Business listing.  
2. When a customer texts, reply with a link to your online calendar.  
3. Confirm the booking via WhatsApp.  

A barbershop in Melbourne saw **25% more bookings** after adding this flow. Their message: "I’ll send you a booking link. Can you confirm your 4 PM slot?"

## Use WhatsApp for Customer Service Without Burning Out  
Set clear boundaries and automate the rest:  

- **Business hours** – configure the app to only allow inbound chats between 9 AM–6 PM. Outside those hours, an **Away Message** can say, “We’re closed now, but you can book a slot for tomorrow here: https://yoursite.com/booking.”  
- **Quick Replies** – save templates for FAQs like “Do you accept walk‑ins?” or “What’s the cancellation policy?” and insert them with a single tap, reducing average response time from 12 minutes to under 2 minutes.  
- **Label urgent issues** (e.g., “Gym equipment broken”) and assign them to a specific staff member; the label appears at the top of the chat list, ensuring nothing slips through.  
- **Escalation workflow** – for high‑volume locations, connect WhatsApp to a simple CRM (e.g., HubSpot free tier) via Zapier; new messages automatically create a ticket, and you can track response SLA compliance.  

A fitness studio in Chicago cut its support workload by **40 %** after publishing a one‑page FAQ PDF, uploading it to the chat, and using Quick Replies for the top five questions.  

<Callout type="warning"> **Don’t reply to every message instantly**: Clients expect fast replies, but you don’t need to drop everything for a text at 9 PM.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions  

**Can I use WhatsApp Business for free?**  
Yes. The basic app costs $0 and includes Labels, Away Messages, and Quick Replies. Paid add‑ons like Broadcast Lists cost $15 / month after the first 100 contacts, and the WhatsApp Business API (for >1,000 messages/month) starts at $0.01 per message.  

**How do I get customers to message me?**  
Place the WhatsApp link (wa.me/YourNumber) on your website header, Google Business profile, email signatures, and physical receipts. A Seattle coffee shop added “Text us for same‑day orders” on cup sleeves and saw a **20 % lift in inbound texts** within a month.  

**Is WhatsApp safe for credit card payments?**  
No. WhatsApp is not PCI‑compliant for direct card entry. Use it to confirm bookings and then direct customers to a secure payment gateway such as Stripe, Square, or PayPal.  

**How do I handle customer complaints?**  
Reply within 1 hour, apologize, offer a concrete solution (e.g., a free service or voucher), and follow up within 24 hours. A Toronto salon turned a bad haircut into a repeat visit by sending a $20 voucher and a photo of the corrected style, boosting that client’s lifetime value by **35 %**.  

**Can I track WhatsApp results?**  
Yes. Append UTM parameters to any link you share (e.g., `whatsapp://send?text=Book%20here%20https://yoursite.com/booking?utm_source=whatsapp&utm_medium=chat`). Google Analytics will then show clicks, conversion rates, and revenue attributed to WhatsApp.
