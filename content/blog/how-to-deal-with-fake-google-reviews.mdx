**Identify Fake Reviews**

The first step in dealing with fake Google reviews is to identify them. Here are some red flags to look out for:

* Reviews that are overly positive or negative
* Reviews that are similar in tone or language
* Reviews from users with no previous reviews or a history of fake reviews
* Reviews that mention specific products or services that are not usually discussed in reviews

**Flagging Fake Reviews**

If you suspect a review is fake, you can flag it for Google's attention. To do this:

* Log in to your Google My Business account
* Click on the "Reviews" tab
* Find the suspicious review and click on the three dots next to it
* Select "Flag as inappropriate"

**Removing Fake Reviews**

If Google removes the fake review, great! But what if they don't? In some cases, Google may not remove the review, especially if it's not a clear case of spam. In this case, you can try to respond to the review and address any concerns the reviewer may have had.

**Responding to Fake Reviews**

Responding to fake reviews can be tricky, as you don't want to escalate the situation. Here are some tips for responding to fake reviews:

* Stay calm and professional
* Address the reviewer's concerns, if any
* Don't engage with the reviewer if they're being abusive or spammy
* Focus on the positive reviews and the great experiences your customers have had with your business
