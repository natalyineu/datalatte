Georgia is a Caucasian country of 3.7 million people at the crossroads of Europe and Asia — ancient Christian heritage (one of the world's first Christian countries), extraordinary winemaking tradition (8,000 years of viticulture), and stunning geography from the Greater Caucasus mountains to the Black Sea coast. Tbilisi is the vibrant, increasingly cosmopolitan capital; Batumi is the Black Sea resort city; Kutaisi is the second city and ancient capital.

Georgia's digital marketing environment differs from its CIS neighbours: Western platforms are fully accessible, Facebook and Instagram are dominant, and Google is the primary search engine. Since 2022, Georgia has become a major destination for Russian and Ukrainian tech workers and entrepreneurs, creating a significant new expat business community.

## Georgia's Digital Platform Landscape

| Platform | Active Users (Georgia) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook | 2.1M (57%) | Dominant social platform |
| YouTube | 2.3M (62%) | Very high consumption |
| Instagram | 1.6M (43%) | Strong lifestyle |
| WhatsApp | 1.8M (49%) | Primary messaging |
| Telegram | 1.5M (41%) | Growing with Russian expat community |
| TikTok | 0.8M (22%) | Fast growing |
| Google | 95%+ search share | Dominant |

**Facebook dominant**: Unlike Russia, Georgia uses Western platforms freely. Facebook has the broadest demographic reach — Georgian families, community groups, and local business pages are central to Facebook use.

**Instagram strong for lifestyle**: Tbilisi's booming café, restaurant, and tourism scene has driven Instagram growth. Wine tourism, boutique hotels, and beauty businesses all benefit from Instagram's visual format.

**Russian expat community**: Since 2022, hundreds of thousands of Russian-speaking expats (tech workers, entrepreneurs) have settled in Tbilisi, creating demand for both Georgian and Russian-language marketing. Some businesses serve both communities.

## Three Georgian Business Examples

### ☕ Wine Bar & Café, Old Tbilisi

**Strategy**: Instagram showcasing Georgian wine culture and café aesthetic (both Georgian and English content for tourists), Facebook for Tbilisi community groups, Google Maps, Google Search Ads in Georgian and English for tourist discovery, WhatsApp for reservations.

**Budget**: GEL 2,500/month (≈$909): GEL 1,000 Google Ads, GEL 900 Meta Ads, GEL 600 content.

### 💇 Beauty Salon, Vake (Tbilisi)

**Strategy**: Instagram transformation content in Georgian, Facebook for Vake neighbourhood community, Google Search Ads, Google Maps, WhatsApp/Telegram for bookings.

**Budget**: GEL 1,800/month (≈$655): GEL 750 Google Ads, GEL 680 Meta Ads, GEL 370 content.

### 🍷 Wine Tour Experience, Kakheti

**Strategy**: English-primary Instagram (international wine tourists), Google Search Ads in English ("Georgian wine tour", "Kakheti wine experience"), TripAdvisor listing, Google Maps, WhatsApp for booking in multiple languages.

**Budget**: GEL 2,200/month (≈$800): GEL 950 Google Ads, GEL 820 Meta Ads, GEL 430 content.

## Georgian Marketing Calendar

| Period | Opportunity |
|---|---|
| January 7 | Orthodox Christmas (Shoba) — major |
| January 14 | Old New Year (Mekvle) |
| March 3 | Mother's Day (unique Georgian date) |
| April/May | Easter (Aghdgoma) — most important religious holiday |
| May 26 | Independence Day |
| August | High tourist season |
| October | Rtveli (grape harvest) — wine festival season |
| November | Giorgoba (St. George's Day — November 23, very important) |

**Mother's Day March 3**: Georgia has its own unique Mother's Day date — March 3. This is NOT Women's Day (March 8 is celebrated but less commercially). March 3 is the major family-oriented commercial day. Florists, beauty salons, and restaurants should start campaigns February 25.

**Rtveli (Grape Harvest)**: Every autumn, Georgia celebrates the grape harvest with winery festivals, feasts (supras), and community celebrations — particularly in Kakheti wine region. For tourism businesses, this is a high-value season.

**St. George's Day (Giorgoba, November 23)**: Georgia's patron saint — one of the most important Georgian holidays. Also the name day for all Georgians named Giorgi (one of the most common names). Restaurants, wine bars, and gift businesses do strong business.

## FAQ

**Georgian or Russian language for Georgia marketing?**
Georgian (ქართული) for all domestic marketing — it is the official language and Georgians are proud of their unique script (one of only 14 independent scripts in the world). Russian may supplement for the expat community. English is important for tourism businesses.

**How important is tourism for Georgian digital marketing?**
Extremely significant. Georgia receives millions of international tourists annually — attracted by wine, history, food, and adventure tourism. Tourism-facing businesses need bilingual Georgian/English strategy.
