## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to learn how to code to use AI for my GBP?**

No. I can barely read a line of code myself. You're using tools like ChatGPT, Google's built-in AI features, and scheduling apps like Booksy or Square. If you can type a sentence and click "publish," you can do this. The AI handles the heavy lifting. You just need to provide the specifics about your business.

**Q: Will customers notice if my responses are written by AI?**

Only if you don't edit them. Pure AI output sounds generic. But if you take the AI draft and add your voice — a specific detail, a mention of your staff, a reference to your location — it sounds human. I write a draft with AI, then I add one sentence that only the business owner would know. "Sarah makes the best cappuccino in Austin" is better than "Our barista is highly skilled."

**Q: How much time does this actually save?**

For a typical small business, I estimate 3-4 hours per week. That's time spent writing posts, responding to reviews, answering Q&A, and updating photos. AI handles the first draft or the data analysis in minutes. You spend 10 minutes reviewing and customizing. Over a month, that's roughly 12-16 hours saved. At an owner's hourly rate, that's real money.

**Q: Is GBP optimization worth it if I already have a website and social media?**

Yes, and here's why: Google Business Profile is often the first thing people see when they search for your business — before your website, before your Instagram. It appears in the local search results, Google Maps, and the knowledge panel. If your GBP is incomplete or outdated, people bounce before they ever reach your site. I've seen a GBP with complete info drive 40% more phone calls than a listing with just the basics. Your website is important. Your GBP is your front door.

**Q: Can AI predict which Google Posts will perform best?**

Not perfectly, but it can help. I use a two-step process. First, I feed the AI the past 30 days of post performance data (views, clicks, bookings). Second, I ask it to identify patterns. Most common insight: posts with a photo of a person (staff, customer with permission) outperform posts with product photos by about 25%. Posts with a specific time-bound offer ("This week only") outperform general announcements. That pattern holds true across coffee shops, salons, and gyms.

**Q: What's the biggest risk of using AI for my GBP?**

Losing your voice. If every post and response sounds like a robot, customers will notice. The second risk is over-automation. I've seen businesses schedule 50 future posts at once, all with the same structure, and Google stops showing them in search results. AI is a tool, not a replacement. You still need to read, edit, and approve everything before it goes live.
