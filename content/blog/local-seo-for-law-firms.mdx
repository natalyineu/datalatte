## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does it take to rank for "lawyer near me" searches?**

If you're starting from zero — no Google Business Profile, no citations, no reviews — expect 3-6 months to see meaningful results. If you already have a profile but it's not optimized, 4-8 weeks. I've seen firms jump to the local pack in two weeks after fixing basic errors, but that's rare. The firms that rank fastest are the ones that fix everything at once: GBP optimization, reviews, citations, and website speed. Doing one thing alone takes longer.

**Q: Do I need to spend money on Google Ads for local SEO to work?**

No. Local SEO and Google Ads are separate channels. A well-optimized GBP and website will drive organic traffic without paid ads. That said, Google Local Services Ads (the ones with the green checkmark that say "Google Screened") work extremely well for law firms. I've seen cost-per-lead between $30-80 for personal injury and family law, depending on the city. But you don't need to run ads to rank organically. Just don't expect results overnight.

**Q: What if I have bad reviews? Should I delete my Google Business Profile?**

Absolutely not. Deleting your GBP removes your biggest local SEO asset — the thing that gets you into the local pack. Bad reviews are manageable. Respond to every negative review professionally. Address the specific issue. Offer to make it right offline. One or two negative reviews among 20+ positive ones won't hurt you. But a pattern of unresolved negative reviews with no owner response? That's a problem.

**Q: Can I do local SEO myself, or do I need to hire someone?**

You can do the basics yourself in about 10-15 hours. Claim and optimize your GBP. Get 10-15 reviews. Fix your website speed. Build citations on the top 10 directories. That will get you to page 1 for your city and practice area in most markets. If you're in a competitive city (NYC, LA, Chicago) or a competitive practice area (personal injury, divorce), you'll need ongoing work: content creation, link building, review management. At that point, hiring someone who knows local SEO for law firms is worth the $500-1,500/month they'll charge.

**Q: Do I need a separate website for each city I serve?**

No. You need dedicated landing pages within your main site. A separate domain for each city creates more work — you'd need separate GBPs, separate citations, separate content strategies. One site with well-optimized location pages is cleaner and easier to manage. But those location pages need to be unique, not copy-pasted. Write 500+ words about that specific city, include a local phone number if you have one, and embed a Google Map.

**Q: What's the one thing I should do today that will make the biggest difference?**

Claim and fully optimize your Google Business Profile. Fill in every section: services, hours, photos, description, Q&A, posts. Then get three new reviews this week. Text your last five clients and ask them to leave a review. That single action — a complete GBP plus fresh reviews — will move you up in rankings faster than anything else.
