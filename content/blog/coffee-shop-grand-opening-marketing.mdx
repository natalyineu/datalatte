Opening a coffee shop is a sprint and a marathon at the same time. The first 30 days can make or break your regular customer base — the people who will account for 80% of your revenue once the grand opening buzz fades.

This is a day-by-day actionable framework for the 30 days around your opening: the two weeks before, opening day, and the three weeks after. No vague advice — specific tasks with timelines.

## Days 1–7 Before Opening: Foundation

### Day 1: Claim and complete your Google Business Profile

This is non-negotiable and takes 2–3 hours. Without a complete GBP, you won't appear in "coffee shop near me" searches — and that's where most of your first-week customers will find you.

**Complete these fields:**
- Business name, address, phone number (exactly matching your signage and receipts)
- Business hours (including holiday hours if applicable)
- Website URL
- 10+ photos: exterior, interior, bar setup, pastry case, coffee drinks, baristas working
- Business description (200+ words including your neighborhood name, specialty, and differentiators)
- Attributes: WiFi, drive-through, outdoor seating, parking, accessibility — everything that applies

**Verify your listing**: Request postcard verification or phone verification. Without verification, your listing won't rank properly.

### Day 2: Set up your Instagram and Facebook

Create business accounts on both. Your Instagram handle should be your coffee shop name or as close as possible. Fill out the bio completely: location, hours, link to your website.

Post your first content immediately — even "we're opening soon" with construction-in-progress or design photos. Start building an audience before you open.

**Week-before Instagram content to post:**
- Day 2: "Opening in 7 days" announcement with your logo and address
- Day 4: Coffee equipment or the build-out — "almost ready" content
- Day 6: A close-up of something visually beautiful — your espresso machine, your signage, your pastry display

### Day 3: Build your pre-opening email list

Create a simple one-page "Coming Soon" website (or use a tool like Mailchimp's free landing page builder) that collects email addresses in exchange for a launch offer.

**Example landing page:**
"[Coffee Shop Name] opens [date] in [neighborhood]. Sign up for a free drink on opening weekend."

This does two things: builds an audience you own (not dependent on Instagram algorithm), and gives you a reason to collect emails from day one.

**Where to promote it:**
- Your GBP "products" or "posts" section
- Instagram bio link
- Local Facebook community groups (neighborly, not spammy)
- Physical sign in your window during final setup

Target 100+ emails before you open. Even 50 is meaningful — these are your first regulars.

### Day 4–5: Neighborhood outreach

Walk within a 3-block radius of your shop and introduce yourself to neighboring businesses. This isn't about getting them as customers — it's about:

1. **Cross-promotion**: Nearby businesses (gyms, offices, boutiques) may send foot traffic your way if you offer their employees a perk. "Show your [gym name] membership for 10% off" creates a referral network.

2. **Google Maps anchor**: Search engines notice when nearby businesses link to each other. Ask neighboring businesses to mention you in their Google or Yelp reviews ("great new coffee shop opened next door!") — this builds local authority faster than anything else.

3. **Neighborhood credibility**: Local business owners talk to each other. Being known as "the friendly new coffee shop owner who introduced herself" matters more than most marketing.

### Day 6–7: Set up your loyalty program

Decide on and set up your loyalty program before you open. Customers who join on day one are 3x more likely to become regulars than those who join at week 3.

**Low-tech option**: Paper punch card. "Buy 9 coffees, get 1 free." Simple, universal, no tech required. Major downside: no data.

**Mid-tech option**: Square Loyalty or Clover Rewards. Built into your POS if you're using Square (most common for new coffee shops). Customers earn points per dollar spent. You can see who your top customers are and send them targeted offers. Monthly cost: $45–$75.

**Best option for new shops**: Toast Loyalty or a standalone app like Stamp Me or Fivestars. More customer data, push notification capability, and better for building repeat visit habits. Monthly cost: $50–$150.

## Days 1–7 Before Opening: Buzz Building

### Run a "soft launch" for community

1 week before your official opening, invite 20–30 people for a soft opening. Include: neighboring business owners, local influencers, friends who fit your target demographic, and a few journalists or food writers if you can reach them.

**Goal**: Get 10+ Google reviews before your official opening day. A business that opens with 15 reviews and 4.8 stars looks established. A business that opens with 0 reviews looks like it's not quite real.

**How to get those early reviews**: At the end of your soft opening, personally ask each person: "Would you be willing to leave us a Google review? It really helps a new small business." Hand them a card with a QR code that links directly to your Google review page (use Google's review link generator).

### Run your first Instagram promotion

1 week before opening, run a "like and tag a friend to win a free month of coffees" giveaway. This isn't about the prize — it's about:
- Getting 50–200 new local Instagram followers who are already aware you're opening
- Algorithmic amplification (shares and tags get your posts seen by people beyond your followers)
- Building social proof before anyone has tried your coffee

**Prize**: 30-day punch card (10 free drinks). Cost to you: 10 coffees (~$45 in COGS). Value of reaching 1,000+ local people who know your name: incalculable.

## Opening Week: Days 1–7

### Opening Day Checklist

**Morning (2 hours before opening):**
- Post on Instagram (stories + grid): "We're open!" with your hours and address
- Post on Google Business Profile: "Opening Day" post with a photo
- Send your email list: "Doors are open — come in and get [your opening day offer]"
- Text/call 5–10 people who said they'd come and remind them personally

**Opening day offer**: Don't discount your coffee — it trains customers to expect lower prices. Instead, offer a free add-on (extra shot, flavor syrup, pastry with coffee purchase). Or run a "first 50 customers get a free branded travel mug" with purchase. Creating urgency and a limited offer drives early-morning foot traffic.

**In-store setup:**
- Loyalty program sign-up prompt at every transaction
- QR code card asking for Google reviews (put it on the receipt or counter)
- Email list sign-up (physical card or iPad — both work)
- "Tell a friend" card with your Instagram handle

### Days 2–7: Capture the opening week momentum

Every day of opening week, post once on Instagram. Rotate between:
- Your best-looking drinks
- Staff introductions (customers remember faces; this builds connection)
- Behind-the-scenes of morning setup
- Customer moments (with permission — often people are happy to be featured)
- Neighborhood context ("Our view from the corner of 5th and Main")

**Goal for opening week:**
- 50+ Google reviews
- 200+ Instagram followers
- 100+ loyalty program members
- 200+ email list subscribers

If you hit these numbers by end of week 1, you have a foundation that makes month 1 dramatically more successful.

## First Month: Days 8–30 — Building Regulars

### Week 2: Your first email campaign

On day 10, send your first email to everyone on your list:
- Subject: "Your first week with us — what we've learned"
- Content: What your best-sellers turned out to be, a barista spotlight, a teaser for something new coming next week
- CTA: "Follow us on Instagram for daily updates" and "Show this email for a free cookie with your next coffee"

**Frequency**: Email once per week during month 1. After that, bi-weekly is enough unless you have specific promotions.

### Week 2–3: Run your first targeted social ad

By day 10–12, you have 50+ reviews and photos of real drinks from your actual shop. Now it's time for a small paid campaign.

**Setup:**
- Platform: Instagram and Facebook ads (Meta Ads Manager)
- Target: 18–45, within 2 miles of your address
- Budget: $10/day ($300/month)
- Creative: Your best-looking drink photo + "Now open in [neighborhood]. Your morning ritual, upgraded."
- Offer: "First drink free with any food purchase — mention this ad"

Even at $300/month, 2-mile radius targeting on Instagram in most US markets reaches 8,000–15,000 local people per month. At a 1% response rate, that's 80–150 new trial customers per month.

### Week 3: Google Ads for "coffee shop near me"

Set up a Google Ads campaign specifically for local search intent.

**Campaign setup:**
- Keywords: "coffee shop near me," "best coffee [your city]," "coffee [your neighborhood name]"
- Bid: $2–$3 per click (local coffee searches in non-NYC markets)
- Budget: $5–$10/day
- Ad copy: "Now Open: [Your Coffee Shop] — [Neighborhood]. Specialty drinks, [differentiator]. Walk in or order ahead."
- Landing page: Your GBP listing or website menu page

**Expected results**: 30–50 clicks per day at $5–$10 spend. Even 5% conversion (walk-in attribution is hard to track) = 1–3 new customers per day from this channel alone.

### Days 20–30: First retention push

By day 20, you know who your early regulars are. They've come in 3+ times in 20 days.

**Retention tactics for week 4:**

**1. Name them by name**: Train your baristas to remember names. "Your usual, [Name]?" is worth $1,000 in advertising. This is what makes a neighborhood coffee shop irreplaceable.

**2. Thank your reviewers**: Reply personally to every Google review. "Sarah, thank you — we're so glad you love the oat milk latte. See you tomorrow!" This is visible to everyone who reads reviews and signals that you care.

**3. Launch a "regular" tier**: After 10 visits, customers reach "regular" status in your loyalty program. Give them a tangible recognition: their name on the wall, a custom drink named after them for a week, or a 10% permanent discount. The recognition is worth more than the discount.

**4. Create a reason to come back next week**: On week 3 or 4, announce a weekly special. "Matcha Monday," "Weekend brunch menu," "New seasonal drink every Friday." Routines are the backbone of coffee shop loyalty.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How early should I start marketing before opening?**

Start 4–6 weeks out. Claim your Google Business Profile, set up your social accounts, and begin "coming soon" content at the 6-week mark. The earlier you start building awareness, the longer the runway you have to build your email list and create pre-opening anticipation.

**Q: Should I do a big grand opening event or just open normally?**

A soft opening for close contacts + community 1 week before, followed by a grand opening event on your first official public day, outperforms either alone. The soft opening generates reviews and word-of-mouth before the event. The grand opening event generates buzz and foot traffic with a ready-made base of people who already know you.

**Q: What's the most important thing to do in the first month?**

Get Google reviews. Everything else can be built later — more Instagram followers, a better email list, a bigger ad budget. But your Google review count and rating are effectively permanent brand equity that every future customer will see. A coffee shop with 100 reviews at 4.8 stars will consistently outrank one with 10 reviews at 4.9 stars in local search.

**Q: How long before I should start paid advertising?**

Don't spend money on Instagram or Google Ads until you have: (1) at least 20 Google reviews and a 4.5+ rating, (2) a complete GBP listing with 10+ photos, (3) a working website or menu page to send ad traffic to, and (4) a loyalty program to capture the customers ads bring in. With those foundations in place, paid ads amplify a working system. Without them, you're paying to send people to an incomplete business online presence.
