Most hair salon Google Ads articles tell you to "target local keywords" and "use a conversion-optimized landing page." This one tells you the specific keywords, the specific bids, and the specific campaign structure that generates 20+ bookings per month from Google Ads at a budget most salons can actually afford.

## What 20 Bookings Per Month From Google Ads Looks Like

Before the tactics, let's anchor the math:

**Assumptions**: Mid-size US market, salon average ticket $85, new client conversion rate 45%, 3-mile service radius.

| Step | Number |
|---|---|
| Monthly ad budget | $500 |
| Average cost per click | $3.50 |
| Monthly clicks | ~143 |
| Landing page conversion rate | 12% |
| Leads (calls + form fills) per month | ~17 |
| Lead to booked appointment rate | ~65% |
| Booked appointments per month | ~11 |

To hit 20 bookings/month: budget $800–$900/month, or improve landing page conversion from 12% to 20%+ (better design, offer, reviews visible on page). Both paths work — the conversion rate improvement is often higher ROI than increasing budget.

## Keyword Structure: 3 Tiers

Hair salon Google Ads work best with a tiered keyword approach:

### Tier 1: Near-Me Intent (Highest Priority)

These are the highest-converting searches. Someone searching these is ready to book:

- "hair salon near me"
- "hair stylist near me"
- "haircut near me"
- "hair salon [city name]"
- "hair salon [neighborhood name]"
- "best hair salon near me"

**Bid strategy**: Maximize Conversions or Target CPA ($30–$50 target cost per lead). Let Google's algorithm optimize, but set a maximum CPC bid cap of $6–$8 to prevent runaway costs.

### Tier 2: Service-Specific Intent

These searches have medium intent but higher ticket potential:

- "highlights near me"
- "balayage [city name]"
- "hair color salon [city name]"
- "keratin treatment near me"
- "women's haircut near me"
- "men's haircut salon [city name]"
- "blowout near me"

**Bid strategy**: Manual CPC at $3–$5. These have lower search volume but often higher average ticket.

### Tier 3: Comparison/Consideration

These have lower conversion but can build awareness in competitive markets:

- "best hair salon in [city]"
- "top rated hair salon [city]"
- "[neighborhood] hair salon"
- "[specific style] hair salon" (e.g., "natural hair salon", "curly hair specialist near me")

**Bid strategy**: Lower CPC ($1.50–$3), or use as brand awareness campaigns.

### Negative Keywords (Critical)

Add these from day one to prevent wasted spend:

- jobs / careers / hiring / employment / stylist job
- school / training / course / cosmetology school
- supplies / products / wholesale
- DIY / at home / self
- free
- cheap (unless you're positioning on price)

## Campaign Structure That Works

**Campaign 1: Near-Me + Local** (60–70% of budget)
- Ad group 1: "near me" keywords
- Ad group 2: "[city/neighborhood] + hair salon" keywords
- Bidding: Maximize Conversions, CPC cap $7
- Target: 3-mile radius around salon

**Campaign 2: Services** (20–25% of budget)
- Separate ad groups per high-value service: color, balayage, keratin, blowout, extensions
- Bidding: Manual CPC $3–$4
- Targeting: 5-mile radius (people travel further for specialty services)

**Campaign 3: Remarketing** (10–15% of budget)
- Target people who visited your website but didn't book
- Show a "reminder" ad with your offer or a review
- Budget: $50–$100/month
- Requires Google Analytics linked to your Ads account with proper tracking

## Landing Page Setup

Never send Google Ads traffic to your homepage. Create a dedicated landing page with:

**Above the fold** (visible without scrolling):
1. Salon name + tagline
2. Photo of your best work (before/after or styled client)
3. Offer: "New clients: 20% off your first service" or "Book online and get a free conditioning treatment"
4. One big booking button

**Below the fold**:
- Your Google review rating and review count (trust signal)
- 3–4 photos of your actual work (not stock)
- Your services with prices
- Map showing your location + hours
- A second booking button

**Booking integration**: Connect directly to your booking software (Vagaro, Square, Fresha, Mindbody). Every extra step between "landing on page" and "completing booking" reduces conversion by 10–20%. Make it one click from the ad to the booking form.

**Page load speed**: Google's core Web Vitals factor matters here. Your landing page must load in under 3 seconds on mobile. Use Google PageSpeed Insights to check. Slow pages bleed click budget.

## Tracking Bookings (Not Just Clicks)

Most salons run Google Ads and have no idea which bookings came from which source. Fix this:

**Step 1**: Link Google Analytics 4 to your Google Ads account

**Step 2**: Set up conversion tracking for:
- Phone calls that originate from the ad (Google call conversion tracking)
- Booking form submissions or confirmations (set as "Purchase" or "Lead" goal in GA4)

**Step 3**: In your booking intake flow, add "How did you hear about us?" with options including "Google search" or "Google ad"

**Step 4**: Review weekly: which keywords generate the most bookings (not just clicks)? Shift budget to high-converting keywords, pause low-converting ones.

## Common Hair Salon Google Ads Mistakes

**Using broad match for all keywords**: Broad match lets Google serve your ad for tangentially related searches ("cosmetology school near me," "hair products wholesale"). Use Phrase Match ("[keyword]") or Exact Match ([keyword]) for your Tier 1 and 2 campaigns.

**No negative keywords**: Not adding "jobs," "school," "products," "DIY" negatives wastes 20–30% of your budget on irrelevant clicks immediately.

**Sending traffic to homepage**: Your homepage is designed to introduce your salon. Your Google Ads landing page should be designed to get one thing: a booking. They're different jobs.

**Running ads 24/7**: Hair salons mostly get bookings during certain hours (Tuesday–Saturday, 9am–7pm for most markets). Use ad scheduling to concentrate your budget during peak booking times and reduce spend at 2am.

**Giving up after 2 weeks**: Google Ads needs 4–6 weeks to collect enough data to optimize. The first 2 weeks have the highest CPC and lowest conversion rates. Most of the improvement happens in weeks 3–8. Salons that pause after 2 weeks never see the ROI that comes from optimization.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should I run branded keywords (my salon name)?**

If your salon name has search volume, yes. Run a branded campaign ($20–$50/month) to ensure you appear at the top for your own name — especially if competitors might bid on it. Branded keyword CPCs are almost always under $1.

**Q: Can I run Google Ads without a website?**

You need somewhere to send traffic. If you don't have a website, you can use your Google Business Profile as a landing destination — but conversion rates are lower because you have less control over the presentation. A simple 1-page landing page with a booking button outperforms GBP as an ad destination.

**Q: How do I know if my Google Ads are actually profitable?**

Track cost per new client booking. If your ad spend / booked appointments = $X, and your average new client generates $Y in first-visit revenue + $Z in lifetime value, you know your ROI. Most salons should target under $50 cost per new client booking in mid-sized markets.
