## What the $250 Minimum Actually Gets You

At $250 and a $20 CPM, you're buying **12,500 impressions**. That sounds like a lot, but audio ads on Spotify require **frequency** to work — most listener research suggests 5–7 exposures before an audio ad drives action. With a 5km geo-targeting radius in a mid-sized US city, your addressable audience might be 40,000–60,000 free-tier listeners. Your $250 budget reaches each person **less than once**.

This is why $250 campaigns often feel like they did nothing — because they statistically almost did.

**The real minimum to see results: $500–$1,000 per month**, sustained for at least 3 months. Here's why:

- $1,000/month at $20 CPM = 50,000 impressions
- With tight local geo (5km radius), that's realistic frequency (2–3x per month per listener)
- Sustained for 3 months = 150,000 total impressions, 6–9 touches per listener
- At that frequency, brand recall lifts measurably and actions follow

<Callout type="tip">
Spotify Ad Studio is self-serve and lets you start, pause, and restart campaigns freely. Start with a $500 test over 4 weeks, monitor reach and frequency in the dashboard, then scale if the numbers make sense.
</Callout>

## Spotify Ad Studio Pricing Breakdown in 2026

Spotify uses a CPM model for Ad Studio campaigns. Your actual CPM varies based on:

**Targeting specificity:** Geo-targeting to a 2km radius costs more than targeting a full state. Narrow demographic targeting (age + gender + playlist genre) also increases CPM.

**Format:** 15-second audio ads run at lower CPM than 30-second. Video "Sponsored Sessions" are higher CPM but offer longer format.

**Inventory demand:** CPMs fluctuate by season (higher in Q4, lower in Q2) and by time of day.

Typical 2026 CPM ranges for local business audio ads:
- **National targeting (US):** $12–$18
- **City-level targeting:** $18–$28
- **Radius targeting (5km):** $22–$35
- **With interest or playlist overlay:** +$3–$8 on top

## What Kind of Audio Ad Can You Create for Free

Here's one of the best-kept secrets about Spotify Ad Studio: **voice-over production is included at no extra cost.** You write the script (max 75 words for a 30-second spot, 40 words for 15 seconds), choose a voice actor from their library, add background music from their licensed catalog, and Spotify produces the ad automatically.

This means your $500 campaign budget goes entirely to media — you don't need to hire a production studio. The quality is serviceable, not broadcast-quality, but for a local business with a clear message, it's more than enough.

What makes a good Spotify audio ad for a local business:

1. **Name your location in the first 5 words.** "Hey Chicago coffee lovers..." immediately signals relevance.
2. **One clear offer.** Not "great coffee, great vibes, great staff." Just: "First drink free this week — mention Spotify at the counter."
3. **Your URL spoken clearly at the end.** Keep it simple (yourshop.com, not yourshopname-bookings-chicago.com).
4. **Background music that matches your brand.** A yoga studio and a CrossFit gym should sound different.

Script: "Good morning, Austin — we're [Shop Name] on South Congress, and your first cold brew this week is on us. Come say hi at [address] or order ahead at [URL]." After 60 days: 340+ redemptions tracked via promo code. Cost per new customer: $4.12.

## Who Should (and Shouldn't) Use Spotify Ads

Spotify ads work well for:

**Coffee shops and cafés** — Spotify listeners are your customer. They're often listening during commutes, morning routines, and work. Audio ads reach them before they decide where to stop.

**Fitness studios** — workout playlist listeners are an obvious fit. Target people listening to "workout" or "running" playlists in your area.

**Hair salons and barbershops** — music listeners, social media-heavy demographic. Spotify's targeting by music genre (pop, hip-hop, R&B) can reach your specific customer.

**Event-based businesses** — bars, restaurants, escape rooms promoting specific events respond well to time-sensitive audio ads.

Spotify ads are less effective for:

- **High-consideration purchases** (expensive services where someone needs to research extensively before acting)
- **Emergency services** (plumbers, electricians — people search Google in a crisis, they don't think "what was that audio ad?")
- **Very niche audiences** where the Spotify user base doesn't overlap well with your customer

<Callout type="warning">
Spotify ads are **awareness and recall**, not direct response. Don't expect someone to pause their run, pull out their phone, and book an appointment the second they hear your ad. The conversion path is: hear ad → see your name later → search Google → book. Track this with brand name search lift in Google Search Console, not last-click attribution.
</Callout>

## How Spotify Ads Fit Into a Broader Local Marketing Strategy

Spotify works best as a **layer on top of** Google Ads and a solid Google Business Profile — not as a replacement. Here's the logic:

1. Someone listens to Spotify running in Wicker Park, hears your yoga studio ad
2. Three weeks later, they search "yoga studio near me" on Google
3. They see your Google Maps listing (optimized with 80+ reviews) AND your Google Search Ad
4. They recognize the name — "oh, I've heard of them" — and click
5. They book

The Spotify ad didn't get last-click credit. But it dramatically increased the probability of that person clicking your result over a competitor's. This is how audio advertising works, and it's measurable through brand search lift and direct traffic growth.

## Step-by-Step: Starting a Spotify Ad Studio Campaign

1. Go to [adstudio.spotify.com](https://adstudio.spotify.com) and create a free account
2. Click "Create Ad" and choose "Audio Ad"
3. Set your campaign objective: **Awareness** (reach) or **Consideration** (visits/actions)
4. Define your audience: location (radius or city), age, gender, genre interests
5. Write your script (Spotify shows you a word count and read time preview)
6. Choose voice, background music, and let Spotify generate the ad
7. Set your budget (minimum $250, recommend $500+) and flight dates
8. Launch — ads typically go live within 24–48 hours after review

Total time to launch: 30–60 minutes. No agency required for the basics.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is Spotify Ad Studio's minimum budget in 2026?**
The official minimum is $250 per campaign. However, $500–$1,000/month for 3+ months is the practical minimum to generate enough frequency (5–7 exposures) to see measurable impact on brand awareness and foot traffic.

**How much does Spotify advertising cost for a local business?**
CPMs for local audio ads typically range from $18–$35 depending on geographic targeting tightness and demographic overlays. At $25 CPM, $1,000 buys 40,000 impressions — realistic frequency in a focused local area.

**Can I create a Spotify ad for free?**
Your Spotify Ad Studio account is free. Voice-over production and background music are included at no cost. You only pay for the media buy (minimum $250 total).

**How long does a Spotify audio ad have to be?**
Spotify Ad Studio supports 15 or 30-second audio ads. 15-second ads are cheaper and can still be effective if your message is tight. 30-second ads allow more context and storytelling.

**Do Spotify ads work for local businesses?**
Yes, with realistic expectations. Spotify ads build brand familiarity and recall. They work best when layered with Google Ads and an optimized Google Business Profile, creating multiple touchpoints that compound over time.

**How do I track if Spotify ads are driving results?**
Monitor: (1) brand name searches in Google Search Console, (2) direct traffic in Google Analytics, (3) coupon or promo code redemptions if you include one in your ad, (4) ask new customers how they heard about you. Don't rely on last-click attribution — Spotify is an awareness channel.

**Can I target specific neighborhoods with Spotify ads?**
Yes. Spotify Ad Studio allows radius targeting down to approximately 1 mile (in the US). This lets you target a specific neighborhood or trade area precisely.
