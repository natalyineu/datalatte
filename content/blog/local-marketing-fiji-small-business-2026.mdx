Fiji is the Pacific's most well-known tourism destination — 920,000 people across 330 islands in the South Pacific, with Suva as the capital and Nadi as the tourism gateway city (home to Fiji's international airport). Fiji is famous for overwater bures, pristine reefs, world-class surfing (Cloudbreak), and extraordinary Fijian hospitality (known as "Bula spirit" from the universal greeting "Bula!").

Fiji uses the Fijian Dollar (FJD). English is an official language (alongside iTaukei Fijian and Fiji Hindi), making digital marketing relatively straightforward. Tourism is the dominant economic driver — Fiji attracts over 900,000 international visitors annually, primarily from Australia, New Zealand, and the US.

**Facebook dominant**: Fiji's social media centres on Facebook — community groups by island and region are where Fijians discover local businesses and share news. Facebook is genuinely universal among urban Fijians.

**Tourism is the economy**: For Nadi, the Mamanuca Islands, and the Coral Coast, virtually all commercial activity revolves around international tourists. Australian and New Zealand visitors are the primary markets.

**English native**: Fiji's English-medium education system means English content reaches all educated Fijians. No translation barrier for most commercial marketing.

### Key Fiji Area Keywords

```
"hair salon Suva Fiji" (capital local)
```

**Key areas**: Suva (capital, government/commercial), Nadi (tourism gateway — hotels, restaurants, shops near airport), Lautoka (second city), Mamanuca Islands (day trip islands from Nadi), Yasawa Islands (remote luxury), Coral Coast (highway between Nadi and Suva), Savusavu (eco-tourism north).

## Three Fijian Business Examples

### 🏄 Surf/Water Sports School, Nadi/Coral Coast

**Strategy**: English Instagram showcasing Fijian surf (Cloudbreak content gets global surf community attention), Google Search Ads in English ("surf lessons Fiji", "Cloudbreak surfing"), TripAdvisor listing, Google Maps, WhatsApp/email for Australian and NZ bookings.

**Budget**: FJD 1,100/month (≈$496): FJD 490 Google Ads, FJD 400 Meta Ads, FJD 210 content.

### 🍽️ Restaurant, Nadi

**Strategy**: English Instagram food photography, TripAdvisor listing (essential for airport/hotel tourist discovery), Google Maps, Facebook for Fiji community, WhatsApp for reservations.

**Budget**: FJD 890/month (≈$401): FJD 390 Google Ads, FJD 330 Meta Ads, FJD 170 content.

### 💇 Hair/Beauty Salon, Suva

**Strategy**: Instagram transformation content in English, Facebook for Suva community groups, Google Maps, Google Search Ads, WhatsApp for appointments.

**Budget**: FJD 670/month (≈$302): FJD 290 Google Ads, FJD 260 Meta Ads, FJD 120 content.

## Fiji Marketing Calendar

| Period | Opportunity |

| February 14 | Valentine's Day |
| May | Mother's Day (2nd Sunday May) |

| July | Diwali preparation (significant Indo-Fijian population) |

**Dry season tourism peak (May-October)**: Fiji's dry season aligns with Australian/New Zealand winter escape season and European summer. This is the peak visitor period. Tourism businesses should run highest Google Ads spend April-September to capture planners.

**Diwali (October)**: Fiji's Indo-Fijian community (approximately 37% of the population, descended from Indian indentured labourers) makes Diwali a major national celebration. Indian-owned businesses and the beauty sector see significant demand.

**Fiji Day (October 10)**: Independence from Britain in 1970. National celebration with commercial activity.

## FAQ

**English, iTaukei, or Fiji Hindi for marketing?**
English for all commercial marketing — Fiji's English-medium education means universal comprehension. iTaukei Fijian phrases ("Bula!" — hello, "Vinaka" — thank you) create authentic warmth and cultural connection. Fiji Hindi content can reach the Indo-Fijian community specifically. Most businesses default to English.

**How important is TripAdvisor in Fiji?**
Critical for tourist-facing businesses. Australian and NZ tourists rely heavily on TripAdvisor for Fiji restaurant and experience selection. Accumulating TripAdvisor reviews should be a deliberate priority for any business serving international visitors.
