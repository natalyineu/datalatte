March Madness is three weeks of compressed, high-attention sports content that reaches an audience fundamentally different from NFL or NBA viewers. The 68-team NCAA Tournament draws fans who don't watch regular-season college basketball — casual fans, bracket participants, coworkers who only care about "their" team. That broad, engaged audience makes it one of the best live sports CTV advertising opportunities of the year for local businesses.

## Why March Madness Is Different From Other Sports Advertising

March Madness has unique characteristics that affect how you should plan your campaign:

**Compressed timeline**: 3 weeks, mid-March to early April. Unlike NFL (5 months) or MLB (6 months), you have a short window. Front-loading your budget in the first 2 weeks (when the most games air and viewership is highest) outperforms spreading spend evenly.

**Bracket culture drives engagement**: An estimated 70M+ Americans fill out NCAA brackets, creating personal investment in games across the country — not just local-market matchups. A business in Charlotte gets good response rates even during Midwest Regional games.

**Audience breadth**: March Madness skews younger than NFL (18–44 vs. 25–54), includes more female viewers than typical sports content, and reaches college-educated households at higher rates. For businesses targeting younger professionals or college communities, it's excellent demographic alignment.

**Multi-screen behavior**: Viewers frequently watch multiple games simultaneously on a phone/tablet while a TV game plays. This dual-screen engagement makes QR codes in CTV ads especially effective during March Madness.

## Where March Madness Streams in 2026

The NCAA Tournament is broadcast across CBS (broadcast), TBS, TNT, and TruTV (cable) — and streams on the following platforms:

**Paramount+ (CBS games)**: CBS games stream live on Paramount+. Local business advertising on Paramount+ during NCAA games is available through ViacomCBS's ad platform or programmatic DSPs.

**Max (formerly HBO Max — TBS/TNT games)**: Warner Bros. Discovery's streaming platform carries TBS and TNT tournament games. Ad inventory is available through programmatic platforms.

**NCAA March Madness Live app**: The official tournament app streams all 67 games. Ad inventory is sold through Turner Sports / Warner Bros. Discovery.

**Hulu Live TV / YouTube TV / Sling**: These vMVPD services carry CBS, TBS, TNT, and TruTV through their channel packages, making tournament content available to cord-cutters. Advertising within these streams is sold by the platforms.

**Key access point for local advertisers**: The most practical entry for local businesses is buying "sports CTV" inventory through a DSP (StackAdapt, Simpli.fi, The Trade Desk) that aggregates Paramount+, Max, and vMVPD inventory. You don't pick specific games — you buy against the sports content category in your geo — but your impressions will be served heavily around March Madness during the tournament window.

## CPM Benchmarks for March Madness CTV

| Timing | CPM Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| First Round (days 1–4) | $35–$55 | High volume, efficient CPMs |
| Second Round | $40–$60 | Viewership builds as brackets shake out |
| Sweet 16 | $50–$70 | Premium — 16 marquee matchups |
| Elite Eight | $55–$80 | Near-peak viewership |
| Final Four | $70–$110 | National audience, highest non-Super Bowl CPMs |
| Championship Game | $90–$140 | Approaches Super Bowl territory |

**Budget recommendation for local businesses**: Concentrate spend in the First Round through Sweet 16 window (weeks 1–2). CPMs are 30–40% lower than the Final Four while reaching a comparable audience size. The Final Four and Championship are dominated by national advertisers with much larger budgets.

## Who Watches March Madness (Demographic Breakdown)

Understanding the audience helps align your business with the opportunity:

- **Age**: 18–44 core, with particularly strong 25–39 representation — younger than NFL
- **Gender**: 58% male / 42% female — one of the most gender-balanced sports audiences
- **Education**: Over-indexes heavily toward college graduates and current college students
- **Income**: Skews mid-to-upper income — bracket culture is correlated with disposable income
- **Location**: Strong in markets with major college programs (SEC country, Big Ten region, ACC cities) but genuinely national

**Best-fit local businesses**:
- Bars, sports bars, restaurants: "Watch the tournament here" is a natural fit
- Pizza and food delivery: Game day ordering spikes during March Madness
- Gyms and wellness: College-educated, active demographic
- Quick-service restaurants near college campuses: High geographic overlap
- Retail and home goods: Broad 25–44 audience with spending power

## Setting Up Your March Madness CTV Campaign

**Step 1: Choose your access point**

For $2,000–$5,000 budgets: Use a DSP like StackAdapt or Simpli.fi. Select "Sports" content category, set your geographic radius, and run the campaign from Selection Sunday (mid-March) through the Sweet 16 (end of third week).

For $5,000+ budgets: Contact Warner Bros. Discovery's local ad sales team for Turner Sports inventory (TBS/TNT games), or NBCUniversal for Paramount+ CBS games. Direct buys get better placement control.

**Step 2: Set your flight dates**

The tournament runs across 3 weekends + weekdays in between:
- **Week 1**: First and Second Rounds — maximum game volume, best CPM efficiency
- **Week 2**: Sweet 16 and Elite Eight — higher CPMs, intense viewership
- **Week 3**: Final Four + Championship — premium pricing, national audience

Spend 60% of your budget in weeks 1–2 and 40% in week 3 for the best balance of reach and efficiency.

**Step 3: Build tournament-specific creative**

Don't run your generic brand ad during March Madness. Create a tournament-specific version:

*"Fill out your bracket. Then come fill up on [your food/drink] at [Business Name]. Game Day specials all tournament long."*

Or for fitness/wellness: *"3 weeks of upsets, buzzer-beaters, and brackets. Your fitness goals don't wait for overtime. [Business Name] — fits in your schedule."*

Tournament-themed creative outperforms generic ads during March Madness by 25–35% in recall tests.

**Step 4: Time your ads to game flow**

If your DSP supports dayparting, concentrate during:
- Afternoon games (noon–6pm ET weekdays, first round): Viewers at home or watching at work
- Evening games (7–11pm ET): Peak engagement window
- Game breaks and halftime: When viewers are most receptive to ads

## Bracket Promotion Integration

March Madness offers a unique marketing hook: the bracket. 70M people fill them out. Integrate this into your campaign:

**Bracket giveaway** (highly effective): "Submit your bracket at [yourbusiness.com/bracket] to win [prize]." Even a $200 prize generates hundreds of submissions, email captures, and social shares if promoted through CTV ads and Instagram simultaneously.

**Pick-to-win** for restaurants/bars: "Predict tonight's winner at the door and get [offer]." Simple in-person mechanic that drives foot traffic during games.

**The Final Four special**: Announce a promotion specifically for the Final Four weekend in your week 2 CTV creative. Creates urgency and gives fans a reason to choose your location for the event.

## Combining CTV With March Madness Social

March Madness is one of the highest social media engagement events of the year. CTV + social creates a multiplier:

- Run CTV ads for awareness during game broadcasts
- Run Instagram/Facebook Stories ads with bracket-themed creative during the same period
- Use your email list to announce your tournament specials the week before the tournament starts

The combination of TV-scale reach (CTV) + mobile-first engagement (social) during March Madness creates the awareness-to-action funnel that drives measurable foot traffic and online orders.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to sponsor the NCAA Tournament specifically to advertise during it?**

No. You're buying ad inventory on streaming platforms that carry tournament games — you're not sponsoring the tournament. NCAA sponsorship is a separate (and very expensive) national arrangement. Local CTV ads run within the stream through the normal ad-supported viewing experience.

**Q: Is March Madness more or less expensive than NFL for local CTV?**

March Madness regular-round games (First/Second Round) are typically 20–30% less expensive per CPM than NFL regular season games. The Sweet 16 and beyond approaches NFL pricing. For budget-conscious local advertisers, the first two rounds are the most efficient window.

**Q: What if the local college team gets eliminated early — does that hurt my campaign?**

If you're specifically targeting fans of the local team (like running a campaign near a university during their team's run), early elimination reduces local viewership of that team's games. But March Madness viewership remains high regardless — fans shift to following other story lines. A restaurant advertising "Game Day specials all tournament" isn't dependent on a specific team's run.

**Q: How far in advance should I plan my March Madness CTV campaign?**

Start planning in January for a mid-March tournament. Submit creative to your DSP or platform by early March. The tournament bracket isn't set until Selection Sunday (typically second Sunday in March), but your campaign targeting, budget, and creative should be ready to activate within 48 hours of Selection Sunday.
