Azerbaijan is a South Caucasian country of 10 million people on the Caspian Sea — an oil-rich state that transformed Baku from a Soviet-era city into a gleaming modern capital with Formula One racing and the Eurovision Song Contest on its résumé. Baku is one of the most architecturally striking capitals in the former Soviet space, blending a UNESCO-listed medieval Old City with futuristic towers and luxury brands. Ganja is the second city; Sumqayit is an industrial city north of Baku.

Azerbaijan's digital marketing environment is a mix of Western and CIS platforms — Instagram is dominant for lifestyle and business discovery, Facebook has broad reach, and Google is the primary search engine. Russian is widely understood, but Azerbaijani (Azərbaycan dili) is the primary language and should be used for all domestic marketing.

**Instagram is Azerbaijan's primary business discovery platform**: Unlike neighbouring Georgia and Armenia where Facebook leads, Azerbaijan leans more heavily on Instagram for business discovery, beauty, food, and lifestyle. Baku's affluent consumer class uses Instagram heavily for shopping and service discovery.

**WhatsApp dominant messaging**: WhatsApp is the universal communication tool across all demographics in Azerbaijan. WhatsApp Business is essential — all customer bookings, inquiries, and follow-ups happen via WhatsApp.

**Oil-driven affluence**: Baku's oil wealth has created a strong luxury and premium services market. High-end beauty salons, restaurants, and lifestyle businesses command CPCs higher than many larger countries.

## Three Azerbaijani Business Examples

### 💇 Premium Beauty Salon, Narimanov Baku

**Strategy**: Instagram showcasing premium beauty services with Azerbaijani and Russian content, Facebook for Baku community groups, Google Search Ads, Google Maps, WhatsApp for appointments. Focus on luxury positioning — Baku premium market is strong.

**Budget**: AZN 800/month (≈$471): AZN 330 Google Ads, AZN 300 Meta Ads, AZN 170 content.

### ☕ Specialty Café, Baku Centre

**Strategy**: Instagram with Baku modern café aesthetic (Azerbaijani and Russian language), Facebook for Baku foodie groups, Google Maps listing, Google Search Ads, WhatsApp for reservations.

**Budget**: AZN 600/month (≈$353): AZN 250 Google Ads, AZN 220 Meta Ads, AZN 130 content.

### 🏋️ Premium Fitness Club, Yasamal

**Strategy**: Instagram fitness content in Azerbaijani, Facebook for Baku health community, Google Search Ads, Google Maps, WhatsApp for membership.

**Budget**: AZN 700/month (≈$412): AZN 290 Google Ads, AZN 260 Meta Ads, AZN 150 content.

**Novruz Bayramı (March 20-21)**: The spring equinox festival is Azerbaijan's most important national celebration — shared with Iran, Afghanistan, and Central Asian Turkic peoples. Four symbolic Tuesdays precede Novruz (Su Çərşənbəsi, Od Çərşənbəsi, Torpaq Çərşənbəsi, Külək Çərşənbəsi). Gifts, beauty appointments, new clothes, and elaborate table settings (xonça) are traditional. Start campaigns March 5.

**Ramadan**: Azerbaijan is majority Muslim and Ramadan is observed, though Azerbaijan is a secular state and fasting is personal. Iftar restaurant events and evening activity increases are commercially significant.

## FAQ

**Azerbaijani or Russian for marketing?**
Azerbaijani (Latin script, since 1991) is the official and national language — use it for all domestic marketing. Russian is understood by most adults (post-Soviet) and is appropriate for supplementary content. Avoid Cyrillic — Azerbaijani uses Latin script.

**Is Instagram really more important than Facebook in Azerbaijan?**
For beauty, lifestyle, and food businesses — yes. Instagram's visual format matches Baku's aspirational consumer culture. Facebook is still important for community groups and cross-demographic reach. Run both for maximum coverage.
