SMS has a 98% open rate. Hair salon email marketing has about 20%. Yet most salons still rely on email for client communication, wondering why campaigns get ignored.

The math is simple: if you send 100 text messages to your client list, 98 of them will be read — usually within 3 minutes. If you send 100 emails, 20 open them, 8 click, and 2–3 take action.

For time-sensitive salon communication — appointment reminders, last-minute openings, seasonal offers — text beats email every time.

## What Salon SMS Marketing Actually Covers

SMS marketing for salons falls into three categories, each requiring different approaches:

**1. Operational texts**: Appointment reminders, confirmations, and cancellation notifications. These are expected by clients, appreciated, and have near-perfect open and action rates.

**2. Re-engagement texts**: Messages to clients who haven't booked recently. These have the highest revenue potential but require careful timing and messaging.

**3. Promotional texts**: New service announcements, seasonal offers, last-minute availability. High return on investment when sent to the right list at the right time.

## Compliance First: Getting Opt-In Right

You cannot text clients without explicit consent. Here's how to collect it properly:

**At booking**: Add a checkbox to your online booking flow or intake form: "I'd like to receive appointment reminders and occasional exclusive offers via text message. [Opt-in checkbox]" — this must be an unchecked box they actively check, not pre-checked.

**In person**: "Do you want to receive appointment reminders by text? It's easier than email for most people." If they say yes, record it in your system. If you're using a booking software with SMS capability (Vagaro, GlossGenius, Square), it handles consent logging automatically.

**TCPA compliance**: The Telephone Consumer Protection Act requires documented consent, an easy opt-out method ("Reply STOP to unsubscribe"), and your business name in every message. All major SMS platforms (Twilio, SlickText, SimpleTexting) handle these requirements automatically.

## The 8 SMS Templates That Drive Salon Revenue

### Template 1: Appointment Reminder

**When to send**: 24 hours before appointment AND 2 hours before

**24-hour reminder**:
"Hi [Name]! Just a reminder: you're booked for [service] with [Stylist] tomorrow at [time]. Reply C to confirm or CHANGE to reschedule. — [Salon Name] 📍 [address]"

**2-hour reminder**:
"See you soon! Your appointment at [Salon Name] is in 2 hours at [time]. Running late? Just text us. Reply STOP to opt out."

**Why two messages**: The 24-hour reminder allows cancellation with enough notice for rebooking. The 2-hour reminder dramatically reduces no-shows for clients who forget same-day. Combined, these two texts reduce no-shows by 35–40% compared to no reminders.

### Template 2: Booking Confirmation

**When to send**: Immediately after booking

"[Name], your [service] appointment with [Stylist] is confirmed for [day], [date] at [time]. Directions: [link]. Questions? Text us here. — [Salon Name]. Reply STOP to opt out."

### Template 3: Post-Appointment Review Request

**When to send**: 3–5 hours after appointment

"Thanks for coming in today, [Name]! We hope you love your new look. If you have 2 minutes, a Google review helps our small business grow so much: [Google review link]. — [Stylist Name] at [Salon Name]. Reply STOP to opt out."

**Why this works**: Short, personal, clear action. Sending 3–5 hours after (not immediately) gives the client time to show off their new style before you ask them to review it.

### Template 4: Rebooking Prompt

**When to send**: 5–6 weeks after last appointment (adjust for your service cadence)

"Hi [Name], it's been about 6 weeks since your last visit — your hair is probably ready for some love! 😊 Ready to book? [booking link]. Your usual slot on [day] is available. — [Salon Name]. Reply STOP to opt out."

**Performance**: Rebooking prompts sent at the right interval (matched to the client's typical booking frequency) convert at 25–35%. At 50 clients eligible per month and 30% conversion, that's 15 appointments recovered automatically.

### Template 5: Last-Minute Availability

**When to send**: Morning of day with cancellations, targeting clients you know are flexible

"[Name], we just had a cancellation open up for this [afternoon/morning] at [time] with [Stylist]. Interested? Reply YES and I'll book you in. — [Salon Name]. Reply STOP to opt out."

**Key**: Send this to clients who have previously responded to last-minute offers or who have mentioned scheduling flexibility. Sending it to everyone is annoying; sending it to the right 10–15 people fills the slot.

**Conversion rate**: 30–50% of last-minute offers convert when sent to the right segment. This is often the highest-ROI campaign type by far.

### Template 6: Seasonal or Service Launch

**When to send**: 1 week before a seasonal event or new service launch

"[Name], summer is here! 🌞 We're now offering [new service or seasonal treatment] at [Salon Name]. Introductory offer: [specific offer]. Book in the next 7 days here: [booking link]. Only [X] slots available this month. Reply STOP to opt out."

**Best timing for seasonal messages**:
- Balayage/highlight launch: March (pre-spring refresh)
- Keratin/smoothing: September (back-to-school)
- Holiday party styles: November
- Color correction specials: January (new year refresh)

### Template 7: Win-Back for Lapsed Clients

**When to send**: When a client hasn't booked in 90+ days

"Hi [Name], it's been a while — we miss you! 💛 Your [favorite service] is waiting. Come back this month and get 20% off: [booking link]. Offer expires [date]. Hope to see you soon! — [Salon Name]. Reply STOP to opt out."

**Note**: Don't offer discounts to clients who book regularly — you're training them to wait for discounts. Reserve win-back offers for genuinely lapsed clients (90+ days).

### Template 8: Birthday Offer

**When to send**: On their birthday (morning)

"Happy Birthday, [Name]! 🎂 From all of us at [Salon Name], we'd love to help you celebrate. Enjoy [offer] on your next visit this month. Book here: [booking link]. Have a wonderful day! Reply STOP to opt out."

## Timing and Frequency Rules

**Best days to send marketing texts**: Tuesday through Thursday. Avoid Mondays (everyone's overwhelmed) and Fridays (people are already in weekend mode and may resent marketing).

**Best times**: 10am–12pm and 2pm–4pm. Never before 9am or after 8pm (TCPA restriction for non-operational texts).

**Frequency cap**: For promotional texts, no more than 2–3 per month. Operational texts (reminders, confirmations) don't count toward this cap — clients expect and appreciate them.

**Opt-out rate warning sign**: If your opt-out rate on any text exceeds 2%, your messaging frequency or content needs adjustment. Normal opt-out rates are 0.1–0.5%.

## Which Platform to Use

**Vagaro, GlossGenius, Square Appointments, Fresha**: All include SMS appointment reminders built-in. If you're already using one of these, start with what's already in your system before adding a separate SMS platform.

**SlickText**: $29/month for 500 messages. Best for salons that want a dedicated SMS marketing platform separate from their booking system. Good segmentation tools for running campaigns to specific client groups.

**SimpleTexting**: $29/month for 500 credits. Similar to SlickText with strong analytics.

**Twilio**: Developer-friendly, used with automation tools like Make.com or Zapier to trigger personalized messages at specific moments. Most flexible but requires setup time. Best for salons with someone technical on staff or willing to use Make.com.

**Attentive**: Enterprise-level SMS platform — overkill for most salons. Designed for e-commerce.

## Building Your SMS List

Start with existing clients who are already opt-in through your booking system. Most booking platforms have an opt-in checkbox built into the process.

**How to grow it**: At every appointment check-in: "Do you want appointment reminders by text? It's easier than email, and we'll occasionally let you know about openings and offers." Most clients say yes when asked in person.

**Goal**: 80%+ of your active client list opted in for SMS within 6 months of starting. At 80% opt-in, you're reaching the vast majority of your client base directly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Won't clients find frequent texts annoying?**

Operational texts (confirmations and reminders) almost never annoy clients — they're useful. Promotional texts become annoying when they're too frequent (more than 2–3/month) or irrelevant. Personalized, relevant, infrequent marketing texts have very low opt-out rates. The goal is quality over quantity.

**Q: What if a client replies to a reminder text with a question?**

Your SMS platform should either route responses to your phone or a team inbox. Respond to all direct replies promptly — treating an SMS conversation as one-directional is a missed opportunity and can frustrate clients. Most booking-integrated systems handle this automatically.

**Q: Do I need a separate phone number for business SMS?**

Yes — use a dedicated business number, not your personal cell. SMS platforms provide dedicated numbers. This keeps business and personal separate and ensures you're compliant with TCPA (using a business number in all business communications).

**Q: Can I send the same message to my entire client list?**

Technically yes, but it underperforms compared to segmented messages. A win-back message sent to everyone (including clients who came in last week) will generate opt-outs. A last-minute availability message sent to clients who live or work near the salon converts better than one sent to your entire list. Segment by last visit date, service type, and location when possible.
