## FAQ: Your Google Ads Keyword Questions Answered

### 1. How do I find low-competition keywords for dog grooming?
Use the "Keyword Gap" tool with competitors' domains. Look for 20-30 keywords with under 0.3 competition score and >100 monthly searches.

### 2. Should I use exact match for all keywords?
No. In 2026, the optimal split is:
- 40% modified broad
- 30% phrase match
- 20% exact match
- 10% broad match (with 5-7 negatives)

### 3. How often should I update my keyword list?
Every 2 weeks, but focus on:
- New Google Trends spikes
- Competitor changes
- Seasonal fluctuations

### 4. What's the best way to test new keywords?
Create a "Testing" ad group with 3-5 keywords. Set daily budget to $10 and run for 14 days. Use the "Top Converting Keywords" report in Google Ads to evaluate.

### 5. Can I use the same keywords for search and display ads?
No. For display retargeting, use:
- Dynamic remarketing for service pages
- Contextual targeting with broader pet-related terms
- Lookalike audiences based on service keywords

### 6. How do I handle negative keywords?
Start with this core list:
```markdown
- cat
- self-grooming
- pet food
- pet stores
- vet
- dog training
```

Add 3-5 new negatives monthly using Google's "Search Terms Report."

### 7. What's the ideal keyword count per ad group?
2026 best practice: 15-20 keywords per ad group. Too many and your Quality Score drops. Too few and you miss opportunities.
