College football is arguably the most geographically loyal sports audience in America. NFL fans spread across national networks — but an Alabama fan in Tuscaloosa, a Michigan fan in Ann Arbor, or a Texas fan in Austin is watching every single Crimson Tide, Wolverines, or Longhorns game with intensity that most NFL viewers reserve only for playoffs. For local businesses, this makes college football CTV advertising one of the highest-relevance, most cost-efficient opportunities in live sports.

## Why College Football Works for Local Business CTV

Three factors combine to make college football particularly valuable for local advertisers:

**Geographic intensity**: College football fanbases are concentrated in specific markets far more than NFL. The entire state of Alabama watches Alabama football. Columbus, Ohio essentially shuts down for Buckeyes games. A business in any of these markets reaches a hyper-local, intensely engaged audience.

**Lower CPMs than NFL**: College football CTV CPMs range from $28–$55 for regular season games — 30–40% lower than comparable NFL inventory. You're still reaching a passionate live sports audience, just at more accessible prices.

**Full fall calendar**: College football runs from Labor Day weekend through the College Football Playoff in January — 4+ months of consistent content on Saturdays. This is enough time to build meaningful frequency with your local audience.

## Where College Football Streams in 2026

College football is distributed across the most platforms of any major US sport:

**ESPN+ / ESPN / ABC (Disney)**:
Disney has the most comprehensive college football rights, including:
- SEC on ABC (Alabama, Georgia, LSU, Tennessee, etc.)
- Big 12 on ESPN/ABC
- All College Football Playoff games (12-team bracket)
- Extensive mid-level conference games on ESPN+

Games on ESPN/ABC also stream simultaneously on ESPN+. ESPN+ also carries overflow games from every major conference.

**Fox / FS1 / Tubi**:
Fox carries Big Ten football prominently (Michigan, Ohio State, Penn State, USC, Oregon). Fox Sports games stream on the Fox Sports app. Tubi (Fox's AVOD service) carries some content — and advertising on Tubi reaches a cost-efficient streaming audience.

**CBS / Paramount+**:
CBS's Saturday afternoon national game (typically a high-profile SEC matchup) streams on Paramount+. These are some of the most-watched college games of the week.

**NBC / Peacock**:
Notre Dame Football. All Notre Dame home games run exclusively on NBC and stream on Peacock. No other major college football program has an exclusive network deal. Notre Dame games are watched by alumni networks across the country, making them valuable beyond just the South Bend market.

**Pac-12 / Big Ten Network / conference streaming**:
Conference-specific networks and streaming apps carry overflow games. These typically have limited third-party advertising access but may be available through programmatic DSPs.

Compared to NFL regular season CPMs of $45–$80, college football offers strong value — especially in the early and afternoon windows where CPMs are $28–$50 for genuinely passionate, engaged viewership.

## The College Market Opportunity

If your business is located in or near a college town or a city with a major university program, college football CTV is one of the best-targeted advertising vehicles you'll find.

**Why college markets are different**:
- 30–70% of the population may be alumni or students for the local university
- Game day creates concentrated social activity and spending
- Football season aligns with key commercial periods (back-to-school, fall, holiday approach)
- Alumni households skew educated and higher-income

**High-value college markets by conference**:
- **SEC**: Tuscaloosa, AL; Athens, GA; Baton Rouge, LA; Nashville, TN; Knoxville, TN; Auburn, AL
- **Big Ten**: Columbus, OH; Ann Arbor, MI; State College, PA; Madison, WI; Champaign, IL; Lincoln, NE
- **Big 12**: Austin, TX; Norman, OK; Manhattan, KS; Morgantown, WV; Fort Worth, TX
- **ACC**: Clemson, SC; Tallahassee, FL; Durham/Chapel Hill, NC; Blacksburg, VA

In these markets, targeting "sports CTV" on Saturdays September–November essentially means reaching the local population's primary social activity.

## How to Buy College Football CTV Ads

### For Disney/ESPN+ Content (Most Volume)

Disney's ESPN+ and ESPN/ABC carry the most college football of any platform. Access options:

**Programmatic DSP** (best for $1,500–$4,000/month):
- Use StackAdapt, Simpli.fi, or similar
- Target "Sports" and "College Sports" content categories
- Set geographic targeting to your market
- Run campaigns on Saturdays, September–January
- Include ESPN+ in inventory selection if the platform allows

**Disney Advertising partner** (for $5,000+/month):
- Work with an agency certified by Disney Advertising
- Buy local CTV spots around specific games or conferences relevant to your market
- More precise program selection: you can prioritize SEC, Big Ten, or your specific conference

### For Peacock/NBC (Notre Dame)

If you're in a market with strong Notre Dame affiliation (Chicago, South Bend, Boston, New York, major Catholic-university cities), Peacock's Notre Dame coverage is highly relevant:

- Contact NBCUniversal's local ad sales for your DMA
- Ask for "local CTV adjacency to Notre Dame Football on Peacock"
- Budget: $2,000–$5,000/month for the Notre Dame home game schedule (5–7 games)

### For Fox/FS1 Content (Big Ten)

Fox's Big Ten rights are the other major college football package. Access via:
- Fox's ad sales team for direct local buys
- Programmatic DSP buying Fox Sports CTV inventory

### The Practical Approach: Programmatic Saturdays

For most local businesses, the simplest and most cost-effective approach is:

1. Open a StackAdapt or Simpli.fi account (or work with an agency)
2. Create a CTV campaign targeting "Sports" content
3. Set daypart targeting to Saturday afternoons and evenings (noon–11pm)
4. Set geographic targeting to your metro area
5. Run September through early January
6. Budget $1,500–$2,500/month

Your ads will run across college football content on ESPN+, Paramount+, and Fox Sports streaming during Saturday game windows. You're not controlling which specific game — but you're in the right context, in the right geography, at the right time.

## College Football Season Calendar for Advertisers

| Period | What's Happening | Advertising Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 (Labor Day) | Season opener — high excitement, big ratings | "Welcome back football" messaging |
| September | Full regular season underway | Establish awareness before peak competition |
| October | Rivalry games building, conference races | Lean into local team / community angle |
| October–November | Final regular season push, rankings intense | Highest regular season viewership |
| Late November | Rivalry week (Ohio State-Michigan, Iron Bowl, etc.) | Peak regular season audiences |
| December | Conference Championships + bowl season | Natural "end of year" or "celebrate" messaging |
| January | College Football Playoff | Premium audience, national scope, highest CPMs |

**Best value window**: September through mid-October. Strong viewership, lower CPMs than the end-of-season crunch, and enough time in the season to build frequency before rivalries.

## Creative Ideas for College Football CTV

Unlike NFL where you're reaching a national sports audience, college football lets you target fan culture directly:

**Game day specials**: "Come watch the game at [Business Name]. Kickoff special: [offer]. [Specific day] only." This works especially well for bars, restaurants, and delivery services.

**"We love our team" messaging**: In college towns, businesses frequently identify themselves as supporters of the local team. This builds community affinity without needing sports trademark rights (you can't use team logos or names, but "proud supporter" language and game-day themes are safe).

**Alumni homecoming tie-ins**: Homecoming weekend at major universities drives massive foot traffic from returning alumni. A restaurant or bar that runs CTV ads the week before homecoming, followed by social media ads on homecoming weekend, can capture this traffic surge.

**The 15-second local formula**:
*"[Business Name] — [City]'s home for game day. [Specific offer]. Tonight's kickoff is at 7:30. See you there."*

Short, local, with a specific time reference. This performs better than generic sports ads because it's specifically anchored to the viewer's game-day ritual.

## College Football vs. NFL: Which Is Right for Your Business?

| Factor | College Football | NFL |
|---|---|---|
| CPM cost | $28–$55 (regular season) | $40–$80 (regular season) |
| Season length | 4+ months (Sep–Jan) | 5 months (Aug–Feb) |
| Game frequency | Multiple games every Saturday | 3 games per week (Thu/Sun/Mon) |
| Geographic loyalty | Very high (team = region) | High in franchise markets |
| Audience age | Younger (18–44 skews) | Slightly older (25–54) |
| Best market type | College towns, university cities | NFL franchise metro markets |
| Audience size per game | 2–20M (varies widely) | 10–25M (consistently high) |

**When to choose college football**: You're in a college town or major university metro, your budget is $1,500–$3,000/month, you want Saturday coverage, or your audience skews younger (18–44).

**When to choose NFL**: You're in a major metro without a dominant college program, your budget allows for premium CPMs, you need week-round coverage (Thursday + Sunday + Monday), or your audience skews 30–54.

Many businesses run both simultaneously with staggered spend: college football on Saturdays, NFL on select Thursday/Sunday/Monday nights.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I target fans of a specific team with college football CTV?**

Through programmatic DSPs, targeting is by content category and geography — not team. If you want to reach Alabama fans specifically, you target the DMA surrounding Tuscaloosa/Birmingham and run on Saturdays during the SEC fall calendar. The team is implied by the geography and timing.

Through Disney Advertising direct buys, it may be possible to specify SEC on ABC or specific game windows that align with your target team's schedule.

**Q: Is the College Football Playoff worth advertising around?**

Yes, if your budget allows for the higher CPMs ($65–$95). The CFP (now 12 teams) has expanded the playoff to multiple rounds of high-viewership games. First-round games (early January) at campus sites draw passionate local fans; the semifinal and final games are national-audience events. For businesses in CFP host cities, the month around the game creates significant visitor and local fan economic activity.

**Q: How does college football advertising on ESPN+ differ from watching on cable ESPN?**

ESPN (cable) and ESPN+ (streaming) audiences are separate. Some games are simulcast on both, reaching both streaming and cable viewers. ESPN+-exclusive games are streaming-only. When you buy through a programmatic DSP, you're reaching the streaming audience. Disney Advertising's platform can offer cross-platform buys across ESPN cable, ABC broadcast, and ESPN+ streaming simultaneously for larger budgets.

**Q: What's the minimum budget to run a meaningful college football CTV campaign?**

$1,500/month through a programmatic DSP is the practical minimum for generating enough impressions to matter. At that budget in a mid-size market (metro 250K–750K), you can reach 20,000–35,000 unique households per month — enough to build brand recognition among your local sports audience over a full fall season.
