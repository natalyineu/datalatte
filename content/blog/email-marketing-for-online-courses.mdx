### Build a Targeted Email List (No Spammy Lead Magnets)

Start with a simple opt-in: "Get my free 5-day email course on [topic]." For a yoga studio owner in Seattle, "5 Days to Better Posture" generated 300+ leads in 2 weeks. Use this exact formula:

1. Offer a hyper-specific resource (avoid "free guide")
2. Embed the opt-in on your course landing page
3. Use your Google Business Profile to drive traffic

<Callout type="tip">**Pro tip:** Add urgency with "Join 200+ students who started this course today" to boost opt-ins by 25%.</Callout>

For automation, [email & SMS marketing](/services/email-sms) tools like MailerLite or ConvertKit cost $15–$30/month. These let you send automated emails when someone signs up, abandons a purchase, or completes a lesson.

<Callout type="warning">Don’t send the same offer to everyone. Cold leads need education, warm leads want urgency ("Last 2 spots!"), and inactive users need re-engagement.</Callout>

Try these segments:
- **New subscribers:** Send a 3-email "why take this course" sequence
- **Abandoned carts:** Offer 10% off with a 24-hour deadline
- **Course completers:** Pitch advanced classes with "90% of you finished this course"

### Automate Without Over-Automating

Automation works best when it feels human. A coffee shop owner in Austin taught barista courses using this flow:

```
Day 1: "Hey [First Name], I'm Maria from Coffee University"
Day 3: "Watch this 2-minute video on espresso basics"
Day 7: "Join my live Q&A next Tuesday [calendar link]"
Day 10: "Enroll now and get free coffee beans"
```

<Callout type="coffee">DataLatte's favorite tactic: Add a PS to every email with your phone number. 1 in 5 replies turn into sales conversations.</Callout>

Use [AI agents & automation](/services/ai-agents) to personalize subject lines ("John, your barista certification is ready"). The right tools save 10+ hours/week while increasing open rates by 40%.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I get people to join my email list?**  
Start with a free resource that directly solves your ideal student's pain point. A fitness studio owner in Toronto used "7 Mobility Drills for Desk Workers" to get 500+ signups in a month.

**What's the best email frequency?**  
Test 1-2 emails/week for new subscribers, then 1 email/biweekly for engaged users. Use [analytics & reporting](/services/analytics) to track when open rates drop below 30%.

**Can I reuse course content in emails?**  
Absolutely. Split your course videos into 2-minute clips and send them as "teasers." A photography course creator increased enrollments by 65% using this method.

**Should I add a money-back guarantee?**  
Yes. "30-day full refund" reduces cart abandonment by 35%. Place it prominently in your email footer.

**How do I fix a dying email list?**  
Send a "tell me what you need" survey and offer a $10 gift card for responses. This re-engages 15-20% of inactive subscribers.

**Is it legal to sell course access via email?**  
Always get explicit opt-in (not cookie consent banners). Use double opt-in services for compliance.

**Can I do this with a small budget?**  
Start with free tools like Mailchimp (first 500 emails free). Spend $20/week on Facebook ads retargeting your email list.
