### Fresha, Square, or Vagaro: What You Need to Know
Let’s break down the pros and cons of each platform for a 4-chair hair salon in Chicago.

#### 1. **Fresha: Best for client retention**
Fresha focuses on reducing no-shows with SMS reminders and a client loyalty program. You pay **$49/month + 7.5% per booking**. For a salon booking 60 appointments weekly at $70 each, that’s $315/month in fees — over 40% of your revenue. But their AI chatbot can cut no-shows by 30%, saving you 12 lost appointments weekly.

<Callout type="tip">Use Fresha if you have 10+ staff and want to reduce no-shows. Pair with [email & SMS marketing](/services/email-sms) to boost loyalty program signups.</Callout>

#### 2. **Square: Best for simple pricing**
Square’s booking app costs **$29/month + 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction**. For a 3-chair Brooklyn nail bar with $5,000/month in bookings, fees drop to $150/month. It integrates seamlessly with Square’s point-of-sale system, so you can track inventory and payments in one place. But there’s no built-in loyalty program or advanced reporting.

#### 3. **Vagaro: Best for multi-location salons**
Vagaro charges **$99/month + 6.5% per booking**. For a franchisee managing 3 Austin spas, this scales better than Fresha. It includes appointment history tracking and employee scheduling. But the learning curve is steep — expect to spend 5 hours training staff to use all features.

<Callout type="warning">Vagaro’s 6.5% fee adds up faster than Square’s 2.9%. Only use it if you need multi-location management.</Callout>

### Integration with Your Tools
All three platforms integrate with Google Business Profile for online bookings, but Fresha’s integration is the weakest. Square works best with [local SEO services](/services/local-seo) to boost visibility in search. Vagaro connects with QuickBooks for accounting — vital for salons with 5+ employees.

<Callout type="coffee">I’ve helped 15 salons automate their bookings. Square wins most often for its simplicity and low fees — unless you need loyalty programs or franchise tools.</Callout>

### What About Hidden Fees?
All three platforms charge for transaction fees, but Vagaro and Fresha add monthly subscription costs. Square’s $29/month is the lowest baseline. For a Denver pet groomer with 20 weekly appointments, Square’s total fees would be $87/month vs. $150 for Fresha.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Which is best for a 1-chair hair salon?**  
A: Square is cheapest at $29/month. Avoid Fresha’s high per-booking fee unless you have 20+ weekly clients.

**Q: Does any platform work with my existing POS system?**  
A: Only Square integrates with its own POS. Vagaro and Fresha require separate systems.

**Q: Can I track client preferences?**  
A: Vagaro stores notes per client. Fresha lets you tag clients (e.g., “allergic to dyes”) but Square lacks this feature.

**Q: Which has the best mobile app?**  
A: Square’s app is the most user-friendly for on-the-go scheduling. Vagaro’s app is clunky for staff.

**Q: How do fees compare for a $10,000/month spa?**  
A: Square costs $350/month. Vagaro would charge $750. Fresha jumps to $800/month with 7.5% fees.

### Final Thoughts
If you’re a solo stylist in Toronto with 40 weekly bookings, Square is your best bet. For a 10-employee chain in Miami, Vagaro’s multi-location tools justify the higher cost. And if 30% of your clients no-show every week? Fresha’s reminders might be worth the extra $100/month.
