**Getting Started with TikTok Ads**

To start advertising on TikTok, you'll need to create a business account and set up a TikTok Ads account. This will give you access to TikTok's ad platform, where you can create and manage your ads. Don't worry if you're not tech-savvy – TikTok's ad platform is user-friendly and easy to navigate.

<Callout type="tip">Before creating your ad, make sure to set up your TikTok business account and claim your restaurant's profile. This will help you access valuable insights and analytics.</Callout>

When creating your ad, you'll need to choose your target audience, set a budget, and select your ad format. TikTok offers a range of ad formats, including video ads, image ads, and carousel ads. Each format has its own unique features and benefits, so be sure to choose the one that best suits your needs.

Video ads are a game-changer for restaurants on TikTok. They allow you to showcase your food, share behind-the-scenes content, and build a loyal following. With video ads, you can increase brand awareness, drive website traffic, and even generate leads.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I run a diner in Ohio. My food looks good but not “viral” good. Will TikTok ads still work?**

Yes, because you don’t need your food to go viral. You need it to be noticed by 500 people within 3 miles of your diner who are hungry right now. I’ve seen ads of a guy flipping a burger on a flat top with zero special effects get 10,000 views and 50 conversions. The algorithm cares about relevance and completion rate, not production value. Film your best-looking dish in natural light, add a text overlay with your address and hours, and run it at $5 a day. Test for a week.

**Q: Do I need to post organic TikToks before I run ads?**

No, but it helps. If you have zero organic content, TikTok’s algorithm has less information about your restaurant. That doesn’t mean ads won’t work — it means you need to give the algorithm stronger signals through targeting. Skip organic entirely if you don’t have time. Just make sure your ad content feels native. Don’t use stock footage. Film yourself. That’s enough.

**Q: How much should I spend per day on TikTok ads?**

Start at $10 per day for one week. That’s $70. If you spend $70 and don’t get any conversions, you have a targeting problem or a creative problem, not a budget problem. Scale up to $25 per day only after you see consistent conversions under $10 each. I’ve seen restaurants spend $5 a day and get 15 orders in a week. I’ve also seen restaurants spend $100 a day and get zero because their ad was boring.

**Q: How do I track in-store visits from a TikTok ad?**

TikTok has a “Store Visit” conversion metric, but it requires a business account with a verified address and at least 1,000 store visits in the last 90 days. Most small restaurants don’t qualify. Instead, use a trackable offer. Set up a landing page with a coupon code like “TIKTOK15” and ask customers to show it at checkout. That’s the most reliable way to measure offline conversions. If your staff forgets to ask, put a sign at the register.

**Q: What if my food looks terrible on video?**

Then fix your food. No, seriously — if your dishes don’t look appetizing on camera, they probably don’t look appetizing on the plate either. Get a cheap ring light ($30 on Amazon) and shoot at a 45-degree angle. Use garnishes. Clean the plate edges. If it still looks bad, shift your ad focus to something else: the atmosphere, a fast turnaround time, a customer testimonial. Nobody buys ugly food on TikTok.

**Q: Can I run TikTok ads myself or do I need to hire someone?**

You can run them yourself. The TikTok Ads Manager dashboard is straightforward. Create an account, upload your video, set your budget, pick your targeting, and hit launch. The learning curve is two hours. What you can’t do yourself is consistently improve the ad based on data. Most owners run one ad, get middling results, and give up. The value of working with someone is the iteration cycle — testing different hooks, swapping visuals, adjusting targeting. If you have time to do that yourself, go for it. If you don’t, pay someone who will.
