## Focus on the metrics that drive revenue

As a local business, your primary goal is to drive revenue. Here are the key metrics you should be tracking:

### 1. Revenue Growth

Revenue growth is the ultimate metric for any business. It's a clear indicator of your success, and it's what investors, lenders, and partners care about most. To track revenue growth, you'll need to compare your current revenue to your revenue from the same period last year.

### 2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC is the cost of acquiring a new customer. It's essential to track CAC because it helps you understand how much you're spending to acquire new customers. A high CAC can indicate that your marketing efforts are inefficient or that you're targeting the wrong audience.

### 3. Conversion Rate

Conversion rate is the percentage of website visitors who make a purchase or complete a desired action. It's a critical metric for e-commerce businesses, but it's also essential for brick-and-mortar stores. A high conversion rate indicates that your website or store is effectively converting visitors into customers.

## Don't forget about the little things

While revenue growth, CAC, and conversion rate are crucial metrics, don't forget about the little things that can make a big difference in your business. Here are a few metrics to keep an eye on:

* Social media engagement rate: This metric helps you understand how well your social media marketing efforts are resonating with your audience.
* Customer retention rate: This metric indicates how well you're retaining your existing customers.
<Callout type="tip">Track customer retention rate to identify areas for improvement in your customer service and loyalty programs.</Callout>
* Average order value (AOV): This metric helps you understand how much your customers are spending on average.

## What to track, and what to ignore

Here are a few metrics that are often recommended but have little to no impact on your growth:

* Website traffic: While website traffic is essential for e-commerce businesses, it's not as critical for brick-and-mortar stores or service-based businesses.
* Social media followers: Having a large following on social media doesn't necessarily translate to revenue growth.
<Callout type="warning">Don't get caught up in vanity metrics like website traffic and social media followers. Focus on metrics that drive revenue growth and customer acquisition.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Why is revenue growth so important?

A: Revenue growth is essential because it indicates how well your business is performing. It's a clear indicator of your success, and it's what investors, lenders, and partners care about most.

### Q: What's the average customer acquisition cost for a small business?

A: The average customer acquisition cost for a small business varies depending on the industry and marketing channels used. However, a general rule of thumb is to aim for a CAC of 20% or less of your average order value.

### Q: How do I track conversion rate for my brick-and-mortar store?

A: To track conversion rate for your brick-and-mortar store, you'll need to use a combination of analytics tools and in-store tracking methods. Consider using a point-of-sale system that integrates with your e-commerce platform to track sales and conversion rates.

### Q: Can I track revenue growth on my own, or do I need a consultant?

A: While it's possible to track revenue growth on your own, it's often beneficial to work with a consultant or marketing expert who can provide guidance and support.
