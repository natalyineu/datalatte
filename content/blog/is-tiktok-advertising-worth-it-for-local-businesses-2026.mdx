## Who Is TikTok Actually Good For?

TikTok is not a one-size-fits-all platform. It's not Google Ads, where you can target anyone, anywhere. But if your ideal customer is between 16 and 34 years old, and you're selling something that can be shown in creative, visual, or emotional ways (like a new coffee shop latte art bar or a trendy hair color), TikTok could be a great fit.

### Best use cases for local businesses on TikTok

- **Youth-oriented services**: Things like fitness studios, beauty salons, and fashion boutiques see strong returns.
- **Visual, emotional products**: Pet grooming services, wellness studios, and cafes that want to build community often do well.
- **Trendy or seasonal offers**: TikTok is great for creating buzz around limited-time offers, pop-up events, or new menu items.

If your audience skews older (40+), or your business is B2B, TikTok may not be the best use of your budget — at least not in 2026. But if you're in the sweet spot, here's where to start.

## How Much Do TikTok Ads Cost in 2026?

Let’s get real. You’re not here to hear fluff — you want hard numbers. In 2026, the average cost per click (CPC) for TikTok Ads is **$0.50 to $1.50**, depending on your niche, location, and competition. For local services like salons and fitness studios, we've seen average CPCs closer to $0.75–$1.00.

Cost per mille (CPM) is usually between **$3 and $10**, again depending on the niche. That’s on the lower end of what Google Ads costs for local services, which are typically $5–15 CPM.

What's the takeaway? **TikTok isn't the cheapest**, but it's more affordable than Google or Meta for many local businesses — especially when targeting demographics where TikTok dominates (Gen Z and millennials).

We've worked with a few local clients who saw positive ROI even with small budgets:

- A pet grooming studio ran a TikTok ad campaign for $150/day and saw **a 22% increase in bookings**.
- A boutique yoga studio spent $100/day and gained **30 new weekly class attendees**.
- A specialty coffee shop used TikTok to promote a seasonal latte and saw a **35% increase in foot traffic** during the campaign.

So yes — for the right business, TikTok *can* be worth it. But it's not a magic bullet. It requires creativity, testing, and patience.

## How to Run a TikTok Ad Campaign (Without Breaking the Bank)

If you're thinking of running TikTok Ads, here’s a step-by-step guide to get you started — with tips to keep costs low and results high.

### 1. Define your goal
Are you driving more foot traffic? Building brand awareness? Increasing bookings?

**Examples:**
- *Goal: 15 new client sign-ups in 30 days.*
- *Goal: 1000 additional followers in 2 weeks.*
- *Goal: 10% increase in online orders.*

TikTok Ads lets you target based on:
- Age
- Location
- Interests
- Behaviors
- Custom audiences (upload your email list)

For local businesses, we recommend starting with a campaign targeting a 10–15 mile radius of your location. That way, you're reaching people who can actually walk into your store or book a service.

### 2. Set a realistic budget
Start small — we recommend $50–$100/day for your first 10–15 days. Use the "performance" or "conversion" mode if you want to track bookings or sign-ups.

### 3. Create a compelling video ad
TikTok users scroll fast — you have 3 seconds to grab attention. We recommend:
- 15–30 second video
- Clear call to action (e.g., “Book now,” “DM for details,” “Click the link in bio”)
- Strong visuals and music
- Text overlay for key points

We've found that ads with real people (like customers or staff) doing something authentic (e.g., a pet groomer showing a dog’s transformation) perform better than polished studio content.

If you’re not comfortable making your own TikTok videos, consider hiring a creator or using a simple editing tool like Canva or CapCut.

### 4. Target the right audience
Use TikTok’s Lookalike Audience feature to target people similar to your existing customers. You can also use custom audiences based on:
- GMB data
- Email list
- Website visitors

For example, a coffee shop might upload their email list to target past customers with a seasonal offer.

### 5. Test, learn, and refine
Run at least 2–3 ad variations at once to see what works. Test:
- Different call to actions
- Different music or visuals
- Different audience segments

After 2–3 weeks, pause the underperforming ones and double down on the winners.

## Should You Run TikTok Ads in 2026?

So, is TikTok advertising worth it for your local business in 2026?

**Short answer:** It depends. But for the right business, the answer is *yes*.

Here’s a quick checklist to help you decide:
- ✅ You serve a younger, Gen Z/millennial audience.
- ✅ You can create engaging, visual content.
- ✅ You're open to testing and refining.
- ✅ You're not already maxing out Google Ads budget.

TikTok offers a fresh way to connect with a growing audience — and for the right local businesses, it's worth the investment.

Still not sure? Try a small test campaign with $50/day over 2 weeks. If it works, scale it up. If not, pivot to another channel like Google Ads or Meta Ads.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Google Ads worth it for small businesses?

Yes — especially if you’re looking to drive local traffic and conversions. Google Ads is often more reliable for small businesses than TikTok, but it can be more expensive per click. It’s great for services like hair salons, fitness studios, and coffee shops that want to convert local searchers.

### Is $10 a day enough for Google Ads?

It depends. If you’re testing, $10/day can work, but it’s often too small to get reliable data. We recommend $50–$100/day for new campaigns to get meaningful results.

### Is $100 enough for Google Ads?

Yes — for a trial or test campaign. It’s not enough to run full-scale, but it’s a good budget to see if your ads perform before scaling up.

### Is $20 a day good for Google Ads?

$20/day is a decent starting point for small businesses, but again — it’s not enough for long-term growth. It’s best for testing and learning.

### How much should a small business spend on Google Ads?

We recommend starting with $100–$200/day and adjusting based on performance. The ideal budget will depend on your industry, competition, and goals.
