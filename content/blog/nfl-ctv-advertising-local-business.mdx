NFL football is the most-watched content in American television history. In 2025, every one of the top 30 most-watched broadcasts in the US was an NFL game. And increasingly, those games are on streaming — Amazon Prime Video, Peacock, ESPN+, and Hulu Live — which means local businesses can now buy targeted, geo-fenced ads around the nation's biggest audience.

This guide covers exactly where NFL games live on streaming, what it costs to advertise around them, and how a local business in any city can set up a campaign.

## Where NFL Games Stream in 2026

The NFL's broadcasting rights are split across multiple streaming and traditional platforms. Here's the current landscape:

**Amazon Prime Video — Thursday Night Football**
Every Thursday Night Football game streams exclusively on Prime Video (plus select local broadcast affiliates). This is the most accessible CTV entry point for local advertisers — Amazon's self-serve DSP allows local geo-targeting down to metro area.
- Games: ~15 Thursday Night Football games per season (September–January)
- Audience: 13–18M viewers per game
- Local ad availability: Yes, through Amazon DSP or managed service

**Peacock (NBCUniversal) — Sunday Night Football + Exclusive Games**
NBC's Sunday Night Football streams on Peacock for all subscribers. Peacock also carries exclusive games (they aired a Wild Card playoff game exclusively in 2024, drawing 23M+ viewers — the most-watched streaming event in US history at the time).
- Games: All Sunday Night Football + select exclusive games
- Audience: 15–23M per game
- Local ad availability: Yes, through Peacock's ad platform or NBCUniversal's local ad sales

**ESPN+ / ABC — Monday Night Football + Playoffs**
ESPN+ carries Monday Night Football (simulcast with ESPN/ABC) and select playoff games. Disney's DSP provides local advertiser access.
- Games: MNF games + divisional playoff games
- Audience: 10–15M per game
- Local ad availability: Yes, through Disney Advertising or programmatic DSP

**NFL+ (NFL's Own Streaming)**
The NFL's direct streaming service carries live local and primetime games on mobile. Limited third-party ad inventory — primarily accessible for larger advertisers.

**Hulu + Live TV / YouTube TV / Sling**
These vMVPD services (virtual pay-TV) carry NFL games through their local network affiliates and ESPN. Ads within these streams are sold by the vMVPD platforms directly.

## What NFL CTV Ads Cost

NFL CTV commands the highest CPMs of any streaming content — you're paying for the most engaged, loyal, and demographically valuable audience in American television.

**Typical CPM ranges for NFL CTV:**

| Platform | Regular Season CPM | Playoff CPM |
|---|---|---|
| Amazon Thursday Night Football | $45–$75 | $70–$110 |
| Peacock Sunday Night Football | $50–$80 | $80–$120 |
| ESPN+ Monday Night Football | $40–$65 | $65–$95 |
| Hulu Live (NFL content) | $30–$55 | $50–$80 |
| Programmatic (across all NFL) | $35–$60 | $55–$90 |

These CPMs are 2–3x higher than general streaming CTV ($18–$30 CPM). The premium is justified by completion rates (95%+), audience engagement, and the exclusivity of premium sports content.

**Minimum effective monthly budget**: $1,500–$2,500 to reach meaningful reach around NFL content. At $2,000/month and $55 CPM, you're reaching ~36,000 unique households per month — targeted to your metro area.

## Who You're Reaching: NFL Viewer Demographics

NFL viewership has diversified significantly. The audience is no longer just men 18–49:

- **Age**: 25–54 is the core (55% of viewers), with strong growth in 18–24 female viewers
- **Gender**: 54% male / 46% female (2025 data — a massive shift from 70/30 a decade ago)
- **Income**: NFL viewers skew higher income than average TV audience — median HHI ~$75k
- **Location**: Metro areas drive the highest viewership — particularly cities with NFL franchises (Dallas, New York, Chicago, Philadelphia, Los Angeles, etc.)

For local businesses in NFL team markets, the audience-geography alignment is exceptional. A Dallas restaurant advertising during Cowboys games is reaching its exact geographic and demographic target.

## How to Buy Local NFL CTV Ads

### Option 1: Amazon DSP (Thursday Night Football)

Amazon's demand-side platform offers the most accessible local buying for TNF. You can target by metro area, demographic, and even Amazon purchase behavior.

**Setup via Amazon DSP**:
1. Access through a managed service partner (Amazon doesn't offer fully self-serve local buying — you need to work through an Amazon Advertising partner or a DSP that's plugged into Amazon inventory)
2. Set geographic targeting to your DMA (Designated Market Area) or specific zip codes
3. Select NFL/Sports content category
4. Set budget and flight dates (typically a season-long campaign or specific game windows)

**Minimum spend**: $5,000–$10,000 for a managed Amazon DSP campaign. High barrier for very small businesses.

**Alternative**: Access Amazon TNF inventory through a DSP like The Trade Desk or StackAdapt, which buy programmatically across Amazon's supply. Minimums are lower ($1,500–$3,000/month) but targeting is less precise.

### Option 2: NBCUniversal/Peacock Local Ad Sales

For Peacock Sunday Night Football, contact NBCUniversal's local sales team in your market. Many local NBC affiliates sell streaming adjacency alongside their broadcast spots.

**What to ask for**: "Local streaming adjacency to Sunday Night Football" — these are ads that appear within the Peacock stream for viewers in your DMA who are watching SNF.

**Access level for small businesses**: Peacock/NBCUniversal local sales typically requires $5,000+ minimum buys. More accessible through programmatic intermediaries.

### Option 3: Programmatic DSP (Most Accessible for Local Businesses)

The most practical approach for budgets under $5,000/month: use a DSP like StackAdapt, Simpli.fi, or The Trade Desk to buy NFL content adjacency programmatically.

**How this works**: You don't buy specific game placements. You buy a package of "sports CTV inventory" with NFL content included, targeting your geographic area and demographic. Your ad runs in sports-adjacent streaming content — including NFL games on the platforms that make their inventory available programmatically.

**Budget**: $1,500–$3,000/month to reach 25,000–50,000 unique households in your metro area around NFL content.

**Best DSP options for local businesses**:
- **StackAdapt**: Strong sports content access, local geo-targeting, $1,500 minimum
- **Simpli.fi**: Excellent for small-to-mid local advertisers, strong zip code-level targeting
- **Basis Technologies**: Mid-market option with NFL content access

## Matching NFL Advertising to Your Business

**Best-fit businesses for NFL CTV advertising**:

**Sports bars and restaurants**: Highest natural alignment — you're advertising to exactly the people who choose where to watch the game. A sports bar in a Cowboys market advertising during Cowboys games is a perfect match. Message: "Watch the game here — reservations at [link]."

**Auto dealerships and service centers**: NFL's male 25–54 demographic is the core auto buyer. High-ticket purchases and repeat service create strong LTV to justify higher CPM.

**Home improvement, HVAC, plumbing**: NFL viewers' demographics align with homeowners. Weekend game-time ads for "watch the game, think about your home" adjacent messaging works well.

**Gyms and fitness studios**: "Train like your team" angles around NFL seasons drive January fitness marketing. Position your studio as the place fans take care of their health between games.

**Beer, wings, and food delivery**: The obvious adjacency. Local restaurants and delivery services see measurable lift during NFL game days.

**Businesses to reconsider**: B2B services, children's services, and businesses with hyper-narrow local radius (a single yoga studio in a city of 3M may waste NFL-level CPMs on too broad an audience).

## Creative Best Practices for NFL CTV Ads

**15 seconds is optimal**: NFL viewers are watching intently. 15-second non-skippable spots perform at near-identical completion rates to 30-second spots in sports content — and cost half as much for the same household reach.

**Reference the season without using NFL marks**: You cannot use "NFL," team names, or team logos without a license. Instead: "Game Day special," "This weekend's big game," "When the game's on." This is legally safe and still highly relevant.

**Local specificity wins**: "Dallas's home for game day wings" outperforms "best wings in town." Viewers watching a local market NFL game are already in local mode.

**Include a simple action**: A QR code in your last 3 seconds works well for sports content because many viewers watch on a TV while holding their phone. "Scan to order" or "scan for directions" convert during and immediately after games.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can a small local business actually afford NFL CTV?**

Yes, if you use programmatic access through a DSP. You're not buying a 30-second national spot for $8M — you're buying local impressions against NFL content for $1,500–$3,000/month, reaching 25,000–50,000 households in your metro area. The CPM is premium, but the total spend is accessible.

**Q: Do I need to advertise all season or just key games?**

Seasonal approach is more efficient: spike spend during your team's most-watched games (playoff runs, rivalry games, Sunday/Monday primetime) and reduce spend during less-watched Thursday games or early-season matchups against lesser opponents. A $3,000/month average can be structured as $1,000 in slow weeks and $5,000 in playoff windows.

**Q: Can I target only households in specific zip codes?**

Yes, through most DSPs. Geo-targeting can be set at zip code, city, DMA, or radius. For a restaurant, a 5-mile radius buy around NFL content is realistic. For a car dealership with a broader service area, a 25-mile DMA-level buy makes sense.

**Q: What's the difference between buying NFL ads on Amazon vs. Peacock vs. a DSP?**

Amazon (TNF) and Peacock (SNF) are direct buys on specific platforms for specific game windows. A DSP buy aggregates across multiple platforms and gets you NFL content across Amazon, Peacock, ESPN+, and others simultaneously — with less precise game-by-game control but broader reach and lower minimums.
