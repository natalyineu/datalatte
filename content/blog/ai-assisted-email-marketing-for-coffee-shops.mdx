## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I have 200 email subscribers. Is AI worth it for a list this small?**

Yes, for one specific reason: automation. You’re manually sending emails right now, which means you send less often than you should. Set up a welcome sequence once using an AI tool — it’ll run for years. For a list that size, start with Mailchimp’s free plan. The time savings alone pays for itself by week two.

**Q: Won’t AI make my emails sound robotic?**

Only if you let it. The trick is editing. Take the AI’s first draft, remove 40% of the words, and add one specific detail that only you would know — like “the espresso machine that broke last Tuesday” or “that one regular who always orders a cortado.” The AI gives you structure. You give it soul.

**Q: What email platform actually works for a single-location coffee shop?**

Mailchimp is the most straightforward. It’s free up to 500 contacts, the automations are simple to set up, and the AI subject line testing is built in. Klaviyo is better if you want deep purchase history segmentation, but it charges once you hit 50+ contacts. For a small coffee shop, start with Mailchimp. Migrate to Klaviyo when you hit 1,000+ subscribers and need better reporting.

**Q: How do I get people to actually give me their email without offering a discount?**

Instead of a discount, offer something they can’t get in the store. A weekly email with three coffee brewing tips. A recipe for the syrup you use in your lavender latte. A heads-up when your seasonal roast drops. Discounts train people to wait for a sale. Valuable information trains them to open.

**Q: How often should I send emails if I’m using AI to help write them?**

Once a week minimum if you want to stay top of mind. Twice a week if you have something to say. More than that and you’re testing your subscribers’ patience. AI makes it faster to write, but it doesn’t change the fact that people unsubscribe when you email them every day with nothing useful.

**Q: What’s the biggest mistake you see business owners make with AI email tools?**

Thinking the AI will do everything. It won’t. The AI doesn’t know your customers. You do. I see too many people generate a campaign in five minutes, hit send, and wonder why nobody opens it. The extra 20 minutes you spend reviewing, editing, and adding a personal detail is what makes the email worth reading.
