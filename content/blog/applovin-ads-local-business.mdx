AppLovin is one of the largest mobile advertising platforms in the world — and until recently, it was primarily known among gaming app marketers and mobile-first tech companies. That's changing fast.

AppLovin's AXON AI targeting engine reaches over **1.4 billion daily active users** through in-app advertising across mobile games, utility apps, and content apps. Its performance marketing capabilities, previously reserved for app advertisers, are now expanding to include local businesses and service brands.

For local businesses willing to experiment beyond Google and Meta, AppLovin represents a genuinely different audience — mobile gamers and app users who are often unreachable on traditional social platforms.

## What AppLovin Actually Is

AppLovin is a mobile technology company with two core products:

**AppLovin Exchange (ALX):** One of the largest mobile programmatic advertising exchanges. Publishers (app developers) integrate AppLovin's MAX mediation SDK to monetise their apps. Advertisers buy this inventory through AppLovin's demand-side capabilities.

**AXON AI:** AppLovin's AI targeting and optimisation system. AXON analyses user behaviour across AppLovin's vast network to predict which users will respond to which ads — optimising in real time across billions of daily data signals.

**AppDiscovery (Performance+):** AppLovin's performance advertising product, originally designed for app marketers but now expanding to web and local businesses.

The key difference from Google or Meta: AppLovin's inventory is **in-app advertising** — ads that appear inside mobile games, tools, and apps. This is a genuinely different context than social feeds or search results. Mobile gaming audiences in particular are an underserved advertising market for many local businesses.

## The Mobile Gaming Audience

AppLovin built its scale primarily through mobile gaming. Over **40% of US adults play mobile games** regularly — and this demographic is often invisible to traditional digital advertising strategies.

Mobile gamers are not who you might assume. The stereotypical image of young male gamers is outdated. Today's mobile gaming audience skews:

- **Gender:** Roughly 50/50 male/female in casual gaming categories
- **Age:** Strong representation in 25–55 range (older than most assume)
- **Income:** Mixed, but significant affluent segment particularly in puzzle, strategy, and lifestyle gaming
- **Context:** Playing during commutes, waiting rooms, lunch breaks, evenings at home

For local businesses, the opportunity is reaching people who are engaged on their phones but not on social media — a meaningful and growing segment of mobile time that Meta and TikTok don't reach.

## AppLovin's Ad Formats

**Interstitial Ads:** Full-screen display or video ads that appear at natural break points in app use (between game levels, during loading screens). 15–30 seconds. High visibility.

**Rewarded Video:** Users opt to watch a 15–30 second video in exchange for in-game rewards (extra lives, coins, premium features). Near 100% completion rate. High-quality attention.

**Banner Ads:** Small banner at the top or bottom of the screen while the app is in use. Lower CPM, lower attention, but additive for reach and frequency.

**Native Ads:** Ad content formatted to match the app's design — less intrusive, often higher click-through than standard display.

<Callout type="tip">
AppLovin's Rewarded Video format is one of the most underrated ad placements available. Users actively choose to watch your ad for a game reward. Completion rates exceed 95%. For brand awareness, this opt-in attention is exceptionally valuable compared to skippable or passive formats.
</Callout>

## Who Benefits Most from AppLovin

**Fitness and wellness businesses:** Mobile gaming audiences include a significant active wellness segment. A gym or fitness studio ad reaching someone who plays a fitness tracking game or health app is contextually relevant.

**Food and beverage brands:** People playing mobile games while hungry (break time, commute) are receptive to food messaging. Restaurants and food brands can reach this audience in moments of genuine receptiveness.

**Entertainment and experiences:** Movie theatres, escape rooms, bowling alleys, entertainment venues — experiences that a gaming-oriented audience is predisposed to enjoy.

**App-driven local businesses:** If your business has an app (ordering, booking, loyalty), AppLovin's in-app context is naturally aligned. An app-to-app advertising journey has strong conversion logic.

**E-commerce with local delivery:** Online stores with geographic delivery zones can reach local customers through AppLovin's geographic targeting.

**Less suitable:**
- Pure service businesses without app or strong digital presence
- Very high-consideration purchases (home buying, major surgery)
- Businesses targeting exclusively 65+ demographics

## Accessing AppLovin for Local Business

AppLovin's self-serve platform has historically been oriented toward app developers and larger performance advertisers. As of 2026, several routes are available to local businesses:

**AppLovin's expanded self-serve (Performance+):** AppLovin has been opening its platform to non-app advertisers. Access through apps.applovin.com — set up a campaign with a target CPA or CPC goal and let AXON AI optimise.

**Through programmatic DSPs:** AppLovin Exchange inventory is available through The Trade Desk, DV360, and other major DSPs. If you have a programmatic buying relationship, AppLovin's inventory is accessible without a direct AppLovin account.

**Through mobile-focused agencies:** Agencies specialising in mobile or performance marketing often have preferred access and lower minimums through their AppLovin relationship.

Practical minimum to test AppLovin: **$1,000–2,000** per campaign to generate enough data for the AXON AI to optimise against your conversion goals.

## AppLovin Creative Requirements

**Interstitial images:** 320×480 pixels minimum, JPEG or PNG
**Interstitial video:** 15 or 30 seconds, MP4 format, 320×480 or 480×320 minimum
**Rewarded video:** 15–30 seconds, minimum 720p resolution
**Banner:** 320×50 or 728×90

Creative best practices for AppLovin:
- High contrast, bold visuals (competing with app background noise)
- Clear CTA within first 3 seconds of video
- Minimal text — mobile screens are small
- Brand name visible early and at close

<Callout type="warning">
AppLovin's audience is in gaming mode — they're mentally engaged in a game context. Ads that feel interruptive without delivering immediate value get quickly dismissed. The best-performing creative acknowledges the context or delivers an immediate, clear offer rather than brand storytelling.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is AppLovin right for a local coffee shop or hair salon?

For a single-location coffee shop or salon with a limited budget, AppLovin is probably not the first channel to test — Google Ads and Meta Ads will deliver better measurable ROI at lower complexity. AppLovin makes more sense as a supplemental reach channel once you've maximised efficiency on the primary platforms, or if you have a specific reason to reach mobile gaming audiences in your local area.

### How does AppLovin's AXON AI targeting work?

AXON AI analyses behavioural signals across AppLovin's entire network (1.4B daily users) to predict which users will respond to which ad types. You define your goal (CPA, ROAS, CPM), and AXON automatically selects users, placements, and bid levels. Unlike Google or Meta where you define audiences, AppLovin's targeting is largely automated — you're trusting the machine.

### Can I target AppLovin ads by city or neighbourhood?

Yes — geographic targeting is available at the country, state, DMA, and city level. Zip code targeting may be available depending on your buying route. For local businesses, city-level targeting combined with AppLovin's audience optimisation is the standard approach.

### What's the difference between AppLovin and Google App Campaigns?

Google App Campaigns are specifically for promoting iOS or Android apps. AppLovin's inventory is also in-app, but AppLovin now accepts non-app advertisers (web-based businesses, local services). For local businesses without apps, Google App Campaigns aren't relevant — AppLovin's expanding non-app offering is the more applicable comparison point.

## Common Mistakes (And What to Do Instead)

I've now watched enough local businesses poke at AppLovin to spot the pattern of mistakes before they happen. Here are the ones that cost the most.

### Mistake #1: Running AppLovin Like Google Search Ads

**The story:** A barbershop in Austin, Texas — four chairs, solid walk-in traffic, decent Google Ads setup. The owner heard about AppLovin from a podcast, opened an account, and set up a campaign using his Google Search keywords as targeting. "Men's haircut Austin," "best barber South Congress," "fade haircut near me."

He spent $1,400 over two weeks. Got 43,000 impressions. Zero conversions. Not one booking.

**What went wrong:** AppLovin is not intent-based. Nobody is sitting in a mobile game thinking "I should search for a barber." The platform works on behavioral targeting — people who *might* need a haircut based on app usage patterns, not people actively looking for one. Using keyword-style targeting in AppLovin is like handing out flyers at a movie theater and wondering why nobody walks out to buy a lawnmower.

**The fix:** We rebuilt the campaign around lookalike audiences seeded from his existing client list (exported from his booking app). Targeted users who showed behavior similar to his actual customers — people who opened lifestyle apps, checked grooming content, and had location data suggesting they lived within 5 miles of his shop. Creative switched from "Book Now" urgency to casual brand awareness: a 6-second video of a clean fade with the shop name.

**The outcome:** Second month: $800 spend, 12 booked appointments tracked through UTM parameters and phone call tracking. Average ticket $45 — that's $540 in directly attributed revenue. Not profitable yet, but the trend line was moving in the right direction. By month three, with optimized creative and tighter geo-targeting, he hit $1,200 in revenue on $600 spend.

### Mistake #2: No Offline Conversion Tracking

**The story:** A dental practice in Portland, Oregon — cosmetic dentistry, high-ticket procedures ($3,000–$8,000 per patient). The owner heard AppLovin could reach an audience Google couldn't. She set up a campaign, got 200,000 impressions in 30 days, spent $2,100. Website traffic went up. Contact form submissions stayed flat. She called it a failure and shut it down.

**What actually happened:** Her phone started ringing more. The front desk didn't track where calls came from. At least seven new patient consultations mentioned "that ad I kept seeing on my phone." Three of those converted into procedures totaling $14,500 in revenue. She had no way to connect this to AppLovin because she never set up call tracking.

**The fix:** We integrated CallRail (about $30/month) with a unique phone number for the AppLovin campaign. Added a UTM parameter to the website landing page. Tracked form submissions separately. Then relaunched with the same targeting.

**The outcome:** Second month: $1,500 spend, 23 tracked phone calls, 9 booked consultations, 4 procedures completed. Total attributed revenue: $19,200. Cost per acquisition: $375 per procedure. Compared to her Google Ads cost per acquisition of $520 for similar procedures, AppLovin was actually more efficient — once she had the data to prove it.

### Mistake #3: Ignoring Frequency Caps

**The story:** A pet grooming business in Nashville — single location, three groomers, appointment-only booking through Booksy. The owner ran a $500 AppLovin campaign with no frequency cap. AppLovin's algorithm, seeing good engagement, showed the ad to the same users repeatedly. One customer saw the grooming ad 47 times in 10 days. She texted the owner: "I was going to book but your ad is literally everywhere. It feels desperate. Please stop stalking me."

**What went wrong:** AppLovin's optimization favors showing ads to users who engage. If someone watches your video for 3 seconds, the algorithm thinks "this user is interested" and shows them more. Without a frequency cap, you become that person — the brand that follows people around until they resent you.

**The fix:** Set a frequency cap of 3 impressions per user per day. Also implemented a 24-hour cooldown after a click. Created three different creative variations to rotate, so even if someone saw multiple ads, they weren't seeing the same one.

**The outcome:** Frequency dropped from an average of 12.4 impressions per user to 2.8. Annoyance factor went to zero. But here's the interesting part: conversion rate actually *increased* from 0.8% to 1.4%. Less frequency, more intent. The campaign went from $350 in attributed bookings to $680 on the same $500 spend.

## Attribution: The Hard Part Nobody Explains

Here's the uncomfortable truth: AppLovin's click-through attribution is unreliable for local businesses. The platform was built for app installs, where a click leads to an immediate download. For a local business, a click leads to a website, then maybe a phone call, then maybe a booking days later. AppLovin's pixel doesn't track that well.

**What I've seen work:**

Use UTM parameters religiously. AppLovin lets you append tracking parameters to your destination URL. Use `utm_source=applovin&utm_medium=cpm&utm_campaign=summer2024`. Then check those in Google Analytics. But GA4 will undercount because of iOS privacy changes and cookie limitations.

The better approach: **unique phone numbers**. Services like CallRail, WhatConverts, or even Google Voice (free) let you assign a unique number to your AppLovin campaign. Every call that comes through that number is attributed. For service businesses — plumbers, dentists, lawyers — phone calls are the primary conversion anyway. This is non-negotiable.

**Booking integrations matter more than pixels.** If you use Booksy, Square Appointments, or Vagaro, many of these have built-in ad tracking or at least allow UTM tracking through their booking forms. A Miami massage therapist using Square Appointments found that 31% of her AppLovin-driven bookings came through the Square booking page without ever clicking an ad — they typed her business name directly into Google after seeing the ad. The UTM showed as "direct" in analytics. The booking system showed the time correlation. Without Square's internal tracking, she would have attributed those bookings to organic search.

**The rule I follow:** If your attributed revenue from AppLovin is at least 60% of your spend by month three, the actual revenue is probably 2–3x higher due to view-through conversions and delayed bookings. Don't judge the platform on last-click attribution alone. Judge it on overall business growth.

## Creative That Actually Works on Mobile Apps

AppLovin inventory runs inside mobile apps. Think about the context: someone is playing Candy Crush, checking a weather app, or scrolling through a news app. They are not in shopping mode. Your ad is an interruption.

**What I've tested and what worked:**

**Video under 10 seconds wins.** A 6-second video of a Denver bakery's croissant being pulled apart generated 4x the engagement of a 15-second version. People in mobile apps have zero patience. Get to the point or get swiped past.

**Static ads with bold text work better than you'd think.** AppLovin's algorithm likes simple creative with high-contrast text. A chiropractor in Phoenix ran two campaigns — one with a video of him adjusting a patient, one with a static image of a spine graphic and the text "Neck Pain? Same-Day Appointments Available." The static ad had a 40% lower CPM but a 70% higher conversion rate. Sometimes simple works.

**No music unless your video has sound-off value.** Most mobile app ads play without sound. If your video needs audio to make sense, you've lost before you started. Use captions, text overlays, or visual storytelling. The Nashville pet groomer who initially failed ran a second campaign with silent video of a matted dog transforming into a fluffy groomed dog. Text overlay: "20 minutes. $45. Your dog will thank you." That campaign drove 34 bookings in three weeks.

**Avoid "Book Now" as the only CTA.** Direct response doesn't work well in this environment. Instead, try "See How It Works," "Learn More," or "Get A Quote." A Portland landscaper used "See Our Work" and got a 4.2% CTR — absurdly high for AppLovin — because the creative showed before/after yard photos. Curiosity beats urgency in a gaming context.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is AppLovin just for app developers? I own a hair salon. Will this even work for me?**

It was built for app developers, yes. But AppLovin's self-serve platform now allows web-based businesses to run campaigns. The inventory is the same — mobile app users. The question isn't whether it *can* work for a hair salon. It's whether you can track conversions properly and tolerate a learning period where results look mediocre. I've seen it work for salons, dentists, landscapers, and plumbers. I've also seen it fail for businesses that expected Google Ads-level intent signals. It's a different channel. Treat it like one.

**Q: What's the minimum budget? I don't want to waste money.**

AppLovin's platform technically lets you start with any budget. Practically, anything under $500/month is pointless. You won't get enough data for AXON AI to optimize. You'll get maybe 50,000 impressions scattered across random users. At $500–$1,000/month, you have a chance. At $2,000+, you can actually iterate on creative and targeting. I would not recommend AppLovin to any business unwilling to commit at least $1,500 over two months minimum.

**Q: How is this different from Facebook or Instagram ads?**

Facebook and Instagram show ads in a social feed where users scroll intentionally. AppLovin shows ads inside games and utility apps — a more interruptive environment. The audience is also different: mobile gamers tend to be older and more diverse than social media users. A plumber in Cleveland found that AppLovin reached homeowners aged 35–55 who don't use Instagram but play word games on their phone for 20 minutes every night. Facebook missed that audience entirely. AppLovin's targeting is also less sophisticated than Meta's right now — you can't layer in interests or behaviors as granularly. Trade-offs everywhere.

**Q: Can I target people in my specific city, or is this national inventory?**

Yes, you can geo-target down to a 5-mile radius. The issue is inventory availability. In a small town, you might not get enough impressions to make the campaign worth running. In a metro area like Austin, Chicago, or Portland, you'll have no problem. I'd recommend starting with a 10-mile radius and narrowing if you have enough volume.

**Q: How do I know if someone booked because they saw my AppLovin ad?**

Honestly? You won't know for sure with 100% accuracy. That's the hard truth. You'll need to use a combination of tracking methods: unique phone numbers, UTM parameters in website traffic, booking system integrations, and asking new clients how they heard about you. Even then, some will say "online" or "I don't remember." If you need perfect attribution, stick with Google Ads. If you're okay with "good enough" data that shows a strong correlation between ad spend and business growth, AppLovin can work.

**Q: What's the biggest risk with AppLovin for a local business?**

Blowing your budget on irrelevant clicks and ad fatigue. Without proper frequency caps and creative rotation, you'll show the same ad to the same person 30 times, they'll get annoyed, and you'll have spent $2,000 with nothing to show for it. The second biggest risk is not tracking offline conversions. If you rely on click-through attribution alone, you'll conclude AppLovin doesn't work — when in reality it's driving phone calls and walk-ins you never measured.
