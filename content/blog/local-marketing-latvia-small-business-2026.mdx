Latvia is the Baltic States' most digitally advanced economy and home to Riga — one of Northern Europe's most beautiful Art Nouveau capitals. With 1.9 million people, euro adoption since 2014, and a highly educated, trilingual (Latvian/Russian/English) population, Latvia offers a unique digital marketing environment. Riga holds the majority of the country's commercial activity; Jūrmala is the upscale beach resort town; Daugavpils is the largely Russian-speaking second city.

Latvia's digital marketing landscape is distinguished by Draugiem.lv — a homegrown social network that has survived the Facebook era and maintains significant local penetration. Combined with standard European platforms, Latvian digital marketing requires awareness of this distinctive local ecosystem.

**Draugiem.lv — Latvia's own social network**: Founded in 2004, Draugiem is Latvia's locally-built social network that has retained a meaningful user base despite Facebook's global dominance. It has lower engagement than Facebook but reaches a demographic that is often older and more locally-oriented. For businesses wanting to reach native Latvian consumers, Draugiem advertising is a supplementary channel worth considering alongside Facebook and Google.

**Trilingual market**: Latvia has three language communities — Latvian speakers (~60% of population), Russian speakers (~35%, concentrated in Riga and Daugavpils), and English-speaking expats/internationals. Language targeting strategy depends on which community you're serving.

**Small but affluent market**: Latvia's small population (1.9M) is EU-level affluent, with strong purchasing power for quality local services. Riga's tech community has created a young professional class with high discretionary spending.

## Language Strategy for Latvian Business

**Three-language reality**:

1. **Latvian** (required for reaching native Latvian consumers): Always produce Latvian-language content and run Latvian Google Ads campaigns. Use Latvian on your Google Business Profile.

2. **Russian** (significant for Riga and eastern Latvia): Approximately 35% of Latvians speak Russian as their primary language. For businesses in central Riga or Daugavpils, Russian-language campaigns reach a significant audience. This is commercially sensible, separate from political considerations.

3. **English** (tech/expat segment): Riga has a meaningful expat and remote-worker community. English content and ads reach this higher-spending demographic.

Best practice: Separate Google Ads campaigns in Latvian, Russian, and English — each targeting the appropriate audience.

## Draugiem.lv for Local Marketing

If targeting native Latvian consumers (particularly 30+), Draugiem offers:
- **Business pages** with posting capability
- **Draugiem.lv ads**: Display and native advertising within the platform
- **Community groups**: Local and interest-based groups with engaged Latvian users

Draugiem is not a primary channel but provides supplementary reach to a Latvian-identified demographic not fully captured by Facebook targeting.

**Jāņi (Midsummer, June 23-24)**: Latvia's most important cultural holiday — singing, dancing, bonfires, oak garland crowns, and Jāņu siers (caraway cheese) are central to the celebration. It is a national holiday and deeply meaningful to Latvian identity. Businesses market around Jāņi throughout mid-June; food and beauty businesses are particularly busy.

**Independence Day (November 18)**: Latvia's proclamation of independence in 1918 — a significant national pride day. Patriotic content referencing Latvian identity and heritage resonates.

## FAQ

**Is Latvian or Russian better for local marketing?**
Both — in separate campaigns. For the native Latvian community, Latvian is essential and outperforms. For Riga's significant Russian-speaking community (concentrated in central areas), Russian-language marketing reaches a distinct demographic. Running separate campaigns in both languages doubles reach and is standard practice for Riga businesses wanting to serve the full local market.

**How important is Draugiem.lv?**
It's supplementary, not primary. Prioritise Google Ads, Facebook/Meta, and Instagram first. Add Draugiem when you've optimised your primary channels — it reaches a Latvian-identified audience at lower cost than equivalent Facebook targeting.

**Is the Latvian market small enough to dominate easily?**
Yes — Latvia's small population means less competition. Outside central Riga, competition for digital advertising is minimal. A well-optimised Google Business Profile with 30-40 Latvian reviews can rank #1 in many service categories across Latvia's regional cities (Jelgava, Jēkabpils, Valmiera, Rēzekne).
