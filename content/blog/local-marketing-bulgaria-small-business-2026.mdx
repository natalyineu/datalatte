Bulgaria is the EU's southeasternmost member state and one of the bloc's most affordable digital marketing markets. With 6.4 million people, Sofia as a growing tech hub, and Bulgaria's position at the crossroads of Europe and the Balkans, the country offers excellent digital marketing opportunities — particularly for early movers. Bulgaria still uses the Bulgarian Lev (BGN) but adopted fixed peg to the Euro, and full euro adoption is in process.

Sofia is the economic capital with a growing tech and startup scene. Plovdiv is Bulgaria's second city and 2019 European Capital of Culture. Varna and Burgas are Black Sea coastal cities with significant tourism. Vitosha Mountain gives Sofia one of Europe's most distinctive city-mountain combinations.

**Facebook remains Bulgaria's dominant social platform**: Facebook usage in Bulgaria is higher than most Western EU peers, particularly among 25-55 demographics. Bulgarian Facebook Groups for local communities (Sofia district groups, professional communities) are extremely active.

**TikTok growing very fast**: Bulgarian TikTok adoption has accelerated significantly. For businesses targeting under-35 Bulgarians, TikTok organic content is increasingly important.

**Viber alongside WhatsApp**: Like Serbia and other Balkan markets, Bulgaria has meaningful Viber penetration alongside WhatsApp. Businesses should be reachable via both.

**Cyrillic script**: Bulgarian uses Cyrillic script — all marketing content must be in Bulgarian Cyrillic. Do not use Latin-script transliterations for formal marketing.

## Bulgarian Cyrillic Digital Marketing

All Bulgarian digital marketing must be in Cyrillic:

- Google Ads: Write ad copy in Cyrillic. Google Ads supports Bulgarian Cyrillic fully.
- Facebook/Instagram: Bulgarian Cyrillic in all posts and ads
- Keywords: Research keywords in Cyrillic — Bulgarian users search in Cyrillic
- **Beware transliteration**: Some older Bulgarians use latinitsa (Latin-script Bulgarian) online — "frizyorski salon Sofia" — but formal marketing should be Cyrillic

Bulgarian Cyrillic is distinct from Russian Cyrillic — there are character differences. Ensure your copywriter is a native Bulgarian speaker, not a Russian speaker adapting copy.

| March 3 | Ден на Освобождението (Liberation Day) — national holiday |

**Баба Марта (Baba Marta — March 1)**: Bulgaria's unique spring holiday — red-and-white cord trinkets called мартеници (martenitsi) are exchanged as gifts on March 1. It is one of Bulgaria's most beloved cultural traditions. For beauty, gifting, and retail businesses, Baba Marta is a distinctly Bulgarian marketing moment with no parallel elsewhere in Europe.

**Liberation Day (March 3)**: Bulgarian national day celebrates liberation from Ottoman rule in 1878 — a significant national pride moment. Content connecting to Bulgarian heritage resonates strongly.

## FAQ

**How low is digital competition in Bulgaria outside Sofia?**
Extremely low. In Plovdiv, Varna, Burgas, Stara Zagora, or Ruse, most business categories have minimal properly-optimised Google Ads competition. A well-managed €100-150/month Google Ads campaign with a complete Google Business Profile can achieve top ranking in most local service categories in any Bulgarian city outside Sofia.

**Is Bulgarian digital marketing growing?**
Yes, rapidly. Bulgarian digital marketing investment has been growing 15-20% annually. Early movers who establish strong Google Maps rankings and review profiles now will enjoy first-mover advantages as competition gradually increases over the next 3-5 years.

**Should I accept card payments in Bulgaria?**
Yes — card penetration has grown significantly. POS terminals are expected in Sofia and tourist areas. Cash remains important in smaller cities and older demographics. ePay.bg is Bulgaria's local online payment system for e-commerce.
