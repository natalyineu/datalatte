When a new client signs up, do you send them a series of automated emails with instructions on how to get started? Probably not. But you should. This is a crucial step in creating a seamless experience for your clients and setting the tone for their journey with your studio.

Here's an example of a simple onboarding workflow:

* Email 2: Scheduling and payment instructions
* Email 3: Tips and advice for getting the most out of their membership

### Leveraging SMS Marketing for Last-Minute Cancellations

Let's face it: cancellations happen. But did you know that using SMS marketing can help minimize the impact of last-minute cancellations? By sending automatic reminders to clients who have scheduled classes, you can reduce no-shows and increase revenue.

Here's an example of a simple SMS workflow:

* Send a reminder 2 hours before class
* Send a second reminder 1 hour before class
* Send a final reminder 30 minutes before class

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much time does this actually take to set up?**

Depends on how many systems you have. A basic email automation in Mailchimp for new leads takes about 90 minutes. SMS reminders through Booksy take 30 minutes. Review response automation via Zapier takes two hours. Most studio owners can knock out the core automations in a single afternoon. After that, you're looking at maybe 30 minutes per week to monitor and tweak.

**Q: Won't this feel impersonal to my clients?**

No, because you're not replacing human interaction. You're replacing the stuff that isn't personal anyway: mass emails, forgotten follow-ups, and inconsistent responses to reviews. Automation handles the repetitive work so you can actually talk to people when they walk in the door. The yoga studio owner in Portland still writes personal texts to her regulars. She just doesn't have to manually remind everyone about class caps anymore.

**Q: What if I'm not tech-savvy?**

None of my clients think they're tech-savvy. The woman in Nashville couldn't find her referral clipboard. The CrossFit owner in Denver didn't know what Zapier was. They all figured it out because the tools are designed to be set and forget. If you can create a contact in your phone, you can set up a Mailchimp automation. If you get stuck, there's a YouTube tutorial for literally every step.

**Q: How much should I budget for automation tools?**

For a single-location studio, you're looking at $100–$200/month total. Mailchimp Essentials is $79/month for up to 1,500 contacts. Booksy is $30/month for the base plan. Zapier is $20/month for the starter tier. Google Ads cost depends on your budget but the scripts are free. If you're spending more than $200/month on automation tools as a single studio, you're probably overpaying.

**Q: What happens if the automation breaks?**

You'll know within 24 hours because something will stop happening. A lead won't get a welcome email. A class reminder won't send. The fix is usually a reauthorization of the integration — 30 seconds to click "reconnect." I recommend checking your automation logs once a week. Most platforms send you an email if something fails. If you ignore that email for three weeks, that's on you, not the software.

**Q: Do I need to hire someone to manage this?**

No. Most studio owners overestimate the complexity. You need a one-time setup and a weekly 15-minute check-in. If you're doing 60+ classes per week and have 200+ members, you might want a part-time VA to monitor and adjust. But for a typical studio with 100–150 members, you can handle it yourself. The whole point of automation is that it does the work for you.
