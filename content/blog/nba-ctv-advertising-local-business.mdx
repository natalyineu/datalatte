The NBA's new broadcast deal, which began with the 2025–26 season, fundamentally changed where basketball lives on streaming. Amazon Prime Video picked up a major package, NBC returned to the NBA for the first time since 2002, and ESPN/ABC retained playoff and Finals rights. For local businesses, this means NBA CTV inventory is now more accessible than ever across a wider range of platforms.

## Where NBA Games Stream in 2026

The NBA's 11-year broadcast rights deal (starting 2025–26) splits games across:

**Amazon Prime Video**: Amazon won a package of regular-season games, including exclusive Thursday night games and select playoff games. This makes Prime Video the first streaming-exclusive NBA rights holder for a major game package — and gives local advertisers access through Amazon DSP.

**NBC / Peacock**: NBC returned to the NBA after a 23-year absence, carrying both regular season and playoff games. NBC primetime games air on broadcast, while all NBC games also stream on Peacock. Local business advertising on NBC games runs through NBCUniversal's local ad sales or programmatic access to Peacock.

**ESPN+ / ABC / ESPN**: Disney retained the Saturday night package, Finals rights, and select playoff series. Games are simulcast across ESPN, ABC, and ESPN+. Disney's advertising platform provides local access.

**TNT / Max**: Warner Bros. Discovery retained some regular season games on TNT, streaming on Max. 

**NBA League Pass**: The league's direct-to-consumer streaming option for out-of-market games. Limited third-party ad inventory.

**vMVPDs (Hulu Live, YouTube TV, Sling)**: These services carry NBC, ESPN, and TNT through their channel packages. Game-day advertising within these streams is handled by the platform directly.

## NBA Season Structure and Advertising Windows

Understanding the season helps you time your spend:

| Period | When | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Preseason | October | Low CPMs, limited viewership — good for testing creative |
| Regular Season | October–April | 82 games/team, 5 game nights/week across platforms |
| Play-In Tournament | April | Growing viewership, lower CPMs than playoffs |
| First Round Playoffs | April–May | Per-series intensity, viewership spikes |
| Conference Semifinals | May | Significant national audience |
| Conference Finals | May–June | Premium CPMs, major-market focus |
| NBA Finals | June | Maximum CPMs and audience |

**Best value for local businesses**: Regular season games in October–February offer the best CPM efficiency. The December holiday window is particularly good for businesses targeting NBA fans with gift/experience messaging. The Play-In Tournament is underrated — strong viewership, lower CPMs than the full playoff bracket.

## Who Watches the NBA

NBA demographics differ significantly from NFL and college sports — which changes which local businesses should advertise around it:

- **Age**: 18–34 is the NBA's strongest demo — younger than any other major US sports league
- **Gender**: 60% male / 40% female, with consistent female viewership growth
- **Race and ethnicity**: Most racially diverse major sports audience in the US
- **Urban skew**: NBA fans heavily over-index in major metro markets vs. suburban/rural
- **Income**: Broad middle-income distribution, with particularly strong 25–44 urban professional viewership
- **Interests**: Technology, fashion, music, and lifestyle content overlap heavily with NBA fan base

**Best-fit local businesses for NBA advertising**:
- Bars and sports bars in NBA team markets (obvious fit)
- Fast casual and delivery restaurants: strong demographic overlap with 25–40 urban diners
- Fitness and lifestyle brands: active, health-conscious demographic
- Urban retail: sneakers, apparel, streetwear (NBA fan culture aligns)
- Entertainment and nightlife: post-game crowd in major markets
- Tech and app-based services: 18–35 tech-savvy viewers

**Lower fit**: Home improvement, auto service, and traditional family services — these demographics skew older and more suburban than the NBA's core audience.

## How to Buy NBA CTV Ads Locally

### Amazon Prime Video (Thursday Night NBA)

Amazon's exclusive NBA package is the most accessible entry point for local businesses:

- **Access**: Amazon DSP through a managed service partner or DSP like The Trade Desk / StackAdapt that buys Amazon inventory programmatically
- **Targeting**: Metro area, zip code radius, demographic filters
- **Budget minimum**: $1,500–$3,000/month for programmatic access; $5,000–$10,000 for managed Amazon DSP
- **What you get**: Impressions served around Amazon's NBA game nights, targeted to your geographic area

### NBC / Peacock Local Sales

For games on NBC and Peacock, contact NBCUniversal's local ad sales office in your market:

- **Access**: NBCUniversal local affiliate sales (call your local NBC station's advertising department) or programmatic DSP
- **What to ask**: "Local CTV adjacency to NBA games on Peacock" or "local adjacency to NBC Sports"
- **Budget**: $3,000–$8,000/month for managed; lower through programmatic intermediaries

### Programmatic DSP (Most Accessible)

For budgets under $5,000/month, buy NBA content adjacency through a DSP that aggregates across Amazon, Peacock, ESPN+, and TNT inventory:

- **Best options**: StackAdapt ($1,500 minimum), Simpli.fi (strong local targeting), The Trade Desk
- **Targeting**: Sports content category + your geographic radius
- **Expected reach**: 20,000–40,000 unique households/month in your metro area at $2,000/month spend

## NBA Market-Specific Targeting

Unlike NFL (which has national appeal), NBA advertising is especially effective when you're in or near an NBA team's market. Here's why:

Local fans watch their team's games at much higher rates than neutral national viewers. A pizza shop in Phoenix advertising during Suns games, a bar in Miami advertising during Heat games, or a gym in Denver during Nuggets games — each of these enjoys audience-market alignment that multiplies the relevance of your ad.

**City-to-team pairings for local targeting**:
Run your CTV campaign specifically during your local team's game windows for maximum geographic relevance. Most DSPs let you select specific days of the week and time windows — use this to concentrate spend on home game nights.

**Multi-market consideration**: If your business serves multiple locations (a regional chain), NBA's diverse metro-market coverage means you can run simultaneous city-specific campaigns around each market's local team.

## Seasonal NBA Campaign Ideas for Local Businesses

**Opening Night (late October)**: "Basketball is back" campaigns align perfectly with fan excitement. Run highest-impact creative in the first 2 weeks of the season.

**December Holiday Gifting**: NBA games are heavy over the December holidays. A sports bar can run "gift cards for the fan in your life" creative; a fitness studio can run "new year starts now" messaging to the health-conscious NBA audience.

**All-Star Weekend (February)**: The NBA's biggest cultural event draws a non-traditional sports audience. Strong for lifestyle, fashion, and entertainment brands.

**Playoff Push (March–April)**: As teams fight for seeding and play-in spots, local market viewership spikes. Budget up 30–40% in the final 4–6 weeks of the regular season.

**NBA Finals (June)**: Two-market event, but nationally watched. If one or both Finals teams is in your local market, heavy spend here is justified. Otherwise, the national audience is large but CPMs are premium.

## Creative Best Practices for NBA CTV

**Reference the game without NBA trademarks**: You cannot use "NBA," team names, or player likenesses in ads without a license. Use: "Game night specials," "When the game's on," "Game day delivery," "Your team's watching from [Business Name]."

**Urban and lifestyle tone**: NBA audiences respond to ads that feel culturally current. Generic corporate ads underperform with this audience vs. ads that feel relevant to their lifestyle.

**15-second format preferred**: NBA viewers are engaged and skipping ads is not common during live sports. 15-second non-skippable spots are highly effective and cost less than 30-second spots.

**Game-day urgency**: NBA games happen most nights of the week. Lean into this with messaging like "tonight only" or "game day special" to create time-specific urgency.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is NBA CTV cheaper than NFL CTV for local businesses?**

Yes, typically by 15–30% for comparable inventory. NFL commands the highest CPMs in all of US television. The NBA, while premium, is a slightly more efficient entry point for local businesses with sports audiences in mind.

**Q: Do I need to be in an NBA market to advertise around NBA games?**

No, but it helps. The NBA has national appeal, and any local business can buy NBA content adjacency targeted to their geographic area regardless of whether there's a local team. But the strongest match is businesses in NBA team markets where fans are especially invested.

**Q: How many nights per week are NBA games on?**

During the regular season (October–April), there are typically 4–6 NBA games every weeknight and more on weekends. This means NBA CTV campaigns can run nearly every day of the week during the season — much higher frequency than NFL (1–3 games/week).

**Q: How early do I need to plan an NBA CTV campaign?**

2–3 weeks of lead time is sufficient for programmatic DSP campaigns during the regular season. For playoff or Finals buys (especially through direct platform sales), plan 4–6 weeks ahead as premium inventory fills up quickly.
