ESPN+ is not just a streaming service — it's Disney's combined live sports advertising platform. When you buy ads on ESPN+ through Disney's advertising stack, you're accessing Monday Night Football (simulcast with ESPN/ABC), college football, UFC Fight Nights, MLB, and a wide range of international sports. Disney's ad platform is the third major entry point for live sports CTV advertising, alongside Amazon (TNF) and Peacock (SNF).

## What ESPN+ Carries for Live Sports

ESPN+ has an extensive sports rights portfolio:

**Monday Night Football**:
- ESPN and ABC simulcast MNF, which also streams on ESPN+
- Approximately 17–18 MNF games per season
- Average 10–15M viewers (lower than SNF or TNF due to Monday fatigue, but still a premium audience)

**College Football**:
- ESPN/ABC carries the vast majority of college football including College Football Playoff
- ESPN+ carries overflow conference games, mid-tier matchups, and some exclusive streaming games
- SEC on ABC, Big 12 on ESPN — all stream on ESPN+

**College Basketball**:
- ESPN+ carries extensive regular-season college basketball
- Leading up to March Madness, ESPN+ provides months of content adjacency

**UFC**:
- ESPN+ is the exclusive home of UFC Fight Pass-equivalent content in the US
- UFN (UFC Fight Night) streams exclusively on ESPN+
- 15–20M+ UFC viewers per major card

**MLB**:
- ESPN/ABC Sunday Night Baseball streams on ESPN+
- Some exclusive ESPN+ games mid-week

**NBA (from 2026)**:
- Disney retained NBA Saturday Night coverage plus Finals rights
- Disney's ESPN/ABC coverage simulcasts on ESPN+

**International sports**:
- Champions League, La Liga (via ESPN+ partnership)
- Grand Slam tennis (Wimbledon, US Open, Australian Open)
- NHL

This breadth makes ESPN+ the widest-coverage sports CTV platform by number of sports, even if individual events draw smaller audiences than Amazon TNF or Peacock SNF.

## Disney's Advertising Platform: How It Works

Disney controls ESPN, ABC, ESPN+, Disney+, and Hulu — and all of these are connected through a unified advertising platform called **Disney Advertising** (formerly Disney Advertising Sales).

This matters because:
1. You can buy CTV ads across ESPN+, Hulu, and ABC content through one platform or partner
2. Disney's data includes Disney+ subscriber data, Hulu viewership data, and ESPN viewing data — one of the most comprehensive first-party audience datasets in streaming
3. Buying ESPN+ ads means you can target viewers cross-platform if you want ESPN + Hulu reach simultaneously

For local businesses, the Disney advertising ecosystem is primarily accessed through:

### Disney Advertising Direct / Partner Agency

For campaigns $5,000–$10,000+/month, Disney Advertising works through a network of certified advertising partners. These agencies can buy local spot adjacency within ESPN+ and ESPN content for your specific DMA.

**What to ask for**: "Local CTV adjacency to Monday Night Football on ESPN+" or "local streaming sports adjacency on Disney platforms"

**Benefit**: You can buy against specific programs (MNF, specific college games) rather than just sports category

**Access point**: Search "Disney Advertising Sales" + your market, or contact an agency with Disney platform access

### Programmatic DSP (Best for Under $5,000/Month)

Disney makes ESPN+ inventory available programmatically through platforms like The Trade Desk and StackAdapt. This is the most accessible route for local businesses with smaller budgets:

1. Open an account on StackAdapt or through a DSP partner
2. Create a CTV streaming TV campaign
3. Target "Sports" content category and include ESPN+ or "Disney" as an inventory source
4. Set geographic targeting to your metro area or radius
5. Run during Monday nights, Saturdays (college football), and year-round for UFC/MLB/international sports

**Expected reach**: $2,000/month → approximately 30,000–50,000 impressions in a mid-size market

## Monday Night Football vs. Thursday Night Football vs. Sunday Night Football

These three primetime NFL packages have different viewer profiles and advertising characteristics:

| Metric | TNF (Amazon) | SNF (Peacock/NBC) | MNF (ESPN+/ESPN) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Average viewers | 13–18M | 15–23M | 10–15M |
| Exclusive streaming | Yes (Amazon) | Streaming + broadcast | Streaming + broadcast |
| Key demo | 25–54, Prime subscribers | 25–54, broad | 18–49, sports super-fans |
| CPM range | $45–$75 | $50–$80 | $40–$65 |
| Best for local business | Purchase data targeting | Broadest reach | Engaged sports fans |

Monday Night Football viewers are generally considered more dedicated sports fans than the broader SNF/TNF audience — they're watching on a Monday night, which requires more deliberate choice. This makes MNF viewers particularly valuable for businesses targeting sports-passionate audiences.

## College Football: An Underrated CTV Opportunity

College football is one of the most loyal fan bases in American sports — and most of it runs through ESPN's family of networks, including ESPN+. For local businesses in college markets, college football CTV is one of the best-targeted advertising investments available.

**Why college football CTV works for local businesses**:

- **Geographic loyalty**: College football fans identify intensely with their regional team. A business in Athens, GA advertising around UGA games, or a business in Austin advertising around Longhorns games, reaches fans who are already in local mode
- **Weekend morning and afternoon slots**: College football runs noon–11pm ET on Saturdays. This captures a consumer in a social, entertainment mindset
- **Alumni network advertising**: College towns and nearby metros are full of alumni who watch every game. These tend to be educated, mid-to-upper income households
- **Lower CPMs than NFL**: College football CTV CPMs ($35–$55) are 20–30% lower than NFL equivalents for similarly engaged viewership

**How to buy local college football CTV**:
- Through Disney Advertising / ESPN+ for your team's games
- Through programmatic DSP targeting "college sports" or "college football" content category
- Target your DMA during Saturdays in September–January (regular season + bowl games + College Football Playoff)

**Spend recommendation**: For businesses in major college markets, run a $1,500–$2,500/month college football campaign September–November and consider adding a burst for your team's bowl game or playoff run.

## UFC on ESPN+: The Most Efficient Sports CPM

UFC Fight Night events on ESPN+ offer some of the most cost-efficient live sports CTV inventory:

**Why UFC is underpriced for advertisers**: UFC viewership (15–20M+ for major events) is strong, but UFC Fight Night cards on ESPN+ draw smaller audiences (3–6M) at much lower CPMs ($25–$40) than NFL or college football.

**UFC demographic**: Male 21–40, above-average engagement with health/fitness, sports supplements, auto, tech, and gaming categories. For businesses targeting young adult males, UFC is frequently the best CPM value in live sports CTV.

**For example**: A gym, supplement shop, auto parts store, or sports bar that wants to reach 25–40-year-old males can do so via UFC on ESPN+ at $30–$40 CPM — 30–50% cheaper than reaching similar demographics through NFL CTV.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: If I buy ESPN+ ads, do they also appear on regular ESPN on cable?**

Not automatically. ESPN (cable) and ESPN+ (streaming) are different inventory pools. Through Disney Advertising's platform, you can buy cross-platform packages that include both. Through programmatic DSP, you're primarily reaching streaming viewers (ESPN+), not cable TV viewers.

**Q: Is ESPN+ more or less expensive than Amazon Prime Video for the same NFL content?**

ESPN+ MNF CPMs ($40–$65) are generally 10–20% lower than Amazon TNF CPMs ($45–$75) for comparable regular season games. The main reason: Amazon has the exclusive streaming audience (no broadcast alternative for TNF), concentrating all streaming viewers on Amazon. ESPN+ MNF is simulcast with broadcast ESPN/ABC, which splits the streaming audience across platforms.

**Q: What's the College Football Playoff situation for advertising?**

The CFP expanded to 12 teams starting in 2024, meaning more high-interest games stream on ESPN+. Quarterfinal and semifinal games are among the highest-rated college sports broadcasts of the year. Advertising around CFP games through Disney Advertising's platform requires advance planning — contact 6–8 weeks before the games.

**Q: Can small local businesses realistically afford ESPN+ CTV advertising?**

Yes, via programmatic DSP. StackAdapt and Simpli.fi allow campaigns starting at $1,500/month targeting ESPN+ sports content in your local area. This is significantly lower than direct buy minimums. The trade-off: less precise program selection, but you'll still be running against live sports content.
