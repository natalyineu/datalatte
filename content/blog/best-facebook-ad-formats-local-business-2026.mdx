Facebook ads can be a game-changer for small local businesses, but with so many ad formats to choose from, it can be overwhelming. Did you know that the average return on ad spend (ROAS) for small businesses on Facebook is $7.60 for every dollar spent? This is significantly higher than the average ROAS for large businesses. However, not all ad formats are created equal.

As a local business owner, you want to know which ad formats will drive the most results for your business. In this article, we'll explore the best Facebook ad formats for small local businesses, including coffee shops, salons, pet groomers, and fitness studios.

**1. Image Ads: The Classic Choice**

Image ads are the most popular ad format among small businesses on Facebook. They're easy to create and can be very effective, especially for businesses with a strong visual brand. For example, a coffee shop in New York City used image ads to promote their new summer menu, increasing sales by 15% in just one month.

<Callout type="tip">When creating an image ad, make sure to use high-quality images that are visually appealing and relevant to your business.</Callout>

**2. Video Ads: Tell a Story**

Video ads are a great way to tell a story and showcase your business in a more engaging way. They're particularly effective for businesses that have a strong narrative or want to showcase their products or services in action. For example, a pet groomer in Los Angeles used video ads to showcase their pet grooming services, increasing bookings by 25% in just three months.

**3. Carousel Ads: Showcase Multiple Products**

Carousel ads are an excellent way to showcase multiple products or services in a single ad. They're particularly effective for businesses with a wide range of offerings, such as a salon that offers haircuts, makeup services, and waxing. For example, a salon in San Francisco used carousel ads to promote their summer specials, increasing sales by 20% in just one month.

**4. Collection Ads: Showcase Products**

Collection ads are similar to carousel ads but are designed specifically for e-commerce businesses. They allow customers to browse and purchase products directly from the ad. For example, a fitness studio in Miami used collection ads to promote their fitness gear, increasing sales by 30% in just two months.

**5. Shopping Ads: Drive Sales**

Shopping ads are a type of ad that allows customers to purchase products directly from the ad. They're particularly effective for e-commerce businesses but can also be used by businesses that want to drive sales. For example, a pet groomer in Chicago used shopping ads to promote their pet grooming services, increasing sales by 25% in just one month.

**6. Lead Ads: Generate Leads**

Lead ads are a type of ad that allows customers to generate leads directly from the ad. They're particularly effective for businesses that want to generate leads, such as a salon that wants to collect email addresses from potential customers. For example, a salon in New York City used lead ads to generate leads, increasing their email list by 50% in just one month.

<Callout type="warning">Make sure to use lead ads responsibly and only collect email addresses from customers who have given their consent.</Callout>

**7. Story Ads: Reach a Wider Audience**

Story ads are a type of ad that can be used to reach a wider audience, including people who don't follow your business on Facebook. They're particularly effective for businesses that want to increase brand awareness, such as a coffee shop that wants to promote their new location.

**8. Carousel Cards: Showcase Multiple Products**

Carousel cards are a type of ad that allows customers to browse and purchase products directly from the ad. They're particularly effective for businesses that want to showcase multiple products, such as a salon that offers haircuts, makeup services, and waxing.

**Frequently Asked Questions**

Q: What is the best ad format for my business?
A: The best ad format for your business will depend on your goals and target audience. If you're looking to drive sales, consider using shopping ads or collection ads. If you're looking to generate leads, consider using lead ads.

Q: How do I create a Facebook ad?
A: To create a Facebook ad, log in to your Facebook Ads Manager account and click on the "Create Ad" button. From there, you can select an ad format, set your budget, and target your audience.

Q: How much does it cost to run a Facebook ad?
A: The cost of running a Facebook ad will depend on your budget and the ad format you choose. On average, small businesses spend between $500 and $2,000 per month on Facebook ads.

Q: How long does it take to see results from a Facebook ad?
A: The time it takes to see results from a Facebook ad will depend on your ad format, budget, and target audience. On average, small businesses see results from Facebook ads within 1-3 months.

Q: Can I track the performance of my Facebook ads?
A: Yes, you can track the performance of your Facebook ads using Facebook Ads Manager. From there, you can see metrics such as clicks, conversions, and return on ad spend.
