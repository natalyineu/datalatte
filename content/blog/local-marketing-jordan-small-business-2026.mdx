Jordan is the Middle East's most digitally engaged middle-income country. With 10 million people, one of the Arab world's highest literacy rates, and Amman as a sophisticated regional business hub, Jordan offers a distinctive digital marketing environment. Amman is a cosmopolitan capital with a vibrant café, restaurant, and creative scene; Aqaba is the Red Sea resort city; Petra and Wadi Rum draw international tourism.

Jordan's digital market is characterised by very high Facebook and Instagram penetration (significantly higher than Gulf states per capita), an educated and engaged online population, and a dynamic local economy across retail, food, beauty, and professional services. Jordan also hosts a large Palestinian and Syrian refugee population that has become an integral part of the economic and digital landscape.

**Facebook is Jordan's dominant social platform**: Unlike Gulf states where Snapchat and Instagram lead, Jordan's social media landscape is Facebook-first. Facebook Groups for Amman neighbourhoods, interest communities, and business networks are very active. Facebook marketplace and Facebook Shops are extensively used for commerce.

**WhatsApp for all business communication**: WhatsApp at 60% penetration is the universal business communication tool. WhatsApp Business is essential for any Jordanian SMB — inquiries, bookings, orders, and customer service all happen via WhatsApp.

**TikTok exploding**: Jordanian TikTok is one of the Levant's fastest-growing platforms. Arabic-language content — comedy, food, beauty — performs very well organically.

## Arabic Content for Jordan

Jordanian Arabic (الشامي — Levantine Arabic) is the local dialect:

- **Levantine Arabic** for casual social media posts, TikTok, and Stories — connects with local Jordanian consumers authentically
- **Modern Standard Arabic (فصحى)** for formal advertising, Google Ads, and professional content
- **English** for business-to-business, tourist-facing content (Petra, Aqaba), and reaching international expats

Best practice: MSA/Levantine mix for Facebook and Instagram posts; MSA for Google Ads; English for tourism-related content.

## Three Jordanian Business Examples

### ☕ Specialty Café, Abdoun Amman

**Strategy**: Arabic/English bilingual Instagram (Abdoun has expats and international community), Facebook for Amman lifestyle groups, Google Maps for "café Amman" (tourist/expat searches), Google Search Ads in Arabic, WhatsApp for reservations, TikTok food content in Levantine Arabic.

**Budget**: JOD 600/month (≈$845): JOD 240 Google Ads, JOD 220 Meta/TikTok Ads, JOD 140 content.

### 💇 صالون حلاقة (Hair Salon), Sweifieh Amman

**Strategy**: Instagram before/after content in Arabic, Facebook for Amman women's community groups, Google Search Ads in Arabic, Google Maps, WhatsApp for bookings.

**Budget**: JOD 400/month (≈$563): JOD 160 Google Ads, JOD 150 Meta Ads, JOD 90 content.

### 🐾 Pet Groomer, Amman

**Strategy**: English-Arabic bilingual Instagram (expat pet owners in Amman are significant), Google Search Ads in English and Arabic, Google Maps, WhatsApp for appointments.

**Budget**: JOD 300/month (≈$423): JOD 130 Google Ads, JOD 110 Meta Ads, JOD 60 content.

**Christmas in Jordan**: Jordan has a significant Christian minority (approximately 6% of population) and strong Western cultural influence. Christmas is commercially celebrated — decorations in Amman malls, restaurant Christmas menus, and consumer spending around December 25. Businesses in Amman can run Christmas campaigns without cultural issues.

**Ramadan**: Like all Muslim-majority markets, Ramadan drives specific marketing patterns — family iftar, food and beverage promotions for suhoor/iftar timing, beauty salon surge before Eid. Start Ramadan marketing 2 weeks before the month begins.

## FAQ

**Is Facebook or Instagram more important for Jordan?**
Facebook for broader demographic reach and community engagement (older demographics, groups, marketplace). Instagram for younger consumers and lifestyle businesses (beauty, café, fitness). Both are essential — Facebook for community discovery, Instagram for brand aesthetic and product showcase.

**How does Jordan compare to Gulf markets for digital marketing?**
Lower consumer spending power than Gulf states but more authentic digital engagement per user. Jordan has less influencer saturation and more genuine community-driven marketing. CPCs are significantly lower. For businesses serving the Jordanian middle class, very efficient digital marketing is achievable on moderate budgets.

**Is cash still dominant in Jordan?**
Cash remains very common across Jordan, particularly in traditional commercial areas and smaller cities. Card payment is standard in upscale Amman restaurants and shops. CliQ (Jordan's instant payment platform) is growing for digital transfers. Accept both cash and card for maximum accessibility.
