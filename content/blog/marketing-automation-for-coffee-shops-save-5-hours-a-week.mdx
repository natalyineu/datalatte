**What if you could save 5 hours every week on marketing and still see a boost in sales?**

It's not just about saving time; it's about getting more customers through your door. With marketing automation, you can create targeted campaigns that speak to your customers' needs and interests.

**Step 1: Set Up Your Marketing Mix**

To start automating your marketing, you need a solid foundation. This includes:

* A website that showcases your products and services
* Social media profiles that engage with your customers
* Email marketing that keeps them informed about promotions and events
* A data source that integrates with all these channels

You can either build this from scratch or use existing services like [Google My Business](/services/google-business-profile) and [social media management](/services/social-media).

**Step 2: Create a Content Calendar**

A content calendar is the backbone of any marketing automation strategy. It helps you plan and schedule content in advance, ensuring consistency and reducing the workload.

For example, if you're a coffee shop in New York City, your content calendar might look like this:

* Monday: Share a new coffee of the month on social media
* Tuesday: Send an email to subscribers about a special promotion
* Wednesday: Post a blog about the benefits of coffee for productivity
* Thursday: Share a behind-the-scenes look at your roasting process
* Friday: Share a customer testimonial on social media

Use a tool like [Trello](/services/analytics) or [Asana](/services/analytics) to create and manage your content calendar.

**Step 3: Automate Your Email Campaigns**

Email marketing is a powerful tool for coffee shops. With automation, you can create campaigns that resonate with your customers and drive sales.

For example, you can create a welcome email for new subscribers that includes a discount code for their first purchase. Or, you can create a series of emails that showcase different products and promotions.

To automate your email campaigns, use a tool like [Mailchimp](/services/email-sms) or [Constant Contact](/services/email-sms).
