Bosnia and Herzegovina (BiH) is a Balkan country of 3.5 million people with one of Europe's most complex constitutions — the result of the Dayton Peace Agreement (1995). Sarajevo is the capital — a remarkable city where Ottoman, Austro-Hungarian, Yugoslav, and modern influences collide, famous for the Baščaršija old bazaar. Mostar is the jewel of Herzegovina, known for the rebuilt Stari Most (Old Bridge) over the Neretva River. Banja Luka is the main city of Republika Srpska.

BiH uses the Bosnian Convertible Mark (BAM), permanently pegged to the Euro at 1 EUR = 1.955 BAM. This peg makes budget planning stable. The country has three constituent peoples — Bosniaks, Croats, and Serbs — with different language labels (Bosnian, Croatian, Serbian) for what is a mutually intelligible South Slavic language.

| Google | 95%+ search share | Dominant |

**Facebook dominant**: BiH's social media is firmly Facebook-centric. Community groups, local business pages, and marketplace are all heavily used. For most BiH businesses, Facebook is the core digital marketing channel.

**Viber holds on**: Unlike most of Western Europe, Viber remains relevant in BiH — particularly for older demographics. WhatsApp has broadly overtaken it, but some customer segments still prefer Viber.

**Sarajevo tourism boom**: Sarajevo has become one of Europe's most discussed tourism destinations — drawn by its unique history, food (ćevapi is famous), and cultural complexity. Mostar's Stari Most is one of Europe's most photographed bridges. Tourism businesses need multilingual English/German/Italian content.

## Three BiH Business Examples

### 💇 Frizerski Salon, Sarajevo

**Strategy**: Instagram transformation content in Bosnian, Facebook for Sarajevo community groups, Google Search Ads, Google Maps, WhatsApp for appointments.

**Budget**: BAM 600/month (≈€307): BAM 250 Google Ads, BAM 225 Meta Ads, BAM 125 content.

### ☕ Kafić/Restaurant, Sarajevo Baščaršija

**Strategy**: Bilingual Bosnian/English Instagram (locals + tourists), Facebook for Sarajevo groups, Google Maps with English description, TripAdvisor, WhatsApp for reservations.

**Budget**: BAM 800/month (≈€409): BAM 340 Google Ads, BAM 290 Meta Ads, BAM 170 content.

### 🌉 Tourism Experience, Mostar

**Strategy**: English-primary Instagram (Stari Most photography content), Google Search Ads in English ("Mostar tour", "Old Bridge Mostar"), TripAdvisor listing, Google Maps, WhatsApp for multi-language bookings.

**Budget**: BAM 700/month (≈€358): BAM 300 Google Ads, BAM 260 Meta Ads, BAM 140 content.

**Multi-religious calendar**: BiH is unique in Europe — a country where Catholic Christmas (Dec 25), Orthodox Christmas (Jan 7), and Eid all generate commercial peaks. Beauty salons and restaurants see demand spikes around all three celebration clusters.

**Ramadan and Eid**: Bosnia has a significant Muslim Bosniak population — Ramadan and Eid al-Fitr are major observances. Sarajevo's Baščaršija area sees extraordinary activity during Ramadan evenings (iftars). Eid gifts and beauty appointments are significant commercial moments.

## FAQ

**Bosnian, Croatian, or Serbian — which language for marketing?**
All three names refer to the same mutually intelligible language. In Sarajevo and Federation territory, "Bosnian" is the preferred label. In Republika Srpska, Serbian. In Herzeg-Bosnia, Croatian. For nationwide marketing, use neutral terms. The script varies: Bosniaks and Croats use Latin; Serbs use Cyrillic. Latin script is safe for all audiences in BiH.

**Is Sarajevo growing as a tourism destination?**
Rapidly. Sarajevo has been featured in major international travel publications as an emerging destination for its unique history, food scene, and affordability compared to Western Europe. Tourism businesses should invest in English-language digital presence.
