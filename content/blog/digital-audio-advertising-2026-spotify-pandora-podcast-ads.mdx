Audio reaches people when other ad formats can't. While CTV requires eyes on a screen and DOOH needs physical proximity, digital audio ads reach your audience in their car, at the gym, walking the dog, or cooking dinner — moments when they're mentally receptive but visually unavailable to any other ad medium.

In 2026, digital audio advertising means five distinct channels: streaming music (Spotify, Pandora), programmatic radio (iHeart, Audacy), podcast ads (host-read, programmatic, and dynamic insertion), smart speaker ads (Amazon Alexa, Google Home), and streaming audio via DSPs. Each has different audiences, CPMs, minimums, and creative requirements.

This guide covers the real numbers — and which channels make sense for local businesses spending $250–$2,000/month.

## Why Audio Deserves a Place in Your Local Ad Stack

Audio's core advantage is **reach into unavailable moments.** Think about your customer's day:

- 7am: Driving to work, listening to Spotify or a podcast
- 12pm: Eating lunch at their desk with AirPods in
- 5:30pm: Commuting home, streaming a local radio station via iHeart
- 7pm: Cooking dinner with Alexa playing background music

None of these moments are reachable by CTV (they're not watching TV), social (they're not scrolling), or DOOH (they're not near your billboard). Audio is the only ad format that competes for those minutes.

For local businesses, the most valuable use case is **contextual timing + geographic precision:** a 30-second audio ad for your coffee shop appearing at 7:15am for listeners within 3 miles of your location, right as they're deciding whether to stop somewhere on their commute.

## Podcast Advertising: Three Models

Podcast advertising is a separate channel from streaming music audio. There are three distinct ways to buy it:

### 1. Host-Read Ads (Direct Publisher Deals)

The original podcast ad format: you negotiate directly with a podcast host, they read your ad in their own voice and style during the episode. These ads achieve exceptional trust transfer — listeners often can't distinguish where the show ends and the ad begins.

**CPM range:** $18–$50+ depending on show and audience quality
**Minimum spend:** Varies by show; many mid-tier shows (10,000–50,000 listeners/episode) will negotiate at $200–$500/episode
**Best for:** Service businesses that benefit from trust and personality (consulting, premium services, local businesses with a story to tell)
**How to buy:** Directly with the podcast team, or via Podcorn / Gumball (marketplaces that connect advertisers with independent podcasters)

### 2. Programmatic Podcast Ads (Dynamic Ad Insertion)

Ads are dynamically inserted into podcast episodes at download time — so your ad appears in any episode the listener downloads, regardless of when it was recorded. The podcast host doesn't read your ad; it's a standard produced audio spot.

**CPM range:** $12–$25
**Platforms:** Spotify Audience Network (SAX), Pandora/Stitcher, Audacy, AdsWizz, Triton Digital
**Minimum spend:** $250 via Spotify Ad Studio (includes podcast inventory); programmatic DSP access via AdsWizz at $1,500+
**Targeting:** Podcast genre, listener demographics, location, behavioral segments
**Best for:** Scaling audio campaigns beyond music streaming into the podcast listener base

### 3. Branded Content / Sponsorships

Longer-form brand integration — sponsored episodes, branded series, or host-read mid-roll segments that appear in every episode for a defined period. These require significant budgets and longer lead times. Not typically relevant for small businesses.

## Creative Best Practices for Audio Ads

Audio has no visuals — your message has to work entirely through sound, structure, and voice.

**30-second script structure that converts:**
1. **Hook (0–3 seconds):** Open with the problem or a bold claim. "Tired of slow Wi-Fi ruining your morning?" or "[Business name] — [city]'s highest-rated coffee shop"
2. **Body (3–22 seconds):** One benefit, delivered clearly. Don't try to say five things.
3. **Call to action (22–30 seconds):** Tell them exactly what to do. "Find us on [Street], open from 7am. Search [Business Name]." Repeat the name twice.

**Production notes:**
- Keep background music at 20–30% volume — it should support, not compete
- Use a voice that matches your brand personality (warm and local for coffee/food; energetic for fitness; calm and reassuring for health/wellness)
- No jargon — listeners are multitasking and can't rewind
- State your location or key identifier early — "on Main Street in [neighborhood]" is better than just a URL

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the minimum budget to advertise on Spotify?

Spotify Ad Studio requires a $250 minimum per campaign. At $15–$25 CPM, this reaches approximately 10,000–16,700 targeted listeners. AudioGo also starts at $250 and provides access to Spotify, Pandora, and iHeart simultaneously at a blended CPM of $8–$18 — often a better value for first-time audio advertisers.

### Is podcast advertising worth it for local businesses?

It depends on the format. Host-read ads on local or niche podcasts with strong listener engagement can work well for service businesses that benefit from trust transfer (premium services, local specialists, wellness brands). Programmatic podcast ads (dynamic insertion) are better for scale and reach. Start with programmatic via Spotify Ad Studio ($250 minimum) before investing in direct host-read deals.

### Can I target people within 5 miles of my business with audio ads?

Yes. Spotify Ad Studio, AudioGo, and Pandora all support city-level and zip code targeting. Spotify specifically supports radius-based targeting around a location. For granular hyperlocal targeting (sub-1-mile radius), StackAdapt's programmatic audio via AdsWizz provides the most precise geographic controls.

### What kind of audio ad creative do I need?

A produced 30-second audio spot with background music. Spotify's Ad Studio includes a free voiceover tool — provide a script and they generate broadcast-quality audio at no cost. For AudioGo, you can record via browser directly. Professional voiceover production typically costs $150–$400 via platforms like Voices.com or Voice123 if you want a custom recording.

### How do I measure whether audio ads are driving results?

Digital audio platforms provide impressions, reach, frequency, and completion rates. For conversion attribution: Spotify and AudioGo offer post-exposure website visit tracking (was your site visited after the listener heard your ad?). For foot traffic attribution, combine audio with Roku or StackAdapt campaigns that offer device-level location attribution — the cross-channel reporting shows lift in location visits for the overall campaign period.

## Common Mistakes (And What to Do Instead)

### Mistake 1: Writing a Radio Spot for Streaming Audio

A coffee shop in Austin spent $800 on Spotify ads in March 2025. They took their existing radio commercial — the one they ran on a local FM station for three years — and uploaded the exact same :30 file to Spotify Ad Studio. Their skip rate hit 78% in week one. They burned $230 before I got a panicked email.

The problem: Radio commercials are built for passive listening. They repeat the business name three times, cram a phone number and an address into the last five seconds, and use an announcer voice that sounds like a 1995 car dealership. Streaming listeners have a skip button. If you sound like a commercial, they will hit it.

The fix: Write a 15-second script with a single offer, a single benefit, and a single call to action. No phone number — use the ad's display card for that. No "we're located at 123 Main Street." Just the problem your customer has and the solution they want. One voice, conversational, recorded on a decent USB microphone, not in a studio.

The outcome: We rewrote the spot, re-recorded it in an afternoon, and relaunched. Skip rate dropped to 42%. Their cost per website visit went from $1.87 to $0.89. Same $800 budget, double the traffic.

### Mistake 2: Targeting the Whole City Instead of the Drive Zone

A pet groomer in Portland ran Pandora ads targeting the entire Portland metro — about 650,000 people. Her CPM was $18. She spent $1,200 in June and got three calls she could trace back to the ads. One of those calls was from someone in Beaverton, a 35-minute drive away, who hung up when she gave the address.

The fix: I told her to draw a 15-minute drive radius around her shop. She serves $65 dog grooms. Nobody is driving 35 minutes for a $65 groom. We uploaded her existing customer list as a custom audience seed, then layered a 5-mile geo-fence. We also excluded ZIP codes we knew had three competing groomers within walking distance.

The outcome: She cut her CPM to $14 (smaller audience, higher density of actual potential customers). Monthly spend dropped to $900. She got 11 booked appointments in July — $715 in revenue from a $900 spend doesn't look great on paper, until you realize those customers have a 68% repeat rate at her shop. Twelve-month LTV on those 11 appointments: about $4,800.

### Mistake 3: No Call Tracking, No Attribution, No Clue

A salon owner in Chicago's Wicker Park neighborhood ran iHeart podcast ads for three months. She spent $1,500 total. She told me the ads "didn't work." I asked how she knew. She said nobody mentioned the ad when they called.

That's not proof of failure. That's proof she didn't ask.

The fix: We set up a unique phone number through CallRail — $45/month. We ran the same ads with the same creative, just swapped the phone number. In week one, she got four calls from that number. Two booked appointments ($340 in services). Two showed up and spent another $120 on retail. She was on track to break even at month three with a 60%+ retention rate on those new clients.

The uncomfortable truth: If you can't measure something, you're not allowed to say it didn't work. Put a unique phone number, a promo code, or a dedicated landing page behind every campaign. Otherwise you're guessing, and your guess is probably wrong.

## How to Set Up a Podcast Ad That Doesn't Sound Like a Podcast Ad

I've placed programmatic podcast ads for brands spending $50,000/month. I've also helped a Nashville coffee roaster spend $800/month on host-read spots in a local food podcast. The same principles apply at both budgets, and most small businesses get this wrong.

**Programmatic podcast ads** (the ones inserted dynamically by platforms like iHeart, AdsWizz, or Spotify) give you targeting and scale. But they sound like they were dropped in from space — because they were. The host just finished a story about their dog, and suddenly a polished voiceover is telling you to get your oil changed. Listeners check out.

**Host-read ads** cost more and take longer to set up. But they work better for local businesses because the host's voice is trust. A host in Pittsburgh talking about your hair salon in Lawrenceville is more valuable than a perfectly produced spot that sounds like corporate radio.

### What Actually Works at $500–$2,000/month

1. **Find a local podcast your actual customers listen to.** Not "people like your customers." Your actual customers. One way: Ask the last 20 people who walked through your door what they listen to. Three of them name the same show? That's your target.

2. **Negotiate a host-read package.** Most local podcasts charge $200–$800 per mid-roll, depending on download numbers. Offer a three-month commitment at 20% off. That's $480–$1,920 for three months. Do not buy one-off ads from podcasts that accept everyone's money. If they'll sell a host-read spot to a mattress company AND a plumbing service in the same episode, the listener is already tuning out.

3. **Give the host a script — but let them rewrite it.** Write three bullet points you want covered. Let the host turn it into their own words. The Denver gym I worked with got a 4x lift in promo code redemptions when they let the host describe the gym as "the place where you can deadlift without someone filming you for TikTok" — because that's what the host actually said when he visited.

4. **Use a unique URL or promo code.** Not "visit our website." "Go to denverstrength.com/podcast20" or "use code PODCAST20" at checkout. Track it. If your podcast ad generates 0 redemptions in month one, either the audience isn't there or the offer isn't compelling. Test a different offer before you kill the channel.

## Streaming Radio: The Channel Everyone Forgets Exists

Everyone obsesses over Spotify and podcast ads. Meanwhile, streaming radio via iHeart, Audacy, and TuneIn reaches 40% of US adults monthly — and most small businesses ignore it entirely.

Programmatic radio works differently than Spotify. You buy ads that insert into live or on-demand radio streams. Someone in Austin listening to a sports talk show on iHeart hears your spot. Someone in Boston listening to the same show hears a different spot from a local business there.

### Why Programmatic Radio Works for Local Businesses

**Lower CPMs than streaming music.** I've seen $10–$18 CPMs on iHeart programmatic radio, compared to $15–$25 on Spotify. For a business spending $500/month, that's 30,000–50,000 impressions versus 20,000–33,000. Not nothing.

**Better for older demographics.** Spotify skews 18–34. Podcasts skew 25–44. Streaming radio? 35–65. If your business serves homeowners, car owners, or people with kids, you're reaching the right age group.

**More inventory in drive time.** Between 6–9am and 4–7pm, streaming radio has more active listeners than Spotify. That's when people are driving, which is when they're most likely to act on a local ad.

### What a $500/Month Streaming Radio Buy Actually Looks Like

A yoga studio in Denver signed up for a three-month programmatic radio campaign through iHeart's platform. They bought 60-second spots in morning and afternoon drive time, targeting a 5-mile radius around their studio.

- CPM: $14
- Monthly impressions: ~35,700
- Monthly spend: $500
- Promo code: "YOGA20" for $20 off a first class
- First month redemptions: 8 ($160 in first-class revenue)
- Second month redemptions: 14 ($280)
- Third month redemptions: 21 ($420)
- Three-month total spend: $1,500
- Three-month total revenue (first classes only): $860

Not profitable yet. But of those 43 first-time customers, 17 bought a 10-class pack ($180 each = $3,060) and 5 signed up for monthly unlimited ($130/mo = $650/mo recurring). That changed the math entirely. By month six, that $1,500 had generated about $4,200 in attributable revenue.

The catch: You need a way to track first-class redemptions and follow up with those customers. If you're not doing that, programmatic radio will look like a black hole.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I just use Google Ads instead of audio? Isn't that more trackable?**
Yes, search ads are more trackable. No, you shouldn't just use Google Ads. Your customers aren't searching for your business all day. They're driving, cooking, walking the dog. Audio reaches them in those moments. The right approach is to use search to capture demand and audio to create it. They're different tools, not substitutes.

**Q: How do I know anyone actually heard my ad?**
You don't — not perfectly. But you can get close. Use unique promo codes, dedicated phone numbers, and UTM-tagged landing pages. Measure lift in branded search volume during your campaign. If more people are Googling "your business name + your city" while the ad is running, it's working.

**Q: My budget is $300/month. Is audio worth it?**
Probably not on Spotify ($250 minimum, $300 gets you ~15,000 impressions — too small to measure). Podcast ads at that budget mean one or two host-read spots in a small show. That can work if the show's audience overlaps perfectly with yours. But at $300, you might be better off with hyper-targeted Facebook ads or local search until you have $500–$1,000 to test audio properly.

**Q: Isn't audio just for millennials who live in cities?**
Spotify and podcast audiences skew urban and under 45. But streaming radio via iHeart reaches rural and older listeners just fine. A tractor supply store in rural Iowa would get more value from streaming radio than from Spotify. Know your customer, pick the channel they actually use.

**Q: How long does it take to see results?**
If you're running Spotify or programmatic radio, expect two to four weeks before you have enough data to make decisions. If you're running host-read podcast ads with a promo code, you'll know after the first episode runs — either the code gets used or it doesn't. Podcast results come fast. Streaming takes patience.

**Q: Do I need a professional voiceover?**
No. Record on a decent USB microphone in a quiet room. Speak like a human, not a radio announcer. Some of the best-performing spots I've produced were recorded by the business owner in their car. If you sound authentic, your local audience will trust you more than a polished voice they recognize from 12 other ads.
