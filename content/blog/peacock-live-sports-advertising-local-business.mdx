Peacock has become one of the most sports-dense streaming platforms in the US. It carries Sunday Night Football (the most-watched show in American television), Premier League soccer (the US's highest-rated soccer), the Olympics, NASCAR, and golf — making it a full-year live sports destination, not just a seasonal option. For local businesses targeting sports fans through CTV, Peacock is one of the most important platforms to understand.

## What Live Sports Peacock Carries

Peacock's sports rights portfolio covers a remarkably broad range of events:

**NFL Football**:
- All Sunday Night Football games (also on NBC broadcast)
- Exclusive streaming games (Peacock had an exclusive Wild Card playoff game in 2024 drawing 23M+ viewers — the most-streamed live event in US history at the time)
- Select Thursday Night Football simulcasts

**Premier League Soccer**:
- 175+ Premier League matches per season, including exclusive Peacock-only games
- Running August through May (year-round sports content)
- 2M–4M viewers per match in the US

**Olympics**:
- All Paris 2024 and future Olympic coverage streams on Peacock (in addition to NBC/cable)
- Among the highest-viewership events in sports

**NASCAR**:
- Multiple race weekends per season
- Growing streaming audience particularly strong in South and Midwest markets

**Golf**:
- PGA Tour events including exclusive rounds
- The Masters (NBC/Peacock has morning rounds)

**Notre Dame Football**:
- All Notre Dame home games (NBC/Peacock exclusive rights)
- Strong viewership particularly in Catholic-majority markets and Notre Dame alumni cities

This year-round sports calendar is what makes Peacock uniquely valuable for local advertisers. You can maintain presence around live sports content across all 12 months, not just during NFL season.

## Peacock's Advertising Model

Peacock has two tiers:
- **Peacock Free** (ad-supported, limited content)
- **Peacock Premium** (subscription, still ad-supported for most plans — this is the tier where live sports live)

Key insight: Unlike some streaming services that offer ad-free subscriptions, Peacock Premium (the most common subscription plan) still shows ads. Live sports content on Peacock Premium is ad-supported. This means your CTV ads reach Peacock's full paid subscriber base, not just free-tier users.

**Ad load**: Peacock runs 4–5 ad minutes per hour during live sports — significantly less than traditional broadcast TV (12–16 minutes/hour). Lower ad load means your spot stands out more in each break.

## Who Buys Peacock Ads and How

### NBCUniversal Local Ad Sales (Recommended Starting Point)

NBCUniversal — Peacock's parent company — has a local ad sales infrastructure in most major US markets through its NBC affiliate stations.

**How to access it**:
1. Contact your local NBC station's advertising department (search "NBC [your city] advertising sales")
2. Ask specifically about "local streaming adjacency to Peacock live sports"
3. Most local NBC stations can sell local spots that appear within Peacock streams for viewers in their DMA

This direct approach gives you:
- Better inventory access to premium windows (SNF, playoff games)
- Ability to negotiate by-program placement (buy specifically around SNF vs. just general sports)
- A direct relationship for managing creative and reporting

**Budget requirements**: Local NBC sales teams typically want $3,000–$8,000+ minimum monthly spends. Higher for playoff windows.

### One Platform (NBCUniversal's Ad Tech Platform)

NBCUniversal's self-serve and managed advertising platform, One Platform, is the programmatic access point for Peacock inventory.

For larger local advertisers or agencies managing multiple client budgets, One Platform allows portfolio buying across Peacock, NBC broadcast, and NBCUniversal digital properties simultaneously.

**Access**: Through NBCUniversal advertising partners or direct if spending at sufficient scale.

### Programmatic DSPs (Best for $1,500–$5,000 Budgets)

The most accessible route for local businesses: buy Peacock live sports inventory through a programmatic DSP.

Platforms like **StackAdapt**, **Simpli.fi**, and **The Trade Desk** buy Peacock inventory on the programmatic open market and private marketplaces. You're not buying specific shows — you're buying sports CTV inventory in your geography that Peacock makes available programmatically.

**Setup**:
- Platform: StackAdapt or Simpli.fi
- Content category: Sports streaming / Live sports
- Inventory source: Include Peacock if the platform allows publisher selection
- Geographic targeting: DMA, metro area, or zip radius
- Budget: $1,500–$3,000/month minimum for meaningful reach
- Flight: Run year-round or align with your target sport's season

**What to expect**: Impressions served across Peacock's ad-supported inventory in your geography, concentrated during live sports windows. You won't control placement at the game level but will see strong sports adjacency.

## Premier League as a Local Business Opportunity

Premier League soccer is one of the most underutilized CTV advertising opportunities for local businesses in the US. Here's why:

**Schedule advantage**: PL games air at 7:30am, 10:00am, and 12:30pm ET on weekends (due to time zones). This unusual timing creates ad inventory during a morning/midday window when most live sports ads run in evenings. For businesses targeting weekend morning traffic (brunch spots, coffee shops, weekend service businesses), PL morning windows are valuable.

**Demographic alignment**: Premier League US viewers skew younger (21–38 is the core), more urban, and more international than NFL viewers. If you're a business in a metro area serving a young professional demographic, PL viewers are often a better match than NFL.

**CPM efficiency**: At $25–$40 CPM, Premier League CTV is 30–50% cheaper than NFL CTV for a live sports audience. For businesses with limited budgets who still want the brand-building benefit of live sports adjacency, Premier League is the most accessible option.

**Market relevance**: In markets with large British expat communities, Irish-American communities, or Latin American communities (many of whom follow European soccer), Premier League CPM efficiency compounds with audience relevance.

## The Olympics: High Viewership, Short Window

Every two years, Peacock carries the Summer or Winter Olympics alongside NBC broadcast. For local businesses, this represents a unique opportunity:

**Characteristics**:
- 2–3 weeks of peak-awareness content
- Extremely broad audience (Olympics are one of the most universally watched events)
- Strong female viewership (women are majority Olympics viewers — unusual for sports)
- Premium CPMs ($60–$100 for primetime events) but high impact

**Best use**: Brand awareness campaigns during Olympic windows are an efficient way to reach a very broad audience. For businesses targeting women 25–54, the Olympics are one of the best live sports contexts available.

**Timing**: Plan Olympic campaigns 6–8 weeks in advance — inventory tightens significantly as the games approach.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Peacock advertising the same as buying a spot on NBC?**

No, they're separate buys. A traditional NBC local broadcast spot appears on the broadcast channel. A Peacock CTV ad appears in the streaming version of the same content — reaching different viewers (those watching on streaming vs. those watching on broadcast TV). Many agencies buy both simultaneously for maximum reach, but they are priced and managed separately.

**Q: Does Peacock offer exclusive streaming events that broadcast doesn't have?**

Yes. Peacock has run exclusive NFL games (the 2024 Wild Card game was streaming-only, no broadcast option). These exclusive events are high-value advertising opportunities because the entire audience is concentrated on Peacock — there's no broadcast alternative drawing viewers away.

**Q: Can I target Premier League viewers specifically without also buying NFL content?**

Yes, if you work with an agency that has direct access to Peacock's inventory (through One Platform or NBCUniversal direct deals). Through programmatic DSPs, you're often buying "sports CTV" as a category, which mixes content types. If content-specific targeting matters, direct buying is more precise.

**Q: How do I measure whether Peacock CTV ads are driving business results?**

Peacock and NBCUniversal provide reach and frequency reporting. To connect TV exposure to business outcomes, use landing page analytics (unique URL in ad), post-campaign lift studies (available through NBCUniversal for larger spends), or foot traffic studies through DSPs like StackAdapt that offer location-based conversion tracking.
