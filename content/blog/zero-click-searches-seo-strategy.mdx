**What are Zero-Click Searches?**

Zero-click searches refer to the increasing trend of users finding what they're looking for without ever clicking on a link. This can happen in a few ways:

* Direct answers: Google displays the answer to a user's question directly in the search results, eliminating the need for a click.
* Featured snippets: Google highlights a relevant answer or piece of information in a box at the top of the search results, making it easy for users to find what they're looking for.
* Google My Business (GMB) listings: Google displays a business's GMB listing in the search results, including their address, hours, and reviews.

**Why Should You Care About Zero-Click Searches?**

Zero-click searches may seem like a threat to your business, but they can actually be a blessing in disguise. By optimizing your GMB listing and website for zero-click searches, you can increase your visibility and drive more customers to your door.

* **Local searches increase by 25%** when users are looking for a specific type of business.

**Optimizing Your GMB Listing for Zero-Click Searches**

To make the most of zero-click searches, you need to optimize your GMB listing for maximum visibility. Here are a few tips to get you started:

* **Claim and complete your GMB listing**: Make sure your listing is accurate and up-to-date, including your hours, address, and contact information.
* **Use high-quality photos and videos**: Showcase your business and products with high-quality photos and videos.
* **Encourage reviews**: Encourage your customers to leave reviews on your GMB listing.
* **Use relevant and accurate categories**: Use relevant and accurate categories to help Google understand your business.

**Callout: Tip**

Don't forget to keep your GMB listing up-to-date! Google favors businesses with accurate and consistent information.

**Callout: Warning**

Be careful not to overdo it with the photos and videos. You don't want to overwhelm your users with too much information.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: If people don't click, how do I track if zero-click searches are working?**

You look at Google Business Profile insights — impressions, phone calls, direction requests, booking clicks. Those are all zero-click conversions. You also check your Google Local Services Ads dashboard if you're running that. The number that matters is: how many people interacted with your listing without visiting your site. If that number is going up and your phone is ringing, you're winning.

**Q: Does zero-click mean SEO is dead for local businesses?**

No. It means the goal of SEO has shifted. Ranking for "coffee shop near me" is still valuable, but what you want people to *do* with that ranking has changed. You don't need them to click your website. You need them to walk through your door or call. The ranking gets you the visibility. The zero-click conversion gets you the revenue.

**Q: I don't have time to update my Google Business Profile every week. Is it worth automating?**

Partially. You can schedule Google Posts using tools like Semrush or local SEO automation platforms. But the things that matter most — responding to reviews, updating hours for holidays, answering Q&A — can't be fully automated and shouldn't be. If you cannot spend 20 minutes a week on your GMB, outsource it to someone on your team or hire a freelancer. For most businesses, that 20 minutes generates more revenue than any other marketing activity.

**Q: I'm a plumber in a small town. Does this still work?**

Yes, sometimes better. In a small town, your Google Business Profile might be the only source of new customers outside of word-of-mouth. If someone in your town searches "plumber near me," your profile appears. If you have a Google Guaranteed badge, you appear at the top. If you have recent reviews and posts, you appear more often. The smaller the market, the more weight Google gives to local signals. I've seen a HVAC company in rural Missouri get 90% of its new leads from Google Business Profile alone — zero clicks, zero website traffic, just phone calls from a well-maintained listing.

**Q: Won't Google eventually stop showing my website altogether?**

Google's interest is keeping people on their platform. That's real. But for local businesses, your website still matters for credibility, for deep content (pricing pages, FAQs, service descriptions), and for email capture. The website is no longer your front door. It's your office — where you close the deal after someone knocks at the GMB window.

**Q: Should I spend money on Google Ads if zero-click is the goal?**

Yes, for specific purposes. Local Services Ads are essentially zero-click ads — you pay per lead, not per click. That's a great investment for plumbers, electricians, locksmiths, and similar trades. Standard Google Ads can also drive zero-click conversions if your ad points to a click-to-call or a direct booking page on mobile. But do not run ads for the sake of traffic. Run them for the sake of a phone ringing or a calendar slot filling.
