### Step 1: Create and Install the Facebook Pixel

First, you need to create and install the Facebook pixel on your website. This will allow you to track conversions and retarget users who have interacted with your ads.

To create the pixel, go to the Facebook Ads Manager and click on "Pixels" in the top navigation bar. Then, click on "Create a pixel" and follow the prompts to set it up.

### Step 2: Add the Pixel to Your Website

Once you've created the pixel, you'll need to add it to your website. You can do this by adding a small piece of code to your website's header or by using a plugin like WordPress's "Facebook Pixel" plugin.

To add the pixel to your website, go to the Facebook Ads Manager and click on the pixel you created in Step 1. Then, click on "Get code" and follow the prompts to add the code to your website.

### Step 3: Set Up Conversions

Now that you've added the pixel to your website, it's time to set up conversions. Conversions are the actions you want users to take on your website, such as filling out a form, making a purchase, or booking an appointment.

To set up conversions, go to the Facebook Ads Manager and click on "Pixels" in the top navigation bar. Then, click on the pixel you created in Step 1 and click on "Conversions" in the left-hand menu.

### Step 4: Track Custom Conversions

In addition to setting up standard conversions, you can also track custom conversions. Custom conversions allow you to track specific actions on your website that aren't already included in the standard conversion types.

To track custom conversions, go to the Facebook Ads Manager and click on "Pixels" in the top navigation bar. Then, click on the pixel you created in Step 1 and click on "Custom Conversions" in the left-hand menu.
