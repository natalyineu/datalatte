## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much does an AI local SEO audit cost? Is it worth it for a business making $5,000/month?**

The DataLatte AI audit is $97 for a one-time report. I’ve seen it uncover issues that cost businesses $500–$1,500 in missed revenue per month. If your business is smaller, the free version of BrightLocal can run a basic check on your Google Business Profile and citations. It’s not as thorough, but it’s free and catches the big stuff. I wouldn’t pay more than $200 for a DIY audit tool. Anything above that is usually a resell of the same data with a higher price tag.

**Q: Can I do this myself or do I need to hire someone?**

You can absolutely do the basics yourself: fix your NAP consistency, respond to reviews, update your hours, add photos. That’s probably 60% of the value right there. The AI audit just surfaces the things you’d miss, like unclaimed duplicate listings or negative sentiment patterns in reviews. If your time is worth more than $75/hour, pay for the tool and spend the saved time on revenue-generating work.

**Q: How quickly will I see results after fixing the issues?**

I usually see movement in 2–4 weeks for citation and profile fixes. Review response changes take a month or two because Google needs to accumulate data on your engagement. Keyword changes from new landing pages take longer — 6 to 12 weeks on average. If you see no change after 8 weeks, you’re probably fixing the wrong things. Re-run the audit.

**Q: I’m already paying for Google Ads. Do I still need local SEO?**

Two different channels. Google Ads drives immediate traffic while the budget lasts. Local SEO builds organic visibility that compounds. I’ve managed accounts where Ads spend was converted into 70% fewer leads after an SEO fix, because people started finding the business organically. Ideally, you run both — but if budget is tight ($500/month or less), fix your SEO first, then layer in ads. Otherwise you’re just paying to overcome your own bad data.

**Q: What if my business is service-based (plumber, electrician, cleaner)? Does this still work?**

Yes, maybe more so. Service-area businesses lose more from inconsistent NAP and missing categories because customers search by specific service plus location (“plumber in 37206”). The AI audit’s service-area analysis matters more for you than for a storefront business. I had an HVAC company in Phoenix gain 40% more calls by cleaning up their service-area zip codes and adding 8 missing directories.

**Q: Do I really need to respond to every single review?**

Not every single one, but aim for 100% of three-star and below reviews within 12 hours, and 80%+ of positive ones within 48 hours. Google’s algorithm weights response rate as an engagement signal. More importantly, potential customers read your responses and draw conclusions about your business. A defensive, argumentative response to a negative review can lose you more customers than the original complaint. Respond professionally, fix the issue, and move on.
