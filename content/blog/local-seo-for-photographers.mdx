## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I really need a physical studio address to rank for local searches?**
Not necessarily. You can rank as a service-area business without a public-facing address. Just set your GBP to "service area" mode and hide your address. You’ll still show up in local search results for the areas you serve. I’ve seen photographers in Nashville, Austin, and Portland rank in the top 3 without a physical studio. The trade-off is that you may rank slightly lower than businesses with verified physical locations. But if you serve clients on location, hiding your address is often better — it prevents people from showing up unannounced.

**Q: How long does it take to rank for "photographer near me" in a competitive city?**
If you’re starting from nothing — no GBP, no reviews, no citations — expect 3-6 months before you see consistent leads. If you already have a GBP with some reviews, 6-12 weeks is realistic for noticeable movement. For a city like New York or Los Angeles where everyone is a photographer, you’re looking at 6-12 months minimum. The photographers who succeed in competitive markets are the ones who build local relevance faster — reviews, backlinks, and service-area pages. In less competitive markets like Boise or Raleigh, you can hit page 1 in 4-8 weeks with consistent effort.

**Q: Should I pay for Yelp advertising as a photographer?**
Yelp ads work for some businesses but generally not for photographers. Yelp’s algorithm prioritizes advertisers in search results, but the click-through rates are low — typically under 2% for photography services. I’ve seen photographers spend $300/month on Yelp ads and get exactly two inquiries. Neither booked. Meanwhile, their GBP optimization was generating 10-15 inquiries per week for free. Test Yelp ads with $100 for one month. If you get a booking that covers the cost, scale it. If not, pull the money and put it into Google Ads or review generation instead.

**Q: Can I rank for multiple cities without having a studio in each one?**
Yes, but you need separate service-area pages for each city. Don’t use the same page for "Denver photographer" and "Boulder photographer." Create a page titled "Boulder, CO Photography Services" with local content, testimonials from Boulder clients, and local backlinks. You won’t rank as high as a business with a physical Boulder address, but you can absolutely get page 2 or page 1 for less competitive terms. I helped a photographer in Portland rank for "Vancouver, WA photographer" — a city 15 minutes away — using this exact strategy. Took 3 months and generated $8,000 in bookings.

**Q: Is it worth buying Google Ads for local photography searches?**
Depends on your city and competition. In expensive markets like San Francisco, "wedding photographer" can cost $15-25 per click. You need a high conversion rate to make that work. Start with $200/month and track everything. Use Google’s location targeting to show ads only within a 20-mile radius. Exclude cities you can’t realistically serve. I’ve seen photographers in mid-sized cities like Columbus, Ohio, get bookings for $8-12 per click — that’s profitable if your average booking is $2,500+. Set a bid cap, use exact-match keywords ("Austin wedding photographer" not "photographer"), and watch your cost per conversion like a hawk.

**Q: How many reviews do I need to rank on the first page?**
There’s no magic number. I’ve seen a photographer in Chicago with 22 reviews rank above a competitor with 87 reviews. The difference was recency and relevance. The 22-review photographer had 12 reviews from the past 30 days — all mentioning "engagement photography" — and his reply rate was 100%. The 87-review photographer hadn’t replied to a review in six months and his last review was 45 days old. Google cares about velocity, not just volume. If you get 3-5 new reviews per month and reply to every single one within 48 hours, you’ll outrank someone with 100 reviews that went stale.
