## What Is LinkedIn Ads?

LinkedIn Ads is a professional networking platform best known for B2B advertising, but increasingly viable for local service businesses targeting other businesses as customers — bookkeepers, commercial cleaners, B2B caterers, and office-services contractors.

## Ad Formats Available

- **Sponsored Content** — native feed ads
- **Message Ads** — direct InMail-style outreach
- **Lead Gen Forms** — pre-filled forms reducing friction
- **Sponsored Content with job-title and company-size targeting**

## Who Should Actually Use LinkedIn?

LinkedIn Ads works best for **B2B-facing local services: commercial cleaning, accounting/bookkeeping, office catering, IT support, and B2B contractors**.

<Callout type="tip">
LinkedIn's targeting by job title, company size, and industry is unmatched for reaching local business decision-makers specifically — no other platform lets you target "office managers at companies with 20-200 employees within 15 miles."
</Callout>

## Where LinkedIn Falls Short

CPCs and CPMs run significantly higher than Meta or Google — LinkedIn is not cost-effective for consumer-facing local businesses (salons, restaurants, gyms) and should be reserved for genuinely B2B offerings.

<Callout type="warning">
Don't move budget away from Google Ads or Meta Ads to fund a LinkedIn test unless you have a specific reason to believe your audience is there. Treat LinkedIn as incremental budget for testing, not a replacement for proven channels.
</Callout>

## Cost Expectations

Expect to spend **$30-$65 (or $5-$12 CPC)** depending on targeting and competition in your market. Most local businesses should budget at least **$10/day** for 4-6 weeks to gather enough data to judge whether the channel is working — shorter tests rarely produce statistically meaningful results.

## How to Test LinkedIn Without Wasting Budget

1. **Start with retargeting or your warmest audience**, not cold prospecting — this gives you a faster read on whether the platform's users respond to your offer at all.
2. **Run one campaign, one objective, one offer** for the first 30 days. Resist the urge to test multiple variables simultaneously.
3. **Set a hard budget cap** ($300-$500 total) for the test period so a poor-performing channel doesn't quietly drain your overall ad budget.
4. **Track cost-per-booking, not cost-per-click.** A cheap click that never converts is more expensive than an costly click that does.
5. **Compare against your existing channels' baseline** — a new platform only deserves ongoing budget if it beats (or meaningfully diversifies) what you're already getting from Google or Meta.

## LinkedIn vs Google Ads and Meta Ads

For most local businesses, Google Ads (capturing existing demand) and Meta Ads (building awareness + retargeting) should remain the foundation of a paid strategy. LinkedIn Ads is best treated as a supplementary or test channel — valuable specifically when your audience or business model matches its strengths, not as a general-purpose replacement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is LinkedIn worth it for small local businesses?

It depends entirely on your audience and business model. LinkedIn Ads works well for B2B-facing local services: commercial cleaning, accounting/bookkeeping, office catering, IT support, and B2B contractors, but isn't a good general-purpose channel for every local business type.

### How much does LinkedIn advertising cost?

Typical costs run $30-$65 (or $5-$12 CPC). Budget at least $10/day for a meaningful 4-6 week test before drawing conclusions.

### Should I use LinkedIn instead of Google Ads?

No — treat it as a supplementary channel. Google Ads and Google Business Profile should remain your foundation; LinkedIn is worth testing once those are performing well and you have budget to experiment.

### How do I know if LinkedIn is right for my business?

Look at where your existing customers spend time online and what kind of decision-making process your service involves. If your audience and offer match the platform's strengths described above, it's worth a controlled test.
