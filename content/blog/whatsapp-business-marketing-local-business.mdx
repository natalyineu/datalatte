### Setting Up WhatsApp Business

Before you can start marketing on WhatsApp, you need to set up your WhatsApp Business account. This is a free service that allows you to create a business profile, add a catalog, and even use automated messaging tools. But don't worry, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step.

To set up your WhatsApp Business account, follow these simple steps:

* Download the WhatsApp Business app from the app store
* Verify your business phone number
* Create your business profile and add a profile picture and cover photo
* Add a catalog of your products or services
* Start sending automated messages to your customers

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is WhatsApp Business actually free? What's the catch?**

Yes, it's free. The catch is that if you want to send outbound messages to people who haven't messaged you first within 24 hours, you have to pay per message. This only matters if you're doing mass broadcasts. For appointment booking — where the customer messages you first — you'll never pay a cent. I've had clients use it for a year without spending anything on messaging fees. The only cost is your time.

**Q: Won't this annoy my customers? I don't want to be spammy.**

It will annoy them if you do it wrong. Don't send broadcast blasts. Don't message people who never opted in. Do use it for appointment confirmations, reminders, follow-ups, and answering questions. The data doesn't lie: WhatsApp has a 98% open rate within 3 minutes. Email? About 20% within 24 hours. If you're using it to serve your existing customers better, they'll appreciate it. If you're using it to interrupt strangers, they'll block you.

**Q: What if I get busy and can't respond quickly? Will I lose customers?**

Set expectations. Use the "away message" feature to say "We'll respond within 2 hours during business hours." Then actually respond within 2 hours. If you can't commit to that, hire a VA for $3/hour on Upwork to respond with basic info. Or use ManyChat to auto-reply with your catalog and booking link. Most questions are "How much?" and "Do you have availability?" — both can be automated. A client in Atlanta who owns a dog grooming business automated 80% of her WhatsApp interactions and only personally handles custom requests. Her customers don't know the difference.

**Q: Can I use WhatsApp Business without a separate phone?**

You can, but I wouldn't recommend it. The app requires its own phone number. You can use a VoIP number (Google Voice, Skype) — it's free and works fine. But if you use your personal number, you'll mix personal and business messages, and you'll never turn it off. A prepaid phone is $30. A Google Voice number is free. Pick one.

**Q: How do I get my existing customers to start using WhatsApp instead of phone calls?**

Don't force it. Add a "Book via WhatsApp" link to your email signature, your website contact page, and your Google Business profile. When someone calls and you miss it, send a text: "I missed your call. Can you message me on WhatsApp? Link here." People naturally migrate to the easier option. I've seen businesses shift 40% of their phone inquiries to WhatsApp within 60 days just by making the link visible and responding faster on chat than on phone.

**Q: What about privacy? I'm worried about data security.**

WhatsApp uses end-to-end encryption. Meta can't read your messages. That said, Meta owns WhatsApp, so if you don't trust Meta, don't use it. But for a coffee shop taking appointment bookings, the risk is minimal. Don't send credit card numbers, Social Security numbers, or medical info over WhatsApp. Use Square or Stripe for payments. Use your booking system for scheduling. WhatsApp is just the conversation layer — treat it like SMS with better features.

**Q: Can I use WhatsApp for marketing to people who don't know me?**

Officially, no. WhatsApp's terms say you need opt-in consent before messaging someone. Practically, Google and Yelp ads that go to WhatsApp are fine because the customer initiates contact. But importing a list of phone numbers you bought from a data broker? That's a fast way to get your account banned. Don't do it. I've seen accounts permanently disabled for this.
