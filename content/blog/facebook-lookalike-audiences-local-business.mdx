## What Facebook Lookalike Audiences Actually Do

Facebook’s algorithm compares your best customers (like email subscribers or in-store buyers) to its own user data. It finds people who share 150+ traits with them—location, browsing habits, even the type of pet they own. It’s not magic—it’s math that scales your best relationships.

For a yoga studio in Austin, this meant targeting people who:
- Followed 3+ local wellness pages
- Searched for “yoga for beginners” in the last 30 days
- Had a check-in history at parks within 5 miles

---

## How to Create a Lookalike Audience (Step-by-Step)

1. **Pick a source audience** (your best customers). Use:
   - Email list (ideal for salons or gyms)
   - Website visitors who filled out a form
   - People who bought your $35 dog grooming package

2. **Set a similarity percentage**:
   - 1–5% = Find people *very* similar (smaller audience, higher intent)
   - 20% = Broader reach (good for new service launches)

3. **Choose the right location**:
   - For a barbershop in Toronto, set “within 15 miles” with a 5% similarity
   - For a national brand, expand to 50+ miles or other cities with existing fans

<Callout type="tip">Start with your email list as the source. It’s clean data and usually has your most loyal customers.</Callout>

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using too small a source audience**  
   If your source has under 50 people, Facebook can’t find enough matches. Wait until your email list hits 100+ engaged contacts.

2. **Ignoring seasonality**  
   A dog walker in Phoenix using “last summer’s customers” as a source might miss the new pet owners who moved in during the winter.

3. **Not testing variations**  
   Try 1% vs. 10% similarity audiences. For a $20 coffee shop latte, the 1% audience might convert at 6.2% vs. 3.8% for 10%.

<Callout type="example">A nail salon in Sydney boosted their lookalike audience results by combining it with a 10% discount code in their ad. Conversion rate jumped from 3% to 7%.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much do Facebook lookalike audiences cost for local businesses?**  
$4–$6 per click is typical for salons and fitness studios. Coffee shops often see $2–$3 per click in major cities.

**How long does it take to see results?**  
Give it 2 weeks for Facebook to optimize. Some businesses see first conversions in 72 hours, others take up to 3 weeks.

**Can I combine lookalike audiences with other targeting?**  
Yes—add 2–3 interests (like "pet owners" or "yoga enthusiasts") but don’t overdo it. 5+ interests makes your audience too narrow.

**What if I don’t have 100 email subscribers yet?**  
Use website visitors who spent 2+ minutes on your "Services" page. Or start a loyalty program to build your source audience.

**Are lookalike audiences legal under data privacy laws?**  
Yes, as long as you’re not sharing personal data. Facebook handles the matching—your customer emails never leave your account.
