Now, let's dive into the simple fixes you can implement to improve your website's accessibility.

**1. Use Clear and Consistent Navigation**

Your website should be easy to navigate, even for users with disabilities. Use clear and consistent navigation menus, and make sure your website is mobile-friendly. This will improve user experience and help you rank higher in search engines.

*Tip:* Use a website builder like Wix or Squarespace that offers built-in accessibility features and easy-to-use navigation menus.

**2. Ensure Color Contrast**

Ensure that your website's color scheme provides sufficient contrast between text and background colors. This will help users with visual impairments read your content easily.

**3. Use Headings and Subheadings**

Use headings and subheadings to structure your content and make it easier for users to scan. This will also help search engines understand your content better.

*Warning:* Avoid using too many headings and subheadings, as this can make your content look cluttered.

**4. Add Alternative Text to Images**

Add alternative text to images to help users with visual impairments understand what the image is about. This is also important for search engine optimization (SEO).

*Example:* If you have an image of your coffee shop's menu, add alternative text like "Menu board with coffee drinks and prices."

**5. Use Closed Captions for Videos**

Use closed captions for videos to make them accessible to users with hearing impairments. This is also important for SEO.
