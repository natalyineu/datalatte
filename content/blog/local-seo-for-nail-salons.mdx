## Optimize Your Google Business Profile First

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is your #1 free tool to get local clients. Start with these steps:

1. **Claim your profile** immediately—even if your salon isn’t open yet.
2. Add **high-quality photos** of your workstations, staff, and client transformations.
3. List **specific services** (e.g., "gel manicures from $25") instead of vague categories.
4. Use **Google Posts** to promote deals like "$10 off pedicures this month."

## Build Local Keywords That Convert

Generic keywords like "nail salon" cost $8.50 per click on Google Ads. Instead, target **hyperlocal phrases** people actually search for:

- "acrylic nails near me"
- "nail salons in [Your City] under $30"
- "gel manicures near [Your Neighborhood]"

## Get Reviews Like a Pro

Positive reviews are your social proof. Negative ones? They’re still valuable if handled right.

**Review strategy checklist:**
- Ask clients to leave a **5-star review** after their appointment via text or email.
- Respond to all negative reviews instantly. Example: "We’re sorry about your last experience—we’ve trained our staff to do [X] and would love to give you a free manicure."
- Create **review incentives** without violating Google’s rules (e.g., "Leave a review and get 10% off your next gel manicure").

<Callout type="example">A Brooklyn nail salon doubled their 5-star reviews in 3 months by sending a personalized thank-you text with a review link immediately after each service.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long until local SEO works for my nail salon?**  
You’ll see small changes in 6–8 weeks. Major ranking shifts take 3–6 months. Be patient but adjust tactics if no progress after 2 months.

**Should I pay for Google Ads if I’m doing local SEO?**  
Yes—but only $200/month max to test. Use [Google Ads management](/services/google-ads) to target hyperlocal phrases like "nail salon near me" and track if they convert.

**What if big salons near me already dominate search?**  
Focus on **niche services** (e.g., "non-toxic gel manicures") and **client experience**. A 4.8-star rating can beat a 4.5-star one in rankings.

**How do I handle fake negative reviews?**  
Flag them on Google. Reply professionally and offer to resolve the issue in person. Never argue publicly.

**Can I do local SEO myself or should I hire help?**  
You can DIY with tools like [local SEO services](/services/local-seo). But if you’re spending more than 5 hours/week on it with no results, hire a pro. Time is money.

**Why aren’t my GBP updates showing up?**  
Google takes 1–7 days to process changes. Post **original content** (not screenshots of ads) and use the correct categories (e.g., "Nail Salon > Manicure and Pedicure").

**Is social media important for local SEO?**  
Indirectly. It builds awareness but doesn’t directly impact rankings. Use [social media management](/services/social-media) to drive traffic to your GBP and website.
