### 1. Conversion Rate by Traffic Source  
Your conversion rate tells you how well your website turns visitors into customers. For local businesses, this rate varies wildly by traffic source. A coffee shop in Austin, Texas, saw their Google Ads conversion rate drop from 8% to 3% in 2 weeks—until they fixed their landing page.  

- Coffee shops: Aim for 4–7%  
- Salons: 5–8% is typical  
- Pet groomers: 3–6%  
- Fitness studios: 2–5%  

<Callout type="tip">Track this in Google Analytics under “Acquisition > All Traffic.” If one source is underperforming, pause or rewrite the ad.</Callout>  

### 2. Cost Per Click (CPC) vs. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)  
You’re paying for every click, but does that lead to profit? A dog walker in Sydney, Australia, paid $2.50/click on Facebook Ads—until she realized her LTV was only $30. She cut ads and doubled down on referral discounts instead.

### 3. Local Search Ranking for “Near Me” Keywords  
61% of local searches happen on mobile, and 76% of them convert. A yoga studio in Toronto climbed from #5 to #1 for “yoga near me” in 4 weeks by claiming their Google Business Profile and adding 3 fresh reviews/week.  

- Audit your top 10 “near me” keywords monthly.  
- Fix NAP (Name, Address, Phone) errors across directories.  
- Add photos weekly to boost visibility.  

<Callout type="coffee">I always recommend starting with 3 high-intent keywords: “book now,” “near me,” and your city + service. Example: “dog grooming in Seattle.”</Callout>  

### 4. Email Open Rates and Automation Performance  
Your email list is your cheapest marketing channel—if you use it right. A barbershop in Chicago grew their email list from 200 to 1,200 subscribers in 6 months by adding a “free styling guide” pop-up. Their automated “birthday discount” sequence drove 35% of monthly revenue.  

- Open rates: 18–25% is good  
- Click-through rates: 2–5% is standard  
- Abandoned cart recovery: 5–8% works for local services  

### 5. Social Media Engagement by Post Type  
Not all content is equal. A coffee shop in Melbourne found that “behind-the-scenes” reels got 4x more engagement than generic “buy coffee” posts. They now post 3x a week: 1 story, 1 reel, and 1 review highlight.  

<Callout type="example">Post 1: “Our barista’s secret to latte art” (900 likes). Post 2: “Buy 5 coffees, get 1 free” (200 likes). Result? Story content drove 60% more app downloads.</Callout>  

### Frequently Asked Questions  
**How do I track local business marketing metrics for free?**  
Use Google Analytics, Google Business Profile insights, and free tools like Canva for social analytics.  

**What if my CPC is too high?**  
Pause underperforming ads, test tighter keywords (e.g., “organic coffee near me” instead of “coffee”), and add location extensions.  

**Should I track metrics daily or weekly?**  
Weekly is enough. Daily tracking wastes time. Set calendar reminders to review stats every Friday afternoon.  

**How do I calculate LTV for my local business?**  
Average monthly revenue per customer × 12 months × average customer lifespan. Example: $150/month × 12 × 2 years = $3,600 LTV.  

**Can I improve rankings without paid ads?**  
Yes. Fix your Google Business Profile, get 50+ reviews, and post 3x/week to Google Posts. A 1-star review drop can cost a salon $2,000/month in lost revenue.  

**Why is my traffic up but sales down?**  
Check your conversion rate. Maybe your website isn’t mobile-friendly, or your CTAs are unclear. A/B test your booking button colors.  

**How do I get more reviews?**  
Ask customers directly. A fitness studio in Vancouver added “Can we ask for a 5-star review?” at the end of every class. Their reviews doubled in 1 month.
