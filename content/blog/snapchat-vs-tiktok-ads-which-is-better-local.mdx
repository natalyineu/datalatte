### Choosing the Right Platform for Your Local Business

Before deciding between Snapchat and TikTok, consider your business's unique needs and goals. Ask yourself:

#### Do you have a younger audience?

If your target audience is under 30, TikTok might be the better choice. 70% of Gen Z users are on TikTok, compared to 40% on Snapchat.

#### Are you looking for short-term conversions?

Snapchat is ideal for driving foot traffic and sales quickly. With a 20% conversion rate, Snapchat ads are more likely to result in immediate purchases.

### Which Platform is Best for Your Local Business?

Ultimately, the choice between Snapchat and TikTok comes down to your business's unique needs and goals. Consider the following:

#### Are you looking for a high-conversion rate?

Snapchat might be the better choice, with a 20% conversion rate.

### Callout: Be Cautious of Ad Burnout

Ad burnout can occur when users see the same ad repeatedly. To avoid this, rotate your ad creative regularly and use lookalike targeting to reach new users.

### Callout: Consider TikTok's Duet Feature

TikTok's Duet feature allows you to partner with popular creators to reach a wider audience. This can be an effective way to increase brand awareness and drive sales.

### Callout: Don't Forget About Snapchat's Geofilters

Snapchat's geofilters can help you target specific locations and increase brand visibility. Use them to promote special offers or events at your business.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I own a dental practice. Should I even bother with Snapchat or TikTok?**
Depends on your patient age. If you do cosmetic dentistry for women under 35, yes. If you do dentures and root canals for retirees, no. I’ve seen a cosmetic dentist in Scottsdale, AZ generate $18,000 in new patient revenue from Snapchat ads over three months. But that’s because she targeted “brides in Phoenix” with Invisalign offers. If your average patient is 60+, put your money into Google Ads and local radio.

**Q: How do I track if someone walks into my store after seeing an ad?**
You don’t, perfectly. But you can get close. Use unique promo codes or dedicated phone numbers. Also, Snapchat has foot traffic attribution (Visits) that uses location data. It’s not 100% accurate, but it’s good enough for most small businesses. I’ve tested it against actual POS data and found it overcounts by about 30% — so cut reported numbers by a third for a realistic estimate.

**Q: Is $500/month even worth it?**
For most businesses, yes — if you test first. I’ve seen a food truck in Portland generate $4,000 in monthly revenue from $500 in Snapchat ads. But I’ve also seen a boutique in Nashville spend $500 and get $180 back. The difference was testing. If you can’t afford to lose $500, start with $200 and see what happens. The platforms won’t penalize you for a small budget.

**Q: Which platform works better for restaurants?**
Snapchat, by a wide margin. The Geofilter targeting lets you hit people within walking distance during lunch hours. I had a ramen shop in NYC’s East Village run Snapchat ads only between 11 AM and 2 PM, targeting a 1-mile radius. Cost per visit: $1.20. Average ticket: $22. That’s a 18x return on ad spend for their lunch rush. TikTok doesn’t have the same urgency mechanic.

**Q: Do I need to make professional-looking videos?**
No. In fact, overly polished content performs worse on both platforms. I’ve tested: an iPhone video shot in your store, natural lighting, no script, no editing — it outperformed a $2,000 produced spot by 3x in conversion rate. People want to see your actual space and your actual staff. They don’t want a commercial.

**Q: How do I know if my audience is on one platform vs. the other?**
Look at your existing customer data. If you export your email list from Mailchimp and most people are 18–34, both platforms work. If most are 35–54, Snapchat still has decent reach. TikTok skews younger — heavily under 30. If your customers are 50+, skip both and use Facebook and Google.
