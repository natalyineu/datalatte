## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Won't AI make my content sound like everyone else's?**
Yes, if you let it. The default output from any AI tool is the average of everything on the internet — which means it sounds like a generic business from a generic city. The fix is not to use the AI's first draft. Give it specific, weird, local details. Tell it you're a coffee shop in Portland where regulars know each other's dog names. Tell it you're a hair salon in Nashville that specializes in curly cuts for humid weather. The more specific you are, the less generic the output. If it still sounds generic, you aren't giving it enough context.

**Q: How much time will these tools actually save me?**
Depends on how you use them. If you're generating 30 posts and publishing them unedited: 2 hours saved per week, but you'll lose customers. If you're using AI for drafts, then editing for 10 minutes per post: 3-4 hours saved per week, and you'll keep your voice. The small businesses I've worked with who save the most time use AI for the parts they hate doing — scheduling, drafting variations, writing FAQs — and keep the parts they're good at (knowing their customers, having real opinions, telling actual stories). Realistic time savings: 3-6 hours per week. That's enough time to take one afternoon back, not enough to completely check out.

**Q: Can AI tools replace a social media manager?**
For a local business spending under $1,000/month on marketing? Partially. For a business that needs strategy, community management, crisis handling, and real customer interaction? No. AI can write a post. It can't decide what post matters. It can't tell when a customer's complaint contains genuine distress versus just a bad day. It can't know that the local high school just won state and your coffee shop should immediately post congratulations. I've seen businesses try to fully replace a human with AI — three out of four came back within six months asking for a human again. The fourth had such low engagement they didn't notice the difference.

**Q: Which AI tool should I start with if I'm on a $0 budget?**
ChatGPT free tier. Not because it's the best — because it's free and you'll learn what questions to ask. Most small business owners don't know what they need from an AI tool until they've used one badly for a month. Start with ChatGPT, generate 20 posts for your business, edit all of them, and see which parts of the process feel useful and which feel like extra work. After a month, you'll know whether you need a tool with image generation, scheduling integration, or multi-platform publishing. If you start with a paid tool, you'll pay for features you don't use. That's how $50/month tools become $600/year of nothing.

**Q: Can I use AI content for Google My Business posts?**
Yes, but Google's algorithm is sensitive to low-effort content. AI-generated posts that are generic, keyword-stuffed, or don't include location-specific information tend to get lower visibility. I've tested this: a bakery in Austin posted AI-generated GMB updates for two weeks — zero impressions on one post, 12 on another. They switched to human-written posts about actual daily specials (e.g., "We burned the first batch of croissants today. Second batch is perfect. Come get them while they're warm."). Impressions jumped to 340 per post. Google rewards specificity and timeliness, not volume.

**Q: What's the one tool you'd recommend to a business owner who has zero time?**
Not a tool. A process. Pay a virtual assistant $15/hour for 5 hours per week. Give them a template for collecting real stories from your business (what broke today, what a customer said, what's about to change). Have them feed that into ChatGPT to draft posts. You spend 10 minutes per day approving or tweaking. I've seen this work for a plumbing company in Chicago, a coffee shop in Austin, and a hair salon in Portland. It costs $300/month and gives you back 15 hours of your week. No tool does that alone.
