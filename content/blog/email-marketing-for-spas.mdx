### Build a Seasonal Campaign Calendar That Works

Every spa has peak seasons: summer for outdoor treatments, winter for holiday gift cards, and spring for post-holiday resets. Start by mapping your year into 3-month blocks. For example:

- **Summer (May–Aug):** Focus on outdoor packages, UV nail services, and cooling facials.
- **Holiday (Nov–Jan):** Push gift cards, family spa packages, and last-minute December bookings.
- **Spring (Feb–Apr):** Highlight detox treatments, discounts for early bookings, and Valentine’s Day specials.

Add 2–3 email triggers per season. A Seattle facial spa boosted bookings by 32% using a 3-email chain:  
1. "Last Chance for Summer Glow" (3 weeks before peak)  
2. "Weekend Escape: 20% Off Hot Stone Massage" (1 week before)  
3. "Final Hours: 15% Off with Code SPRING25" (24 hours before)

<Callout type="tip">Use city-specific examples. An Austin spa saw a 27% increase by tying emails to local events like South by Southwest (SXSW) wellness packages.</Callout>

### Create Urgency Without Burning Subscribers

Spa clients hate pushy emails—but they love *strategic* urgency. Test these formats:

- **Gift Card Pushes:** "Only 10 gift cards left at 2025 prices" (works best 2–4 weeks before Christmas)
- **Package Bundles:** "Book 3 massages, get 1 free this month" (ideal for summer slow periods)
- **Time-Limited Add-ons:** "Add a foot scrub to your pedicure for $10 (extra charge ends 8/15)"

Don’t overdo it. Send 1–2 urgent emails per month max. A Denver nail salon lost 18% of subscribers after sending daily "sale ending" alerts in November.

### Automate Without Losing Your Personal Touch

Automation works best when it *feels* personal. Set up 3 key workflows:

1. **Birthday Surprises** – Send a "Your Massage Awaits" email 10 days before their birthday with a $10 credit
2. **Post-Visit Follow-ups** – "We missed you! Your 15% off is waiting" 7 days after their last appointment
3. **Seasonal Reminders** – "It’s getting cold out – try our warming reflexology" in October

Use [AI agents & automation](/services/ai-agents) to personalize subject lines. A Phoenix day spa increased open rates by 41% using "Sarah, your summer glow is fading" instead of generic headers.

<Callout type="warning">Don’t automate every email. Keep 30–40% of your sends as one-off creative campaigns to maintain freshness.</Callout>

### Design Emails That Convert (Without Being Fancy)

Spa clients click on emails that *solve their problem*. Use this template:

- **Subject Line:** [First Name], Your 15% Off Expires Tomorrow  
- **Body:**  
  "Hi [First Name],  
  We noticed you haven’t booked since [Month]. Let’s fix that! Use code [CODE] for 15% off any treatment this week.  
  Your favorite therapist, [Name] is available on [dates].  
  P.S. Our new avocado body scrub is perfect for spring!"  

Use [email & SMS marketing](/services/email-sms) tools to track which elements work. A Portland spa found that adding a single sentence about their therapist’s name increased bookings by 19%.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the peak seasons for spas that I should be targeting with email marketing?

The peak seasons for spas vary depending on location, but generally include summer for outdoor treatments, winter for holiday gift cards, and spring for post-holiday resets. According to a Spa Industry Report 2025, 68% of spas report that summer is their busiest season, followed by winter and spring. By targeting these seasons, you can increase your chances of driving bookings and revenue.

### How do I create a seasonal campaign calendar for my spa?

To create a seasonal campaign calendar, start by mapping out your year into peak and off-peak seasons. Identify specific holidays and events that align with your spa's offerings, such as Mother's Day or Valentine's Day. Use a tool like a calendar or planner to visualize your campaigns and ensure you're sending emails at the right time.

### What is the average return on investment (ROI) from seasonal email campaigns for spas?

According to DataLatte analysis, spas that use seasonal email campaigns can expect an average ROI of 22% per month. This is significantly higher than the 12% ROI reported by spas that don't use email marketing. By sending targeted emails during peak seasons, you can increase your revenue and drive bookings.

### Can I automate my email marketing campaigns for seasonal promotions?

Yes, you can automate your email marketing campaigns for seasonal promotions using email marketing software. Automation can help you save time and ensure that your emails are sent consistently during peak seasons. For example, you can set up an automated email campaign to send a series of emails promoting your summer packages in June and July.

### How often should I send email marketing campaigns to my spa subscribers?

The frequency of email marketing campaigns depends on your subscribers' preferences and your spa's offerings. A good rule of thumb is to send emails 1-2 times per week during peak seasons and 1 time per month during off-peak seasons. This will help you stay top of mind with your subscribers and keep them engaged with your spa's promotions and services.
