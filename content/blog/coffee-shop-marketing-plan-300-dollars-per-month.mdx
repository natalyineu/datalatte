## Allocate Your $300 Budget Like a Pro

Your $300 isn’t for flash—it’s for smart, targeted moves. Split it like this:

- **$120 (40%)** to Google Ads (targeting lunchtime searchers)
- **$90 (30%)** to local SEO (Google My Business + directory listings)
- **$60 (20%)** to email marketing (loyalty rewards)
- **$30 (10%)** to free social posts (Instagram Stories, Facebook events)

## Win Local SEO for Free (Then Spend $90 to Polish)

92% of coffee shop Google searches happen on mobile—**and 78% end in a visit within 24 hours**. Your first step: claim your Google Business Profile. Add:

- 5 high-res photos (show your espresso machine in action)
- 10+ posts (daily specials, "Monday Motivation" lattes)
- 100% accurate address/hours (even if you’re "closed on Mondays")

Then spend $90/month on directory listings. Use tools like **Yext** or **BrightLocal** to sync your info across 100+ sites like Yelp, TripAdvisor, and Foursquare. One Melbourne café boosted reviews by 40% using this tactic.

<Callout type="example">Case study: "The Daily Grind" in Austin spent $90/month on Yelp + Google My Business. Result: 22% more walk-ins from 18-35-year-olds in 6 weeks.</Callout>

## Google Ads That Don’t Burn Your Budget

You don’t need a $1,000/month ad budget to win. Focus on **geo-targeted search ads** with time-based triggers:

- **Lunchtime targeting**: Bid more for "coffee near me" between 11am-2pm
- **Weather targeting**: Run ads for hot drinks when temps drop below 60°F
- **Device targeting**: Prioritize mobile ads (83% of coffee shop clicks happen on phones)

Set daily budget to $10–$15. A 30-piece ad group with 3 variations per keyword works best. Test phrases like:

- "Coffee near me now"
- "Best espresso in [City]"
- "Open 24 hours coffee shop"

<Callout type="warning">Avoid broad match keywords. "Coffee shop" will cost $5+/click in NYC. Use phrase match for better control.</Callout>

## Email Marketing That Keeps People Coming Back

Your email list is your most profitable asset. 68% of coffee shop customers return if they get a **personalized loyalty email**. Use $60/month to:

- Run a "Buy 9, Get 1 Free" program (use **Recharge** or **Klaviyo**)
- Send a "Happy Birthday" discount (35% open rate)
- Promote "After-work specials" at 3pm

Pro tip: Segment your list by visit frequency. Offer **double points** to customers who haven’t come in 14+ days. A Seattle café increased retention by 28% using this tactic.

<Callout type="coffee">DataLatte’s favorite tool? Mailchimp + SMS. Free tier handles 500 subs, which is perfect for small shops.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I track if this works?**
Use free Google Analytics (track website visits) and your CRM (track email open rates). Track "store visits" via Google Ads’ built-in metric.

**Can I skip the Google Ads?**
Not if you're in a busy area. Even $50/month ads outperform $300 in "mystery" social media posts.

**What if my budget is less than $300?**
Cut social media first. Use free Canva templates for Instagram. Keep the core: $100 Google Ads + $100 Google My Business.

**How long before I see results?**
Local SEO takes 2–3 months. Google Ads can work in 7 days. Email marketing shows results within 2 weeks.

**Should I hire a marketing agency?**
Only if you can’t spend 2 hours/week on this. Most agencies charge $500+/month for what you can do yourself.

**Do I need a website for this?**
Yes. Even a free Wix or WordPress site with 3 pages (Home, Menu, Contact) helps. Link it to all ads and Google Business.

**Can this work for 24-hour coffee shops?**
Absolutely. Add "24 hour coffee near me" to your keywords. Run Google Ads between 10pm-5am for night owls.
