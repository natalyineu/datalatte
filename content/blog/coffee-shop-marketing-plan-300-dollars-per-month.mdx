Your money needs to work as hard as you do. Here’s the exact allocation I use with my coffee shop clients:

**Google Ads ($105/month):** Start with 3-5 keyword-focused ads in your city. Example: “best coffee in Austin TX” or “organic lattes near me.” Target a $1.75 max CPC (cost-per-click) and pause underperforming keywords after 2 weeks.

<Callout type="tip">Use Google’s “Search Favourites” to identify high-traffic, low-competition keywords specific to your city.</Callout>

**Local SEO Tools ($75/month):** Claim and optimize your Google Business Profile, then use tools like BrightLocal or Yext to manage citations and track reviews. 72% of customers won’t visit a business with fewer than 3 reviews.

<Callout type="warning">Don’t waste money on generic SEO services. Focus on your city + niche keywords like “Austin coffee shops with dog-friendly patio.”</Callout>

**Social Media Ads ($75/month):** Run targeted Facebook/Instagram ads for your 3-mile radius. Example: A Portland coffee shop spent $75/month on “25% off cold brews” ads to 25–45-year-olds and gained 15 new daily customers.

**Email Marketing ($45/month):** Use Mailchimp or Canva to create a 3-message drip campaign. Offer a free pastry with your first email (25% open rate) and follow up with loyalty rewards.

## The 3 Local SEO Must-Dos (Zero Extra Cost)

Your $300 budget is great, but you’re missing 40% of potential customers if you ignore these free tactics:

1. **Claim Your Google Business Profile**  
   Add high-quality photos (show your baristas, not just your logo), list your hours clearly, and respond to every review. 61% of users trust reviews as much as personal recommendations.

2. **Fix Your NAP Consistency**  
   “Name, Address, Phone” must match exactly across Yelp, Google, and Bing. Inconsistent listings drop your local ranking by 50%.

3. **Build Local Backlinks**  
   Partner with nearby bookstores or yoga studios for cross-promotions. Ask them to link to your “coffee and book club night” event page on their site.

## Paid Ads That Don’t Drain Your Wallet

You don’t need a $1,000/month budget to outsmart chains. Try these:

- **Google Search Ads**  
  Target 3-5 ultra-local keywords like “coffee shops near me open on Sundays.” Set a $105/month cap and monitor clicks daily.

- **Facebook Retargeting**  
  Spend $30/week to re-engage website visitors with a “Buy 5 coffees, get one free” offer. Works best when paired with your email list.

- **Google Showcase Ads**  
  Use the remaining $20 to promote your morning rush hours with discounted breakfast combos. These perform best between 7–9 AM.

<Callout type="example">A Phoenix café boosted lunchtime sales by 22% with a $25 Google Showcase ad for “$5 avocado toast + coffee combo.”</Callout>

## Track These 3 Metrics (Ignore the Rest)

You paid $300 to grow, not to become a data analyst. Focus on:

1. **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**  
   Aim for $10–$15 per new customer. If it’s higher, pause the ad group.

2. **Website Conversion Rate**  
   Use Google Analytics to track if 10%+ of visitors become email subscribers.

3. **Review Growth**  
   Target 2-3 new 5-star reviews per week. Reply to every negative review within 2 hours.

<Callout type="warning">Don’t obsess over vanity metrics like “Impressions.” They don’t pay your rent.</Callout>
