## Audit What You’re Already Spending

Before adding new strategies, track every dollar you’re currently spending. Most coffee shops I work with discover they’re paying $150–$250/month for Instagram “influencers” who never drive repeat customers. Instead:

1. Cancel any contracts without clear attribution (e.g., “This influencer brought us 3 new daily regulars”)
2. Redirect funds to tools you can measure: Google Ads, local SEO, and automation
3. Set up Google Analytics 4 to track website traffic from each channel

**Example:** A Seattle café spent $200/month on TikTok ads but had no conversion tracking. After redirecting $150 to Google Ads with a 5% click-through rate, they doubled morning rush sales.

<Callout type="tip">Use free tools like Google Search Console to see which keywords already bring traffic to your site. Build on those.</Callout>

## Local SEO: The 0-Cost Traffic Generator

Coffee shops rank #1 in local search when they do these 3 things:

1. **Claim Google Business Profile** and complete all 12 sections (add photos, business hours, 5–7 categories)
2. **Get 10+ reviews/month**—not just stars, but “Great for morning lattes” or “Best croissants in [City]”
3. **List your shop in 5–10 local directories**: Yelp, Citysearch, and niche sites like [CafeReview](https://www.cafereview.com)

**Real-world example:** A Toronto café ranked #2 in Google Maps after adding “best cold brew near me” as a primary keyword. They now get 30% of their daily traffic from local searches.

## Google Ads: Spend $150 to Get $5 in Profit Per Click

For a $150/month budget, focus on **high-intent keywords** like:

- “best coffee near me [city]”
- “[City] coffee shop with free Wi-Fi”
- “open now coffee shop [city]”

**Step-by-step setup:**

1. Set daily budget to $5 (Google will distribute it smartly)
2. Bid $1–$2 per click on keywords with 10+ searches/day
3. Use location targeting and schedule ads for 7–9am/5–7pm

## Social Media: 1 Post Per Week That Sells Coffee

You don’t need a social media manager. One post per week with these elements works:

- **Visuals**: A 45° shot of your signature latte or pastry
- **Text**: “Monday special: $1 off oat milk lattes”
- **CTA**: “Tag us in your photos for 10% off your next order”

**Case study:** A Denver shop posted a “Barista of the Month” photo every Friday. Within 3 months, tagged user photos increased walk-ins by 18%.

<Callout type="example">Use Canva or Fotor to create professional-looking graphics in 10 minutes. No design skills needed.</Callout>

## Automation: Save 5 Hours/Week While Growing

Marketing automation isn’t just for big brands. For $100/month, use tools like Mailchimp or ConvertKit to:

- Send a “Welcome back!” email with 10% off to lapsed customers
- Create a loyalty program with points for every $1 spent
- Share user-generated content on your socials (with permission)

**Pro tip:** A Portland café automated a “Your birthday coffee is on us” SMS. It brought in $800 in revenue and 25 new reviews in one month.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know if my $300/month is working?**  
Track daily website visits (use Google Analytics), new Google reviews, and compare sales during marketing vs non-marketing weeks.

**Can I do this myself without hiring help?**  
Yes. Focus on 1–2 strategies at a time. Try local SEO for 3 months, then add Google Ads if you have 15+ reviews.

**What tools should I use for under $300/month?**  
Google Business Profile (free), Google Ads ($150), Mailchimp ($100), and Canva (free). Total: $250/month.

**How long until I see results?**  
Local SEO takes 3–6 months. Google Ads can drive traffic in days, but need 2+ weeks to optimize. Automation builds slowly but steadily.

**Is this worth the time for a small shop?**  
Yes. One Austin café increased morning sales by 40% in 3 months using this plan—without raising prices.
