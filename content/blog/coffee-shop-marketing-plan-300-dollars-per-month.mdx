## What to Spend Your $300 On (and What to Skip)

Start with Google Ads — but only if you know how to nail keyword targeting. Use the Coffee Shops category in Google Business Profile to claim your free listing first. This alone boosts visibility in local searches and maps results. Then, allocate **$150/month** to Google Ads with a tight focus on keywords like “{your city} coffee shop with Wi-Fi” or “best latte near me.” 

Don’t waste money on vague terms like “coffee” — they’re too broad and expensive. Instead, target hyper-local searches by including your neighborhood name and specific offerings. For example, “downtown Austin coffee shop with outdoor seating” is cheaper and converts better. 

<Callout type="tip">Use Google Keyword Planner to find keywords with low competition and high intent. Prioritize phrases with “near me” or city names.</Callout>

## Local SEO: The Free Traffic You’re Missing

You already have a website — now make it work. 60% of all Google searches are local, yet most coffee shops skip the basics. Start with:

- **Google Business Profile optimization**: Add high-quality photos of your baristas, pastries, and workspace. Write a 200-word “About” section that mentions your neighborhood and unique selling point (“Since 2010, we roast our beans in-house daily”).
- **Local citations**: List your coffee shop on Yelp, Citysearch, and Google Maps. Make sure your address, phone number, and hours match exactly across all platforms.
- **Review strategy**: Ask every 5th customer to leave a review. Respond to all 1-star reviews within 24 hours — even if the complaint is unfair.

## Social Media That Doesn’t Waste Time

Post 3x/week on Instagram and Facebook with these rules:
- **70% behind-the-scenes content**: “Meet our barista Maria” or “How we clean our espresso machines”
- **20% customer shots**: Repost photos of your customers with a thank-you tag
- **10% promotions**: “Buy 1 latte, get 1 free — but only if you tag us on social media”

Use a $20/month tool like Later or Buffer to schedule posts. Spend $30/month on Facebook Ads targeting a 25–45 age group within 5 miles of your shop. Create a carousel ad showing your seasonal drinks and a call-to-action: “Try this drink at our shop near {landmark}.” 

<Callout type="coffee">My client “Brew Haven” in Portland saw a 35% increase in weekend foot traffic after switching to this strategy.</Callout>

## The $150 Google Ads Plan

Split your $150/month budget like this:
- **$90 for search ads**: Target location-specific terms with 10–15 search keywords
- **$60 for shopping ads**: If you sell branded mugs, this helps (skip if not)

Set your bids to $1.20–$1.50 per click, and run ads for 3 weeks. Monitor which keywords get phone calls and conversions (use Google Call Tracking). Pause underperformers and reinvest in winners. 

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Callout type="example">Q: How do I track if this works?  
A: Use Google Analytics to track “new users” and “session duration.” If 40% of your traffic is new, you’re doing it right.</Callout>

<Callout type="example">Q: Is $300/month enough for a chain of 3 coffee shops?  
A: No. This plan is for solo shops. Chains need separate local SEO strategies for each location.</Callout>

<Callout type="example">Q: What about email marketing?
