## Step 1: Fix Your Google Business Profile—It’s Free

Your Google Business Profile is the first thing locals see when they search “coffee near me.” 89% of customers trust local reviews as much as personal recommendations. Yet 72% of coffee shops have incomplete profiles. Start by:

- Adding 10+ high-res photos (show your latte art, interior, and morning crowd)
- Claiming all 3 Google Business categories (e.g., “Coffee Shop,” “Café,” “Bakery”)
- Posting 3 weekly updates (happy hour, new menu items, community events)

Example: A Seattle café increased store visits by 34% after adding a “Weekend Brunch Special” weekly post. No ads—just better profile optimization.

<Callout type="tip">Claim your profile for free at business.google.com. It takes 15 minutes but wins you 30%+ more local traffic.</Callout>

## Step 2: Spend $200 on Google Ads That Convert

You don’t need a $1,000/month ad budget. With $200/month, you can target hyper-local keywords like “Austin coffee shop with Wi-Fi” or “downtown Seattle espresso.” Here’s how:

1. **Search Ads**: Bid $2–3 per click for keywords like “coffee near me.” Avg. conversion rate: 4.5%.
2. **Maps Ads**: Get a 2nd spot in Google Maps. Bid $5–7/day for top positioning.
3. **Local Audience Ads**: Retarget people who visited your shop or searched for competitors.

## Step 3: Automate Email Marketing for $50/month

You’ve got a list of 200 email subscribers but never use it. Email marketing costs $0.85 per acquisition—10x cheaper than ads. With $50/month, you can:

- Use Mailchimp or Subscribers.io to build 3 automated flows:
  - **Welcome series**: 3 emails over 7 days with a 10% discount
  - **Birthday rewards**: $5 off coffee for loyal customers
  - **Abandoned Cart**: If you sell merch online, send a reminder 24 hours later

Example: A Denver coffee shop boosted email revenue by 42% after adding a “Buy 5 Coffees, Get 1 Free” loyalty email. They used a $50/month template from Subscribers.io.

<Callout type="coffee">I recommend Subscribers.io over Mailchimp for coffee shops—no coding needed and built-in loyalty tools.</Callout>

## Step 4: Audit Your Marketing Every 2 Weeks

A $300/month budget needs constant tweaks. Set time aside every 14 days to:

- Check Google Ads: Which keywords bring the most walk-ins?
- Review Google Reviews: Respond to 1-star reviews in 2 hours.
- Analyze Email Metrics: If open rate <20%, test a new subject line.

Example: A Chicago café cut Google Ads costs by 30% after realizing “espresso near me” was outperforming “coffee shop downtown.”

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I spend money on Facebook Ads?**  
Only if you already have a strong Instagram presence. Facebook Ads for coffee shops have a 2.1% average conversion rate—half of Google Ads. Focus on Google first.

**What if I can’t afford $300/month?**  
Cut Google Ads to $100/month and invest $200 in local SEO: clean up your website, fix broken links, and get listed on Yelp, Foursquare, and Citysearch.

**How do I track results?**  
Use Google Analytics to track store visits from ads. For email, track redemption rates on discounts. A 5% redemption rate is excellent.

**Can I do this without a website?**  
No. 82% of customers visit a coffee shop’s website before visiting. Use Wix or Shopify to build a $50/month site with online ordering.

**How long before I see results?**  
Local SEO takes 3–6 months. Google Ads show results in 2 weeks. Email marketing lifts sales in 1 month.
