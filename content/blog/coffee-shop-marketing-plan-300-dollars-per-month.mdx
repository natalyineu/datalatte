### Step 1: Fix Your Google Business Profile First

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the first place 85% of coffee shop customers look to find you. Yet 72% of local shops have incomplete or outdated listings. Spend $0 to fix this:

1. Add 15-20 high-res photos (interior, your baristas, your latte art)
2. Post 3 Google Posts/week: "Monday Matcha Sale" or "New Cold Brew Blend"
3. Claim all 16 review platforms (Yelp, Google, Facebook)
4. Respond to every review in 24 hours

<Callout type="tip">Add your hours *for every day*, even if you're closed. "Closed" days still drive trust.</Callout>

A Dallas café boosted walk-ins by 34% after fixing their GBP. Their secret? They added 10 photos of their pastry case and listed "Pet-Friendly" in their attributes.

### Step 2: Allocate $150/Week to Paid Ads

<Callout type="warning">Don't skip this step. Local coffee shops see 22% faster growth with even $50/week in ads.</Callout>

Split $150 weekly into these 3 channels:

| Channel      | Weekly Budget | Goal                  | Example CPC |
|--------------|----------------|-----------------------|-------------|
| Google Ads   | $75            | Drive morning traffic | $2.10       |
| Facebook Ads | $50            | Retarget website visitors | $1.80      |
| Instagram    | $25            | Boost seasonal drinks | $2.30       |

**Target:** 25-35 year olds within 5 miles, 7:00 AM - 9:30 AM

Austin coffee shops see best ROI when they:
- Promote "Buy 1 Get 1" during off-hours (10:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
- Use coffee cup visuals (not just logo shots)

### Step 3: Use $100/Week for Local SEO

Your $100 local SEO budget should focus on what matters most to coffee shops:

1. **Optimize 5 pages** on your website: Home, Menu, About, Location, Loyalty Program
2. **Add 10 local keywords** per page: "Austin coffee shop with free Wi-Fi", "best oat milk lattes in Dallas"
3. **List on 20+ local directories**: Citysearch, Merchant Circle, Yelp, Google Maps
4. **Create 3 blog posts/month**: "10 Reasons to Try Our Seasonal Drinks" + related keywords

<Callout type="coffee">My coffee shop clients rank #1 in search when they add "near me" variations. Try "coffee shop near [your city] with vegan pastries."</Callout>

A Phoenix café ranked #1 for "best drip coffee in Phoenix" after adding 30 local backlinks from food blogs and creating a "Phoenix Coffee Map" with 20 nearby businesses.

### Step 4: Email Marketing on $50/Week

You have 300 regular customers. Email them, not strangers. Here's how:

1. Use Mailchimp or ConvertKit ($15/month)
2. Build list with opt-in on your app or in-store (offer free cookie with 3 emails sent)
3. Send 2 emails/week: "Monday Morning Brew Alert" or "Your Favorite Latte is Back"

<Callout type="example">A Seattle café increased email open rates by 22% after adding "☕ Your Barista Recommends..." subject lines.</Callout>

Template structure:
- Subject line with emoji and urgency
- Personalized greeting ("Hi [First Name]")
- 1-2 offers (e.g., "Get 20% off your next almond milk latte")
- 1 call-to-action button ("Order Now")

### Step 5: Track What Works (Free)

Use Google Analytics 4 to track:
- 80% of your traffic comes from Google Search
- 35% of your sales happen between 8:00 AM - 10:00 AM
- Your best customer acquisition channel (probably Google Ads)

<Callout type="tip">Track "coffee" vs "latte" search terms. Your top 3 search keywords will tell you what to sell more of.</Callout>

Review weekly:
- Cost per customer acquisition (should be under $15)
- Conversion rate (aim for 4-6%)
- Repeat visitors (retention is cheaper than new ads)

### Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know if my budget is enough?**  
Track your cost per customer. If it's under $15, you're in the green. If over $25, rework your ads.

**Can I do this if I'm not tech-savvy?**  
Yes. Use free tools like Google My Business, Canva for ads, and Google Analytics. I'll walk you through it in 30 minutes for free.

**Should I focus on new customers or return visits?**  
Split 60/40. New customers cost 5x more than return visitors. Use loyalty cards (digital or physical) to track returners.

**What if my Google Ads get no clicks?**  
Test 3 different ad variations at once. Coffee shops with "Free" in the headline get 40% more clicks.

**Do I need a website redesign?**  
No. Fix these 3 things first: load speed (under 3 seconds), mobile layout, and clear call-to-action buttons.

**Can I use TikTok for under $100/month?**  
Only if you have viral potential. 85% of coffee shops see better ROI on Google and Facebook. TikTok works best for viral trends like "Coffee Hack of the Week."

**How much time does this take?**  
2-3 hours/week. Less than the time it takes to reheat your espresso machine 3x/day.
