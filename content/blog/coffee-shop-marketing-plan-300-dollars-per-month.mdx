- Claiming and verifying your GBP

### 2. Allocate $150/Month to Google Ads  
You don’t need a $1,000 budget to compete. A well-managed **$150/month Google Ads** campaign can generate 30–50 new weekly customers. Focus on:  
- **Location-based keywords**: “coffee near me open now,” “best espresso in [City]”  
- **Time-based targeting**: Bump bids for 7–9 AM (breakfast) and 3–5 PM (afternoon coffee)  
- **Landing pages**: Use your homepage with a visible “Order Online” button (not a separate landing page)  

Average cost-per-click for coffee shops is **$1.20–$2.50**, with a 68% conversion rate for local searches.  

<Callout type="warning">Don’t waste money on “brand awareness” campaigns. Local coffee shops need targeted clicks, not impressions.</Callout>  

### 3. Build a Local SEO Foundation  
For $50/month (tools like Ahrefs or SEMrush), you’ll gain the power to:  
1. Audit your website for missing local keywords  
2. Build 3–5 high-quality backlinks per month (partnerships with nearby bookstores, co-working spaces)  
3. Add schema markup for “Coffee Shop” to help Google understand your business  

A local SEO audit revealed one Phoenix café increased organic traffic by **210%** after adding “near me” to 10 blog posts.  

<Callout type="coffee">I always recommend starting with your 10 closest competitors. If they’re not ranking for “latte near me,” you’ve found a gap!</Callout>  

### 4. Use Email Marketing to Retain Regulars  
Your regulars are worth 10x your one-time customers. For $100/month (Mailchimp or Klaviyo), send:  
- A “Welcome Back” series for new subscribers  
- Weekly deals (e.g., “Buy 5 coffees, get 1 free”)  
- Birthday discounts (20–30% off)  

**42% of coffee shop customers** say they’d switch brands for a better loyalty program. Track open rates (aim for 25%+) and A/B test subject lines like “Your Free Cappuccino Awaits ☕” vs. “This Week’s Specials.”  

### Frequently Asked Questions  

#### How do I track my coffee shop marketing ROI?  
Use Google Analytics to track website traffic and GBP insights for phone calls. For every $1 spent on Google Ads, aim for at least $5 in revenue.  

#### Is $150/month enough for Google Ads?  
Yes, if you focus on tight keyword targeting and location-based bids. I’ve helped 3 Austin roasters hit 5x ROI with $100–$200/month budgets.  

#### What’s the best time to post on social media?  
Post 3 times/week between 9–11 AM and 5–7 PM on weekdays. Use Instagram Stories to showcase your baristas and limited-time offers.  

#### Can I do this without a website developer?  
Absolutely. Use free tools like Google My Business, Canva for graphics, and free landing page builders like Carrd.  

#### How do I get reviews without annoying customers?  
Ask in person: “We’d love a 2-minute review on Google if you’re happy with your order.” Offer a small incentive like a free pastry for 5-star reviews (follow FTC guidelines).  

#### Should I run Facebook Ads or Google Ads?  
Google is better for coffee shops. 78% of coffee buyers search Google before buying, while only 22% use Facebook for local decisions.  

#### How long before I see results?  
Local SEO takes 3–6 months to show consistent gains. Paid ads can drive traffic in 1–2 weeks, but require weekly optimization.
