## What to do with a $300/month coffee shop marketing budget

### 1. Build a Google Business Profile like it’s your superpower
Google gives free tools to local businesses. Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the most important one. Here’s how to use it for $0:

- Claim your profile if you haven’t already
- Add 10+ high-res photos (focus on your baristas in action)
- Post 3x weekly Google Posts (e.g., “New seasonal drink alert! 20% off for first-time tasters”)
- Ask customers to leave 5-star reviews after purchases

Example: A Seattle coffee shop increased GBP visibility by 217% in 3 months by posting daily photos of their roastery process. No ads. No magic—just consistency.

<Callout type="tip">Use the “Google My Business Stories” feature to showcase your morning rush or latte art contests. Stories appear at the top of search results and cost zero.</Callout>

### 2. Allocate $150/month to Google Ads
Yes, even a small budget works. Focus on:

- **Search ads**: Target “coffee shops near me” and “best lattes in [city]”
- **Maps ads**: The top 3 spots get 70% of clicks
- **Schedule ads**: Run from 7–11 AM and 3–6 PM (peak coffee hours)

Example: A Dallas shop spent $150/month on search ads. After 3 months, they got 82 new customers who spent $12/visit. Cost per acquisition? $18.

### 3. Spend $50/month on local SEO
SEO is free, but it takes time. Here’s how to prioritize:

- Claim your GBP (already done?)
- Get listed in 3–5 local directories (Yelp, Citysearch, Google Maps)
- Create 1 blog post/month (e.g., “Why Portland Lovers Choose Our Cold Brew”)
- Add schema markup for reviews (advanced but worth it)

Example: A Toronto café climbed from page 3 to page 1 for “best coffee shops in Toronto” by optimizing their GBP and adding 10 local backlinks from food bloggers.

<Callout type="coffee">DataLatte tip: Use Ubersuggest for free keyword ideas. Search “coffee shops + [your city]” and grab the top 5 keywords for free local SEO.</Callout>

### 4. Keep $100/month for loyalty and email
Your regulars are your best customers. Use that $100 to:

- Launch a 5-star review request on receipt (e.g., “Rate us on Google for a free pastry”)
- Create a simple email list with 10% off for subscribers
- Run a “Buy 9 coffees, get 1 free” program (Track via a spreadsheet)

Example: A Chicago shop used a free 5-email sequence to re-engage inactive customers. Their open rate? 31%. Their repeat rate? Up 27% in 2 months.

<Callout type="example">Try this email subject line: “Your favorite barista is back in town ☕️ (Here’s 10% off your latte)”</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I track if this works?**  
Use Google Analytics for website traffic and your GBP insights dashboard. Look for “store visits” and “phone clicks” to measure GBP success.

**Can I do this without an agency?**  
Absolutely. Google Ads has a free “Smart Bidding” feature that works for small budgets. Just set your max CPC to $1–$2.

**What if my city is too competitive for Google Ads?**  
Focus on GBP optimization first. In hyper-competitive areas like NYC, GBP visibility beats paid ads for $300/month budgets.

**Should I add Instagram Stories for free?**  
Yes! Post your daily specials on Stories. Use a “Swipe Up” link to your GBP or website. Example: “Swipe to try our new caramel macchiato 🥛”

**How do I get reviews without annoying customers?**  
Ask politely. Use paper receipts with a QR code to your review page. Add a note: “Help us be the best coffee shop in [city]!”

**Is $300/month enough for a chain with 3 locations?**  
No. This plan works for solo shops. Chains need at least $750/month to target all locations effectively.

**Can I split the budget differently?**  
Sure. Try $200 on Ads, $80 on SEO, and $20 on loyalty. Test for 2 months and adjust based on results.

## Final thoughts

This plan works because it mixes free tools (GBP, SEO) with tiny paid bets (Google Ads). Over 6 months, a $300/month budget can grow your customer base by 30–50% without fancy tech or agencies.
