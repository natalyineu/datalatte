## Allocate Your $300 Budget Like a Pro

Start by splitting your budget into four parts: **paid ads (40%)**, **local SEO (30%)**, **social media (20%)**, and **automation (10%)**. This mix works because local coffee shops see the most immediate results from paid ads, but long-term growth hinges on SEO and repeat customers. A $150/month ad budget splits evenly between Google and Facebook, while $30 buys a Mailchimp plan for email automation.

## Google Ads That Don’t Suck for Coffee Shops

Start with a **Google Business Profile** (it’s free). Then run Google Ads targeting keywords like “best coffee near me” or “latte [City Name]”. Set a $75/month budget with $0.60–$1.20 max CPC. Example: A Seattle café spent $90/month on Ads and saw a 22% increase in walk-ins after 3 months. Use location-based keywords like “Seattle coffee shop with Wi-Fi” to attract nearby customers.

<Callout type="tip">Use “Coffee Shop + [Your City]” as your main keyword. It’s cheap and converts better than “best coffee in [City].”</Callout>

## Local SEO: The Slow Burn That Pays Off

Claim your Google Business Profile and stuff it with **photos, hours, and 10+ local keywords** in your description. Add “coffee shop in [City]” to your website’s header. A Portland roastery boosted their Google visibility by 45% after adding 50+ local citations (Yelp, CitySearch, etc.). This takes 4–6 months to show results, so pair it with Ads for instant traffic.

<Callout type="warning">Don’t skip the 100+ reviews goal. Coffee shops with 4.5+ stars get **3x more foot traffic** than 4.0-star shops.</Callout>

## Social Media That Feels Human (Not Corporate)

Post 3x/week on Instagram and Facebook, focusing on **behind-the-scenes content**. Share a photo of your barista at 7 AM roasting beans, or your “Monday Mood” flat white. Allocate $60/month for a boosted post budget. A Denver café increased Instagram followers by 120% by collaborating with a local artist (she got 10% of sales from her featured latte art).

<Callout type="coffee">Post “ugly” coffee cup photos. People love seeing the mess behind the magic.</Callout>

## Marketing Automation for Busy Coffee Shop Owners

Use Mailchimp or Canva to create a **weekly email for your regulars**. Include a “Buy 5 coffees, get 1 free” loyalty card and birthday discount. Automate text reminders for your 8 AM loyal customers. A $30/month automation plan helped a San Diego café boost repeat visits by 28% in 2 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I track if this plan is working?**  
Use UTM links in your Google Ads and social posts. Track clicks, store visits, and new reviews weekly. Google Analytics will show which ads drive traffic to your website.

**Should I hire a marketing agency for this?**  
No. Agencies charge $2,000+/month for this work. At $300/month, you’ll learn the skills to handle it yourself. Hire a freelancer only for advanced tasks like video ads.

**What if my Google Ads suck at first?**  
Pause underperforming keywords after 2 weeks. Test 3–4 keyword variations. A Chicago café cut their CPC by 40% after testing “organic coffee [City]” vs “coffee near me.”

**Can I skip the automation part?**  
You’ll miss 35% of potential repeat sales. Even a $10 Canva template for loyalty cards is worth it.

**How long till I see results?**  
Ads = 2–4 weeks. SEO = 4–6 months. Automation = 1–2 months. Be patient with SEO but tweak your Ads weekly.

**Is $300 enough for a new coffee shop?**  
Yes, but focus 70% on Google Ads early. Use SEO and social media to build credibility while Ads drive instant sales.
