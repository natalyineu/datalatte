This plan is built for *your* reality: low cost, high impact, and 100% actionable steps. Let’s turn that $300 into loyal customers and longer lines at your counter.

## 1. Allocate Your Budget Like a Pro (No Guesswork)

Start by splitting your $300 into these 5 core areas:

- **Google Ads ($100/month):** Target local searches with geo-fenced keywords like "[your city] coffee shop" or "best espresso near me."
- **Social Media Ads ($50/month):** Run Facebook/Instagram ads focused on 1-2 key promotions (e.g., "Buy 1, Get 1 Free Mondays").
- **Local Directory Listings ($50/month):** Claim free listings on Yelp, Google Maps, and Foursquare to boost visibility.
- **Email Marketing ($50/month):** Use tools like Mailchimp to build a loyalty-driven email list.
- **Content Creation ($50/month):** Repurpose photos for social media and customer testimonials for your website.

**Pro tip:** Adjust allocations based on results. If Google Ads bring 3x more walk-ins than social ads, shift $20 from the latter to the former.

---

## 2. Optimize Google My Business to Be Found Instantly

Your GMB profile is your digital storefront for 32% of local searches. Here’s how to make it work:

1. **Claim your listing** and verify within a week to unlock features like posts and Q&A.
2. **Add 20–30 high-quality photos** weekly: lattes with foam art, your team, and cozy seating areas.
3. **Respond to all reviews** in 24 hours. For negative reviews, say: *“We’re so sorry about your experience—can we make it right?”*
4. **Use posts for urgency:** Share limited-time offers like “$1 off cold brews this Friday” or “First-time visitor? Scan the QR code for a free pastry.”

**Result:** 74% of customers trust reviews as much as personal recommendations. Don’t let competitors steal your share.

---

## 3. Run Social Media Ads That Convert (Not Just Impressions)

Social media ads work best when you focus on *micro-conversions* (e.g., clicks to your website), not vanity metrics. Here’s how to set up a $50/month campaign:

- **Audience:** Target users within a 5-mile radius who searched for “coffee shops” in the last 30 days.
- **Ad Copy:** “Your morning routine just got better. 50% off cold brews this week. [CTA: Get Directions]”
- **Format:** Use carousels to showcase your menu and a video of your baristas in action.
- **A/B Test:** Run 2 versions with different CTAs (“Book a Table” vs. “See Our Menu”) to see what drives traffic.

**Real-world example:** A client spent $50/month on Facebook ads and saw a 15% increase in weekend foot traffic in 3 months.

---

## 4. Master Local SEO for Free (Yes, Free)

You don’t need to pay for SEO tools—just consistency. Do these 3 things weekly:

1. **Update your Google Posts** with events (e.g., “Live Jazz Every Thursday 6 PM”).
2. **Build citations** by listing your shop on 5 local directories (do it manually; it’s free).
3. **Optimize your website** with keywords like “[Your City] coffee shop with free WiFi” (include this in headers and meta tags).

**Bonus:** Add a “Near Me” button to your website. People searching “coffee shops near me” will land directly on your page.

---

## 5. Turn Regulars Into Loyalists with Email Marketing

Email has a 44% higher ROI than other channels. Here’s how to build a list and keep them coming back:

- **Incentivize signups** with a “10% off your first coffee” coupon.
- **Send 2 emails/month** with:
  - A “Coffee of the Month” feature
  - A referral program (“Bring a friend, get $5 off”)
  - A birthday surprise (free pastry on their day)
- **Track open rates** and segment your list. If 30% open your “Weekend Brunch” emails, prioritize those.

**Case study:** A client with 300 email subscribers drove 12% of total monthly sales through targeted offers.

Measure these 3 metrics weekly to know if your plan is working:

|--------|--------|--------------|

## FAQ: The No-Bullsh*t Answers

**1. Is $100/month for Google Ads too much?**  
No. Coffee shops see a 4x return on Google Ads when targeting local searches. Test for 1 month, then adjust.

**2. How do I get more reviews?**  
Place a QR code at your register: “Scan to leave a review and get 10% off.” Most customers will comply.

**3. Can I skip social media ads?**  
If your budget is tight, focus on Google and email. But social ads are a fast way to test new promotions.

**4. How often should I post on social media?**  
3x/week minimum. Use a content calendar: post photos during peak hours (7–9 AM and 3–5 PM).

**5. What if my competition has a bigger budget?**  
They’re probably not optimized. Stick to your plan and focus on customer retention (it’s cheaper than acquisition).

**6. Do I need a marketing tool like Mailchimp?**  
Yes—if you want to automate emails. Mailchimp’s free tier supports up to 2,000 contacts.

**7. How long before I see results?**  
3–4 weeks for Google Ads, 6 weeks for email marketing. Consistency beats big budgets.
