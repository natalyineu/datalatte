### Why Your $300 Budget Should Focus on Local First  

Before diving into tactics, remember: **local customers are your bread and butter**. 72% of people who search for local businesses on Google visit a store within 5 miles. That means most of your potential customers are already nearby—they just need to find you.  

Allocate your budget to attract locals who’ll become regulars. Tools like Google My Business, Instagram, and simple email marketing are your best friends here. No need to chase broad audiences when your niche is right around the corner.  

---

### 1. Master Google My Business (Free, Zero Cost)  

Your Google My Business (GMB) listing is the digital equivalent of your storefront. If it’s messy or incomplete, you’re losing customers to competitors.  

**What to do:**  
- **Claim your listing** if you haven’t already.  
- Add **high-quality photos** (interior, menu, happy customers).  
- Post **1-2 weekly updates** (e.g., “Our new oat milk latte is here!” or “Monday is 20% off for teachers”).  
- **Encourage reviews** by asking customers to rate you in person or via text.  

**Pro Tip:** Use a tool like [ReviewTrackers](https://www.reviewtrackers.com/) for $15/month to automate review collection. Even with a $300 budget, this is a worthwhile investment.  

---

### 2. Run a $30/Week Instagram Boosted Post Campaign  

Instagram is a goldmine for coffee shops. Here’s how to maximize it:  

**Steps:**  
1. Post 3x/week with eye-catching content (latte art, behind-the-scenes shots, customer photos).  
2. Allocate **$30/month** to boost **one post per week** to your city’s 20-35 demographic.  
3. Track engagement and tweak your messaging.  

**Example:**  
A boosted post for “$3 off lattes today” with a 20% discount code can drive 50-70 new followers and 20+ walk-ins.  

**Budget Breakdown:**  
- $30/month total → $10 per boosted post (3x/month)  
- Use [Canva](https://www.canva.com/) for free designs (no need for paid tools).  

---

### 3. Build an Email List with Zero Upfront Cost  

Email marketing is a 44% ROI beast. For coffee shops, it’s perfect for loyalty programs and seasonal offers.  

**How to do it:**  
- Offer a **free pastry** with a $10 purchase to collect emails.  
- Use [Mailchimp’s free tier](https://mailchimp.com) to send weekly newsletters (e.g., “Loyalty points doubled this week!”).  
- Include a **referral program**: “Refer a friend, get $5 off.”  

**Numbers to hit:**  
- Aim for **50 new subscribers/month**.  
- Send 2-3 emails/month with a clear CTA (e.g., “Stop by before 10 AM for 10% off”).  

**Pro Tip:** Add a QR code on your cups for instant signups.  

---

### 4. Partner with Neighboring Businesses (0-$$ Cost)  

Local partnerships amplify your reach without ads.  

**Examples:**  
- Cross-promote with a bookstore next door: “Buy a coffee, get 10% off books.”  
- Sponsor a local art exhibit and get signage at the event.  
- Offer a discount to employees of a nearby office building.  

**Budget Hack:**  
Collaborate on a **shared Google Ads campaign** with 2-3 local businesses. Split the $50/month cost and target keywords like “best coffee near [neighborhood].”  

---

### 5. Optimize for Local SEO (Free)  

Ranking for “coffee shops near me” is free with these steps:  

**Action Items:**  
- Add **schema markup** for your address and hours. Use [Google’s Structured Data Markup Helper](https://www.google.com/webmasters/tools/rich-snippets).  
- Create a “Contact Us” page with your **physical address, phone number, and Google Maps embed**.  
- Get listed on **local directories** like Yelp, CitySearch, and Google Maps.  

**Bonus:** Add a **FAQ section** on your site for local queries (e.g., “Do you offer vegan pastries?”).  

---

### 6. Run a Loyalty Program with In-Store Swag  

People love being rewarded. For under $200/month, create a loyalty program that feels exclusive.  

**Idea:**  
- Spend $150 on branded merch (e.g., a $5 branded mug for customers who spend $50).  
- Track purchases manually or use a free tool like [SumUp](https://sumup.com) for POS integration.  

**Numbers to Hit:**  
- Aim for 50 program signups in the first month.  
- Announce the program on social media and in-store.  

---

### 7. Use Contests to Generate Buzz  

Contests are a low-cost way to grow your audience and build community.  

**Example:**  
Host a “Latte Art Contest” on Instagram:  
- Ask customers to post their latte art with #MyCoffeeArt + a tag to your page.  
- Offer a **$20 gift card** to the best entry.  
- Repost winners to your page (social proof!).  

**Budget:** $20 for prizes + $10 for promoting the contest via boosted post.

### FAQ: Coffee Shop Marketing on a Budget  

**1. Will this work for a new coffee shop?**  
Yes! Focus on local SEO and partnerships to build word-of-mouth. Newer shops often see faster results than mature competitors.  

**2. How long till I see results?**  
3-4 months for SEO, 1-2 weeks for social media campaigns, and 1 month for email marketing.  

**3. Can I use Facebook instead of Instagram?**  
Absolutely. Facebook’s Audience Insights tool helps target local demographics.  

**4. What if I can’t afford $50 for email marketing?**  
Start with a free email tool (like Mailchimp) and collect emails manually via a sign-up sheet.  

**5. Should I track my marketing spend?**  
Yes! Use [Google Analytics](https://analytics.google.com) to track traffic and sales from each channel.  

**6. Can I hire help?**  
For $250/month, a freelancer can manage your social media and analytics.  

**7. What about Google Ads?**  
Start with a $50/month budget for targeted keywords like “[city name] coffee shop.”
