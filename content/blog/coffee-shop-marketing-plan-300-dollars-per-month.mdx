### Google Ads: Start Small, Test Fast
Let’s begin with paid ads. For a $300/month budget, split $200 into a Google Ads trial. Focus on hyper-local keywords like "best coffee near me" or "organic coffee in [city name]." Target a 5–10 mile radius and set bids to $1–$1.50 per click. 

Most owners panic about costs, but 40% of local searches lead to a purchase within 24 hours. Test two ad variations: one for morning commuters (8–10 AM) and another for weekend visitors (10–1 PM). If your cost-per-conversion exceeds $15, pause the campaign and pivot to Facebook ads. 

<Callout type="tip">Use Google’s Performance Max campaigns for simplicity. They require less daily management than traditional search ads.</Callout>

### Local SEO: The 80% You’re Missing
Now, lock in the $100/month portion for local SEO. Update your Google Business Profile with high-quality images of your latte art and a clear "open now" schedule. Add 3–5 posts per month about seasonal drinks or local events (e.g., "Join our latte art workshop this Saturday!"). 

Next, claim your Yelp and TripAdvisor listings. 72% of consumers trust online reviews more than ads. Aim to collect 50+ reviews with a 4.5+ rating. Ask happy customers to post photos of their orders with a simple prompt: "Tag us in your photos for a free pastry next time!"

### Marketing Automation: Save Hours Weekly
Allocate $50/month to automation tools. Use a platform like Mailchimp or ConvertKit to create a "Welcome Brew" email series. Trigger it when someone signs up for your free WiFi or downloads your loyalty app. Sample flow:
- **Email 1 (Day 1):** Thank you + 10% off your next purchase
- **Email 2 (Day 3):** Tip on brewing methods at home
- **Email 3 (Day 7):** Invite to a live latte art demo

Automation saves 4.8 hours/week manually managing customer outreach. Pair it with a loyalty app like Loyverse ($25/month) to track repeat buyers and send personalized offers.

<Callout type="warning">Don’t automate everything. Keep 30% of outreach personal — handwritten notes to top customers still beat templates.</Callout>

### Budget Breakdown That Works
Let’s get real about numbers. Here’s how your $300/month funds growth:
- **Google Ads:** $200 (test for 4–6 weeks)
- **Local SEO tools:** $75 (Google Business, Yelp, Tripadvisor)
- **Email + loyalty app:** $75 (Mailchimp, Loyverse)
- **Misc/retargeting:** $50 (Facebook Pixel for ads)

If Google Ads underperforms, cut it and shift funds to Facebook. 62% of local business owners see better results there for visual products like coffee.

### Frequently Asked Questions
**How do I track if this plan works?**  
Use Google Analytics to measure website visits and your loyalty app to track repeat customers. Aim for a 15–20% increase in both after 2 months.

**Can I use this for a food truck or espresso cart?**  
Yes. Adjust location keywords to match your serving areas. Focus on "mobile coffee near me" or "[city name] coffee truck."

**What if my Google Ads cost more than $200?**  
Pause it immediately. Shift that budget to Facebook Ads and local SEO. Often, the problem is poor keyword targeting.

**Should I hire a marketing agency?**  
Only if you’re spending $500+/month already. At $300, DIY with tools like Google’s Keyword Planner and free SEO audits.

**How long before I see results?**  
SEO takes 3–6 months. Paid ads can show results in 2–3 weeks. Automation impacts retention, so measure 4–6 weeks after launch.
