## Why the $300 Rule Works for Coffee Shops

Small cafes can’t afford to waste money on vague “brand awareness” campaigns. Your budget needs to target nearby customers hungry for your espresso right now. Divide your $300 into three parts:
- **$150** for Google Ads (local search + maps)
- **$100** for Facebook Ads (retargeting + promotions)
- **$50** for tools like Google Business Profile and loyalty apps

<Callout type="tip">Start by claiming your Google Business Profile for free—it’s the #1 factor in local search rankings.</Callout>

## Step 1: Master Google Business Profile (It’s Free)

Your Google listing is the first place 90% of customers look when craving coffee. Add 20+ high-res photos (show your baristas, beans, and cozy nooks), post weekly updates about seasonal drinks, and respond to reviews instantly. 

For example, a Denver café called *Bean & Brew* saw a 37% increase in “near me” searches after adding 15 new photos and claiming their Google Post feature for a pumpkin spice latte launch.

## Step 2: Run 2 Google Ads Per Month (Budget: $150)

Focus on two ad types:
1. **Search ads** with keywords like “best coffee near me”
2. **Maps ads** targeting 1-mile radius of your shop

A Seattle-based shop, *Cafe Velocity*, spent $150/month on Google Ads and got **120+ clicks/month** at $1.25/click. Use the Google Ads **Keyword Planner** to find free terms people search for in your area. Test two ads for a week, pause the worst performer, and reinvest in the winner.

<Callout type="warning">Don’t bid on broad terms like “coffee” unless you have a $500+ budget. Start hyper-local.</Callout>

## Step 3: Automate Facebook Ads on a Budget ($100)

Facebook Ads work best when you narrow your audience to:
- 3-mile radius
- Age 25–45
- Interests: “Coffee shops,” “Local events”

Create a $50 “traffic-driving” ad (e.g., “Free pastry with any coffee purchase”) and a $50 retargeting ad for people who visited your website but didn’t buy. A Portland bakery-café, *The Daily Grind*, used this split to get 22% more walk-ins with a $100/month budget.

## Step 4: Build a Loyalty Program That Costs $0

Loyalty apps like **Yelp Monocircle** or **LevelUp** let you create a “buy 9 coffees, get 1 free” program for free. Promote it via your Google/Business Profile posts. Track who scans the QR code on receipts. One Austin shop saw **40% repeat visits** within 3 months of launching a QR-based loyalty system.

<Callout type="example">Try this: Print QR codes on your cups. Customers scan to earn points—no app download needed.</Callout>

## Step 5: Track Everything (Even on a Budget)

Use free tools like **Google Analytics** and **Google Search Console** to see:
- Which ads drive the most website visits
- How many calls you get from Google Maps
- Your “bounce rate” (if it’s over 60%, fix your homepage)

A Toronto café, *Mocha Moose*, reduced wasted ad spend by 50% after realizing 70% of their traffic came from people within 0.5 miles.

<Callout type="tip">Set up **Google Analytics Goals** to track form submissions or phone calls. It takes 5 minutes and saves money long-term.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know if $300 is enough?**  
It’s perfect for small shops. If you spend more than $300/month and see no results, you’re probably overspending on ads without tracking.

**Can I skip Facebook Ads?**  
Only if your Google Ads get enough traffic. Most coffee shops need both to reach different customer segments.

**What if my Google Ads cost more than $150?**  
Pause them immediately. Your bids are too high—try narrower keywords like “organic coffee [your city].”

**How long until I see results?**  
SEO takes 8–12 weeks, but Google Ads can drive traffic in days. Loyalty programs start boosting repeat visits in 2–3 weeks.

**Should I post on Instagram too?**  
Only if you have 30 minutes/week to spare. Focus on Google/Google My Business first.
