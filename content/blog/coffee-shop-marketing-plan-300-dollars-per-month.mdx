### Build a Marketing Plan That Works for Your Coffee Shop

Your $300/month should target three areas: Google Ads, local SEO, and email automation. Let’s break it down.

**1. Google Ads: Get Instant Traffic with a $150/month Allocatio**n

Start with Google Ads because coffee shops average 5x higher ROAS (return-on-ad-spend) here than on social media. For $150/month, you can run two campaigns:

- **Location-based search ads** (e.g., “coffee near me”)
- **Google Maps promotions** (e.g., “50% off brunch on Fridays”)

Set bids between $1–$2.50 per click depending on your city. In New York, expect 2.50/click; in Dallas, around $1.50. Focus on keywords that include your city name + “coffee” to attract local customers.

**2. Local SEO: Win for Free with a $100/month Effort**

Local SEO is the backbone of coffee shop growth. 72% of consumers start their search on Google for local services. Here’s what to do:

- Claim & optimize your Google Business Profile (add high-res photos, update hours, add “Open Table” if applicable)
- List your shop in 10+ directory sites like Yelp, Foursquare, and Citysearch
- Get 15+ reviews/month to boost rankings

<Callout type="warning">Don’t waste time on generic SEO courses. Focus on what works: 3 reviews/week + 1 local blog post/month about your neighborhood.</Callout>

**3. Email Marketing: Turn One-Time Buyers into Loyal Customers**

Only 12% of coffee shops use email marketing. That’s your chance to stand out. For $50/month, sign up for Mailchimp or ConvertKit and:

- Create a “Welcome coffee + 10% off” flow for new subscribers
- Send a weekly “Coffee Specials” email with a 20% off referral link
- Automate a “Birthday surprise” campaign with a free pastry

Example: Austin’s Daily Grind increased repeat visits by 35% after adding a “Buy 9 coffees, get 1 free” email automation.

<Callout type="coffee">DataLatte’s favorite hack: Embed your email sign-up in your in-store POS system. Ask baristas to offer a free cookie to customers who leave their email.</Callout>

### Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I track my $300 marketing budget’s ROI?**  
Use Google Analytics to track website visits from ads and your Google Business Profile. For email, check open rates in Mailchimp and link clicks in your automation flows.

**Should I spend more on Instagram Ads?**  
No. Coffee shops get 3x more customers from Google Maps than Instagram. Focus on local search until your daily traffic hits 200+ people.

**What if my budget is only $200/month?**  
Cut email marketing to $25/month and reallocate to Google Ads. Prioritize search ads over social.

**How long until I see results?**  
Google Ads show results in 2–3 weeks. Local SEO takes 3–6 months to fully rank. Start both at the same time.

**Can I do this myself?**  
Yes. Use Google’s free “Local SEO for Coffee Shops” checklist and Mailchimp’s templates. But if you’d rather focus on brewing, book a free audit with DataLatte.
