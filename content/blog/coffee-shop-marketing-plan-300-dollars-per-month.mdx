## Step 1: Own Your Google Business Profile

Your GBP isn’t optional—it’s your digital storefront. 72% of people who search for local businesses on Google visit within a day. Yet most coffee shops treat it like a resume to be completed and forgotten.

Start by claiming your profile if you haven’t. Add high-quality photos (steaming lattes, your baristas, the inside of your shop). Update your hours for holidays. Add “Call to Action” buttons like “Order Online” or “Book a Table.” Post weekly updates: “New seasonal drink” or “Live music every Friday at 6 PM.”

<Callout type="tip">Add 10 new photos quarterly. Google rewards fresh content. Post once a week—no need for marketing jargon. “We’re out of almond milk” works fine.</Callout>

## Step 2: Local SEO That Costs Almost Nothing

You don’t need a SEO agency. Focus on three things:

1. **NAP Consistency**: Your Name, Address, Phone number must match exactly on Google, Yelp, and Bing.
2. **Local Keywords**: Use “Austin coffee shop” instead of just “coffee shop.” Tools like Ubersuggest show free keyword ideas.
3. **Review Strategy**: 90% of customers trust reviews as much as personal recommendations. Ask every 3rd customer to leave a review. Respond to all—good and bad.

## Step 3: Google Ads on a $100 Budget

You can’t afford $500/month Google Ads, but $100/month gets you results. Focus on **Search Ads** and **Shopping Ads** (if you sell merch):

- **Search Ads**: Target “best coffee near me” with a $35 daily budget ($280/month). Use exact match keywords: “☕️ coffee shop Austin TX” or “☕️ espresso near me.”
- **Shopping Ads**: Sell branded mugs or gift cards. Set a $5 daily budget. 28% of searchers click on shopping ads first.

<Callout type="example">A San Francisco café spent $150/month on Search Ads with a 6.2% conversion rate. They gained 15 new daily customers—$10 extra sales per day = $300/month ROI.</Callout>

## Step 4: Automate Email & SMS for Retention

Your regulars are your best customers. 40% of coffee shop revenue comes from 20% of customers. Use tools like Mailchimp or Textedly:

1. **Email**: Send weekly “Top 3 New Customers” list with a discount code.
2. **SMS**: Text “Hey [Name], your morning latte is 20% off this week! Use code [CODE]”
3. **Automation**: Set triggers like “Welcome new subscribers with a 15% off code.”

<Callout type="coffee">DataLatte uses ActiveCampaign for automation because it’s flexible for small budgets. Start with one automated flow—say, a birthday discount—and scale from there.</Callout>

## Step 5: Track What Matters (And Ignore Noise)

You’re not a data scientist. Focus on two metrics:

1. **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**: If your Google Ads cost $10 to get one new customer who spends $15, you’re winning.
2. **GBP Posting Engagement**: High click-throughs on your “Order Online” button mean your content works.

Use Google Analytics and your GBP dashboard. Ignore vanity metrics like “impressions.” They don’t pay your rent.

<Callout type="tip">Check your GBP weekly. Google updates rankings every 48 hours. Small changes matter—like adding “vegan pastries” to your description.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I split my $300 budget?**  
$200 on Google Ads, $50 on email/SMS tools, $50 on creating/optimizing GBP content.

**How long before I see results?**  
Local SEO takes 3–6 months. Google Ads can drive traffic in 1–2 weeks.

**Is hiring a freelancer better than DIY?**  
Only if you can’t spare 5 hours/week. Most coffee shops can handle this plan themselves.

**What if I run out of ideas for GBP posts?**  
Post “Coffee of the Week,” “Behind the Scenes,” and “Customer Spotlight” on rotating days.

**Can I use Facebook Ads instead of Google?**  
Yes, but Google converts 3x better for local services. Start with a $25/month Facebook test if you have extra funds.

**How do I handle competitors with bigger budgets?**  
They can’t out-optimise your GBP or out-automate your email flows. Focus on hyper-local targeting.

**Do I need a website for SEO?**  
Yes—but it can be a simple 3-page site: Home, Menu, Contact. Use free builders like Wix or WordPress.
