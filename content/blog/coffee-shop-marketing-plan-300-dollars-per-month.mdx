## Google Ads That Cost Less Than Your Daily Brew

You’re not a tech company — you need to spend $300/month on the tactics that make people walk through your door. Google Ads, when done right, can bring in 3–5 new customers a week. Start with two targeted campaigns:

1. **Search Ads** for “coffee near me” (set $150/month budget)
2. **Google Maps Ads** for “best espresso in [City Name]” (set $150/month)

<Callout type="tip">Use location extensions and set bids to 80% of the average CPC in your area (currently $2.25 for coffee shops in Austin, TX).</Callout>

Here’s how the math works: If your ad gets 100 clicks at $2.25 each, you spend $225. 15% of those clicks turn into a visit. That’s 15 new customers from $225. If each spends $5, you make $75 — and that’s just week one.

## Local SEO: The $0 Strategy That Takes 3 Months to Pay Off

You’ve heard about SEO. Most coffee shops give up after 2 weeks because they don’t see instant results. Here’s what works:

- **Google Business Profile** (required): Add 12 high-quality photos, 5 unique menu items, and update “Open Now” status daily
- **Claim your NAP** (Name, Address, Phone): Make sure it’s identical on Yelp, Foursquare, and TripAdvisor
- **Review incentives**: Ask for 3 reviews per week (use a QR code on your receipt)

## Email Marketing That Converts Without Feeling Spammy

You think email marketing is dead? The 82% of coffee shops that use it have 2x the repeat customer rate. Here’s the $45/month plan:

1. Use MailerLite or ActiveCampaign (both under $30/month)
2. Build a list with a “20% off your next latte” quiz on Facebook
3. Send 3 automated flows:
   - Welcome flow (3 emails over 7 days)
   - Birthday discount
   - “You haven’t been in 3 weeks” reminder

<Example>A Denver café used this plan to increase repeat visits by 28% in 6 months. They spent $150 on MailerLite and $100 on Facebook ads.</Example>

Most coffee shop owners waste money on:
- Facebook ads without pixel tracking ($450 wasted avg.)
- Email templates from Canva ($300+ in lost conversions)
- “Marketing experts” who don’t know your local market

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long before I see results from this plan?**  
Google Ads can bring new customers in 7–10 days. SEO takes 3–4 months to build momentum. Email marketing shows improvements in 6–8 weeks.

**Should I hire a marketing agency for this?**  
Only if you’ve tried this plan for 60 days and still can’t keep up with the tasks. Most agencies charge $1,500–$3,000/month for work you can do yourself.

**What if my $300 budget is too tight?**  
Cut Google Ads to $150/month and focus on SEO and email marketing. You’ll still see 15–20% growth in 3 months.

**Are Instagram Ads worth adding?**  
Only if your average customer is 18–34 years old. The CPC is $2.80, but engagement rate is 12% lower than Google Ads for coffee shops.

**How do I track ROI?**  
Use Google Analytics to track:
- Ad cost per customer
- Email open rates
- Website to in-store conversion rate (use phone number tracking)
