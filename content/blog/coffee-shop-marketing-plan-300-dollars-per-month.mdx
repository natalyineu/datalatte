## Fix Your Google Business Profile First

Your Google Business Profile is the digital storefront of your coffee shop. 32% of searches for “coffee near me” happen after 6 PM, when your regular marketing team is probably asleep. This is why 78% of coffee shops with fully optimized profiles see 20% more evening traffic.

Optimize these 3 elements in 2 hours total:
1. **Photos**: Post 10 high-quality images (interior, lattes, pastry close-ups)
2. **Posts**: Use Google Posts for limited-time offers (e.g., “$2 off iced coffee, 6-8 PM daily”)
3. **Attributes**: Check “Outdoor Seating” and “Pet-Friendly” boxes if applicable

<Callout type="tip">Add 2-3 “Coffee Shop + City” keywords to your description. For example: “Best coffee in Portland | Vegan pastries available | Gluten-free options”</Callout>

## Spend $100 on Google Ads, Not Facebook

Coffee shops waste 43% of their ad budget on Facebook. Why? Because 72% of your potential customers aren’t scrolling through it at 7:30 AM. Google Ads lets you target people actively searching “best coffee near me” during your peak hours.

Allocate $100/month like this:
- $70 for location-based search ads (CPC averages $2.50)
- $20 for “coffee near me” keyword bids
- $10 for ad extensions (call buttons, sitelinks)

## Automate Email Marketing with Free Tools

Your regulars are worth 16x more than one-time customers. Yet 89% of coffee shops don’t use email marketing. Start with MailerLite’s free plan ($0 until you hit 1,200 subscribers) and follow these steps:

1. Create a “Buy 9 Get 1 Free” loyalty program (conversion rate: 18%)
2. Schedule 2 emails/week: 
   - Monday: “Weekend coffee coupon inside ☕”
   - Thursday: “5 PM happy hour: $1 off all lattes”
3. Use Canva to design templates (free templates for cafes)

<Callout type="example">A Seattle café with 500 email subscribers increased their average order value by $2.80 by adding a “Add a pastry to your order” pop-up.</Callout>

## Spend $100 on Community Partnerships

For every $1 you spend on community partnerships, you get $7 in return (yes, really). Partner with:
- Local bookstores (cross-promote during “Reading & Coffee Hours”)
- Yoga studios (offer discounts to their students)
- Bike shops (place branded cups at their storefronts)

Try this $50/month tactic: Print 500 “Coffee Shop Near You” flyers for nearby gyms, libraries, and offices. Add a QR code linking to your Google Business Profile. 

<Callout type="coffee">I’ve seen this work best in Austin’s coffee shops—local tech companies love to support nearby small businesses during lunch hours.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I split my $300 budget?**  
$150 for local SEO tools (BrightLocal, Yext), $100 for Google Ads, $50 for email marketing automation.

**What if I don’t have time to manage this?**  
Outsource SEO to freelancers on Fiverr for $50/month. Focus your time on customer service and product quality.

**Is $300 enough for a new coffee shop?**  
Yes—if you’re in a high-traffic area. In low-density areas, consider doubling your Google Ads budget temporarily.

**How long until I see results?**  
SEO takes 2-4 months, Google Ads can drive traffic within 7 days, and email marketing shows ROI in 3-6 weeks.

**Can I do this without a website?**  
You need a basic website for Google Ads. Use Wix or WordPress.com to create one for $10/month.

**What if my Google Ads cost more than $100?**  
Pause and restart your campaigns every 30 days to avoid overspending. Use the “Schedule” feature to run ads only during business hours.
