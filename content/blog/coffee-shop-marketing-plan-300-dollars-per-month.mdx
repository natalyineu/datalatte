## Free Local SEO Setup in 2 Hours
Google prioritizes local results for 46% of all searches. Start by claiming your Google Business Profile (GBP) and making it perfect. Add 10–15 high-quality photos of your latte art, team, and cozy seating. Use keywords like “coffee shop near me” and “Austin coffee delivery” in your GBP description. 

Next, build 5–8 solid backlinks. Partner with a local bakery to cross-promote with “Coffee + Pastry” deals. Get listed in hyper-local directories like the Austin Coffee Association or “Best of Texas” guides. Track your GBP performance weekly — look for spikes in “phone” clicks (calls) and “directions” to measure impact.

<Callout type="tip">Use Google’s free “Find Nearby” feature to target walkers: Add a “10-minute walk” radius in your GBP for Google Maps visibility.</Callout>

## Google Ads That Convert with $150/Month
Spend $150/month on Google Ads with a $5/day cap. Focus on keyword match types: Use exact match for terms like “buy coffee Austin” and phrase match for “coffee shop near me.” Test three ad variations targeting different customer intents:
- “Open now” for lunchers
- “Coffee delivery” for convenience seekers
- “Best latte Austin” for quality-focused buyers

Set bids between $1–$1.50. In Boston, a $150/month budget averaged 45 clicks/month and 6–8 new customers. Use the “Conversion Tracking” tool in Google Ads to measure how many clicks lead to in-store purchases.

## Build a Loyalty Program in 1 Week
A $50/month loyalty program can boost repeat visits by 30%. Use a tool like Yotpo or Smile.io to create a “Buy 9, Get 1 Free” coffee card. Promote it in-store with QR codes and on social media with a “Swipe to Save” campaign. 

In Seattle, a $35/month program with 100 customers drove $1,200/month in recurring revenue. Track which rewards work best — 70% of winners chose free pastries over discounts. Combine this with a referral program: Offer a free drink for every two friends who visit.

<Callout type="example">The Daily Grind in Denver used a “Share 3 Referrals = Free Croissant” offer. It increased customer base by 22% in 2 months.</Callout>

## Social Media that Costs Less Than Your Barista’s Hourly Wage
Post 3–4 times/week on Instagram and Facebook. Use Canva to create templates for:
- “Monday Motivation” latte art
- “Meet Your Barista” profiles
- “Limited Time Flavors” countdowns

Spend $50/month on Meta Ads for targeted promotions. Example: A $25 ad for “Students only — $1.50 coffee” near the local university got 120 new customers for a Phoenix café. Use Stories polls like “Caramel vs. Mocha” to drive engagement without spending.

## Monthly Marketing Review in 30 Minutes
Track these 3 metrics every month:
1. Google Business Profile views (Goal: 10% increase)
2. Cost per new customer (Goal: <$15)
3. Loyalty program signups (Goal: 50+ per month)

Adjust your budget quarterly. If Google Ads cost per customer drops below $12, increase the spend by $20/month. If GBP views stagnate, update your photos and add “Amenities” like free WiFi and outlets.

<Callout type="coffee">DataLatte’s secret: Test one new thing each month. Even a 2% improvement adds up — and keeps your marketing fresh.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I split the $300 budget?**  
$150 Google Ads, $50 loyalty program, $50 Meta Ads, $50 for tools (like Canva Pro). Adjust based on what works.

**Can I do this if I hate marketing?**  
Yes! Use pre-made templates and automate loyalty rewards. Spend 2 hours/week on adjustments.

**What if my Google Ads cost too much?**  
Switch to exact match keywords and lower bids. Focus on high-intent terms like “coffee shop open now.”

**How do I track results without analytics skills?**  
Use Google Analytics 4 and your GBP insights. Track “New Customers” and “Revenue” reports weekly.

**What if I don’t have time for social media?**  
Post once/week with a 48-hour countdown for a seasonal drink. Use free stock photos from Unsplash.

**Can I do this with a $100/month budget?**  
Yes, but cut Meta Ads and loyalty programs. Focus on free SEO and low-cost Google Ads.

**Where do I find time for marketing?**  
Automate 60% with Canva and loyalty software. Block 1 hour/week for ad reviews and photo updates.
