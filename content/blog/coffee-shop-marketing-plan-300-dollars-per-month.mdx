## Allocate Your $300 Budget Like This

Break down your money into three parts: **Google Ads ($150)**, **local SEO ($100)**, and **loyalty programs ($50)**. Let’s unpack how this works in practice.

- **Google Ads ($150/month):** Run hyper-local campaigns targeting coffee lovers within a 5-mile radius. Use keywords like “best coffee near me” with a $2.50 max bid. Aim for 60 clicks/month at $2.50 CPC, costing $150 total.
- **Local SEO ($100/month):** Claim and optimize your Google Business Profile. Add 10 new citations/month (e.g., Yelp, Citysearch). Spend $20/month on tools like Yext or BrightLocal.
- **Loyalty Programs ($50/month):** Use apps like Foap or LoyaltyLion to reward repeat customers. Spend $50/month on discounts (e.g., “5th coffee free”) to boost return visits.

<Callout type="tip">**Start with Google Ads first.** Even $50/month can show if your shop’s Google search traffic is strong enough to justify bigger spending.</Callout>

## Google Ads: The $150 Coffee Shop Playbook

Focus on **geo-targeted keywords** and **mobile-first ad copy**. For example, target “Seattle coffee shop with free Wi-Fi” or “Austin coffee with vegan pastries.” Your budget allows 50–60 clicks/month—enough to test different offers.

Here’s how to structure your ads:
- **Headline:** “Hot Coffee in [Your City] + [Unique Feature]”
- **Description:** “Try our [signature drink]—15% off first visit! Open daily 7am–7pm.”
- **Call to Action:** “Order Online” or “Find Us on Google Maps”

## Local SEO: The $100 Monthly Checklist

Local SEO is your cheapest way to get **free traffic**. Follow this 3-step routine:
1. **Update Google Business Profile weekly:** Add new hours, photos, and seasonal offers (e.g., “$1 off cold brew this week”).
2. **Build 5 local citations/month:** List your shop on sites like:
   - City-specific directories (e.g., San Francisco Coffee Guide)
   - Local event calendars
   - Chamber of commerce pages
3. **Ask for 3 reviews/week:** Train your baristas to politely request reviews. Respond to all 1-star reviews within 24 hours.

<Callout type="example">*Case study:* A Melbourne café boosted local SEO by 30% after adding 10 citations to local food blogs and fixing their Google Profile address. Traffic rose from 120 to 180 daily visitors in 3 months.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I adjust the budget if I’m a tiny shop?**  
Cut Google Ads to $75/month and focus on **free local SEO** tactics first. Test with a “$100/month mini-plan” before scaling.

**How long until I see results?**  
Google Ads can show results in 2–4 weeks. Local SEO takes 3–6 months to rank higher. Loyalty programs boost repeat visits in 1–2 months.

**What if my coffee shop has no website?**  
You still need a **Google Business Profile**. 70% of customers use Google Maps to find local shops—your website isn’t as critical as your Google listing.

**Can I skip Google Ads?**  
Only if your shop is near a university or office building with steady foot traffic. Otherwise, skip at your own risk—Google drives 60% of new customers for local coffee shops.

**How do I track success?**  
Use Google Analytics for ad traffic and your loyalty app’s dashboard for repeat customers. Track daily walk-ins vs. your Google Business Profile’s “weekly visitors” stat.
