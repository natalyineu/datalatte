## Google My Business: Your Free Marketing Superpower

Your Google My Business (GMB) listing is the foundation of *every* coffee shop marketing plan. It’s where 70% of customers start their search for your business. Here’s what to do in 30 minutes:

1. Claim your listing (if you haven’t already).
2. Add 10+ high-res photos (especially of your most popular latte art).
3. Fill out the “About” section with keywords like “best cold brew in Austin” or “quiet downtown coffee.”
4. Post weekly Google Posts for events (e.g., “Monday Music Night at 5 PM”).

<Callout type="tip">**Use Google Posts for urgency.** Share “25% off your first espresso if you visit this week!” to drive new customers.</Callout>

Example: A café in Seattle boosted walk-ins by 22% after adding “quiet work hours” and “pet-friendly seating” to their GMB profile.

## Local SEO That Doesn’t Cost Extra

You don’t need a $1,000/month SEO agency to rank for local searches. Focus on three things:

- **On-page SEO**: Add location-based keywords to your website. For example, “organic coffee in Portland” instead of just “best coffee.”
- **Backlinks**: Partner with local blogs or community sites to get listed in “Top 10 Coffee Shops in [Your City]” guides.
- ** Reviews**: Ask every 10th customer to leave a 5-star review with a specific prompt: “Tell your friends about us on Google.”

## Paid Ads That Don’t Burn Your Budget

With $200/month, you can run **Google Search Ads** targeting hyper-local keywords. Here’s how to avoid wasting money:

- **Budget**: $200/month, $5–$10 daily spend.
- **Keywords**: “coffee near me,” “espresso in [City Name]” (search volume: 1,000–3,000/month).
- **Bidding**: Use “Maximize Conversions” with a $1.50 CPC cap (average for coffee shops is $1.20–$1.80).

Example: A Chicago shop spent $150/month on ads like “cold brew in downtown Chicago” and got 150 new customers/month at $1.05 CPC.

## Marketing Automation to Save You Time

You’re busy roasting beans and taking orders—marketing automation handles the rest. For $50/month, tools like Mailchimp or ZipBooks can:

- Send birthday emails with a free pastry coupon.
- Automate reminders for loyalty program members.
- Track customer purchase history to suggest upsells.

Example: A Portland café increased repeat visits by 30% using an email sequence that said, “You’re 20% to a free latte!”

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I allocate my $300/month?**  
Split it into $200 for Google Ads, $70 for Mailchimp/automation, and $30 for printing flyers (yes, they still work for local foot traffic).

**Can I skip Google Ads and focus only on SEO?**  
No. Local SEO builds trust, but Google Ads drives immediate traffic. Do both.

**What if my city is saturated with coffee shops?**  
Focus on your unique angle. Are you the only shop with a gluten-free menu? Or the only one open until 2 AM?

**Is $1.50 CPC too expensive?**  
It depends. If your average customer spends $5 and visits 3x/month, a $1.50 CPC is worth it.

**How long until I see results?**  
2–3 months for local SEO, 1–2 weeks for Google Ads, and 1 month for automation to show repeat customer growth.
