### Step 1: Master Your Google Business Profile for Free

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is your #1 tool. 61% of customers expect you to have one, and 42% leave if it’s missing. Start by claiming your GBP and adding these must-haves:

- **10+ high-res photos** (show your baristas, latte art, and cozy nooks)
- **Open 7 Google Posts** weekly with discounts (e.g., “$2 off oatmilk lattes this Thursday”)
- **3–5 keywords** in your description: “organic coffee shop near me,” “Pet-friendly café,” etc.

### Step 2: Local SEO for $50/Month

You don’t need an agency to rank for “coffee near me.” Focus on three areas:

1. **NAP Consistency** – Ensure your Name, Address, Phone number match across Yelp, Google, and Foursquare
2. **Local Backlinks** – Partner with nearby bookstores or yoga studios for cross-promotions
3. **Schema Markup** – Use tools like [Schema App](https://schemaapp.com) to highlight your menu prices and hours

A Melbourne café boosted their local ranking from #9 to #2 by fixing 3 listing errors and adding 15 local citations (cost: $0).

<Callout type="coffee">I recommend [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com) for tracking local SEO progress—it’s $49/month but worth it.</Callout>

### Step 3: Facebook Ads That Cost $1.88/Customer

Google Ads are too expensive for most coffee shops. Instead, use Facebook to target within a 5-mile radius:

- **Budget**: $150/week ($300/month)
- **Audience**: 25–45-year-olds, “Coffee Lovers” custom audience
- **Ad Format**: Video showing your barista making a latte + “Buy 1 Get 1 Free” offer

Your goal is 2%–4% conversion rate. If 500 people see your ad and 20 buy a coffee, you’re profitable. A Denver roastery hit 3.1% conversion with a $1.88 cost per customer.

<Callout type="example">One client spent $200/month on Facebook ads for a new cold brew line. They got 45 new customers who became regulars in 3 months.</Callout>

### Step 4: Automate Loyalty with Email for Free

You don’t need a $100/month loyalty app. Use your email platform (Mailchimp, ConvertKit) to create a simple system:

1. **Collect emails** with a “Free Muffin on Your Birthday” offer
2. **Send weekly emails** with:
   - A free drink after 9 purchases
   - Exclusive event invites (e.g., “First Cup of the Month”)
3. **Re-engage dormant users** with a “We Miss You” email + 15% off

A Toronto coffee shop increased repeat visits by 34% using this method. Their average customer now spends $12/month instead of $7.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**How much should I spend on Google Ads?**  
Stick to Facebook Ads first. Google Ads for coffee shops average $2.62 per click, but many small shops can’t profit from that high cost.

**Can I do this without a website?**  
You need a basic website for GBP and Google Ads. Use WordPress + free themes—it takes 2 hours to build and costs $0.

**When will I see results?**  
Local SEO takes 3–6 months. Facebook Ads can drive 10–20 new customers in the first month if targeted correctly.

**Is it worth it if I’m the only barista?**  
Yes! 58% of customers choose businesses with reviews. Even one 5-star review makes a difference.

**What if I can’t afford $300/month?**  
Cut the Facebook budget to $150 and focus on GBP optimization + weekly Google Posts. You’ll still get 5–10% more traffic.
