### 1. Google Business Profile: Your $50/Week Traffic Generator  
Google Business Profile (GBP) is free, but most coffee shops waste its potential. Here’s how to fix it:  

- **Post 3x/week**: Share your daily latte art, seasonal menu updates, or “Meet Barista Jamie” stories.  
- **Claim “Open Now” badges**: 43% of users click places marked as available.  
- **Add 12+ photos**: Include shots of your cozy corners, morning rush, and that weird plant on your counter.  

Example: A Portland café saw +28% walk-ins after posting “quiet hours” for remote workers.  

<Callout type="tip">Set a recurring GBP post schedule on Google’s Content Calendar. No app? Use [Google’s desktop tools](https://business.google.com).</Callout>  

### 2. Local SEO: The $100/Week No-Brain Strategy  
Coffee shops in Austin, Denver, and Seattle all battle the same issue: *“I need my name to show up when people search ‘best coffee near me.’”*  

Your fix: **Claim 3-5 “near me” keywords** and optimize for them.  

1. Pick keywords like *“coffee shop with free Wi-Fi in [City]”* or *“best iced latte [City] under $5.”*  
2. Update your website’s “Services” page with these phrases (add a 150-word paragraph per keyword).  
3. Build backlinks by:  
   - Listing in 5 local directories (Yelp, Citysearch, Eventbrite)  
   - Sponsoring a community event (e.g., a poetry night at your café)  

<Callout type="coffee">DataLatte’s favorite hack: Write a “Coffee Shop Culture in [City]” blog post. Link to it from your main page. Searchers will find you, and locals will feel connected.</Callout>

Start with **Google Ads** for keyword targeting and **TikTok Ads** for young crowds. Example:  

- Run a “$2 Off Iced Coffee” Google Ads campaign with a $50 budget.  
- Target “last-mile” competitors like *“Starbucks [City]”* with *Ad Extensions* (link to your 5-star GBP).  
- Use TikTok for a “Latte Art Challenge” with a $10/day budget to hook Gen Z.  

<Callout type="warning">Don’t waste money on “brand awareness” ad types. You need *leads*, not likes.</Callout>  

### 4. Marketing Automation: The $100/Week Retention Machine  
You’re busy roasting beans. Let automation handle repeat customers.  

1. **Email list**: Offer a *“Buy 5 Coffees, Get 1 Free”* punch card.  
2. **SMS triggers**: Send a “9 AM reminder” to past customers: “Your usual cappuccino is ready!”  
3. **Review automation**: Use tools like Yotpo to prompt 5-star reviews.  

A Denver bakery increased repeat sales by 34% after automating birthday emails with free pastry coupons.
