## How to Split Your $300 Budget Like a Pro

You’re not a chain with a marketing team. Allocate your budget like this:

1. **$150/month for Google Ads** – Compete where customers search
2. **$75/month for local SEO** – Build lasting visibility
3. **$50/month for email automation** – Turn one-time buyers into regulars
4. **$25 for social media tools** – Schedule content efficiently

Let’s break it down by tactic.  

<Callout type="tip">Start with a $50 Google Ads trial first. If you get 3–5 new customers daily, scale up. If not, pivot.</Callout>

## Google Ads That Don’t Require a Marketer

Coffee shops in Austin, Seattle, and Denver see 40%+ of their online orders from Google Ads. Here’s how to do it cheaply:

- Use **location + time-based bidding** (e.g., higher bids during rush hours)
- Focus on **“near me” keywords** like “coffee near me” or “best latte downtown”
- Set **$15/day max** with a $150 monthly cap

Example: A Phoenix café spent $120/month on “coffee near me” ads and got 120 new app downloads. Cost per customer? $1.80.

## Local SEO That Works While You Sleep

Local SEO is free but takes 3–6 months to show results. Prioritize these:

- **Google Business Profile optimization**: Add 20+ photos, 500+ characters description
- **Review management**: Reply to every review, ask for feedback after purchases
- **NAP consistency**: Ensure name, address, phone match everywhere

A Toronto café increased walk-ins by 28% after fixing their Google listing errors. No ads? Just clean data.

<Callout type="warning">Don’t buy “SEO packages” from agencies. You’ll get the same result doing it yourself.</Callout>

## Email Marketing That Doesn’t Suck

You have 300+ email subscribers. Use them.  

**1. Automate welcome flows** (3 emails over 7 days)  
**2. Send a “Happy Birthday” free cookie coupon**  
**3. Create a “Loyalty Points” update email**  

Tools like Mailchimp cost $15/month. A San Diego roastery boosted repeat visits by 45% using automated emails.

## Avoid These 3 Coffee Shop Marketing Mistakes

- **Paying for social media influencers**: A 17-year-old TikToker won’t bring your 45–55 target demographic.
- **Buying “traffic” tools**: They cost $200/month and deliver fake leads.
- **Ignoring your Google Reviews**: One negative review can lose 5% of your business.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I track results on a small budget?** Use free tools like Google Analytics and track unique Google Ads promo codes.

**Can I do this without quitting my day job?** Yes. Spend 2–3 hours/week on Google Ads + email scheduling.

**What if my $300 runs out?** Pause Google Ads and focus on SEO + email. Those are free after setup.

**Is Meta Ads worth it?** Only if your target drinks coffee while scrolling Instagram. Otherwise, skip it.

**How do I handle Google Ads if I’m not tech-savvy?** Use templates from the Google Ads library. They’re simple and effective.
