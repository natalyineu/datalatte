## Start with Local SEO — It’s Free (Mostly)
Local SEO is your foundation. 70% of people find local businesses via Google, but 50% of coffee shops skip optimizing their Google Business Profile. Here’s what to do:  
1. Complete your Google Business Profile with high-quality photos (use your phone’s best camera mode)  
2. Add "coffee shop in [Your City]" as your primary keyword  
3. Post weekly updates (e.g., “New seasonal latte in Austin today!”)  
4. Ask 5 regular customers to leave 5-star reviews monthly  

Budget: $0–$20/month for Google Maps ads if you want to upgrade. A coffee shop in Phoenix saw +40% walk-ins after fixing their Google listing basics.

<Callout type="tip">Claim your Google Business Profile first. It takes 20 minutes but gives you free visibility forever.</Callout>

## Google Ads: Spend $150/Month on Targeted Search Ads
You’re not wasting money on “coffee” ads. Focus on hyper-local search terms like “[Your City] coffee shop near me” or “best espresso downtown.”  

**Budget breakdown:**  
- $100/month for search ads (aim for 50–100 weekly clicks)  
- $50/month for Google Maps ads (shown in the local 3-pack)  

Example: A Denver coffee shop with a $100/month Google Ads budget got 2.3% conversion rate (150 new customers/month). Their best keyword? “Coffee shops near 15th Street.”

## Use $100/Month for Social Media Engagement
You don’t need a content calendar. Post 3x/week on Instagram and Facebook with these templates:  
1. **Behind-the-scenes** shots (baristas roasting beans, your morning routine)  
2. **Customer shoutouts** (“Sarah from the 9am yoga class loved her latte art ☕️”)  
3. **Limited-time offers** (“Buy 2 coffees, get 1 free — ends Friday!”)  

Tools: Use Canva ($0) for designs and Buffer ($12/month) to schedule posts. A Seattle café increased Instagram followers by 150% using just 20 minutes/week on these tactics.

<Callout type="coffee">Try the “3-2-1” rule: 3 posts, 2 platforms, 1 offer. It balances effort with results perfectly for small shops.</Callout>

## Automate Loyalty & Email Marketing for Free
You already have a spreadsheet of customers’ names and emails. Turn it into a tool:  
- **Loyalty automation**: Use tools like Smile.io ($0–$30/month) to reward 5 purchases with a free pastry  
- **Email campaigns**: Send a weekly “Barista Pick” email with a discount code (use Mailchimp’s free tier)  

A Dallas coffee shop increased repeat customers by 30% after implementing a simple email workflow:  
1. Welcome email with 10% off  
2. Birthday email with a free donut  
3. “You haven’t been in 2 weeks” reminder  

<Callout type="example">One Austin owner used a “Bring this receipt to get free cake” campaign and boosted afternoon traffic by 25%.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions
### How much should I spend on Google Ads vs. SEO?
Start with 60% on Google Ads ($180) and 40% on SEO tools ($120). Rebalance after 3 months based on which brings more walk-ins.

### Do I need a website for local SEO?
Yes—but use free tools like Wix or WordPress.com. A basic site with your address, menu, and “Contact Us” page is enough.

### Can I do this myself without hiring a marketer?
Absolutely. A 1-hour/week commitment to Google Ads and 20 minutes for social posts is realistic for most owners.

### What if my Google Ads cost more than $1.50 per click?
Pause the campaign and test new keywords. Focus on long-tail terms like “[City] coffee shop with free wifi” which cost $0.80–$1.20 per click.

### How long before I see results?
Local SEO takes 3–6 months to build momentum. Google Ads can drive new customers in 2–3 weeks if your budget is optimized.
