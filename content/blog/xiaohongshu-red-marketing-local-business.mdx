If a Chinese tourist arrives in Melbourne, Sydney, London, or New York and wants to find the best café, salon, or restaurant — they don't open Google. They open Xiaohongshu (小红书), also known as RED or Little Red Book. It's a hybrid platform: part Instagram, part Pinterest, part Yelp, and the single most influential discovery app for Chinese consumers aged 18–35.

With 300 million monthly active users and a culture of detailed, photo-heavy reviews, Xiaohongshu has become the de facto "Chinese TripAdvisor" for experiences outside China. Businesses that appear in its feed get discovered by both Chinese tourists and local Chinese residents. Businesses that don't exist there are, for this audience, invisible.

## What makes Xiaohongshu different from Instagram or Google

Xiaohongshu posts — called "笔记" (bǐjì, literally "notes") — are longer and more detailed than Instagram captions. Users write genuine experience reports: what they ordered, how much it cost, what the vibe was like, whether the staff spoke Chinese. These notes function as trusted peer recommendations — much more powerful than a star rating.

The platform's algorithm surfaces local experiences based on keyword search. When someone searches "悉尼咖啡推荐" (Sydney coffee recommendations) or "伦敦美发" (London hair salon), relevant posts appear — including posts about your business, if they exist.

**The critical insight:** You don't need to create the content yourself. When Chinese customers visit your business and have a great experience, they often post on Xiaohongshu spontaneously. Your job is to (1) make the experience post-worthy, and (2) make it easy for them to tag your business correctly.

## Step 1: Claim or create your business page

Xiaohongshu has a business account feature called **RED Pro** (专业号). Setting up a business page allows you to:
- Be tagged correctly in user posts (they search for your name and pin it to their note)
- Add your location, website, and booking link
- Reply to posts and comments about your business
- Access basic analytics on post reach and follower demographics

**How to register:**
1. Download the Xiaohongshu app (available on App Store and Google Play globally)
2. Create an account — you can use an international phone number
3. Go to Settings → Professional Account (专业号) → select "Business"
4. Choose your category: Restaurant (餐饮), Beauty Salon (美容美发), Café (咖啡/茶饮), Fitness (健身), etc.
5. Complete your profile with Chinese and English text

**Profile essentials:**
- Business name in Chinese characters (not just English)
- Your address and neighbourhood in Chinese (e.g., 悉尼市中心, 伦敦东区)
- A clear, high-quality profile photo (your logo or storefront)
- A short bio that communicates your signature product or service

<Callout type="tip">Put your address in your Xiaohongshu bio in both English and Chinese. Chinese visitors screenshot bios to share with friends or use for navigation — a bilingual address makes this effortless and increases the chance of your bio being shared.</Callout>

## Step 2: Create your first posts (笔记)

Even if you plan to grow primarily through user-generated content, you need at least 6–8 posts on your own account to establish credibility. A blank profile with 0 posts signals an abandoned account.

**What to post:**

**Your signature item:** One beautifully shot photo of your most Instagrammable product — your best latte art, your most elaborate treatment room, your most striking dish. Write a short description in Chinese explaining what makes it special (ingredients, technique, provenance). Include the price — Chinese Xiaohongshu users always include prices in their notes, and its absence makes a post feel incomplete.

**Your location and vibe:** A short video or carousel showing your space — the interior, the street outside, any distinctive design features. Caption it with your suburb and closest landmark or train station.

**"How to visit" post:** A practical guide: address, opening hours, nearest public transport, whether reservations are needed, average spend per person. This type of post gets saved (bookmarked) heavily, which tells the algorithm it's high-value content.

**Chinese New Year / cultural calendar posts:** Posts tied to Chinese cultural events — Spring Festival, Golden Week, Mid-Autumn Festival — get strong seasonal engagement. Offering a special menu or discount for these occasions and posting about it in advance drives reservations.

## Step 3: Encourage and amplify user-generated content

The most powerful Xiaohongshu marketing happens when real customers post about you. A single viral note from a well-followed user can generate hundreds of new visitors. Here's how to encourage it:

**Create a photo moment.** Install something visually distinctive — a neon sign, a beautiful seasonal display, an unusual piece of decor. Chinese tourists specifically seek out "打卡点" (dǎ kǎ diǎn) — literally "stamp check-in spots" — places worth photographing and posting about. If your café has a gorgeous window seat or your salon has beautiful ambient lighting, make sure that's visible and accessible.

**Display your Xiaohongshu QR code.** Link to your business page and display the QR code prominently on your counter, table cards, and window. When Chinese customers are about to post about you, they'll scan it to tag your page correctly — which means the post is discoverable by anyone searching for your business name.

**Thank posters publicly.** When a customer tags your business in a note, comment on it. Even a simple "谢谢你的分享！欢迎再来！" (Thank you for sharing! Please come again!) shows responsiveness and encourages more posts.

**Offer a small surprise.** Some businesses give a small free item (a cookie, a sample product) to customers who show them a Xiaohongshu post about your business. This is within platform rules (you're rewarding the visit, not the post directly) and creates a positive loop.

<Callout type="example">A Brisbane café added a hand-painted wall mural with the text "你好 Brisbane" (Hello Brisbane) after noticing that Chinese tourists were photographing their existing signage. Within 3 months, 147 Xiaohongshu posts tagged their location — more organic marketing than 2 years of Instagram posts.</Callout>

## Xiaohongshu SEO: getting found in search

Like Google, Xiaohongshu has a search function that many users rely on for local discovery. Optimise your posts for search by including keywords naturally in your captions:

**High-value keywords for local businesses (translate to Chinese):**
- [City name] + [business type]: "悉尼咖啡" (Sydney coffee), "伦敦美甲" (London nail salon)
- [Business type] + 推荐 (recommendation): "咖啡推荐", "美发推荐"
- [Neighbourhood] + [business type]: "唐人街附近美食" (food near Chinatown)
- [Experience descriptor]: 超好喝 (super delicious), 性价比高 (good value), 环境好 (nice atmosphere)

Include 3–5 relevant hashtags (话题 tags) at the end of each post. Xiaohongshu hashtags work like topic feeds — posts with the right hashtags surface in topic search results.

## Paid options: Xiaohongshu advertising

Xiaohongshu's paid ads (薯条 — "fries" — for boosting individual posts) are accessible to businesses with a registered account. Boosting costs as little as $5–$10 per day and increases a post's visibility in search results and the feed.

For more sophisticated campaigns, Xiaohongshu's ad platform (蒲公英, Pú gōngyīng — "Dandelion") connects brands with KOLs (Key Opinion Leaders — Chinese influencers). A KOL with 10,000–50,000 followers in a relevant niche can post about your business for $100–$500, generating dozens of secondary posts from followers who visit based on their recommendation.

## FAQ

**Do I need to post in Chinese?**
Yes. While Xiaohongshu has a global user base, the vast majority of users search and browse in Mandarin. Chinese-only or bilingual posts dramatically outperform English-only posts for this audience. Use a professional translator for your captions — machine translation often reads strangely in Chinese.

**How is Xiaohongshu different from WeChat for local business?**
WeChat is primarily a messaging and community platform — great for building ongoing relationships with existing customers. Xiaohongshu is primarily a discovery platform — great for reaching new Chinese customers who don't know you yet. Ideally, use both: Xiaohongshu to attract, WeChat to retain.

**Can I get found on Xiaohongshu without posting myself?**
Yes — if your customers post about you and tag your location correctly. But you need a claimed business profile so your location is in their search results when they try to tag you. Without a profile, user posts about you are scattered and harder for others to find.

**Is Xiaohongshu only for Chinese tourists, or also local Chinese residents?**
Both. Chinese diaspora communities in Australia, UK, Canada, and the US actively use Xiaohongshu to find local businesses that cater to their preferences — particularly food, beauty, and wellness. In cities like Sydney, Melbourne, and London, local Chinese residents are often a bigger opportunity than tourists.

**How quickly can I expect results?**
Organic Xiaohongshu growth is slower than paid ads — expect 2–4 months before you see consistent foot traffic attributed to the platform. Businesses that invest in a photo-worthy space and actively encourage customer posts see results faster. One viral post from a popular account can change your trajectory overnight.
