Wolt Promoted Placement is a form of advertising that allows restaurants to promote their menu items to potential customers on the Wolt food delivery platform. By creating targeted ads, restaurants can increase their visibility, drive more sales, and reach a wider audience. But, is it worth the money? Let's break it down.

### Benefits of Wolt Promoted Placement

<Callout type="tip">Targeted ads on Wolt can lead to a significant increase in orders, with some restaurants seeing a boost of up to 25% in sales.</Callout>

#### Increased Visibility

Wolt Promoted Placement allows restaurants to reach a large audience of potential customers who are actively searching for food delivery options. By creating targeted ads, restaurants can increase their visibility, making it more likely for customers to discover their brand.

#### Cost-Effective Advertising

While the average cost per click (CPC) for Wolt ads can be high, the benefits of increased sales and visibility often outweigh the costs. Restaurants can set a budget and target specific demographics, locations, and interests to ensure their ads are seen by the right people.

#### Easy to Use

Wolt's advertising platform is user-friendly and easy to navigate, even for restaurants without extensive marketing experience. Restaurants can create and manage their ads in just a few clicks, making it simple to get started.

### Drawbacks of Wolt Promoted Placement

<Callout type="warning">Be cautious of the average CPC, which can range from $1 to $5 or more per click, depending on the target audience and ad format.</Callout>

#### High CPC

As mentioned earlier, the average CPC for Wolt ads can be high, especially if you're targeting a specific audience or location. This can add up quickly, and restaurants need to carefully consider their budget and ad targeting to get the most out of their ad spend.

#### Ad Fatigue

If not managed correctly, Wolt Promoted Placement ads can lead to ad fatigue, where customers become desensitized to the ads and ignore them altogether. This can result in a decrease in sales and a wasted ad budget.
