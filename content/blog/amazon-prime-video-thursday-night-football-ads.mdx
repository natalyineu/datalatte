Amazon Prime Video's exclusive Thursday Night Football deal is one of the biggest shifts in sports advertising in decades. When the first exclusive TNF game streamed in 2022 without a broadcast TV option, it changed what "live sports advertising" means for local businesses. You can no longer just buy time on your local ABC affiliate to reach Thursday night football fans — you need to be on streaming.

This guide covers exactly how to buy ads around TNF on Amazon Prime Video, what it costs, and whether it's worth it for a local business.

## What Amazon Thursday Night Football Actually Is

Every Thursday Night Football game (approximately 15 games per season, running September through January) streams **exclusively** on Amazon Prime Video. That means:

- No free broadcast option for viewers
- All 130M+ Prime subscribers in the US can watch
- Average viewership: 13–18M per game (higher for marquee matchups)
- Viewers include many people who don't watch other streaming content regularly — they subscribe for Prime shipping and watch sports

This matters for advertisers because Amazon has first-party data on every viewer — their purchase history, household income proxies, product interests, and location. No other sports advertising platform gives you this kind of targeting layer on top of live sports inventory.

## How to Buy Ads on Amazon Prime Video TNF

### Option 1: Amazon DSP (Direct / Managed)

Amazon's demand-side platform is the official way to buy advertising on Prime Video — including TNF.

**What it is**: Amazon DSP (Demand-Side Platform) is Amazon's self-serve and managed advertising platform. For Prime Video sports, you're buying "streaming TV" inventory within Amazon's ecosystem.

**Access routes**:
- **Amazon Advertising managed service**: Amazon's own sales team manages campaigns for you. Minimum spend: typically $35,000–$50,000+ for a season — too high for most local businesses
- **Amazon DSP self-serve**: Available through Amazon Advertising portals but requires significant technical expertise and higher minimum spend than most small businesses want to start with
- **Amazon Advertising partner**: Work through a certified Amazon Advertising partner (agencies or tech platforms) that has lower minimum spend thresholds — typically $5,000–$15,000 per campaign

**What makes Amazon DSP different**: You can target viewers by Amazon purchase categories. A local auto parts store can target "automotive buyers." A pet groomer can target "pet product buyers." A gym can target "fitness equipment buyers." This behavioral targeting on top of sports content is unique to Amazon.

### Option 2: Programmatic DSP Buying Amazon Inventory

For smaller budgets ($1,500–$5,000/month), the most practical approach is buying Amazon Prime Video inventory through a programmatic DSP like StackAdapt, The Trade Desk, or Simpli.fi.

These platforms buy Amazon streaming ad inventory on the open market and pass it through to your campaign. You won't get Amazon's full behavioral data overlay, but you'll still be reaching TNF viewers in your target geography.

**Setup**:
1. Open a StackAdapt or Simpli.fi account (or work with an agency that has one)
2. Create a CTV campaign targeting "streaming TV" or "Sports" content category
3. Set geographic targeting to your metro area, DMA, or zip codes
4. Select "Amazon Prime Video" or "Sports streaming" as your inventory target if the platform allows it (StackAdapt and The Trade Desk both have this)
5. Set your flight dates to cover the TNF season (September–January)

**Expected CPMs**: $45–$75 for regular season TNF, $70–$110 for playoff-period games

### Option 3: vMVPDs Carrying TNF

Amazon TNF is exclusive to Amazon — but if you buy ads through Twitch (which Amazon sometimes simulcasts TNF on), or if regional affiliate exceptions apply (some local TV stations in home markets still air some games), your DSP may capture those streams.

This is a supplementary option, not a primary one.

| Regular season (December–January) | $55–$85 | Higher demand as playoff picture develops |
| Playoff-adjacent games (Wild Card week) | $70–$110 | Near-playoff CPMs on the TNF window |
| Super Bowl build-up (Conference Championship) | $85–$130 | Maximum non-Super Bowl pricing |

**Budget guide**:
- $1,500/month: ~20,000–30,000 impressions in your target market via programmatic
- $3,000/month: ~40,000–60,000 impressions
- $5,000/month: ~65,000–100,000 impressions (entering territory where frequency builds meaningfully)

A local business in a mid-size market (pop. 500K–1M) can reach 10–15% of the market's TNF streaming viewers with a $2,000–$3,000/month campaign. At 3–5 impression frequency over a game, that's meaningful brand exposure.

## Amazon's Unique Targeting Advantage

This is what sets Amazon TNF advertising apart from every other sports platform:

**Purchase behavior targeting**: Because Amazon knows what 130M Prime subscribers buy, you can target your TNF ads to people who have purchased in your category. Examples:

| Business Type | Amazon Targeting Angle |
|---|---|
| Pizza or wings restaurant | "Grocery buyers," "food delivery spenders" |
| Auto repair shop | "Automotive accessory buyers," "auto parts purchasers" |
| Pet groomer | "Pet food and pet product buyers" |
| Gym or fitness studio | "Exercise equipment buyers," "fitness supplement buyers" |
| Home services (HVAC, plumbing) | "Home improvement buyers," "tools and hardware" |

Combined with geographic targeting, this lets a local auto shop in Phoenix target TNF viewers in Phoenix who have bought car parts on Amazon in the last 90 days. No other sports advertising platform offers this.

**Household income and affluence signals**: Amazon has strong signals for household income based on purchase patterns. For services that need to reach higher-income households, Amazon DSP's TNF inventory is particularly valuable.

## Who Watches Thursday Night Football on Amazon

TNF viewership has evolved since Amazon took over:

- **Average game**: 13–18M viewers
- **Peak games** (marquee matchups, divisional rivalries): 20M+
- **Core demographic**: 25–54, slightly skewing male (55/45), but broad
- **Amazon Prime subscribers**: By definition, disposable income is above average (Prime is a paid subscription with significant annual cost)
- **Geography**: National, but games involving teams from large markets (Cowboys, Patriots, Packers, Chiefs) significantly boost viewership

For local businesses, the combination of disposable-income viewers (Prime subscribers) + behavioral purchase data targeting is a powerful combination not available in broadcast TV.

## Setting Up Your TNF Campaign: Step by Step

**Step 1: Define your season window**

TNF runs September through early January. Decide whether to run:
- Full season (September–January): Maximum reach and frequency, best for brand building
- Key windows only: September (season excitement), December (holiday + playoff push), January (playoffs)

**Step 2: Choose your access method**

- Under $3,000/month → Programmatic DSP (StackAdapt, Simpli.fi)
- $3,000–$10,000/month → Amazon Advertising partner agency
- $10,000+/month → Direct Amazon DSP managed service or partner

**Step 3: Build your targeting stack**

For Amazon DSP:
- Geographic: DMA / metro area (radius around your location)
- Behavioral: Amazon purchase category overlay relevant to your business
- Device: CTV/connected TV (don't waste budget on mobile if your goal is brand TV presence)
- Time: Thursday evenings 7pm–11pm ET (game window)

**Step 4: Build a 15-second TNF-specific creative**

*"[Business Name] — game day's best kept secret. [Specific offer]. Available tonight."*

Keep it simple. TNF viewers are engaged in the game. Short, local, specific messaging outperforms elaborate spots.

**Step 5: Track with a vanity URL or QR code**

Your TNF ads can drive measurable actions if you give viewers a specific URL or QR code to scan. "Go to [business.com/thursday] for tonight's special" — a unique URL that tracks traffic from Thursday nights directly attributes TV ad response.

## Is Amazon TNF Worth It for a Local Business?

**Best fit**:
- Business with strong game-day relevance (restaurants, bars, delivery)
- Budget $1,500–$3,000/month minimum
- Located in or near a major metro area (audience density matters for geo targeting)
- Selling to households with above-average disposable income (Prime subscribers skew here)
- Have the ability to create a video creative (15-second spot)

**Not a good fit**:
- Budget under $1,000/month — not enough to drive meaningful reach
- Hyper-local radius (under 2 miles) — geo targeting this tight on CTV has too much waste
- B2B products or services — TNF reaches consumers, not business decision-makers in a work context
- No video creative and no budget for one — you need video for CTV

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I target only specific Thursday Night Football games?**

Through Amazon DSP managed service, yes. Through programmatic partners, less precisely — you're buying sports content adjacency that includes TNF but may not be exclusive to it. If a specific matchup (Cowboys vs. Eagles, a local team's game) matters to you, work with an Amazon partner who can prioritize that inventory.

**Q: How does Amazon TNF compare to NFL on Peacock or ESPN+?**

Amazon is the only streaming platform with exclusive NFL rights for a full weekly package. Peacock has exclusive Sunday Night Football streaming rights (which simulcasts with NBC broadcast). ESPN+ gets MNF simulcast. Amazon TNF is unique in being streaming-only with no broadcast alternative — which concentrates the TNF streaming audience on a single platform and makes Amazon's targeting the only way to reach them.

**Q: Do I need to have an Amazon seller account to advertise?**

No. Amazon DSP and programmatic TNF advertising is available to businesses regardless of whether they sell on Amazon. You're buying ad inventory on Amazon's platform, not advertising products for sale on Amazon.com. An Amazon Advertising account or a programmatic DSP partner is all you need.

**Q: What type of creative file does Amazon require?**

Amazon Prime Video CTV accepts MP4 or MOV video files, typically 1920x1080 (16:9). Audio must be stereo and properly mixed for TV playback. Most video production studios and local commercial production companies can deliver in this format. Costs for a basic 15-second CTV spot range from $500 (DIY/simple) to $2,000–$5,000 for professional production.
