Haiti is the Caribbean's most populous French-speaking nation — 11 million people on the western third of Hispaniola island, sharing the island with the Dominican Republic. Port-au-Prince is the capital and economic centre; Cap-Haïtien (Okap) is the northern historical city with colonial architecture; Pétionville is Port-au-Prince's upscale suburb. Haiti uses the Haitian Gourde (HTG), but US Dollars are widely used in commerce.

Haiti's digital economy is one of the Caribbean's most dynamic despite significant infrastructure challenges. Digicel (the Caribbean telecom giant) dominates, and MonCash (Digicel's mobile money platform) has become the primary financial instrument for millions of Haitians. Facebook has achieved extraordinary penetration and is used by entrepreneurs for all business activity.

**Facebook is the Haitian digital economy**: Haiti's rapid smartphone adoption in 2015-2020 happened through the lens of Facebook — for millions of Haitian entrepreneurs, Facebook IS the business platform. Facebook groups, Facebook Marketplace, and Facebook Live selling are all standard business practices.

**MonCash has transformed commerce**: MonCash (Digicel) is Haiti's dominant mobile money platform — processing billions of HTG annually. Accepting MonCash is standard for any consumer business. Natcash (Natcom mobile money) is the secondary platform.

**Haitian Creole is the language**: Haitian Creole (Kreyòl ayisyen) is the language of all Haitians — the primary spoken and increasingly written language. French is official and used in formal contexts but Kreyòl is the digital marketing language for reaching all Haitian consumers. Content in Kreyòl resonates far more deeply than French.

## Three Haitian Business Examples

### 💇 Salon Bote (Beauty Salon), Pétionville

**Strategy**: Facebook business page with beauty content in Kreyòl, WhatsApp Business for bookings and MonCash payments, Instagram transformation photos, Facebook Marketplace listing, Facebook Live demonstrations.

**Budget**: HTG 20,000/month (≈$149): HTG 8,500 Facebook Ads, HTG 7,500 Google Ads, HTG 4,000 content.

### 🍽️ Restoran, Pétionville

**Strategy**: Facebook in Kreyòl for community reach, Instagram food photography, WhatsApp for reservations, MonCash for payments, Google Maps listing.

**Budget**: HTG 16,000/month (≈$119): HTG 6,800 Facebook Ads, HTG 5,800 Google Ads, HTG 3,400 content.

### 🏋️ Gym, Port-au-Prince

**Strategy**: Facebook fitness content in Kreyòl, Instagram, WhatsApp for membership inquiries, MonCash for monthly fees.

**Budget**: HTG 14,000/month (≈$104): HTG 5,800 Facebook Ads, HTG 5,000 Google Ads, HTG 3,200 content.

## Haitian Marketing Calendar

| Period | Opportunity |
|---|---|
| January 1 | Fèt Endepandans (Independence Day — 1804) — major national |
| February | Kanaval (Haitian Carnival) — biggest cultural event |
| May 18 | Fèt Drapo (Flag Day) — very important |
| May | Fèt Manman (Mother's Day, last Sunday May) |
| October 17 | Anniversary of Dessalines (national hero) |
| November 1-2 | Fèt Gede (Day of the Dead — Vodou/Catholic) |
| December 25 | Nwèl (Christmas) |

**Kanaval (February)**: Haiti's Carnival is one of the Caribbean's most vibrant — a massive cultural explosion of music, costumed bands (bann rara), and celebration. Beauty salons see enormous demand for Carnival preparation. Musical acts, food, and costumes all peak. Cap-Haïtien's Carnival is particularly famous. Start campaigns January 20.

**Flag Day (May 18)**: One of Haiti's most emotionally important national celebrations — the day the Haitian flag was created in 1803. Intense national pride, wearing of blue and red. Commercial campaigns with Haitian pride imagery perform very well.

**Fèt Gede (November 1-2)**: Haiti's unique Day of the Dead combining Vodou and Catholic traditions — honouring Gede (the spirit of the dead). Costume and celebration in purple, black, and white. Florists and costume businesses see demand.

## FAQ

**Haitian Creole or French for Haiti marketing?**
Haitian Creole (Kreyòl) for all digital marketing targeting Haitians — it is the mother tongue of every Haitian and the language of social media, WhatsApp, and community life. French is formal/written but feels distant in social media. Kreyòl content gets far higher engagement. The Kreyòl orthography (official since 1980) should be used — it is different from French spelling.

**Haiti diaspora marketing potential?**
Enormous. The Haitian diaspora (particularly in Miami, New York, Boston, and Montreal) is estimated at 2-3 million people. Many Haitian businesses in Haiti target diaspora remittances via Facebook groups for diaspora communities. Haitian entrepreneurs in the diaspora also need marketing services.
