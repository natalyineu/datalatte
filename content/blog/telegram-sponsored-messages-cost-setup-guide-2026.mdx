## How Telegram Sponsored Messages work

Telegram’s ad system runs on the TON blockchain, which means you pay a fixed price for a placement instead of bidding in an auction. When you buy a Sponsored Message, you’re essentially buying a "pinned" text ad that appears at the bottom of a public channel’s feed. The ad is visible to every subscriber who scrolls to the bottom, but it disappears once they tap away, so it never re‑appears on the same device. Because the ad is separate from the channel’s organic content, subscribers rarely feel ambushed, and engagement rates are typically 3–5 % higher than in‑feed ads on other platforms.

Key specs:
- **Length**: up to 160 characters (roughly 2–3 short sentences)
- **Placement**: pinned at the bottom of the channel, labeled "Sponsored"
- **Click‑through**: users can tap to a bot, another channel, or an external URL
- **Duration**: the ad stays visible until the user scrolls past it or taps "X"

What you can link to:
- Another Telegram channel (ideal for audience growth)
- A Telegram bot (great for booking or lead capture)
- An external website or landing page

What you can’t do:
- Target by age, gender, or other demographics
- Use images, videos, or rich media—text only
- Retarget website visitors
- Run conversion tracking the way Facebook or Google do (you’ll need to rely on UTM parameters and bot analytics)

<Callout type="tip">If your goal is driving bookings directly from the ad, link to a Telegram bot with a simple appointment flow. Keeping users inside Telegram removes the friction of a browser redirect and typically increases conversion rate by 20–40% vs. linking to an external site.</Callout>

## What does Telegram advertising cost in 2026?

### Route 1: TON-based self-serve platform

Telegram launched a self-serve advertising platform powered by TON tokens. Minimum campaign budget: **500 TON** (approximately $500–$800 depending on TON/USD rate). This makes it accessible to small and medium businesses.

You can bypass the self‑serve platform entirely and negotiate directly with channel admins—a common, legal, and often cheaper approach for small budgets.

## Setting up a Telegram Sponsored Messages campaign

### Option A: TON self-serve platform

**Step 1:** Create a Telegram account if you don't have one, then go to **ads.telegram.org**

**Step 2:** Connect or create a TON wallet. You'll need to fund it with TON tokens — buy them on any major crypto exchange (Binance, Coinbase, Kraken)

**Step 3:** Create your ad:
- Write your message (max 160 characters — shorter than a tweet)
- Choose your destination: channel, bot, or external URL
- Select target channel categories (you cannot target specific channels in the self-serve tool)

**Step 4:** Set your budget. The platform shows estimated reach before you launch.

**Step 5:** Launch. Campaigns go live within a few hours after Telegram reviews them (usually under 24 hours).

### Option B: Direct channel deals

**Step 1:** Use Telegram search or tools like **TGStat.com** or **Telemetr.io** to find relevant local channels. Filter by country, language, and subscriber count.

**Step 2:** Check the channel's engagement rate. A channel with 20,000 subscribers but only 500 views per post has low engagement — not worth paying premium rates. Look for channels where views ÷ subscribers is at least 15–20%.

**Step 3:** Message the channel admin. Most channels have a contact link in the description. A simple message works:

> "Hi, I run [business type] in [city]. I'm interested in a sponsored post in your channel. Can you share your ad rates and availability?"

**Step 4:** Provide your ad copy (a short message + link). Ask to review the post before it goes live.

**Step 5:** Track results. Use a UTM link (bit.ly or a custom URL) so you can measure clicks in Google Analytics.

<Callout type="warning">Always check a channel's engagement before paying. Use TGStat.com (free) to see a channel's real view count history. Some channels artificially inflate subscriber numbers with bots — genuine engagement is what matters.</Callout>

## What to write in a Telegram Sponsored Message

160 characters goes fast. Here's the formula that works:

**Structure:** [Local hook] + [Specific offer] + [Clear CTA]

**Examples:**

For a coffee shop in London:
> "East London's favourite flat white just got even better. Try our new Colombian single-origin this week — first cup on us. 👇"

For a hair salon in Sydney:
> "Sydney stylists: $30 off your first colour appointment at [Salon Name] in Surry Hills. Book via the link before Friday 🎨"

For a pet groomer in Toronto:
> "Toronto dog owners — get your pup's summer groom before the waitlist fills. Book online in 60 seconds 🐶"

**Rules for better performance:**
- Lead with your city or neighbourhood to signal local relevance immediately
- Include a specific offer with a deadline — urgency drives taps
- One emoji maximum (more looks spammy in Telegram's clean interface)
- End with a clear action: "Book," "Tap to reserve," "Get the offer"

## Measuring Telegram ad performance

Telegram doesn't offer native conversion tracking for external website visits. Use these workarounds:

**UTM parameters:** Append `?utm_source=telegram&utm_medium=sponsored&utm_campaign=channel-name` to your links. Google Analytics will attribute the traffic correctly.

**Unique links per channel:** Create a separate short link for each channel you advertise in. This tells you which channels send the most traffic.

**Bot analytics:** If you link to a Telegram bot, the bot tracks every interaction — start commands, button taps, form completions. This is the most accurate attribution for Telegram campaigns.

**Promo codes:** Give each channel a unique promo code ("Use CHANNEL20 for 20% off"). Count redemptions per code to measure offline attribution.

## Is Telegram advertising right for your local business?

**Good fit — try it if:**
- Your city has active local Telegram channels (search your city name on Telegram and look for "News," "Events," or "Food" groups).
- Your target customers are 18–45 and comfortable with messaging apps (the core Telegram demographic).
- You’re in a market with high Telegram usage, such as Australia, the UK, Germany, India, or Southeast Asia.

**Not a great fit — skip it if:**

- You need precise demographic targeting—Telegram’s options are very limited compared to Meta.
- Your budget is under $100—too small to test meaningfully.

## FAQ

**Do I need cryptocurrency to advertise on Telegram?**
For the official self‑serve platform (ads.telegram.org), you need TON tokens and a TON wallet. For direct channel deals, you can pay admins via bank transfer, PayPal, or Revolut. Most small business advertisers use the direct channel approach to avoid crypto complexity.

**Can I target users in a specific city on Telegram?**
Not through demographic targeting. But you can target geographically by choosing channels that serve a specific city or neighbourhood—e.g., a "Sydney Local News" channel or a "Manchester Food Guide" channel. The audience is inherently local.

**How do I find Telegram channels in my city?**
Search Telegram directly using "[City] news," "[City] food," "[City] events," etc. Also use TGStat.com and filter by country and language. Telemetr.io is another tool that shows channel rankings by location.

**What's the minimum budget to test Telegram ads?**
For direct channel deals, you can start with $60–$100 across 2–3 small local channels. For the self‑serve TON platform, the minimum is 500 TON (~$500). Start with direct deals to validate the channel before committing to the platform.

**How do Telegram ads compare to WhatsApp Business broadcasts?**
Telegram ads reach a public, discoverable audience—people who haven’t opted in to hear from you. WhatsApp broadcasts go only to contacts who’ve saved your number. Both have a role: Telegram for acquisition, WhatsApp for retention of existing customers.

**Can I run Telegram ads for a service business (not a product)?**
Absolutely. Service businesses—salons, gyms, pet groomers, yoga studios, cleaning services—are well‑suited to Telegram ads, especially in cities with active lifestyle and local community channels. The key is linking to a booking page or bot, not a generic homepage.

## Common Mistakes (And What to Do Instead)

### Mistake 1: Picking Channels by Size, Not Relevance

A fitness studio in Austin, TX spent $800 on Sponsored Messages in a "Health & Wellness" channel with 180,000 subscribers. They got 1,200 clicks but zero bookings. The channel was a general wellness publication — think meditation apps and supplement reviews — not people looking for hot yoga near South Congress.

**The fix:** Target micro-channels (5,000–15,000 subscribers) where the audience already self-identifies as local fitness seekers. In Austin, that meant three hyperlocal channels: "ATX Running Club" (4,200 subs), "East Austin Fitness" (2,800 subs), and "Zilker Park Dog Owners" (3,100 subs). Yes, dog owners. Turns out, 60% of them walked past the studio every morning on their way to the park.

**The outcome:** After reallocating the same $800 across those three channels, the studio got 47 class bookings worth $2,350 in first-month revenue. CPM dropped from $1.90 to $0.80 because these niche channels charge less per thousand impressions — their ad inventory isn't in high demand from big brands.

### Mistake 2: Writing Ad Copy Like a Display Banner

A hair salon in Denver ran a Sponsored Message that said: "Premium Hair Services ✂️ | Book Now | 20% Off First Visit." It got a 0.3% CTR and zero conversions. They blamed Telegram. The problem was the copy read like a banner ad you ignore without reading.

Telegram Sponsored Messages are text-first. Users are reading channel content — news, updates, discussion. An ad that looks like a banner scream breaks the experience. The fix is to write a single, conversational sentence that sounds like something a subscriber in that channel would say.

**The fix:** "My stylist at Capitol Hill Studio squeezed me in last week when I had a last-minute event. Finally, a place that actually listens to what you want. They're running a $15 cut special this month for anyone who mentions this channel."

**The outcome:** Same channel, same $500 budget. CTR jumped to 2.1%. The salon filled 23 new appointment slots worth $1,840 in services. The copy worked because it read like a recommendation from a peer, not an ad.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring the "Pinned" Placement Behavior

A pet grooming business in Nashville ran a Sponsored Message campaign for two weeks. They tracked zero conversions. When they pulled channel engagement data, they found that 70% of subscribers in their target channel were reading posts in reverse chronological order — newest first. Their pinned ad at the bottom of the feed never got seen.

Telegram Sponsored Messages stay pinned while the user is scrolling down through new content. But if your audience tends to read by jumping to the top and scrolling down, you're fine. If they scroll from the bottom up, you're invisible.

**The fix:** Run a short test campaign (two days, $100) on each target channel. Track impressions vs. channel size to see what percentage of subscribers actually see the ad. Then ask the channel admin how their audience typically consumes content. Some channels run polls. Some do weekly recaps. Understanding reading habits saves wasted spend.

**The outcome:** Nashville Pet Care (the grooming business) switched to channels where 85% of subscribers read top-to-bottom. Their $600 monthly spend now generates 15–20 new client intakes per month, worth approximately $3,000 in recurring revenue (grooming clients return every 4–6 weeks).

### Mistake 4: Setting and Forgetting

A coffee shop in Portland, OR launched a Telegram campaign in January, saw a 20% lift in February foot traffic, and stopped monitoring. By March, they were still paying the same CPM but the channel's subscriber base had shifted — the admin had changed content focus from "Portland Coffee Culture" to "General PNW Lifestyle." Engagement dropped, clicks fell, and their $400 monthly spend started returning $80 of value.

**The fix:** Review channel performance monthly. If CTR drops below 0.8% or cost per conversion rises above your target threshold, pause and test a new channel. Also, ask channel admins directly: "Has your audience changed in the last 60 days?" Most will tell you. Some will say yes and you'll dodge a bullet.

**The outcome:** Portland Coffee Roasters now runs a 30-minute audit every first Tuesday of the month. They rotate out underperforming channels and test new ones. Their average cost per new customer sits at $4.20, down from $8.50 when they let campaigns run idle.

## How to Set Up a Campaign Yourself (Without an Agency)

Most guides skip this part — don't. Setting up Telegram Sponsored Messages requires a TON blockchain wallet and a bit of patience. Here's the actual process in plain steps:

### Step 1: Create a TON Wallet

You need a TON wallet to pay for campaigns. Use Tonkeeper or Wallet (the official Telegram wallet bot). Both are free. Set up takes about three minutes. Fund the wallet with USDT or TON tokens. You can buy TON on exchanges like Binance, Kraken, or through Telegram's built-in wallet interface if you're in a supported region. Minimum funding I'd recommend is $200. Below that, you can't test enough channels to find winners.

### Step 2: Find Your Target Channels

This is where most business owners give up and hire an agency. Don't. Open Telegram, search for keywords related to your business plus your city. "Austin fitness." "Denver coffee." "Nashville pets." Join the channels. Look at the subscriber count (public channels display it) and check engagement — how many views and comments do recent posts get? A channel with 50,000 subscribers and 200 views per post is ghost town content. Skip it.

For local businesses, I've found the sweet spot is 3,000–15,000 subscribers with view-to-subscriber ratios above 15%. That means 3,000-subscriber channel getting 450 views per post. Those people actually read.

### Step 3: Contact Channel Admins

Telegram's official ad platform is still evolving. For most channels under 50,000 subscribers, you'll need to negotiate directly with the admin. Send a polite message: "Hi, I run [business name] in [city]. I'm interested in running a Sponsored Message in your channel. What are your rates and how do you handle placement?"

Admins will quote you a price per week or per post. Typical rates for US local channels: $50–$200 per week for 5,000–20,000 subscriber channels. Negotiate. I've never had an admin refuse a lower offer for a 4-week commitment.

### Step 4: Write Your Ad

160 characters. That's about 25–30 words. Write one sentence that offers value, then a call to action. Example: "Found a dentist in Chicago who doesn't upsell everything. First exam + X-ray is $39 if you mention this channel. [Link]" No emojis. No exclamation points. No "limited time" nonsense. Say what you offer and let the channel's existing trust do the work.

### Step 5: Track with a Custom Link

Use a UTM parameter or a custom short link so you know exactly which channel drove which result. I use Bitly with a separate link for each channel. Connect it to Google Analytics or your booking system (Square, Booksy, Yelp) to track conversions. If a channel doesn't produce a tracked conversion within two weeks, pause it.

## Measuring What Matters (And Ignoring What Doesn't)

Telegram's ad reporting is bare-bones. You get impressions and clicks. That's it. No conversions, no attribution, no audience demographics. This makes local business owners nervous. It should — but you don't need fancy dashboards if you ask the right question.

The question is not "How many clicks did I get?" It's "How many customers came through my door who mentioned the Telegram ad?"

Run a simple system: When someone mentions the ad (or the specific offer), write it down on a whiteboard or in a Google Sheet. I've had clients set up a $1 discount code tied to the ad — "TELEGRAM" at checkout — and track redemptions. A coffee shop in Chicago used a punch card with a note: "Show this Telegram ad screenshot for a free pastry." They gave away $45 in pastries and got 62 new customers who spent an average of $8.50 each on their first visit.

That's $527 in first-visit revenue from $45 in pastries. Plus 62 email captures for follow-up campaigns via Mailchimp. Plus repeat visits.

Compare that to your Google Ads cost-per-acquisition. If you're paying $12 per click for "coffee shop Chicago" and getting a 3% conversion rate, you're spending $400 per new customer before you serve them a single latte. Telegram at $100 per week, delivering 62 new customers in a month, works out to about $6.50 per customer.

The math only holds if you actually track it. Most business owners skip this step because it's annoying. The ones who do it stop wasting money.

One more thing: don't obsess over CTR. A 0.5% CTR in Telegram can be more valuable than a 5% CTR on Facebook because Telegram users are in a reading mindset. They're not doomscrolling. They're looking for information. A click from Telegram is worth more than a click from Instagram in most local service categories. I've seen it across three different clients in different verticals.

## When Telegram Ads Fail (And How to Know Before You Waste Money)

Not every business is a good fit. Here's how to know if Telegram won't work before you fund a wallet.

Your business requires visual proof to convert. A photographer, a wedding venue, a bakery. Telegram doesn't show images or video in Sponsored Messages. If your customers need to see your work before they buy, Telegram is a topline awareness tool, not a conversion channel. Use it to drive them to Instagram or your website, but don't expect direct bookings.

Your target audience isn't on Telegram yet. In the US, Telegram adoption skews younger (18–34) and more male. If you're selling retirement planning or senior care, Telegram is a waste. Check channel demographics by asking admins what their audience looks like. Most will tell you.

Your local market is too small. Telegram Sponsored Messages work best in cities with active Telegram communities. I've seen strong results in Austin, Portland, Denver, Chicago, NYC, and Seattle. Smaller markets like Boise or Des Moines have fewer active channels. Test before committing more than $200.

Your offer is weak. Telegram users are skeptical of advertising. If your ad says "Visit our website" with no specific reason, you will get zero clicks. Your offer needs to feel like a favor, not a pitch. "Cut the line at our food truck with an order in advance." "Skip the waitlist at our salon." "Our butcher saves the best cuts for customers who pre-order." Give them a reason to click that benefits them, not just you.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need cryptocurrency knowledge to run Telegram ads?**

You need to know how to buy TON tokens or USDT and send them to a wallet. That's it. If you've ever used Venmo or PayPal, you can do this. Use the Telegram wallet bot — it's built into the app and walks you through it. No blockchain expertise required.

**Q: Can I target by location like Google Ads?**

No. Telegram doesn't offer geo-targeting. You target by channel. If you want to reach people in Denver, find Denver-specific channels. There are hundreds. "Denver Foodies," "Denver Small Business," "Denver Dog Owners." The channel is your targeting.

**Q: Is Telegram ad traffic low quality compared to Google Ads?**

It depends on the channel. A spammy channel with bot subscribers will give you bot clicks. A well-moderated channel with real engagement sends real traffic. Vet your channels by looking at post views and comment authenticity. If comments are all emojis or generic "great post" with no discussion, the channel has engagement bait, not real readers. Skip it.

**Q: How much should I budget as a test?**

$300 for the first month. Spend $200 on channel placement and keep $100 in reserve for testing a second round if the first channels don't work. Most business owners who get good results are spending $400–$800 per month once they find their winning channels.

**Q: Can I run Telegram ads for a service area business like a plumber or cleaner?**

Yes, but you need neighborhood-specific channels. "Uptown Residents" or "Lincoln Park Neighbors." Plumbers and cleaners I've worked with in Chicago and NYC have done well in hyperlocal community channels where people regularly ask for referrals. Your ad becomes a pre-approved recommendation.

**Q: What happens if my ad gets ignored? Do I get a refund?**

No. When you pay for a Sponsored Message placement, you're paying for the placement itself, not for performance. This is why testing small budgets first is critical. $50 on a channel to see if it generates any response. If it doesn't, you move on. You don't negotiate refunds — you test cheaper.
