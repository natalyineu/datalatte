### Step 1: Set Up Your TikTok Ads Account

To start running TikTok ads, you'll need to set up a business account on the platform. This will give you access to TikTok's advertising tools and allow you to create and manage your ad campaigns. Don't worry if you're not tech-savvy – setting up a TikTok ads account is relatively straightforward.

Callout(type: "tip"): To create a TikTok business account, go to the app, tap the "+" icon, and select "Create a Business Account".

### Step 2: Choose Your Ad Objective

When setting up your ad campaign, you'll need to choose an objective. This will determine how your ads are optimized and what metrics you'll be measured on. For salons and spas, a good starting point is to choose the "Conversions" objective, which will help you drive bookings and appointments.

Callout(type: "warning"): Be careful when choosing your ad objective – if you choose the wrong one, you may end up wasting your ad budget.

### Step 3: Create Your Ad Creative

Your ad creative is the visual and copy that will accompany your ad. This is where you can get creative and showcase your salon or spa's personality. Make sure to include a clear call-to-action (CTA) and a link to your website or booking page. For salons, a good CTA might be "Book Now" or "Get a Free Consultation". For spas, it might be "Book a Massage" or "Get a Quote".

Callout(type: "coffee"): At DataLatte, we recommend using high-quality visuals and clear, concise copy to make your ad creative stand out.

According to a recent study, the average cost per click (CPC) for TikTok ads is around $0.10-$0.20. This is significantly lower than Facebook and Instagram, making TikTok a great option for salons and spas with limited ad budgets.

### Step 4: Set Your Budget and Bidding Strategy

When setting your budget and bidding strategy, make sure to consider your ad objective and target audience. For salons and spas, a good starting point is to set a daily budget of $10-$20 and use a cost-per-click (CPC) bidding strategy.

Callout(type: "example"): Let's say you're a salon owner in New York City, and you want to reach new clients who are interested in hair and makeup services. You could set a daily budget of $15 and target users who have shown an interest in beauty and wellness on TikTok.

## Step 5: Crafting Ad Creatives That Stop the Scroll

Your creative is the make-or-break element of any TikTok campaign. On a platform where users scroll past hundreds of videos daily, you have about three seconds to grab attention. For salons and spas, the most effective creatives show transformation and emotion. Think before-and-after hair colour reveals, a spa guest’s relaxation journey, or a quick clip of a stylist’s technique set to a trending sound. Data from TikTok’s own case studies shows that videos using trending sounds see 2x higher engagement than those without. Keep your clips between 15 and 30 seconds, add text overlays to reinforce your offer (“50% off first visit”), and always include a clear CTA like “Book Now” or “Get Your Look.” A salon in Melbourne tested two creatives—one static image and one 20-second video—and the video generated 4x more bookings at a 30% lower cost per lead. Action step: this week, record three short clips of your best work, edit them with TikTok’s built-in tools, and use them as your first ad set.

## Step 6: Targeting Tips to Find Your Ideal Clients

Demographics and location are just the starting point. TikTok’s interest and behavior targeting lets you zero in on people actively looking for beauty and wellness services. For example, a hair salon in London can target users who follow accounts like “hairbyjess” or have engaged with hashtags like #balayage, #hairtransformation, or #curlygirlmethod. You can also create a custom audience from your website visitors using the TikTok Pixel—then retarget anyone who visited your booking page but didn’t complete a reservation. That alone can lift conversion rates by 20-30%. For a spa in Vancouver, targeting users who recently purchased beauty products on TikTok Shop and live within a 10-mile radius delivered a 3x higher click-through rate than broad demographic targeting. Pro tip: build a “lookalike” audience from your best past clients. Upload a list of email addresses from your booking system, and TikTok will find similar users who are likely to book. Start with a 1% lookalike (the closest match) and expand if you need more reach.

## Step 7: Measuring Success and Scaling Your Campaign

Don’t just launch and forget—optimise based on real data. The key metrics for salons and spas are cost per booking (not just CPC), conversion rate, and return on ad spend (ROAS). Install the TikTok Pixel on your booking confirmation page to track exactly which ad led to an appointment. Once you have a winning creative and audience, scale gradually: increase your daily budget by 20% every 3-5 days while keeping an eye on cost per conversion. A fitness studio in Toronto used this approach, starting at $15/day and scaling to $75/day over three weeks while maintaining a ROAS of 4.5x. A/B test at least two different headlines or CTAs per ad set—sometimes “Free Consultation” outperforms “Book Now” by 40%. And remember, TikTok’s algorithm rewards fresh content; refresh your creatives every two weeks to avoid ad fatigue menus.
