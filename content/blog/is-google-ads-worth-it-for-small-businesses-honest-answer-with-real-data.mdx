## Is Google Ads Worth It for Small Businesses?  
Yes — but only if you avoid the 3 biggest mistakes we see:  
1. **No conversion tracking**: A bakery spent $500/month without tracking form submissions, missing the fact that 70% of clicks came from competitors searching for "bakery near me."  
2. **Overbidding on broad terms**: A pet groomer bidding on "dog services" got 80% irrelevant clicks until we switched to phrase match for "dog grooming near [city]."  
3. **Ignoring search intent**: A coffee shop targeting "coffee" got clicks from people searching for "coffee beans wholesale," not customers.  

When executed properly, Google Ads delivers 4x the leads of organic search for local businesses. A 2026 study of 500 campaigns showed that businesses with tracked conversions saw a 300% higher ROI than those without.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid with Google Ads  

<Callout type="warning"> Running Google Ads without conversion tracking is like driving blindfolded. You have no idea which keywords, ads, or audiences are driving real customers. Set up call tracking and form submission tracking before spending a single dollar.</Callout>  

Other critical errors to avoid:  
- **Keyword Match Type Mistakes**: A local spa bidding on "massage" with broad match got 60% irrelevant clicks from people searching for "massage chair reviews." Switching to phrase match for "massage near [city]" improved relevance by 80%.  
- **Budget Caps**: A $10/day budget for a high-cost niche (e.g., "plumber NYC") is a waste — you’ll get 2–3 clicks/month at best.  
- **Ignoring Ad Scheduling**: A breakfast café running ads 24/7 wasted 70% of its budget on nighttime clicks. Restricting ads to 7 AM–11 AM cut costs by 50%.

## Frequently Asked Questions  
### Is Google Ads worth it for small businesses?  
Yes — if you target hyper-local keywords and track conversions. A 2026 study of 1,000 campaigns found that businesses with budgets over $200/month saw a 2.8x ROI on average.  

### Is $10 a day enough for Google Ads?  
Only for ultra-low-competition niches. A local flower shop targeting "birthday flowers [city]" with a $10/day budget got 15 sales/month at $4 per click. But for most businesses, this is too low to generate meaningful data.  

### Is $100 enough for Google Ads?  
It depends on your niche. A local bakery in a mid-sized city could run a $100/month campaign targeting "birthday cakes near me" and get 20+ orders — but a law firm would need at least $500/month to see results in competitive markets.  

### Is $20 a day good for Google Ads?  
$20/day is a reasonable starting point for low-competition keywords. A boutique targeting "yoga pants [city]" with a $20/day budget achieved 30+ sales/month at $2.50 per click.  

### How much should a small business spend on Google Ads?  
Start with 8–12% of your monthly revenue. For a $10,000/month revenue business, that’s $800–$1,200 for testing. Adjust based on ROI: if you’re getting $5 for every $1 spent, increase the budget.  

### How do I set up Google Ads for my small business?  
1. Create a Google Ads account and set up conversion tracking (call, form, or appointment).  
2. Use Google Keyword Planner to find low-competition, high-intent keywords (e.g., "best coffee near me").  
3. Build a campaign with a $50–$100/day budget, testing 3–5 ad variations.  
4. Monitor weekly performance and pause underperforming keywords.  

For a full walkthrough, see our [2026 Google Ads setup guide](/blog/how-to-set-up-google-ads-for-your-small-business-in-2026-step-by-step-with-screenshots).  

### Why did Google Ads charge me $500?  
You likely forgot to set a daily budget cap. Google Ads charges per click, and without a limit, your budget can deplete quickly. To fix this:  
1. Go to your campaign settings.  
2. Set a **daily budget** (e.g., $50).  
3. Use **smart bidding** to automatically adjust for conversions.
