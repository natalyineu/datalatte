**Choosing the Right Ad Format**

Wolt offers various ad formats, including sponsored listings, banner ads, and even video ads. But which one is right for your restaurant? Here's a breakdown of the most popular options:

- **Sponsored Listings**: These ads appear at the top of search results and can be highly effective for local restaurants. They're also relatively affordable, with a cost-per-click (CPC) of around $0.50-$1.50.
- **Banner Ads**: These ads are displayed on the Wolt homepage and can be a great way to reach a wider audience. However, they can be more expensive than sponsored listings, with a CPC of around $1-$3.
- **Video Ads**: These ads are a great way to showcase your restaurant's atmosphere and cuisine. They can be more expensive than other ad formats, but can also drive more sales.

**Optimizing Your Ad Strategy**

Once you've chosen the right ad format, it's time to optimize your strategy. Here are some tips to get you started:

- **Target Your Audience**: Make sure you're targeting the right audience with your ads. Use Wolt's targeting options to reach customers who are most likely to be interested in your restaurant.
- **Use High-Quality Imagery**: Use high-quality images and videos to showcase your restaurant's food and atmosphere.
- **Offer Promotions**: Offer promotions and discounts to incentivize customers to try your restaurant.
- **Monitor Your Results**: Keep a close eye on your ad results and adjust your strategy as needed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much should I spend on Wolt ads as a small restaurant?**
Start with $300 per month. Run it for 30 days. Track cost per order and average order value. If your CPO is lower than your AOV after food cost, you're in a good place. If not, reduce radius, adjust your listing, or turn off the ad and fix the fundamentals first. I've seen restaurants succeed on $200 a month and fail on $1,000. The number is less important than the metric behind it.

**Q: Can I target only people within walking distance of my restaurant?**
Yes, but Wolt's minimum radius varies by market. In most US cities, you can set a 1-mile radius. For a walk-in coffee shop or a sandwich spot in a dense neighborhood like downtown Chicago or the West Village, that's enough. For a suburban pizzeria, 2–3 miles is more realistic. Test both and compare CPO. The closer the radius, the lower the delivery fee for customers, which improves conversion.

**Q: I already run Yelp and Google Ads. Do I really need Wolt ads too?**
Depends on where your customers actually order. If your Wolt order volume is zero or close to it, start with organic optimization (photos, menu, reviews) before spending money. If you already get 30+ organic orders per week, a small ad budget can amplify that. I've seen restaurants double their Wolt revenue just by adding a $200 sponsored listing on top of a well-optimized organic listing. But if your Yelp and Google ads aren't working, Wolt won't fix a broken business model.

**Q: How long does it take to see results from Wolt ads?**
You'll see impressions within hours of turning on the ad. Orders can take 48–72 hours to stabilize because Wolt's algorithm needs time to learn your audience. Don't judge a campaign on Day 1. But if after two weeks your CPO exceeds your AOV, kill the ad and revisit your listing, radius, or promo. I've waited three weeks on a campaign that never turned around. Don't do that.

**Q: What if my restaurant has low ratings? Can I still run ads?**
You can. You shouldn't. A sponsored ad sends people to your listing, where they see your 3.8-star rating and read a review about cold fries or late delivery. You waste your ad budget. Fix the rating first. Reply to every negative review. Offer replacements. Raise your average to 4.2 or higher. Then turn the ads back on. The ad spend will convert much better when your listing doesn't actively repel customers.

**Q: Is it better to advertise on Wolt or DoorDash (or Uber Eats)?**
That depends on your city. In the US, DoorDash has larger market share overall. But Wolt dominates in certain European markets and is growing fast in the US. I've seen local restaurants in cities like Austin and Portland get better results on Wolt because there's less competition from national chains. Uber Eats tends to be the most expensive for advertisers (higher CPC) and rewards larger spenders. If you're a single-location restaurant, Wolt's smaller advertiser pool can actually work in your favor — you get more visibility per dollar. Test both for 30 days, compare CPO and repeat rate, and put your money where the math says.
