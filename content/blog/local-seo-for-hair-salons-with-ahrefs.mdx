**Getting Started with Ahrefs for Hair Salon Local SEO**

To get the most out of Ahrefs for hair salon local SEO, you need to understand its core features and how to use them. Here are the essential steps to take:

### Step 1: Set up Your Ahrefs Account

*   Sign up for an Ahrefs account and choose the plan that suits your budget.
*   Connect your website to Ahrefs using the "Add new site" feature.

### Step 2: Conduct a Site Audit

*   Run a site audit to identify issues with your website's technical SEO.
*   Use Ahrefs' "Site Audit" tool to scan your website for errors and provide recommendations for improvement.

### Step 3: Analyze Your Competitors

*   Use Ahrefs' "Competitor Analysis" tool to analyze your competitors' websites and identify areas for improvement.
*   Analyze their backlink profiles, content, and social media presence to see what's working for them.

### Step 4: Develop a Content Strategy

*   Use Ahrefs' "Content Explorer" tool to find content ideas and opportunities.
*   Develop a content strategy that targets your audience's needs and interests.

### Step 5: Optimize Your Website

*   Use Ahrefs' "Keyword Research" tool to identify relevant keywords and phrases.
*   Optimize your website's on-page elements (title tags, meta descriptions, header tags) for better search engine rankings.

### Step 6: Build High-Quality Backlinks

*   Use Ahrefs' "Backlink Checker" tool to identify potential link opportunities.
*   Reach out to other websites and offer them high-quality content in exchange for a backlink.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I really need Ahrefs, or can I get by with free tools?**

You can stumble with free tools, but you’ll spend twice as long and still miss things. Ahrefs’ site audit catches technical issues that Google Search Console doesn’t flag (like missing meta descriptions or broken internal links). Its keyword explorer shows actual search volumes and click-through rates — free tools like Google Keyword Planner give you ranges like “100–1k” which are almost useless for a salon. If you’re serious about local SEO and have a budget, Ahrefs pays for itself if you follow the steps above. If you’re under $2,000/month revenue, start with GMB and free tools, then upgrade.

**Q: Can’t I just pay someone on Fiverr to do all this for $50?**

You can. And you’ll get what you pay for. I’ve audited dozens of salons who hired cheap freelancers. The freelancers built spammy backlinks or stuffed keywords into invisible text. One salon in Austin had their site penalized and lost all GMB visibility for six weeks. It cost them $4,500 in lost bookings to fix. The $50 “expert” disappeared. If you’re going to hire someone, check their case studies and ask for a specific breakdown of work for a local business. A generic “I do SEO” pitch from a freelancer who’s never worked on a hair salon is a red flag.

**Q: How long until I see results from Ahrefs-driven SEO?**

It depends on where you start. If your website is clean and you have a verified GMB, expect to see ranking movements within 4–8 weeks for low-competition keywords (like a specific service in a mid-sized city). For competitive terms like “hair salon Austin,” it could take 4–6 months of consistent work. I had a Chicago salon that went from no organic traffic to 120 visitors a month from SEO in 8 weeks — but they were already in a niche (men’s grooming) with low competition. The average salon I work with sees a 15–25% increase in organic bookings within three months.

**Q: Will Ahrefs help me get more reviews on Google or Yelp?**

Indirectly, yes. Ahrefs can show you which competitors are getting the most review traffic (their GMB profile ranking high for “hair salon with reviews” type searches). But the tool can’t write review requests for you. Use Ahrefs to identify review-rich competitors, then create a process: follow-up texts after appointments with a direct link to your GMB review page. I have a client in Denver who added a QR code on their checkout iPad. Reviews went up 3x in two months.

**Q: My salon is mobile-only — I don’t have a full website. Does Ahrefs still work?**

Ahrefs works best on a proper website. If you only have a Booksy profile or a Facebook page, Ahrefs can still help you analyze competitors’ backlinks and keywords, but you won’t be able to run a site audit or track rankings for your own page. My advice: build a simple one-page website using Squarespace or Wix (cost: ~$20/month). Even that gives you a base to optimize with Ahrefs. I’ve seen a mobile-only salon in Nashville go from zero web presence to a single-page site with a booking button. Their GMB ranking improved because they had a real website to link to. Within two months, they added $1,800/month in online bookings.
