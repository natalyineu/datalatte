## What Salon Booking Software Does That Calendly Doesn't

Before the comparison, here's what you actually need:

- **Service menu with duration and pricing**: A haircut takes 45 min; a balayage with toner takes 3.5 hours. Software needs to block calendar time accurately.
- **Multiple staff calendars**: Each stylist has their own schedule, prices, and services.
- **Deposit and payment collection**: Protect your revenue from no-shows.
- **Automated reminders**: Text/email 24 and 2 hours before appointments.
- **Rebooking prompts**: "Ready to book your next appointment?" triggered automatically.
- **Client notes and history**: Know that this client is allergic to PPD dye before you apply color.
- **POS integration**: Check-out with product sales, tips, and service payments in one flow.

Calendly does none of these things natively.

## The 7 Best Salon Booking Alternatives to Calendly

### 1. Vagaro — Best Overall for Salons & Spas

**Best for**: Mid-size salons, spas, barbershops with 2–6 providers

Vagaro is the most feature-complete booking software for beauty businesses. It covers everything from online booking to POS to payroll in one platform.

**Key features**:
- Online booking with a customizable booking page
- Multi-staff calendar management
- Deposit collection and payment processing
- Automated SMS/email reminders
- Built-in reviews and marketing tools
- Loyalty program and gift cards
- Inventory management for retail
- Comprehensive reporting

**Pricing**: Starts at $30/month (1 provider); $10 per additional provider up to 5; flat $90/month for 5+ providers. Credit card processing: 2.75% flat rate.

**The catch**: Vagaro has a lot of features, which means a steeper learning curve than simpler options. Setup takes 2–4 hours. Worth it for established salons.

### 2. Fresha — Best Free Option

**Best for**: Solo stylists, new salons, budget-conscious businesses

Fresha is genuinely free for the core booking software — no monthly subscription. They make money on payment processing and premium marketplace placement.

**Key features**:
- Online booking (free forever)
- Client app for easy rebooking
- Automated reminders (free)
- Deposit collection (small fee)
- Basic reporting
- Fresha Marketplace — clients can discover and book your salon

**Pricing**: Software is free. Payment processing: 2.19% + 20¢ per transaction. Deposit transactions: 2.19% + 20¢. Premium marketplace placement: optional paid add-on.

**The advantage**: Zero monthly cost until you start taking payments. For a solo stylist just getting started, it's an excellent entry point.

**The limitation**: Less comprehensive than Vagaro for multi-provider operations or complex service menus.

### 3. Square Appointments — Best for Salons Using Square POS

**Best for**: Salons already using Square for payments and POS

If your salon uses Square for payments, Square Appointments integrates seamlessly — client data, payments, inventory, and reporting all live in one Square ecosystem.

**Key features**:
- Syncs with Square POS natively
- Online booking page
- Multi-staff scheduling
- Automated reminders
- Basic client profiles
- Gift cards and loyalty (via Square add-ons)

**Pricing**: Free for solo providers. $29/month for 2–5 staff; $69/month for 6–10 staff. Payment processing: Square's standard 2.6% + 10¢ (card present).

**The limitation**: Weaker client retention and marketing features than Vagaro. Not the right choice if you're not already on Square.

### 4. Booksy — Best for Barbershops and Stylists With Strong Social Presence

**Best for**: Barbers, mobile stylists, social-media-driven businesses

Booksy is built around the "book via Instagram/Facebook" use case and is particularly popular among barbers and stylists who drive traffic from social media.

**Key features**:
- "Book Now" button integration for Instagram/Facebook
- Online booking page
- Automated SMS reminders
- Client portfolio (stylist can show work to new clients)
- Multiple provider management
- Deposits and payments

**Pricing**: $29.99/month (solo); additional providers $10/month each. No per-transaction fees for standard bookings.

**The advantage**: Best in class for turning Instagram followers into bookings. If your growth strategy is social media, Booksy's platform is designed for exactly that.

### 5. GlossGenius — Best for Premium/Boutique Salons

**Best for**: High-end salons, independent stylists, boutique spas

GlossGenius positions itself as the premium option — beautiful branded booking pages, white-glove experience, and strong marketing automation.

**Key features**:
- Beautifully designed customizable booking pages
- Client profiles with photo history
- Smart rebooking reminders
- Marketing automation (birthday offers, lapsed client reactivation)
- Tip prompts built into checkout
- Card on file for no-show protection

**Pricing**: $24/month for basic; $48/month for Standard (recommended). One flat processing fee: 2.6% per transaction.

**The advantage**: The most beautiful software on the list. If brand perception matters to your positioning, GlossGenius's interface reflects that — both for you and for clients seeing your booking page.

### 6. Mindbody — Best for Spas With Classes or Multi-Service Offerings

**Best for**: Day spas, wellness centers, businesses combining appointments and classes

Mindbody is the dominant platform for businesses that blend service appointments with group classes — think a spa that also offers yoga, meditation, or wellness workshops.

**Key features**:
- Appointment + class booking in one system
- Membership management
- Comprehensive client management
- Marketing suite (email, push notifications)
- Staff performance reporting
- App for clients

**Pricing**: Plans start around $139/month. Business plan (most commonly recommended): $219/month. Enterprise pricing for multi-location.

**The limitation**: Expensive relative to pure salon booking tools. Overkill for a straightforward hair salon. Right tool for a full-service wellness center.

### 7. Acuity Scheduling — Closest to Calendly's Style, But With Payments

**Best for**: Stylists who want Calendly-like simplicity but with payment collection

Acuity is a Squarespace product that feels the most like Calendly but adds proper payment collection, forms, and multiple service types.

**Key features**:
- Simple booking page setup (easier than Vagaro)
- Custom intake forms
- Payment collection (Stripe or Square integration)
- Automated reminders
- Multiple staff calendars
- Packages and gift certificates

**Pricing**: $20/month (Growing, 1 calendar); $34/month (Powerhouse, 6 calendars). Payment processing through Stripe: 2.9% + 30¢.

**The advantage**: If you're making the switch from Calendly and want the least learning curve, Acuity feels familiar. It's missing the salon-specific features of Vagaro or GlossGenius but is significantly more capable than Calendly for service businesses.

## Which One Should You Choose?

**Solo stylist just starting out**: Fresha (free to start) or GlossGenius ($24/month for beautiful presentation).

**Small salon, 2–4 stylists**: Vagaro (most complete feature set at $50–$70/month) or Booksy (if social media is your primary traffic source).

**Salon already on Square**: Square Appointments (ecosystem simplicity beats feature differences).

**High-end positioning**: GlossGenius (the booking experience matches the salon brand).

**Spa with classes or memberships**: Mindbody (purpose-built for the complexity).

**Closest Calendly replacement with least friction**: Acuity Scheduling.

## Migration From Calendly: What to Expect

If you're switching from Calendly to any of the above:

1. **Export your client list from Calendly** (Settings → Exports → Client data CSV)
2. **Import to your new platform** — all of the above accept CSV client imports
3. **Set up your services** with accurate durations and prices before going live
4. **Test a booking** yourself before announcing it to clients
5. **Send a migration email** to existing clients with your new booking link — most platforms give you a branded URL (like yoursalon.vagaro.com or yoursalon.glossgenius.com)
6. **Set up the "Book Now" button** on Instagram and Facebook with the new link

The full migration takes 3–5 hours for most salons.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I keep using Calendly for non-client scheduling (team meetings, supplier calls) while using salon software for client bookings?**

Yes — there's no conflict. Many salon owners use specialized booking software for client appointments and keep Calendly (or Google Calendar directly) for internal scheduling. They're separate systems serving separate purposes.

**Q: Do any of these integrate with Instagram's booking button?**

Vagaro, Fresha, Booksy, Square Appointments, and GlossGenius all integrate with Instagram's native booking button. This lets clients see your Instagram page and tap "Book" to go directly to your scheduling page.

**Q: What's the difference between Fresha free and paid options?**

Fresha's core booking software — scheduling, client management, reminders — is permanently free. They charge for payment processing (2.19% per transaction) and for premium marketplace placement (to appear higher in Fresha's consumer-facing discovery app). You can run Fresha's core booking system indefinitely at zero cost.
