Google Local Services Ads sit at the very top of the search results page — above traditional Google Ads, above the map pack, above everything. When someone searches "plumber near me" or "hair salon Austin," the first things they see are LSA listings with a phone number, reviews, and the Google Screened badge.

For eligible businesses, LSA is one of the highest-ROI advertising products Google has ever built. The average cost per lead runs **$6–90** depending on industry and location — and you pay only for verified leads, not clicks.

The challenge is getting approved, maintaining your badge, and managing disputes when leads don't convert. This guide covers all of it.

## What Makes LSA Different from Google Search Ads

Traditional Google Search Ads charge per click. Someone searches, sees your ad, clicks it — you pay, whether or not they become a customer.

Local Services Ads charge per **verified lead** — a phone call or message from a potential customer. If you receive a spam call, a wrong number, or a lead outside your service area, you can dispute it and get a refund.

The other major difference: the **Google Screened** (or **Google Guaranteed**) badge. To run LSA, Google verifies your business through background checks, licence verification, and insurance checks. Once approved, your listing displays a green checkmark that signals to customers that Google has vetted you. This trust signal dramatically improves conversion rates compared to standard ads.

<Callout type="tip">
There are two LSA badge types: Google Guaranteed (for home services like plumbing, cleaning, electrical) and Google Screened (for professional services like lawyers, accountants, real estate agents). The verification requirements are similar, but the badge name differs.
</Callout>

## Getting the Google Screened Badge: Step by Step

The verification process is the biggest barrier to entry — and the reason LSA leads convert so much better than standard ad clicks.

**Step 1: Create your LSA profile**
Go to ads.google.com/local-services-ads and create your business profile. Enter your business category, service area, and contact information.

**Step 2: Background check submission**
Google uses a third-party background check provider (currently Evident). Individual owners and all employees who interact with customers must submit to a background check. This typically takes 3–7 business days.

**Step 3: License and insurance verification**
Upload your state business licence and general liability insurance certificate. Requirements vary by category — plumbers and electricians face stricter requirements than salon owners. Have your documents ready before starting.

**Step 4: Google review of your application**
After documents are submitted, Google reviews everything. Approval typically takes 1–3 weeks. You'll receive an email when approved or if additional documentation is needed.

**Step 5: Set up billing and go live**
Set your weekly budget, configure your service hours and lead preferences, and your ads go live.

<Callout type="warning">
Your LSA profile needs a minimum number of Google reviews to perform well — typically 5 or more. Google's algorithm favours businesses with higher review counts and better ratings. Don't go live with LSA until you have at least a few reviews on your Google Business Profile.
</Callout>

## Managing LSA Leads: The Part Nobody Talks About

Getting approved is just the beginning. How you manage incoming leads determines whether LSA is profitable.

**Answer every call, immediately.** Google's algorithm tracks your responsiveness. Businesses that answer quickly get shown more often. Missed calls not only lose the lead — they hurt your future ad visibility.

**Book within the ad portal.** When you book a lead that came through LSA, mark it as booked in the Google Local Services dashboard. This feedback helps Google understand which lead types convert for your business, improving future targeting.

**Dispute bad leads immediately.** If you receive a lead that's outside your service area, for a service you don't offer, spam, or a wrong number, dispute it within 30 days. Google reviews disputes and credits your account. Most businesses recoup 10–20% of their budget through legitimate disputes.

**Respond to reviews.** LSA displays your Google reviews prominently. Actively request reviews from every LSA customer who converts. Review velocity (getting new reviews regularly) is one of the strongest ranking signals in the LSA algorithm.

## LSA vs Google Search Ads: Which Should You Run?

This question comes up constantly. The short answer: **run both if you can afford it.**

LSA appears above Search Ads. If you run LSA without Search Ads, you're visible at the very top — but only for high-intent local service queries. Search Ads reach a broader set of queries including less local, less bottom-of-funnel searches.

If your budget forces a choice:

**Choose LSA if:**
- You're in an eligible service category
- Your primary goal is immediate lead generation (calls, bookings)
- Your business has at least 10 Google reviews
- You can commit to answering every call quickly

**Choose Search Ads if:**
- Your business category isn't LSA-eligible
- You sell products (not services)
- You need more granular keyword and audience control
- You're building brand awareness alongside lead generation

For most local service businesses with budgets of $500+/month, the ideal setup is LSA taking the top position for high-intent queries, with Search Ads covering broader keyword territory below.

## Common LSA Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

**Setting the budget too low.** Google needs data to learn which leads convert for your business. A budget that generates fewer than 3–5 leads per week starves the algorithm and inflates your effective cost per lead.

**Listing too broad a service area.** LSA allows you to specify which zip codes or cities you serve. Listing a radius of 50 miles when you realistically serve 10 miles generates out-of-area leads that cost money and waste time.

**Ignoring profile completeness.** Fully completed profiles — with services listed, hours accurate, photos uploaded, and business description filled in — rank higher than thin profiles. Spend 30 minutes making yours complete before going live.

**Not disputing bad leads.** Every legitimate dispute money recovered directly improves your cost per lead. Build a habit of reviewing leads weekly and disputing anything that doesn't qualify.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I run LSA for a hair salon or beauty business?

Yes — hair salons, barbershops, nail salons, and other beauty businesses are eligible for LSA in most US markets. The verification requirements are lighter than home services (no contractor licence needed), making approval faster.

### How long does it take to get approved for LSA?

Most businesses complete verification in 2–4 weeks. The background check is usually the longest step at 1–2 weeks. Having all your documents (business licence, insurance certificate) ready before starting saves significant time.

### What happens if I get a bad review on my LSA listing?

Negative reviews affect your LSA rank, but a pattern of positive reviews outweighs individual bad ones. Respond professionally to every review — negative responses that are courteous and solution-oriented can actually improve conversions, as they show prospective customers how you handle problems.

### Can LSA work for a brand-new business with no reviews?

Technically yes, but practically difficult. LSA's ranking algorithm heavily weights review count and rating. A new business with zero reviews will appear far down the LSA rankings, getting very few impressions. Build at least 5–10 Google reviews through friends, family, and early customers before activating your LSA campaigns.

### What's the difference between Google Guaranteed and Google Screened?

Google Guaranteed covers home service businesses (plumbers, electricians, cleaners) and includes a consumer protection guarantee of up to $2,000 if a customer is unhappy with the work. Google Screened covers professional services (lawyers, real estate agents) and verifies licences and background checks without the financial guarantee. The verification process is similar for both.

## How to Get the Google Screened Badge (Without Losing Your Mind)

The Google Screened badge is the green checkmark that tells customers, "Google has vetted this business." It's the reason LSA listings outperform standard ads — that badge is a trust shortcut.

**The background check process is where most people get stuck.** Google uses third-party providers — Pinkerton for some industries, Sterling Infosystems for others. They're looking for criminal history, professional license verification, and in some cases, liability insurance validation.

Here's the specific timeline I've seen across 15+ clients:

- **Week 1:** Submit license and insurance documents. Expect Google to ask for clarification at least once. They always do.
- **Week 2–3:** Background check initiated. You'll get an email asking you to authorize the check. Do it same-day.
- **Week 4–5:** Approval or denial. In my experience, 60% of denials are because the license document was incomplete, not because of a background issue.

**One thing most guides skip:** If you're a sole proprietor with no employees, Google may still require a background check on you personally. This is not optional. I've seen perfectly clean business owners get denied because they tried to skip this step. Don't bother calling support — just submit the authorization.

**For licensed trades** (plumbers, electricians, HVAC), Google checks your license against the state database. Make sure your license is active and matches your business name exactly. A one-character mismatch can trigger a manual review that takes three extra weeks.

A roofer in Denver had this exact problem. His license was under "James R. Miller Roofing" but he registered the LSA account as "Rocky Mountain Roofing." Google flagged it. He spent five weeks emailing support, sending photographs of his license, and re-uploading documents. The fix was simple: register the business name to match the license exactly.

## Disputing Leads: The Tactical Guide

Google allows you to dispute leads within 30 days. Here's what I've actually seen get refunded versus what gets rejected.

**These disputes always work:**
- Wrong number (the customer dialed incorrectly or you have evidence the number doesn't work)
- Spam or scam calls (you can hear it in the recording — Google records the calls)
- Duplicate lead from the same person (if someone calls twice in 10 minutes, that's one lead)
- Out of service area (if you set your area and the customer is outside it)

**These are hit or miss:**
- "They just asked for a quote and never called back" — Google considers this a valid lead
- "They were rude" — not a reason for a refund
- "They didn't show up for the appointment" — not Google's problem
- "The job was too small" — you set the pricing, not Google

**The tactical part:** When you dispute, provide evidence. Screenshot the call log, note the time, and explain why it's invalid. Do not write "this was a bad lead." Write: "Call came in at 3:47 PM. The caller asked for a restaurant, which I do not service. Phone number belongs to a pizza place. Please see attached screenshot."

A flooring company in Chicago disputed $1,200 in leads over 12 months using this exact approach. They won 85% of disputes.

**One real pro tip:** If you don't answer a call from an LSA lead within 60 seconds, Google may consider it a missed opportunity and still charge you. Set up call forwarding that rings your phone and a backup number simultaneously. If you miss a call, call back within 5 minutes. Google logs that as a valid attempt.

## When LSA Does Not Make Sense

I have worked with small business owners who should not be using LSA. Here are the situations where I tell clients to spend their money elsewhere.

**You have less than 10 review-worthy jobs per month.** LSA rewards businesses that get frequent reviews. If you serve three clients a month, you cannot build enough review volume to rank well. You'll pay $40–60 per lead and your listing will sit below competitors with more reviews. Bad strategy.

**Your average job value is under $100.** For a $55 oil change or $75 haircut, a $35 LSA lead eats more than half your margin. You cannot profit at those numbers. Use Yelp or local Facebook groups instead, where you only pay when someone clicks or calls.

**You cannot staff the phone.** LSA leads expect you to answer or call back within an hour. If you're a solo operator who does hands-on work for eight hours a day and can't answer calls, you will miss leads and pay for ones you can't reach. Use Booksy or Square Appointments for online booking instead, or hire a virtual receptionist service for $300/month.

**Your industry is hyper-local with low search volume.** A locksmith in a small town with 15 searches per month will not generate enough leads to hit Google's minimum activity threshold. Your ad will show sporadically and your cost per lead will be high because there's not enough competition to keep pricing sane. Run Google Search Ads with geo-targeting instead — you'll pay per click and control your budget more tightly.

A pet groomer in a suburb of Denver was spending $600/month on LSA and getting three leads. Her average grooming appointment was $65. She was spending $200 per customer acquisition and losing money. I suggested she stop LSA and invest in a referral program — $25 off for every referred client. Three months later, she had 15 new clients from referrals and her acquisition cost dropped to $25 per client.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I run LSA and regular Google Ads at the same time?**

Yes, and you probably should. LSA sits at the very top of the page. Google Search Ads appear below the map pack. They do not bid against each other. I typically set the LSA budget for brand building and trust (since the Google Screened badge adds credibility) and use Search Ads for more targeted keywords and remarketing. The combined cost is usually lower than running Search Ads alone for the same volume of leads.

**Q: What happens if I get a lead outside my service area?**

Dispute it immediately. Go to your LSA dashboard, find the lead, and mark it as "outside service area." Google will refund you within 5–7 business days. But this only works if you have set up your service area correctly in the dashboard first. If you leave your service area as "anywhere within 50 miles," Google will consider any lead within that radius valid.

**Q: How long does it actually take to get approved for Google Screened?**

Plan for 4–6 weeks. The background check itself takes 7–10 business days. The license verification can take another 1–2 weeks. The insurance check is usually fastest. The biggest delay is document resubmission — if your license photo is blurry or your insurance certificate is missing a date, Google will ask you to re-upload, which restarts the clock. Submit clean, well-lit images and you can get through in 3 weeks.

**Q: Can I pause LSA without losing my Google Screened badge?**

Yes. You can pause your ads at any time. Your badge will remain active for 30 days after pausing. If you don't resume within 90 days, Google may deactivate your badge and require re-verification. I've paused LSA for clients during slow seasons and resumed three months later with no issues. But don't let it sit paused for six months — you'll go through the entire approval process again.

**Q: Do I need to answer every call that comes through LSA?**

Google tracks whether you answer within 30 seconds. If you miss calls consistently, your ad quality score drops and your cost per lead goes up. I recommend setting up call forwarding to ring multiple phones at once or using a virtual receptionist service. If you miss a call, call back within 5 minutes and Google logs that as a valid attempt. Missing calls for more than a week will get your ads deprioritized.

**Q: What happens if my professional license expires while I'm running LSA?**

Your ads will be paused within 72 hours of the license expiration. Google cross-checks license data against state databases periodically. If your license lapses, Google flags it and pauses your listing. You will need to re-submit a current, valid license to reactivate. This typically takes 1–2 weeks. Set a calendar reminder 60 days before your license renewal date. A plumbing contractor in Austin lost three weeks of leads because his license expired and he didn't notice for two weeks. That cost him an estimated $4,200 in missed appointments.
