## What is an AI Booking System?

An AI booking system uses machine learning to automate scheduling tasks, such as setting appointments, sending reminders, and handling cancellations. These systems can integrate with your existing calendar and CRM, making it easy to manage bookings and customers. For example, a fitness studio in New York City implemented an AI booking system and saw a 25% increase in bookings within the first month.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: My business takes walk-ins. Will an AI booking system interfere with that?**

Walk-ins shouldn't disappear. But they should become *logged* walk-ins. I've seen coffee shops and pet groomers lose track of walk-in customers and never build a relationship with them. The system handles this well if you set up a "walk-in" button at the counter. The staff member taps it, enters a phone number and the service, and the system starts building a profile for that client. A bagel shop in NYC did this and within six months had 800 walk-in customers with purchase histories tied to phone numbers. They ran a birthday discount campaign and generated $3,200 in additional sales from a single text blast. Walk-ins stay walk-ins. But now you know who they are.

**Q: Do I need to train my staff, and how long does that take?**

You need to train them. Plan on about 90 minutes of hands-on time. I've seen staff at a hair salon in Portland fight a new system for three weeks because nobody showed them how to check a client in. The owner thought they'd figure it out. They didn't. They went back to paper schedules. Set aside a slow Tuesday morning, walk everyone through check-in, cancelations, rescheduling, and the refund process. Then keep a cheat sheet next to the computer for a month. After that they'll know it cold. One-time training is cheaper than 18 months of mistakes.

**Q: What happens if the internet goes down?**

Paper backup. It's not elegant, but it's reliable. A yoga studio in Austin lost internet for half a day during a storm. Their booking system locked up completely. They had 60 students show up for 5 classes and no way to check them in. They ended up letting everyone in for free because they couldn't verify who had paid. Total revenue loss that day: $2,400. Print a small stack of appointment forms. Keep a pen. When the system goes down, write down names and numbers. Enter them when it comes back up. Takes 10 seconds per appointment.

**Q: Will the system work if I only have an Instagram link and no website?**

Yes, but you'll lose some functionality. Most booking systems give you a dedicated booking page that you can link from Instagram, TikTok, or even your Google Business Profile. A food truck in Denver operates entirely off an Instagram bio link. Their booking system handles order-ahead pickup times through that single link. No website required. Just don't expect features like embedded booking on a site you don't have. The link is enough.

**Q: How much should I expect to pay per month?**

Between $30 and $120/month is the reasonable range for a single-location small business. I've seen salons pay $300/month for features they never used. I've also seen a coffee shop in Cleveland pay $15/month for a tool that did 80% of what they needed. Start with the free trial. Use it for two weeks with actual clients — not just clicking around in demo mode. If it works for real transactions, pay for it. If it feels like you're fighting the interface, find another tool. The cost of the wrong system isn't the subscription. It's the time you waste.

**Q: Can I charge a deposit or a cancellation fee?**

Yes, and you should. But test the number. A pet groomer in Phoenix started with a 50% deposit requirement and saw a 25% drop in booking completions. Dropped it to 25% and bookings recovered while no-shows still dropped by 40%. Start lower than you think you need. You can always raise it later. I've seen too many small businesses scare off new clients with aggressive deposit policies before they've built any trust.

**Q: What if a client books online but wants to pay cash in the studio?**

Set your booking system to hold the slot without processing payment. The system sends the confirmation and reminder. Your staff marks the booking as "paid in studio" when the client arrives. A massage studio in Austin does this for about 20% of their clients. The process takes the front desk person about 8 seconds. Some booking systems let you set a "payment method" per client profile — cash clients automatically bypass the payment screen. Look for that toggle.
