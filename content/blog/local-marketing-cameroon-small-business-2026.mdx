Cameroon is Central Africa's most economically diverse country — 28 million people with extraordinary cultural diversity (over 250 ethnic groups), officially bilingual in French and English, and often described as "Africa in miniature" for its geographic variety from Sahel in the north to rainforest in the south. Douala is the commercial capital and largest city, dominating trade and business; Yaoundé is the administrative and political capital.

Cameroon uses the CFA Franc (XAF — Central African CFA Franc, shared with five other CEMAC countries), pegged to the Euro. Mobile money is transforming commerce — Orange Money and MTN MoMo are the dominant platforms, with millions of Cameroonians conducting all financial transactions through their phones.

## Cameroon's Digital Platform Landscape

| Platform | Active Users (Cameroon) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook | 6M (21%) | Dominant social platform |
| WhatsApp | 8M (29%) | Primary business messaging |
| YouTube | 7M (25%) | High consumption |
| TikTok | 5M (18%) | Very fast growing |
| Instagram | 3M (11%) | Growing urban youth |
| Google | 90%+ search share | Dominant |

**WhatsApp is business infrastructure**: Every Cameroonian business communicates via WhatsApp. WhatsApp Business, WhatsApp Status promotions, and WhatsApp group marketing are the standard. Orders, payments (via mobile money), and customer service all happen on WhatsApp.

**Facebook for community and discovery**: Facebook groups by city and neighbourhood are where Cameroonians discover local businesses. Douala Facebook groups and Yaoundé community pages are essential for local business visibility.

**Mobile money is king**: Orange Money and MTN Mobile Money (MoMo) have transformed Cameroonian commerce — the majority of business payments are now mobile money. Accept both Orange Money and MTN MoMo for any Cameroonian business.

## Google Ads in Cameroon

| Industry | Avg CPC (XAF) | Approx. USD | Avg CVR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hair & Beauty | XAF 150-600 | $0.25-$1.00 | 3.0% |
| Cafés & Restaurants | XAF 100-400 | $0.17-$0.67 | 2.1% |
| Fitness & Gyms | XAF 200-800 | $0.33-$1.33 | 3.2% |

*1 USD ≈ XAF 600 (CFA Franc XAF; 1 EUR = 655.96 XAF fixed)*

Competition for Google Ads in Cameroon is very low. Most businesses use Facebook and WhatsApp organically. Google Ads achieves strong coverage at minimal cost.

## Three Cameroonian Business Examples

### 💇 Salon de Coiffure, Douala Akwa

**Strategy**: Facebook business page with beauty content in French, WhatsApp Business for appointments (Orange Money for payment), Instagram for transformation photos, Google Maps listing.

**Budget**: XAF 250,000/month (≈$417): XAF 100,000 Facebook Ads, XAF 90,000 Google Ads, XAF 60,000 content.

### 🍽️ Restaurant, Yaoundé Bastos

**Strategy**: Facebook for Yaoundé community groups, WhatsApp for reservations and orders, Instagram food photography, Google Maps, Orange Money for payment.

**Budget**: XAF 200,000/month (≈$333): XAF 85,000 Facebook Ads, XAF 75,000 Google Ads, XAF 40,000 content.

### 🏋️ Salle de Sport, Douala Bonapriso

**Strategy**: Facebook fitness content in French, Instagram for transformation content, Google Maps, WhatsApp for membership, MTN MoMo payments.

**Budget**: XAF 180,000/month (≈$300): XAF 75,000 Facebook Ads, XAF 65,000 Google Ads, XAF 40,000 content.

## Cameroonian Marketing Calendar

| Period | Opportunity |
|---|---|
| February 11 | Fête de la Jeunesse (Youth Day) — national |
| February 14 | Valentine's Day |
| May | Fête des Mères (Mother's Day, last Sunday May) |
| May 20 | Fête Nationale (National Day) — main national holiday |
| June | Fête des Pères (Father's Day) |
| Ramadan | Major for Muslim north |
| November | Ramadan/Muslim holidays (varies by year) |
| December | Noël + Réveillon |

**National Day (May 20)**: Cameroon's main national celebration — "Vingt Mai" is a major commercial and patriotic event. Businesses run national pride campaigns.

**Fête des Mères (Last Sunday May)**: Mother's Day is a major commercial event — restaurants fully booked, florists peak, beauty salons busy. Note: Cameroon's Mother's Day is the LAST Sunday of May, not the second Sunday.

## Language Notes

**French or English for Cameroon?**
French for all of Cameroon except the two English-speaking regions (Northwest and Southwest — the anglophone regions). Approximately 80% of commercial digital marketing is in French. Douala and Yaoundé marketing is French. Buea and Bamenda (anglophone cities) require English.
