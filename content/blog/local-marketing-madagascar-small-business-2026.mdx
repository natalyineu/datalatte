Madagascar is the world's fourth-largest island — 28 million people in the Indian Ocean off the southeastern coast of Africa. Antananarivo (Tana) is the highland capital; Toamasina (Tamatave) is the main port on the east coast; Nosy Be is the northern beach resort island famous for international tourism. Madagascar uses the Malagasy Ariary (MGA).

Madagascar is a French-speaking nation with a growing digital economy. Facebook dominates social media, and MVola (Telma mobile money) alongside Airtel Money are the primary payment systems. The biodiversity-rich island attracts significant ecotourism.

## Madagascar's Digital Platform Landscape

| Platform | Active Users (Madagascar) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook | 5M (18%) | Dominant social platform |
| WhatsApp | 6M (21%) | Primary business messaging |
| YouTube | 4.5M (16%) | Growing consumption |
| TikTok | 3.5M (13%) | Very fast growing |
| Instagram | 1.5M (5%) | Urban youth |
| Google | 88%+ search share | Dominant |

**Facebook is Madagascar's digital hub**: For the urban population with smartphone access, Facebook is the primary platform for business discovery, community groups, and commerce. Facebook Marketplace is widely used for buying and selling.

**MVola and Airtel Money dominant**: MVola (Telma) and Airtel Money are Madagascar's primary mobile money platforms. Accept both for any consumer business. Orange Money is a third option. Cash remains common in many areas.

**French + Malagasy**: French is the language of formal business and education; Malagasy (a unique Austronesian language brought by seafarers from Borneo centuries ago) is the language of home and community. Digital marketing in Antananarivo uses both — French for formal content, Malagasy for community connection.

## Google Ads in Madagascar

| Industry | Avg CPC (MGA) | Approx. USD | Avg CVR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hair & Beauty | MGA 1,000-4,000 | $0.22-$0.88 | 2.9% |
| Cafés & Restaurants | MGA 700-2,800 | $0.15-$0.62 | 2.0% |
| Tourism/Eco | MGA 2,000-8,000 | $0.44-$1.77 | 3.8% |

*1 USD ≈ MGA 4,500 (Malagasy Ariary — MGA)*

Malagasy CPCs are very low. Competition for Google Ads is minimal. Tourism keywords in French and English can reach international visitors.

### Antananarivo Area Keywords (French)

```
"salon de coiffure Antananarivo Analakely" (hair salon Tana centre)
"restaurant Tana Ivandry" (restaurant upscale suburb)
"salle de sport Antananarivo" (gym Antananarivo)
"écotourisme Madagascar" (ecotourism Madagascar — French)
"Nosy Be resort" (international English)
```

**Key Tana areas**: Analakely (downtown commercial market), Ivandry (upscale suburb), Ampefiloha (central), Tsimbazaza (zoo area/residential), Ankorondrano (commercial north). **Nosy Be**: Hell-Ville town, Ambatoloaka (beach resort).

## Three Malagasy Business Examples

### 💇 Salon de Coiffure, Antananarivo

**Strategy**: Facebook business page in French and Malagasy, WhatsApp for bookings and MVola payment, Instagram transformation photos, Google Maps.

**Budget**: MGA 900,000/month (≈$200): MGA 380,000 Facebook Ads, MGA 330,000 Google Ads, MGA 190,000 content.

### 🌿 Ecotourism, Madagascar (Nosy Be/Ranomafana)

**Strategy**: English and French Instagram showcasing lemurs, chameleons, and unique Madagascar biodiversity, Google Search Ads in English and French ("Madagascar ecotour", "lemur trekking"), TripAdvisor listing, WhatsApp for international bookings.

**Budget**: MGA 1,350,000/month (≈$300): MGA 580,000 Google Ads, MGA 500,000 Meta Ads, MGA 270,000 content.

### 🍽️ Restaurant, Antananarivo

**Strategy**: Facebook for Tana foodie groups in French, Instagram food photography, Google Maps, WhatsApp for reservations, MVola for payments.

**Budget**: MGA 720,000/month (≈$160): MGA 300,000 Facebook Ads, MGA 265,000 Google Ads, MGA 155,000 content.

## Malagasy Marketing Calendar

| Period | Opportunity |
|---|---|
| February 14 | Valentine's Day |
| March 29 | Martyr's Day (national holiday) |
| May | Fête des Mères (Mother's Day, last Sunday May) |
| June 26 | Fête de l'Indépendance (Independence Day) |
| August | Peak tourist season (dry season) |
| November 1 | All Saints' Day (Toussaint) — major Catholic holiday |
| December 25 | Noël |

**Independence Day (June 26)**: Madagascar's independence from France in 1960. Major national celebration with commercial activity.

**Dry season (April-November)**: Madagascar's dry season is prime time for ecotourism — lemur sightings, hiking, and beach resorts. Nosy Be and national park tourism peaks. Tour operators should run Google Ads from January to capture advance planners.
