Papua New Guinea (PNG) is the Pacific's largest country by population — 10 million people on the eastern half of the island of New Guinea, with extraordinary cultural diversity (over 800 languages spoken). Port Moresby is the capital; Lae is the industrial city; Mount Hagen is the highlands commercial hub. PNG has significant natural resources (gold, copper, LNG) but also stark inequality — a small urban middle class alongside large rural populations.

PNG uses the Papua New Guinea Kina (PGK). English, Tok Pisin (Pijin), and Hiri Motu are the three official languages — Tok Pisin is the true lingua franca spoken by virtually all Papua New Guineans as a second language, making it the language of commerce and media. Facebook has achieved extraordinary reach.

**Facebook is PNG's internet**: Facebook achieved extraordinary penetration in PNG — many Papua New Guineans use Facebook as their primary internet experience. Facebook Zero (free mobile data access to Facebook) was available through Digicel for years, cementing this dominance. Business pages, community groups, and Marketplace are all heavily used.

**Tok Pisin in social media**: While English is used in formal marketing, Tok Pisin resonates deeply with PNG audiences. Social media posts that mix English and Tok Pisin ("Bai mi kam bek gen — I'll be back!" or product descriptions in Tok Pisin) perform strongly.

**Cash and emerging mobile money**: BSP MiBank and Digicel's MiCash are PNG's mobile banking/payment platforms — growing but cash is still dominant in many transactions.

## Three PNG Business Examples

### 💇 Hair Salon, Port Moresby Boroko

**Strategy**: Facebook business page with beauty content in English and Tok Pisin, WhatsApp Business for appointments, Instagram transformation photos, Google Maps, MiCash for payments.

**Budget**: PGK 1,800/month (≈$489): PGK 750 Facebook Ads, PGK 680 Google Ads, PGK 370 content.

### 🍽️ Restaurant/Café, Port Moresby

**Strategy**: Facebook for POM community groups in English/Tok Pisin, Instagram food photography, Google Maps, WhatsApp for reservations.

**Budget**: PGK 1,400/month (≈$380): PGK 590 Facebook Ads, PGK 530 Google Ads, PGK 280 content.

### 🏔️ Cultural Tour/Trekking, PNG

**Strategy**: English Instagram showcasing Kokoda Track, highland festivals, and extraordinary PNG cultural diversity, Google Search Ads in English ("Kokoda Track trekking", "PNG cultural tour"), TripAdvisor listing, WhatsApp for international bookings.

**Budget**: PGK 2,200/month (≈$598): PGK 970 Google Ads, PGK 850 Meta Ads, PGK 380 content.

**Highlands Shows (August-September)**: Papua New Guinea's extraordinary cultural sing-sings — the Goroka Show (September, Eastern Highlands) and Mt. Hagen Show (August, Western Highlands) draw thousands of traditional warriors, dancers, and performers from across PNG. These are among the world's most spectacular cultural events. International tourists specifically travel for these shows. Tour operators should run Google Ads from May targeting these events.

**Independence Day (September 16)**: PNG's independence from Australia in 1975. Major national celebration.

## FAQ

**English, Tok Pisin, or local language for PNG marketing?**
English for formal/business marketing. Tok Pisin for social media and community content — it reaches everyone and feels culturally genuine. There are 800+ local languages in PNG; local language content is only relevant for very specific geographic communities.

**How does PNG's mining industry affect local marketing?**
PNG's large mining industry (gold, copper, LNG) creates a significant expat and highly-paid local worker community in certain areas (particularly Porgera, Ok Tedi, PNG LNG areas). This community has high disposable income and represents a premium market for services. Port Moresby businesses serving the mining/resource sector community target well-paid expats and local professionals.
