### Setting Up Your Pinterest Account

Before you start running ads, you need to make sure your Pinterest account is set up correctly. This includes:

* Creating a business account
* Uploading a profile picture and cover photo
* Writing a compelling bio that includes your business's name, location, and contact information
* Adding relevant keywords to your profile and pins

### Creating Engaging Pins

Your pins are the face of your business on Pinterest, so it's essential to create engaging and visually appealing content. This includes:

* High-quality images that showcase your services and products
* Using relevant keywords in your pin descriptions
* Including a clear call-to-action (CTA) in your pin description
* Pinning regularly to keep your audience engaged

### Targeting Your Audience

Pinterest's targeting options are robust, and you can target your ads based on a variety of factors, including:

* Location
* Age
* Interests
* Behaviors
* Job title

You can also use Pinterest's Lookalike audiences to target people who are similar to your existing customers.

### Optimizing Your Ads

Once you've set up your ads, it's essential to optimize them for the best possible performance. This includes:

* A/B testing different ad creative and targeting options
* Monitoring your ad spend and adjusting your budget as needed
* Using Pinterest's built-in analytics to track your ad performance

### Measuring Success

To measure the success of your Pinterest Ads, you'll want to track metrics such as:

* Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
* Conversion Rate
* Click-Through Rate (CTR)
* Cost Per Click (CPC)

You can use Pinterest's built-in analytics to track these metrics and adjust your ad strategy accordingly.

### How to Create a Seasonal Campaign Calendar for Your Salon or Spa

One of the biggest advantages of Pinterest is its forward-looking nature. Users plan their next haircut, facial, or manicure weeks in advance. For salons and spas, this means you can capitalize on seasonal trends to fill your appointment book before the rush hits.

Start by mapping out your year around key moments: Valentine’s Day (couples massages and gift cards), wedding season (bridal hair trials and makeup packages), summer (bright nail colors and deep conditioning treatments), and the holiday season (spa gift sets and blowout packages). For each season, create 10–15 pins that highlight these services. Use Pinterest Trends (available in your analytics dashboard) to see exactly when searches for "bridal updo ideas" or "winter skincare routine" peak in your region.

For example, a salon in Toronto might see a spike in "holiday party hairstyles" searches starting in mid-November. By launching a campaign for "Festive Blowout Packages" on November 1st, you can capture those users while they're still planning. Set your budget to ramp up two weeks before the peak search date, then taper off after. This approach helped one of our clients—a spa in Sydney—increase December bookings by 40% with just $800 in ad spend.

### Using Video Pins to Showcase Your Services

Static images are great, but video pins drive 2.5x more engagement on Pinterest. For salons and spas, video is your secret weapon. A 15-second clip of a stylist creating a balayage transformation or a therapist performing a hot stone massage tells a story that a photo simply can't.

Here's how to create video pins that convert: Keep them under 30 seconds (Pinterest's algorithm favors shorter videos for discovery). Shoot in vertical format (9:16 ratio) to fill the screen on mobile devices. Start with a "hook" in the first three seconds—like a before-and-after split or a close-up of a product in action. End with a clear CTA overlay, such as "Book Now" or "Get the Look."

Don't overthink production. A smartphone with good lighting and a tripod is enough. Film during your quiet hours—early morning or late afternoon—so you're not interrupting client experiences. For a hair salon, show a quick styling tutorial. For a spa, film a serene treatment room setup with soft music. Upload these as Idea Pins (Pinterest's native video format), which get bonus distribution in the home feed. One of our pet grooming clients in Austin saw a 55% increase in click-throughs after switching from static pins to short video clips of their fluffiest clients.

### Retargeting Users Who Almost Booked

Not every user who clicks your ad will book immediately. That's normal. But you can bring them back with retargeting campaigns. Pinterest allows you to create custom audiences based on actions users took on your website, like visiting your booking page or viewing a specific service.

Set up the Pinterest Tag on your website (it's a small piece of code that tracks visitor behavior). Then, create a retargeting campaign for users who visited your "Services" page but didn't complete a booking. Offer them a gentle incentive, like "10% off your first appointment" or "Free consultation with any haircut." Because these users already showed interest, your cost per conversion will likely drop by 20–30%.

For spas, consider retargeting users who clicked on a specific service pin—say, "Luxury Facial"—but didn't convert. Show them a new pin featuring a limited-time bundle (e.g., "Facial + Neck Massage for $89") to sweeten the deal. We've seen retargeting campaigns for salons in the UK achieve a 45% higher conversion rate than cold traffic campaigns, with an average cost per booking under $12.
