## Building an Email List
Before launching an email marketing campaign, small businesses need to build an email list. This can be done by creating a sign-up form on the company website, offering incentives for subscribers, and collecting email addresses from customers. It's essential to ensure that the email list is opt-in, meaning that subscribers have explicitly agreed to receive emails from the company. A well-built email list is crucial for the success of any email marketing campaign. For instance, a well-crafted email list can help you create targeted campaigns, such as the ones we discussed in our article [How to Market a Coffee Shop: The Complete 2026 Strategy Guide](/blog/how-to-market-a-coffee-shop-the-complete-2026-strategy-guide).

## Frequently Asked Questions
Here are some frequently asked questions about email marketing for small businesses:
* How does Starbucks use email marketing? Starbucks uses email marketing to promote their loyalty program, offer exclusive discounts, and provide personalized recommendations to their customers.
* How do I attract customers to my coffee shop? You can attract customers to your coffee shop by offering incentives such as discounts, free drinks, and loyalty program rewards.
* How to attract customers with email marketing? You can attract customers with email marketing by creating personalized and engaging email campaigns that offer value and incentives to your target audience.
* What are the 4ps of marketing for coffee shop? The 4ps of marketing for a coffee shop are product, price, promotion, and place.
* What is the 3-3-3 rule in marketing? The 3-3-3 rule in marketing refers to the idea that customers need to see your message at least three times, from at least three different sources, within a three-week period.
* What is the best email marketing for a small business? The best email marketing for a small business is one that is personalized, engaging, and provides value to the customer.
* What is the 30/30/50 rule for cold emails? The 30/30/50 rule for cold emails refers to the idea that 30% of the email should be introduction, 30% should be the value proposition, and 50% should be the call-to-action.
