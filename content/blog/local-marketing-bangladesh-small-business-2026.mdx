Bangladesh is South Asia's rising digital market. With 170 million people, rapidly growing internet penetration now exceeding 55%, and one of the world's largest garment and textile industries fuelling a growing middle class, Bangladesh presents significant digital marketing opportunities. Dhaka — one of the world's most densely populated megacities — is the commercial heart. Chittagong is Bangladesh's major port city and second economic hub. Sylhet has strong diaspora connections to the UK.

Bangladesh's digital ecosystem is shaped by mobile-first internet access (most users access the internet via smartphone), Facebook's dominant role as essentially synonymous with "the internet" for many Bangladeshi users, and bKash — the mobile financial service used by tens of millions of unbanked and underbanked Bangladeshis.

**Facebook is Bangladesh's internet**: For a very large segment of the Bangladeshi population, Facebook IS the internet. Facebook pages function as business websites; Facebook Messenger is the primary business communication channel; Facebook Groups are where local communities organise. Any business not on Facebook is essentially invisible.

**TikTok is exploding**: Bangladesh has one of Asia's fastest-growing TikTok user bases. TikTok content in Bengali performs extraordinarily well. For businesses targeting 15-35 year old Bangladeshis, TikTok organic content is a very high-reach, low-cost channel.

**Mobile-first everything**: Approximately 95% of Bangladeshi internet users access the web via mobile. All digital marketing — ads, landing pages, booking systems — must be mobile-optimised with fast load times (many users on 3G or slower connections).

**bKash dominates payments**: bKash is the country's leading mobile financial service with over 65 million registered accounts. Displaying your bKash number for payments is as essential as displaying a phone number. Nagad (government-backed) and Rocket (Dutch-Bangla Bank) are also widely used.

## Facebook for Bangladeshi Business

Facebook marketing in Bangladesh follows specific patterns:

1. **Facebook Page as your website**: Most Bangladeshi SMBs don't have a website — their Facebook Page IS their business presence. Ensure your Page has complete contact information, bKash number, and service descriptions.

2. **Facebook Messenger for all customer communication**: All inquiries, bookings, and orders happen via Messenger. Set up auto-replies and maintain fast response times — Bangladeshi customers expect very quick responses.

3. **Live video commerce**: Facebook Live is used extensively by Bangladeshi sellers to showcase products in real time with viewers commenting orders. Live selling sessions can drive immediate revenue.

4. **Local Facebook Groups**: Join and engage in relevant Dhaka/Chittagong neighbourhood groups. Groups like "Dhanmondi Community", "Gulshan Housewives Group", and area-specific groups have tens of thousands of members and very high engagement.

5. **Bengali language content**: Post in Bengali script. Roman-script Bengali (Banglish) is commonly used informally but formal marketing in Bengali script shows professionalism and local understanding.

## bKash and Mobile Payment Integration

For any Bangladesh business, accepting bKash is non-negotiable:

- Display your **bKash personal/merchant number** prominently on all materials
- bKash Merchant accounts allow QR code scanning for in-person payments
- Include bKash number in all Facebook posts selling products or services
- Nagad and Rocket are secondary mobile wallets — accept all three for maximum accessibility
- Traditional bank transfer (BRAC Bank, Dutch-Bangla Bank) used for larger transactions

## Three Bangladeshi Business Examples

### ☕ Café/Restaurant, Dhanmondi Dhaka

**Strategy**: Facebook Page as primary presence with daily Bengali content, Facebook Ads targeting Dhaka 25-45, TikTok food content in Bengali, Google Maps for tourist/expat search, bKash for delivery payment, Messenger for orders.

**Budget**: BDT 25,000/month (≈$227): BDT 10,000 Facebook Ads, BDT 8,000 TikTok Ads, BDT 7,000 content/photography.

### 💇 Beauty Parlour, Gulshan Dhaka

**Strategy**: Facebook Page with before/after content in Bengali, Instagram for Gulshan affluent market (English content acceptable), Google Search Ads, Messenger booking, bKash payments.

**Budget**: BDT 18,000/month (≈$164): BDT 7,000 Google/Facebook Ads, BDT 6,000 Meta Ads, BDT 5,000 content.

### 🐾 Pet Shop/Groomer, Banani Dhaka

**Strategy**: Facebook Page in Bengali and English (Banani has expat population), Instagram, Google Search Ads for "pet shop Dhaka", Messenger inquiries, bKash/card payments.

**Budget**: BDT 12,000/month (≈$109): BDT 5,000 Google Ads, BDT 4,000 Meta Ads, BDT 3,000 content.

## Bangladeshi Marketing Calendar

| Period | Opportunity |
|---|---|
| February | Ekushey February (International Mother Language Day) — national observance |
| March | Independence Day (March 26) |
| April | Pohela Boishakh (Bengali New Year, April 14) — biggest cultural celebration |
| May | Eid ul-Fitr (end of Ramadan) — biggest shopping season |
| July-August | Eid ul-Adha — second major Eid season |
| December | Victory Day (December 16) + year-end |

**Pohela Boishakh (Bengali New Year)**: The most important cultural celebration — April 14 marks the Bangladeshi New Year with festivals, traditional food, and cultural events. Businesses run Boishakh promotions; beauty salons are extremely busy with women getting traditional makeup and hair done.

**Eid seasons**: Both Eid ul-Fitr and Eid ul-Adha are massive commercial events — comparable to Christmas in Western markets. Clothing, beauty, and food businesses see enormous spikes. Start Eid marketing 3-4 weeks before each Eid date (which varies annually per Islamic calendar).

**Ramadan**: The month before Eid ul-Fitr — food businesses adjust hours and content for iftar/sehri, and gifting/food spending accelerates in the final week (Shab-e-Qadr and Eid preparation).

## FAQ

**Is Bengali or English better for Bangladesh digital marketing?**
Bengali for the vast majority of consumers. English is understood by Dhaka's educated middle class and professionals but Bengali-language content consistently outperforms. For businesses in Gulshan, Banani, or Baridhara (diplomatic areas), bilingual Bengali/English content works. Everywhere else, Bengali is essential.

**How important is Facebook vs. other platforms?**
Facebook is the primary platform. For local businesses in Bangladesh, a well-managed Facebook Page with consistent Bengali content, fast Messenger responses, and Facebook Ads will outperform any other digital channel. Build your Facebook presence first before investing in anything else.

**Is Google Ads worth it in Bangladesh?**
Yes — CPCs are extremely low, and search intent is high. A small Google Ads budget (BDT 5,000-10,000/month) can generate significant leads, particularly for higher-ticket services in Dhaka. Google Maps optimisation is especially valuable as smartphone navigation grows.

**What payment methods should I accept?**
bKash is mandatory. Also accept Nagad, Rocket, cash, and card (for upscale Dhaka businesses). Displaying multiple payment options increases conversion — many customers will abandon a purchase if their preferred mobile wallet isn't accepted.

**Is TikTok worth investing in for local Bangladeshi businesses?**
For businesses targeting under-35 Bangladeshis, absolutely. TikTok organic content in Bengali can reach hundreds of thousands at zero cost if the content resonates. Food, beauty, and entertainment-adjacent content performs particularly well. Start with organic before investing in TikTok Ads.
