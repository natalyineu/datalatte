AppsFlyer is the world's most widely used mobile attribution platform — the tool that answers the question "which ad campaign led to this app install or in-app purchase?" Used by Coca-Cola, Uber, and thousands of mid-market companies, it sits at the centre of mobile marketing infrastructure for app-driven businesses.

But here's the honest question for local small businesses: **do you actually need it?**

The answer depends almost entirely on one factor: whether you have a mobile app and whether that app is a meaningful revenue driver for your business.

## What AppsFlyer Actually Does

Mobile attribution solves a specific problem. When someone installs your app or makes an in-app purchase, that action came from somewhere — a Google App Campaign, a Meta mobile ad, an influencer's Instagram story link, an organic App Store search. Without attribution, you see the installs but not which channel generated them.

AppsFlyer places a Software Development Kit (SDK) inside your app. When a user installs your app after seeing an ad, the SDK communicates with AppsFlyer's platform to record which specific campaign, ad set, and creative led to that install. You get a clean picture of cost per install and in-app conversion by channel.

**What this enables:**
- Knowing your true CAC (customer acquisition cost) per marketing channel
- Turning off campaigns that look productive but aren't driving quality users
- Scaling campaigns that generate high-LTV customers
- Detecting mobile ad fraud (fake installs from bots or click farms)

**What it doesn't do:**
- Track web-only behaviour (that's Google Analytics 4's territory)
- Help businesses without apps
- Improve ad performance on its own — it only provides the measurement data

<Callout type="tip">
AppsFlyer tracks mobile app events. If your entire business operates through a website (no app), you don't need AppsFlyer — GA4, Segment, or similar web analytics tools are the right solution. The question "do I need AppsFlyer?" is really "do I have an app where marketing attribution matters?"
</Callout>

## When Local Businesses Need AppsFlyer

Most local small businesses don't need AppsFlyer. But these scenarios do warrant it:

**You have a booking or loyalty app with paid marketing.** If your fitness studio, salon, or restaurant has an app for scheduling, ordering, or loyalty points — and you're running paid campaigns to drive app installs — you need attribution to understand which campaigns generate actual users vs. fake installs.

**Your app generates significant revenue.** A coffee shop with a mobile ordering app doing $50,000/month in mobile revenue should be tracking which marketing drives that app usage. Without attribution, you're optimising blind.

**You're running campaigns across multiple ad platforms.** If you run Google App Campaigns, Meta Mobile App Ads, and Apple Search Ads simultaneously, you need a neutral third-party to de-duplicate installs and provide unified reporting. Each platform claims credit for installs — AppsFlyer provides the impartial view.

**You suspect mobile ad fraud.** Fake installs are a real problem at scale. If your app install numbers look suspicious (high installs, low engagement), AppsFlyer's fraud protection tools can identify and filter out fraudulent activity.

## AppsFlyer Pricing: What You'll Actually Pay

Additional costs:

## AppsFlyer Setup: What's Involved

Setting up AppsFlyer requires developer involvement. It's not a plug-and-play tool — your development team needs to integrate the SDK into your app.

**Technical requirements:**
- iOS and Android SDK integration (separate for each platform)
- Event mapping (defining which in-app actions to track: installs, registrations, purchases, bookings)
- Link generation (OneLink deep links for cross-channel attribution)
- Integration with each ad platform (Google, Meta, Apple Search Ads, etc.) through AppsFlyer's partner integrations

Implementation time for a simple app: 2–5 developer days. For complex apps with many custom events: 1–3 weeks.

**Non-technical setup:**
- Dashboard configuration
- Connecting to ad platforms for cost data import
- Setting up conversion events and goals
- Configuring fraud detection rules

<Callout type="warning">
The most common mistake small businesses make with AppsFlyer: installing the SDK but not mapping the right conversion events. Installing the app is only one data point. You want to track in-app bookings, purchases, and meaningful actions — not just installs. Work with your developer to define and implement all relevant conversion events before launching campaigns.
</Callout>

## AppsFlyer vs the Alternatives

AppsFlyer isn't the only mobile attribution solution. Alternatives to consider:

**Adjust:** Direct competitor, similar capabilities, different pricing. Often cheaper for small volumes. Strong fraud protection. Good choice if AppsFlyer's pricing feels heavy for your install volume.

**Branch:** Strong deep linking capabilities, popular for e-commerce apps. Good alternative if cross-channel deep linking is a priority.

**Google Analytics 4 for Firebase:** Free, works for basic attribution if you're primarily running Google App Campaigns. Limited cross-platform attribution and no fraud protection. Fine for starting out.

**Singular:** Enterprise-focused, strong for brands running large multi-channel campaigns. Overkill for small businesses.

For most local businesses just starting with app marketing, **Firebase + GA4** is the right free starting point. Once you're scaling past $5,000/month in mobile ad spend and running on multiple platforms, AppsFlyer or Adjust becomes worth the cost.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does a small local business with a loyalty app need AppsFlyer?

If you're not running paid ads to promote that app, probably not — GA4 for Firebase covers basic analytics. If you're spending money on Instagram or Google ads to drive app downloads, yes — you need attribution to know which campaigns generate loyal customers vs. one-time users.

### Can AppsFlyer work without a developer?

No — AppsFlyer requires SDK integration in your app's codebase. There's no tag-manager-style installation for mobile apps. You need either an in-house developer, a freelancer, or your app development agency to handle setup.

### How long does AppsFlyer attribution data take to appear?

Once the SDK is live and your campaigns are running, attribution data starts appearing in the dashboard within hours. Historical data (before SDK installation) is not available — attribution is forward-looking only.

### Is AppsFlyer worth it if I only run Apple Search Ads?

Apple Search Ads provides its own attribution data within its platform. For single-platform campaigns on ASA only, you may not need third-party attribution. AppsFlyer adds most value when you're running campaigns across multiple platforms and need unified de-duplicated reporting.

## Common Mistakes (And What to Do Instead)

I've watched small business owners burn real money on mobile attribution they didn't need, and I've watched them lose money because they didn't have it. Here are the most common screw-ups I've seen, with names changed but details intact.

**Mistake #1: Installing AppsFlyer before you have a meaningful ad spend**

A coffee shop owner in Portland, Oregon — let's call her Sarah — launched a mobile ordering app through Toast. She spent $800 on a local developer to integrate AppsFlyer's SDK because she "wanted to track everything from day one." Her monthly ad spend was $350 on Google App Campaigns and $200 on Instagram. She paid $1,200 for Setup & Integration fees (AppsFlyer's basic tier starts at roughly $1,500/month, but she was quoted custom because she wanted support). She ran the platform for three months.

What actually happened: She discovered what she already knew — Instagram drove more installs than Google. She already knew that because every customer who walked in and said "I ordered on the app" mentioned Instagram. She spent $3,600 on subscription fees plus the developer cost, and gained zero insights she couldn't have gotten from asking twenty customers.

The fix: Don't pay for attribution until your monthly ad spend hits $5,000. Before that, use UTM parameters in your Google App Campaign links and ask customers at the register. "How'd you hear about us?" costs nothing.

Outcome after she canceled: She saved $3,600/year, redirected that into an additional $200/month of Instagram ads, and tracked installs manually through her POS system. Revenue from app orders increased 12% because she put the money into ads instead of tracking software.

**Mistake #2: Using attribution data to make decisions without accounting for offline behavior**

A hair salon in Nashville — three locations, solid business — had a booking app they pushed hard. Their marketing manager (young kid, fresh out of a digital marketing bootcamp) looked at AppsFlyer data and saw that Meta ads were showing a $4.50 cost per install, while Google App Campaigns were showing $7.20. He cut all Google spend and doubled down on Meta. Within six weeks, new app bookings dropped 22%.

What happened: The AppsFlyer data was technically correct. Meta did drive cheaper installs. But those Meta-driven installs were from people looking for generic "hair salons near me" — they downloaded the app, looked at prices, and never booked. The Google installs, despite costing more, came from people who searched "Nashville hair salon [specific stylist name]" — they already knew the salon, wanted that stylist, and booked within 48 hours.

The fix: Cross-reference attribution data with actual booking value, not just install cost. The owner started tagging which bookings came from which channel using a simple custom field in their Booksy system. Google installs had a 68% booking rate. Meta installs had a 19% booking rate.

Outcome: After three months of corrected strategy, they restored Google spend and adjusted Meta spend to focus on retargeting existing customers. Bookings increased 31%, and their effective cost-per-booking dropped from $38 to $22. The bootcamp kid got a lesson in why attribution without business logic is just expensive noise.

**Mistake #3: Assuming AppsFlyer automatically tracks all revenue**

A pet grooming business in Austin — three vans, mobile dog grooming — launched an app for scheduling. They integrated AppsFlyer and were excited to see "tracked in-app purchases" showing $2,800 in the first month. They believed they had $2,800 in revenue from app-driven sales. Turns out that number only tracked purchases made inside the app. Their actual sales cycle was: customer books a grooming slot ($5 booking fee in the app), pays the remaining $40–$75 when the groomer arrives, paying via Square terminal at the van. All that on-site revenue was invisible to AppsFlyer.

What they missed: $12,400 in actual revenue generated through the app that month, because 94% of payments happened offline. They were looking at a 5% slice of the pie and thinking it was the whole thing.

The fix: Set up server-to-server postback integration between their Square Payments account and AppsFYer. This required a developer ($1,200 one-time) to map the transaction data so AppsFlyer could attribute the full payment amount back to the original campaign. Alternatively, they could have used a simpler tool like Branch with Square integration built-in.

Outcome: After the fix, they saw their true CAC was actually $3.10 per dollar of revenue, not the $12.50 the partial data suggested. They doubled down on the campaign they were about to kill — an influencer partnership with a local Austin dog account — and it ended up being their highest-ROI channel at 4.7x return. Revenue from app-driven bookings increased $3,800 in the following quarter because they finally knew what was working.

Here's the uncomfortable truth I've seen play out at three different clients across Chicago and Denver: without attribution, you will overspend on your worst-performing channel by 30–50% on average, and you won't know which one is the worst until you've burned six months of budget.

I worked with a fitness studio chain in Denver that ran four channels to drive app installs for their class booking app: Google App Campaigns, Facebook/Instagram, TikTok, and Yelp App Connect. They didn't use mobile attribution because they "knew" Yelp was their best channel — their Yelp rep told them so, and they could see referral traffic from Yelp in their analytics. Turns out Yelp App Connect was driving the most installs, but those users were churning at 78% within 14 days. They were downloading to check pricing, then using the website instead. Google App Campaign users, by contrast, had a 52% retention rate at 30 days.

They had spent $16,800 on Yelp App Connect over six months, and essentially nothing to show for it. They cut it to $500/month, redirected $2,300/month into Google, and saw app revenue increase by 41% in the next quarter.

The real cost of no attribution isn't the subscription fee you're skipping. It's the $16,800 you're lighting on fire while believing you're being smart.

## When You Can Get Away with Free or Cheap Alternatives

Let me save you some money. There are three scenarios where you don't need AppsFlyer:

**Scenario 1: You use Google Firebase for Android and SKAdNetwork for iOS.** If you're only running Google App Campaigns and Apple Search Ads, Firebase + Apple's native attribution will cover 80% of what you need. It's free. It won't give you cross-platform deduplication (if the same user sees an ad on both Google and Meta, you'll count two installs), but for a local business running $2,000–$5,000/month in app ads, the double-counting error is usually under 15%. Acceptable.

**Scenario 2: You only have a booking app with no in-app purchases.** If your app is essentially a reservation interface — like a hair salon app, a fitness class scheduler, or a restaurant ordering app — and all payments happen offline or through Square/Toast at the point of service, you don't need mobile attribution. You need to track which channel drove the booking, which you can do with simple UTM parameters in your ad links and a custom field in your booking system. Booksy, Mindbody, and Square Appointments all have this built in.

**Scenario 3: Your entire marketing funnel is within one platform.** If you run everything through Google Ads — app campaigns, search, display — and you don't use Meta, TikTok, or influencers, Google's own attribution is sufficient. You don't need a third-party tool to tell you what Google already tracks.

When does AppsFlyer become necessary? When you have three or more ad platforms running simultaneously, when you're spending above $10,000/month on mobile acquisition, or when you need to track in-app purchases and subscription revenue. Below those thresholds, you're buying a Ferrari to drive to the grocery store two blocks away. It's fun, but it's stupid.

## What to Do Instead of AppsFlyer (Step-by-Step for $0–$200/month)

If you're a coffee shop, salon, gym, or pet service with an app and you're not spending $10k/month on mobile ads, here's a concrete setup that costs you nothing and covers 90% of what you'd get from AppsFlyer:

**Step 1: Set up Google Analytics for Firebase.** It's free. It installs via the same SDK that many apps already have. It tracks installs, in-app events, and can connect to Google Ads. You'll see cost-per-install data for any Google-app campaigns. Takes one developer hour.

**Step 2: Use Apple's SKAdNetwork for iOS.** If you're running any Meta or TikTok ads to iOS users, Apple's SKAdNetwork provides privacy-compliant attribution. It's delayed (you get results 24–72 hours after installs happen) and it's aggregated (you can't see user-level data), but you'll know which campaign drove how many installs within a reasonable margin.

**Step 3: Implement UTM parameters for every ad link.** This is the most underrated tracking hack in mobile marketing. Don't just use the ad platform's own links — append utm_source, utm_medium, and utm_campaign to every URL. Configure Firebase to capture these parameters on install. You'll now see in your analytics which campaign, which channel, and which specific ad creative drove each install. This costs zero dollars.

**Step 4: Set up a simple post-install event tracking.** What action matters to you? Someone creating an account? Booking a service? Spending $25+ in the app? Firebase can track these as "conversion events." Connect them to Google Ads, and you'll start seeing cost-per-conversion, not just cost-per-install.

**Step 5: Build a manual feedback loop.** Once a week, cross-reference your analytics with what you hear in-store. Ask customers how they found your app. This sounds low-tech, but it catches the 10–20% of installs that attribution platforms misattribute anyway.

I've seen a bakery in Chicago use this exact setup — Firebase free tier, UTM parameters, and a clipboard at the counter — and run $3,000/month in app campaigns with clear visibility on which channel drove real orders. They spent zero dollars on third-party attribution. Their ad spend was more efficient than a competitor using AppsFlyer because the competitor was making decisions based on incomplete data (remember, they weren't tracking offline payments). The bakery owner spent that $36,000/year savings on a second location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can't I just use Google Analytics for everything?**
You can use Google Analytics for Firebase, which is different from the web version. It handles mobile app events and ads attribution for free. If you're only running Google ads, it's sufficient. It won't handle cross-platform deduplication between Google and Meta, and it won't give you the fraud detection that AppsFlyer offers. But for a business spending under $10k/month, the missing features won't meaningfully impact your decisions.

**Q: What if I'm only running Instagram ads to promote my app?**
If Instagram is your only channel, you don't need any third-party attribution. Meta's own ads manager will tell you how many installs each campaign drove. You won't get a full-funnel view (you won't see organic installs or search-driven installs), but if you're only running one paid channel, tracking that channel's native data is enough.

**Q: Doesn't Apple's privacy update make AppsFlyer useless anyway?**
Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) broke the old model of deterministic attribution. However, AppsFlyer adapted by building probabilistic models and using aggregated SKAdNetwork data. It still works, but it's less precise than before ATT. Most small businesses don't need the precision that remains. The post-ATT attribution accuracy drop from ~95% to ~70-80% matters to Uber; it doesn't matter to a hair salon trying to decide between Instagram and Google.

**Q: How do I know if my app is actually generating revenue I'm not measuring?**
Look at what happens after someone installs your app. If users create accounts, book services, or make repeat visits, but all payments happen offline (in-store, on-site, by invoice), your app is driving revenue that no attribution tool will capture. The fix isn't a better attribution tool — it's connecting your app analytics to your POS system so you can see which app users become paying customers.

**Q: Is AppsFlyer worth it for a coffee shop app that's basically a loyalty card?**
Probably not. If your app's main function is a digital punch card — buy 10 coffees, get one free — and you're not running paid ads to drive installs, you don't need any attribution tool. If you are running ads, simple Firebase tracking will tell you cost per install. You don't need to know cost per loyal customer because the only in-app event is "scanned loyalty card." The ROI isn't there.

**Q: What's the cheapest way to start tracking app installs today?**
Add the Google Analytics for Firebase SDK to your app. This is a one-time developer task (2–4 hours, $300–$600 depending on your dev's rate). Then add UTM parameters to all your ad links. That's it. You'll have install data, campaign data, and basic event tracking for zero ongoing cost. If you need more in six months, upgrade then.
