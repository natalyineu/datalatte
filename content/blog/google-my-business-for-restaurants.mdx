## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does it take to see results after optimizing my Google Business Profile?**

Usually two to six weeks. Google re-crawls profiles on its own schedule — there's no "submit for review" button. I've seen profiles move up in ranking within ten days if the changes were significant (wrong category fixed, missing hours added). But expect the full impact to show in about a month. If nothing changes after eight weeks, you've probably missed something else — like inconsistent citations on other directories.

**Q: Do I have to pay for Google Business Profile? Is there a catch?**

It's free. I know that sounds suspicious. Google makes money when people search and click ads. They want accurate local profiles because that keeps people searching on Google instead of switching to Yelp or Apple Maps. The "catch" is that Google will try to sell you Google Ads. Just ignore those prompts. Your free profile will still rank fine without spending a cent.

**Q: What if someone leaves a fake negative review? Can I get it removed?**

Yes, but only if it violates Google's policies — which includes fake reviews, spam, off-topic comments, and reviews that contain profanity or personal attacks. "I didn't like the food" is not removable. "This place is a scam and the owner is a criminal" might be. Report it through your profile. If Google rejects your removal request (which happens often), you can respond publicly with a calm, factual statement. That's usually more effective than fighting the removal system.

**Q: Can I have the same Google Business Profile for multiple locations?**

No. Each physical location needs its own profile. If you open a second shop, create a separate profile with that location's specific address, phone number, and hours. Google will connect them if they share a brand name. Do not try to list both locations on one profile — that gets your profile suspended, and getting it reinstated takes weeks.

**Q: Is it worth using Google Posts on my profile? Do they actually help ranking?**

They help indirectly. Google Posts don't boost your ranking the way keywords or reviews do. But they make your profile look active — which tells Google you're an operating business, not a zombie profile. More importantly, they give searchers a reason to click. A post about "Trivia Night Wednesdays" or "New spring menu" can be the difference between someone scrolling past and someone calling. I've seen restaurants get twenty to thirty extra clicks per week just by posting once weekly. Takes five minutes.

**Q: What's the one thing most business owners get wrong about Google Business Profile?**

They think it's set-and-forget. It's not. Google expects to see signs of life — new photos, updated hours for holidays, responses to recent reviews, fresh posts. I've consulted with businesses that set up a perfect profile, then ignored it for two years, and wondered why their rankings dropped. You don't need to obsess over it daily, but spending fifteen minutes per week is the difference between staying visible and getting buried by a competitor who's actually updating theirs.
