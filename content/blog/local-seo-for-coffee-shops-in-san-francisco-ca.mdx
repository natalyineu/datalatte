## Your Google Business Profile Checklist for San Francisco

Your GBP is the #1 ranking factor for local search. Here's the optimisation checklist specific to coffee shops and cafés in San Francisco:

**Category Selection**
Use "Coffee shop" (primary) + "Café" + "Espresso bar" (secondary).

**Business Description (750 characters)**
Lead with your service, your San Francisco location or neighbourhood, and your key differentiator. Example: *"[Name] is [city]'s premier [service type], located in [neighbourhood]. We specialise in [specific service] for [target customer]."* Include "San Francisco" and your specific service type naturally — don't keyword-stuff.

**Service Area Configuration**
Even if you're a physical location, set a service area to capture radius-based searches. A 5–8 mile radius around your San Francisco location typically covers your realistic customer draw area.

**Photos: Upload at Least 20**
Interior (light, cozy), espresso drinks close-up, menu board, exterior storefront, barista making drinks. Update photos seasonally. Google rewards fresh visual content with higher map placement.

**Posts: Weekly Updates**
Post once per week minimum — an offer, a new service, a seasonal message, or a local event mention. Active profiles rank higher. Reference San Francisco landmarks or events when natural.

**Q&A Section**
Seed your own Q&As with the 5 questions customers ask most. This controls the messaging and captures featured snippet opportunities for question-based searches.

**The near-me modifier is your most valuable keyword cluster.** Someone searching "near me" on mobile is requesting location-based results — Google interprets this as high intent and heavily weights proximity and GBP completeness.

**Neighbourhood-level keywords** often convert better than city-wide terms in San Francisco. Research which neighbourhoods your customers come from and incorporate those area names naturally into your website's title tags, service pages, and GBP description.

## Citation Building for San Francisco

Citations are mentions of your business Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP) on external sites. Consistency is everything — one transposed digit across 20 directories will suppress your map ranking.

**Priority citation sources for coffee shops and cafés in San Francisco:**
- **Yelp**
- **TripAdvisor**
- **Foursquare**
- **Zomato**
- **OpenTable (if food menu)**
- **Bing Places**

**NAP consistency rule**: Your business name, address, and phone must be identical character-for-character across every citation. "St." vs "Street", "Ave" vs "Avenue", or "(555) 123-4567" vs "555-123-4567" all count as inconsistencies.

**Local citations for San Francisco**: Beyond the national directories, look for:
- San Francisco Chamber of Commerce member directory
- CA state business directory
- Local neighbourhood association directories
- Industry-specific directories (e.g., Booksy, Mindbody, ClassPass for fitness; StyleSeat for salons)

Run a free citation audit at BrightLocal or Moz Local before building new citations. Fix inconsistencies in existing listings first — adding more bad citations makes the problem worse, not better.
</Callout>

## Review Strategy for San Francisco

Reviews are the second-most-important ranking factor for local search. In San Francisco's most competitive market, the number and recency of reviews directly determines your map pack position.

**Target**: 50+ reviews with a 4.5+ average before expecting top-3 map placement in competitive San Francisco categories.

**What to ask for**: When requesting reviews, prompt customers to mention specific services. The review text signals to Google what searches your business is relevant for. Trigger phrases that help: *great oat milk latte, best pour-over in [city], cozy atmosphere, fast wifi, good for remote work*.

**Review velocity matters**: 3 new reviews per week consistently beats 10 reviews in one week then nothing. Set up a simple follow-up system (text or email) 24 hours after each service.

**Responding to reviews**: Respond to every review within 24 hours — good or bad. In your responses, naturally include your service type and city name. Google reads these responses as content.

## On-Page SEO: Your Website's Role

Your GBP and your website work together. Your website reinforces the signals your GBP sends:

**Title tag format**: `[Primary Service] in San Francisco, CA | [Business Name]`
Example: `Hair Salon in San Francisco, CA | The Style Studio`

**H1**: Lead with your service and city. "Award-Winning [Service] in San Francisco, CA"

**Service pages**: Create a dedicated page for each major service. A hair salon gets separate pages for "Balayage San Francisco," "Keratin Treatment San Francisco," and "Men's Haircut San Francisco" — each targeting a separate keyword cluster.

**Schema markup**: Use CafeOrCoffeeShop (most specific schema type — use instead of generic LocalBusiness) structured data. This helps Google understand exactly what type of business you are, which improves category matching in local search.

**Embed your Google Map**: A Google Maps embed on your contact/location page is a minor ranking signal but easy to implement.

## San Francisco-Specific Seasonality

Back-to-school (Aug–Sep) and winter holiday season (Nov–Dec) drive 30–40% more local searches.

Build your local SEO calendar around these peaks: update GBP photos and posts 4–6 weeks ahead of high-demand periods, run review-collection pushes during busy periods when customer satisfaction is highest, and refresh your service page content with seasonal keywords.
