## How to set up a simple lead‑to‑booking workflow in 5 minutes

1. **Trigger:** Choose “New Lead” from your Meta Lead Form (or Google Form).  
2. **Action 1:** Create a contact in HubSpot or MailerLite.  
3. **Action 2:** Send an instant SMS via Twilio: “Hey, thanks for reaching out! Book your first class with 10% off – link.”  
4. **Action 3:** Add the lead to a Google Sheet for daily review.

Here’s a concrete example: a boutique gym in Brisbane set this up for $0 (Zapier’s free tier). The first week they booked 12 new members, each paying $75/month. That’s $900 in recurring revenue without lifting a finger after the initial setup.

## Common pitfalls and how to avoid them

1. **Over‑complicating the flow.** A 10‑step Zap is hard to troubleshoot. Keep the core loop under five steps.  
2. **Ignoring GDPR/CTPA consent.** If you collect phone numbers, add a “Consent” checkbox in your lead form and store the response.  
3. **Relying on free tiers for high volume.** Once you exceed 100 tasks/month, upgrade to avoid missed triggers.  

A real‑world example: a Liverpool café tried to auto‑post every Instagram story to Facebook using a free Zap. After hitting the task limit, posts stopped, and the owner missed a weekend promotion. The fix was a $24/month plan and a simple “only post during business hours” filter.

**Can Zapier integrate with my existing booking software?**  
Yes. Zapier supports popular tools like Square Appointments, Fresha, Mindbody, and even custom webhooks. If your software isn’t listed, a simple webhook can bridge the gap.

**Will Zapier slow down my website or ads?**  
Zapier runs in the background and only triggers when an event occurs. It does not affect page load speed or ad delivery.

**Is Zapier secure with customer data?**

**Can I automate email and SMS together?**  
Absolutely. Connect Mailchimp, SendGrid, or Klaviyo for email and Twilio or SMSBump for text messages in the same Zap. This multi‑channel approach boosts response rates by 20–30%.
