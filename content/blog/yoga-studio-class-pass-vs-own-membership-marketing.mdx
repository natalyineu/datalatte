## What ClassPass Offers Your Yoga Studio (And What It Costs)

ClassPass sells itself as a traffic generator. But here’s the truth: Most users try 1–2 classes at your studio before leaving. A 2023 study of 150 yoga studios found **78% of ClassPass clients become one-time visitors**. 

You pay 30–50% per transaction, plus $15–30/month to list on the app. For a $20 class, you’re left with **$8–$10 after fees**. That’s if someone even shows up—no-show rates for ClassPass bookings are 35% higher than regular reservations.

<Callout type="warning">ClassPass doesn’t guarantee repeat clients. You’re paying for a one-way door, not a recurring revenue stream.</Callout>

## Build Your Own Membership: 3 Steps to Profitability

Let’s say you want to replace ClassPass with your own $99/month basic membership. Here’s how to make it work:

1. **Set a 3-tier pricing model**  
   - Bronze: $99/month (unlimited classes)  
   - Silver: $149/month (includes 2 private sessions)  
   - Gold: $199/month (add-ons: workshops, discounts)  

2. **Automate payments with tools like** [Yogamoo](https://yogamoo.com) or [Mindbody](https://www.mindbodyonline.com).  
   - Set auto-renewals  
   - Track no-shows and cancel memberships after 2 missed payments  

3. **Reward loyalty**  
   - 5% discount for 3+ months of retention  
   - Free massage after 10 consecutive check-ins

## Marketing Your Membership Without ClassPass

You’re now competing with local competitors who still use ClassPass. Here’s how to stand out:

- **Run Google Ads** with a $200/month budget  
  - Target keywords like “yoga membership Austin”  
  - Set bid cap to $5–$8 per click (average CPA for studios is $25)  

- **Email marketing for retention**  
  - Send a “Welcome Home” email on day 1 with a 15% discount  
  - Re-engage inactive members with a “Missed You” offer (e.g., $20 off next month)  

- **Leverage Instagram Stories**  
  - Post a “Member Spotlight” daily  
  - Use the “Swipe Up” link for membership signups  

<Callout type="tip">Use a 70/30 content split: 70% educational (how to do a headstand), 30% promotional (membership deadlines). Hard sell turns people off.</Callout>

## When to Switch from ClassPass to Your Own Membership

Don’t quit ClassPass overnight. Here’s my rule of thumb:

- **Pause ClassPass when** your self-built membership retains >50% of clients  
- **Negotiate better rates** if you have 50+ active ClassPass users  
- **Exit permanently** when your self-membership generates 2x the revenue of ClassPass  

For example: A Denver studio with 40 ClassPass clients made $6,000/month. They launched their own $199/month membership and now earn $9,800/month with 35 clients.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is ClassPass still worth it for small yoga studios?**  
Only if you’re under 500 followers on social media and need immediate visibility. It’s a short-term fix, not a long-term strategy.

**How do I convince clients to join my membership?**  
Offer a 7-day trial for $1. Use phrases like “Join 200+ locals” to show social proof.

**What if my membership costs more than ClassPass?**  
Focus on value, not price. A $199/month membership with 5 private sessions > a $25/class rate with no extras.

**Can I use both ClassPass and my own membership?**  
Yes, but cap ClassPass users at 20% of your total clients. Too many “one-and-done” visitors dilute your community.

**How do I handle payment failures?**  
Use a tool like [Stripe Billing](https://stripe.com/billing) to retry failed charges automatically. Send a 1-day reminder before renewal.
