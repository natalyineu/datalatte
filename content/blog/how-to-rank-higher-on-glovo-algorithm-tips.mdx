## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to pay for Glovo ads to rank well?**
Not necessarily. Organic ranking is real on Glovo it's not a pay-to-win platform like some others. I've seen businesses rank #1 for their category with zero ad spend because they optimized their photos, categories, and fulfillment speed. Paid ads give you a temporary visibility boost, but if your organic metrics (click-through, conversion) are weak, the ads just waste money. Start with organic optimization. Add paid ads only after you're converting at 4%+ on organic traffic.

**Q: Will giving discounts hurt my brand value?**
Only if you discount everything all the time. A targeted discount (Friday evening, holiday weekend, new product launch) signals urgency to both customers and the algorithm. A blanket 15%-off-everything coupon tells people you're desperate and teaches them to wait for a deal. Discount specific items, at specific times, and track whether the total revenue increase outweighs the discount cost. If it does, you're fine.

**Q: How long does it take to see ranking changes?**
48 to 72 hours for most changes. Photo updates can shift click-through rates within 24 hours. Category changes take up to a week because Glovo re-indexes its search results on a rolling basis. If you make a change and see no movement in five days, undo it and try something else. The algorithm responds to data, not wishes.

**Q: Should I list on Glovo and Uber Eats and DoorDash at the same time?**
Only if you can consistently fulfill orders across all platforms without killing your in-person service. I've seen a dozen small businesses take on three delivery platforms simultaneously, get overwhelmed, and tank their ratings on all of them because orders arrived late or wrong. Master one platform first. Get your Glovo operations tight. Then expand. A 4.8-star rating on one platform is worth more than a 3.2-star rating on three.

**Q: Does my Glovo rating really matter for ranking?**
Yes, directly. Glovo's algorithm weights rating heavily in search results because they don't want to show customers listings with bad experiences. A 4.5+ rating gives you a ranking boost. A 4.0 rating is neutral. Below 4.0, your impressions will drop noticeably. Below 3.5, you're essentially invisible. Respond to every review, especially negative ones. Not with a template either. A specific, apologetic, solution-oriented response can salvage a relationship and encourage the customer to update their rating.

**Q: What if I pause my Glovo account for the winter? Will I lose my ranking?**
You won't lose it entirely, but your ranking history degrades over time. Glovo's algorithm favors recent activity. If you pause for three months, expect to start from a lower position when you resume. You'll need to rebuild impressions and conversions over 2-3 weeks. If you know you'll pause, plan a pre-pause promotional push to maximize revenue before you go dark, and budget for a re-optimization sprint when you come back.
