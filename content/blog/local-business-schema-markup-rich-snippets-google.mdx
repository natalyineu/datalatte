### What is Schema Markup for Local Business?

Schema markup is a way to add extra context to your website's HTML code, so search engines like Google can understand your business better. It's like adding metadata to a photo – it helps search engines show your business in the best possible light.

### Why is Schema Markup Important for Local Business?

Local businesses need schema markup to get rich snippets in Google search results. Rich snippets are the highlighted bits of information that appear below the search result title and URL. They can include your business hours, reviews, and even images.

### How to Add Schema Markup to Your Local Business Website

Adding schema markup to your website is easier than you think. You can use a plugin or a tool like Google's Structured Data Markup Helper.

Here are the steps to follow:

* Choose the right schema markup type for your business
* Use a plugin or tool to add the markup to your website
* Verify the markup with Google Search Console

### Common Schema Markup Types for Local Business

Here are some common schema markup types for local businesses:

* Business Hours
* Reviews
* Address
* Phone Number
* Menu (for restaurants and cafes)
* Services (for salons and spas)

### Callout: Tip

Use schema markup to improve your local SEO, but don't forget to optimize your website for mobile devices. Google favors mobile-friendly websites in search results.

### Callout: Warning

Be careful when adding schema markup to your website. Make sure you follow Google's guidelines to avoid penalties.
