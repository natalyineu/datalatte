If you've looked at Angi (formerly Angie's List) as a lead source, you've probably run into two different products with very similar names: Angi Ads and Angi Leads. Most service professionals don't understand they're distinct products with completely different pricing models, lead quality, and ROI profiles.

This article breaks down exactly what each product is, how the costs work, and which one makes sense for different types of businesses.

## What Is Angi Leads?

Angi Leads (formerly called HomeAdvisor pro) is a **pay-per-lead** model. You pay for every lead contact regardless of whether they hire you.

Here's how it works:
- A homeowner submits a job request on Angi (app or website)
- Angi matches the request to 3–4 contractors in the area
- All 4 contractors receive the same lead simultaneously and pay the same fee
- You call the homeowner within minutes competing against 3 other pros
- You pay whether the homeowner hires you, hires someone else, or ghosts everyone

**What you're buying**: Access to a contact — not an exclusive lead, not a qualified buyer, not a guaranteed job.

**Lead costs**: $15–$120 per lead depending on job type and market. Typical ranges:
- Plumbing: $20–$45/lead
- HVAC: $35–$90/lead
- Roofing: $40–$120/lead
- Cleaning: $15–$25/lead
- Handyman: $15–$30/lead

**The core problem**: Because the same lead goes to multiple contractors simultaneously, you're racing to call first and competing on price. Win rate on Angi Leads is typically 10–20% — meaning you pay for 5–10 leads for every 1 job booked.

## What Is Angi Ads?

Angi Ads is a **subscription/display advertising** model. You pay a monthly fee to appear prominently in Angi search results and category pages.

Here's how it works:
- You pay a monthly fee ($100–$500+/month depending on category and market)
- Your business profile gets elevated placement in Angi search results for homeowners in your service area
- Homeowners who find your profile can contact you directly — you don't pay per contact
- Leads that come through are exclusive to you (the homeowner chose to contact your business specifically)

**What you're buying**: Visibility and brand positioning within the Angi platform, plus the credibility of Angi's review ecosystem.

**Monthly cost**: Varies significantly by market and category. Typically:
- Small markets: $100–$200/month
- Medium markets: $200–$350/month
- Competitive markets (NYC, LA, Chicago) in high-demand categories: $350–$600/month

**The core advantage**: Leads are exclusive. When someone contacts you through Angi Ads, they chose your profile — not a list of 4 contractors. Conversion rates are significantly higher (30–50% typically, vs. 10–20% on Angi Leads).

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Factor | Angi Leads | Angi Ads |
|---|---|---|
| Pricing model | Pay-per-lead | Monthly subscription |
| Lead exclusivity | Shared (3–4 pros) | Exclusive |
| Average lead cost | $15–$120/lead | $3–$20/lead equivalent |
| Typical conversion rate | 10–20% | 30–50% |
| Lead quality | Variable | Higher (self-selected) |

| Best for | High-ticket, high-margin jobs | Recurring services, volume |

## The Real Math on Lead Cost

The sticker price of a lead matters less than the cost per booked job.

**Angi Leads example:**
- Pay $45/lead for HVAC tune-ups
- 5 leads received, 1 booked = $225 per booked job
- Average job value: $180
- Result: Losing money on lead cost before you even pay labor

**Angi Ads example (same contractor):**
- Pay $250/month subscription
- 15 inquiries per month, 6 booked = $41.67 per booked job
- Average job value: $180
- Result: Profitable

The math often inverts completely depending on your category and market. Angi Leads can be profitable for high-ticket jobs (roofing, full bathroom remodel) where a single win justifies paying for 5 shared leads. Angi Ads tends to work better for mid-ticket recurring services (HVAC maintenance plans, house cleaning, lawn care) where conversion rate and retention matter more than first-job profitability.

## Who Should Use Angi Leads

Angi Leads tends to work for:

**High-ticket, infrequent jobs**: If your average job is $3,000–$10,000 (roofing replacement, full kitchen remodel, HVAC system installation), paying $80/lead and converting 1 in 6 still produces profitable economics. The math gets harder below $1,000 average job value.

**Testing a new market**: If you're expanding to a new city and want to understand demand before committing to a subscription, Angi Leads lets you buy leads with no minimum commitment. Use it for 30–60 days to calibrate your conversion rate in the new market before deciding whether to invest in Angi Ads.

**Fast-response operations**: Angi Leads rewards speed. If your team can call within 5 minutes of lead delivery and has a polished phone sales process, you can outperform the 3 competitors receiving the same lead. If you're a solo operator who often can't call back for 2+ hours, leads go cold before you reach them.

## Who Should Use Angi Ads

Angi Ads tends to work for:

**Recurring services with retention value**: House cleaners, lawn care companies, pet services, and HVAC maintenance plans benefit from Angi Ads because each client is worth substantially more than the first job. Getting 6 recurring cleaning clients per month at $200/month each = $1,200/month in recurring revenue from a $250 ad spend. That economics works.

**Businesses with strong Angi review profiles**: Angi Ads buys you visibility — but your conversion rate from profile view to contact depends on your reviews. If you have 50+ reviews averaging 4.8 stars, your Angi Ads spend converts well. If you have 8 reviews averaging 4.1, the same spend converts poorly. Build your review base first.

**Service businesses in medium-competition markets**: Angi Ads is most efficient where there's meaningful homeowner demand but not so much contractor competition that your profile gets buried. In extremely high-competition categories (general handyman in NYC), the subscription cost climbs while your visibility doesn't scale proportionally.

## Angi Lead Quality: What to Actually Expect

Both products are criticized for lead quality, and both have legitimate complaints. Here's what's actually happening:

**On Angi Leads**: Angi generates leads in part from homeowners entering a zip code to "see prices" without strong intent to hire immediately. Some leads are high-intent ("my furnace died, need emergency repair"), others are just browsing. You pay the same $45 either way. The browsing-to-booking ratio is often worse than contractors expect.

**On Angi Ads**: Profile visibility is real, but the homeowners who contact you through your profile are further along in their decision process — they've read your reviews and specifically decided to reach out. Quality is genuinely higher.

**What improves both**: Your response speed and your sales process. On Angi Leads, calling within 3 minutes of lead delivery can double your win rate vs. calling at 45 minutes. On Angi Ads, having a professional profile with photos, detailed service descriptions, and response rate above 90% significantly increases how many profile visitors contact you.

## The Hybrid Approach

Some contractors run both simultaneously with different strategies:

- **Angi Ads** as their foundation: consistent visibility, higher-quality inquiries, builds Angi credibility through reviews
- **Angi Leads** selectively: opt in only for job categories where their average job value makes the math work, pause during busy seasons when they don't need the volume

This approach requires actively managing your Angi Leads settings (you can exclude specific job types, set maximum weekly lead budget, and pause your lead flow) rather than letting Angi send whatever leads they generate.

## Alternatives to Consider

If Angi isn't producing ROI for your business, these alternatives are worth testing:

**Google Local Services Ads (LSA)**: Google's version of a verified local contractor listing — with pay-per-lead pricing but leads that come from Google Search intent ("plumber near me"). Often higher intent than Angi because the homeowner is actively searching. LSA verification includes background checks, so the Google Screened badge provides conversion credibility similar to Angi.

**Nextdoor**: Strong for neighborhood-level service businesses. Less volume than Angi but often higher trust (homeowners know their neighbors are recommending you). Advertising cost is lower; organic presence matters a lot.

**Thumbtack**: Similar to Angi Leads but with different category strength. Thumbtack performs well for personal services (personal trainers, photographers, tutors); Angi performs better for traditional home services.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I cancel Angi Leads at any time?**

Yes — Angi Leads is pay-per-lead with no subscription commitment. You can pause your lead flow, set a maximum weekly budget, or close your account at any time. You're not locked in. This flexibility is one of Angi Leads' genuine advantages.

**Q: Can I cancel Angi Ads?**

Angi Ads typically involves a 12-month contract with early cancellation fees. Read your specific contract terms carefully before signing. Some markets and categories offer month-to-month plans at higher rates. If you're unsure about ROI, negotiate a 3-month trial before committing to 12 months.

**Q: Do I need Angi Leads to get Angi Ads, or vice versa?**

No — they're independent products. You can subscribe to Angi Ads without ever buying a single lead through Angi Leads, and vice versa.

**Q: My Angi Leads win rate is only 5% — is that normal?**

For shared leads where 4 contractors receive the same contact simultaneously, 10–15% win rate is average. 5% suggests one of: your response speed is slow (call within 3 minutes — not 3 hours), your phone sales process needs work, or the job type you're receiving leads for has a very low match rate to your actual services. Review your lead types and exclude any categories where you're not genuinely competitive.

**Q: Should I get Angi's top placement before buying Angi Ads?**

Angi Ads is essentially the mechanism that gets you top placement — that's what you're buying. What matters is having enough quality reviews before you invest in Ads visibility. Spending $300/month to be a prominently displayed profile with 6 reviews is less effective than spending the same amount with 40 reviews.
