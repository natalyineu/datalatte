## Common Mistakes (And What to Do Instead)

### Mistake #1: Treating Every Customer Like They Just Walked In

A pet groomer in Portland, Oregon — let’s call her shop “Paws & Claws” — had 1,200 contacts in her email list. She sent the same monthly newsletter to all of them. Same grooming tips. Same “book now” CTA. Same 10% coupon for first-time clients.

The problem? Half her list had been coming to her for three years. The other half were one-time visitors from a Groupon offer she ran in 2022. The loyal customers didn’t need a discount. The expired contacts hadn’t opened an email in 14 months.

What actually happened: Her open rate dropped to 14%. Her click rate was below 1%. She was paying $49/month for ActiveCampaign’s Lite plan and getting nothing out of it.

The fix: I had her run two lists through a simple import tag in ActiveCampaign — one for clients with 3+ visits in the last 12 months, one for everyone else. Then she set up two different automations.

- Loyal clients got a “glow-up reminder” email 10 days after their last visit, with a photo of their dog from the previous session and a direct link to rebook. No discount. Just convenience.
- Everyone else got a “we miss you” sequence: three emails over two weeks, each offering a $15-off wash-and-brush.

The outcome after 90 days: Her loyal client list rebooked at a 38% rate (up from 19% when they got the generic newsletter). The “we miss you” sequence brought back 14 lapsed customers. That’s roughly $1,400 in additional revenue from one automation. She also canceled her Groupon partnership, saving $200/month in losses.

The takeaway: Segmentation isn’t complicated. A two-bucket system is better than one bucket that fits nobody.

### Mistake #2: Forgetting That Pet Owners Book on Their Phone, Not Their Desktop

A groomer in Austin, Texas, spent three weeks building a beautiful email campaign in ActiveCampaign. Professional photos. Clear pricing. A branded footer with her logo and phone number.

She sent it to her list of 800 clients. Six people clicked through. Zero booked.

She called me frustrated. I asked her to forward me the email. Here’s what I saw: The “Book Now” button linked to her website’s homepage. On mobile (where 73% of her opens came from), that homepage had a popup, a header image, and a booking form that required scrolling past three sections of text. The form itself had 11 fields — name, phone, email, pet name, breed, weight, service type, preferred date, preferred time, notes, and “how did you hear about us.”

Nobody fills out 11 fields on a phone when they’re standing in a parking lot.

The fix: I had her create a dedicated booking landing page in ActiveCampaign. One field for email. One field for pet name. Then a button that linked directly to her Booksy booking page (which already had three steps — date, service, confirm). She put a “Book Now – 30 seconds” button at the top and bottom of the email. No navigation. No distractions.

The outcome: From the same 800-person list, 42 people booked in the first week. That’s a 5.25% conversion rate, compared to the previous 0.75%. At an average ticket of $65, that’s $2,730 in revenue from a single email.

The takeaway: Your email campaign is only as good as the landing page it points to. If your booking process takes longer than 60 seconds on a phone, you’re losing customers you already convinced.

### Mistake #3: Automating Follow-Ups but Never Automating the Pre-Visit Reminder

A groomer in Nashville — five years in business, 900 active clients — had a solid post-visit automation. Thank-you email went out same day. Review request on day 3. “Book again” reminder on day 21. All set up. All running.

Her no-show rate was still 11%. That’s roughly 3 missed appointments per week at $65 each. Roughly $10,000 in lost revenue per year.

I asked her: Do you send a reminder before the appointment? She said she texts them manually the night before. On her personal phone.

That’s the problem. Manual pre-visit reminders fail because:
- You forget when you’re busy
- You text the wrong number
- You send it too early or too late
- You burn out

Fix: She set up an ActiveCampaign automation triggered by her Booksy integration. Two days before the appointment, the client gets an email with the date, time, address, and a “Need to reschedule?” link that goes directly to the Booksy calendar. One day before, they get a text message via ActiveCampaign’s SMS add-on ($0.01 per message). The text just says: “Reminder: [Pet name] is booked with [Shop name] tomorrow at [time]. Reply YES to confirm, or click here to reschedule.”

The outcome after 60 days: No-show rate dropped from 11% to 3.2%. That’s an extra 7 appointments per month. At $65 each, that’s $455/month — $5,460/year — captured from appointments that already existed on her calendar.

The takeaway: Everyone focuses on getting the next booking. Most people forget to protect the bookings they already have. Pre-visit automation pays for itself by reducing the no-show tax.

### Mistake #4: Using the Same Email Subject Line for Every Campaign

A groomer in Chicago sent 22 campaigns over six months. Every single one had a subject line like “February grooming tips” or “Summer grooming special.” Nothing terrible. Nothing compelling either.

Her open rate averaged 18%. The industry average for pet services sits around 24-28%.

I asked her what she did when she opened marketing emails from other businesses. She said she only opens emails where the subject line references something specific to her — like her dog’s name or her last purchase.

So we ran a test. For one month, she sent two versions of the same promotional email to a split test in ActiveCampaign:
- Version A: “August grooming special — 10% off baths”
- Version B: “Bailey’s bath is overdue 😉 — 10% off today only”

Version B got a 41% open rate. Version A got 19%. The personalized subject line more than doubled opens. The revenue from that single email was $1,800 in booked appointments within 48 hours.

The fix: She now uses ActiveCampaign’s personalization tags in every subject line. “Time for [Pet Name]’s trim?” instead of “Time for a trim?” She also runs A/B tests on every campaign that goes to more than 200 people. The winner gets sent to the remaining half automatically.

The takeaway: A subject line with a pet’s name is free. It takes 10 seconds to set up. It will outperform generic subject lines by a wide margin almost every time. You’re leaving money on the table if you’re not using it.

## Integrating ActiveCampaign With Tools You Already Use

If you’re a pet groomer, you almost certainly have at least two or three of these tools in your stack: Booksy, Square, Yelp, Google Business Profile, Mailchimp, or a basic website on Wix or Squarespace. The mistake most business owners make is treating ActiveCampaign as an additional tool that requires extra work. It doesn’t have to be.

### Booksy + ActiveCampaign

Booksy is the most common booking platform for pet groomers in the US. They have a direct integration with ActiveCampaign through Zapier. Here’s what that means for you:

When a client books an appointment in Booksy, their contact info (name, email, phone, pet name, service type) automatically flows into ActiveCampaign. You can set it up so that:
- A new contact gets tagged with the service they booked (e.g., “Full groom,” “Nail trim,” “Bath only”)
- A date field is populated with their next appointment
- They enter a “Pre-visit” automation that sends reminders via email and text

One groomer in Denver set this up in about 45 minutes. She had zero technical experience. Within a month, she had 300 contacts in ActiveCampaign with full booking history, segmented by service type, without typing a single name.

The cost: Booksy runs about $30-60/month depending on your plan. ActiveCampaign starts at $49/month. Zapier adds $20/month for the Starter plan. Total: about $100-130/month for an automated system that replaces the manual work of tracking appointments, sending reminders, and following up.

### Square + ActiveCampaign

If you process payments through Square, you’re sitting on a goldmine of customer data that most groomers never use. Square integrates directly with ActiveCampaign (no Zapier needed). Every time you process a transaction, ActiveCampaign gets:
- Customer name and email
- Purchase amount
- Items purchased (if you set up your Square item library properly)
- Transaction date

A groomer in Denver (same one, actually) used this to identify her top 20% of customers by lifetime value. She set up a “VIP” tag in ActiveCampaign that applied automatically when a customer’s total spend hit $500. Those customers get a different email sequence — priority booking slots, first access to holiday appointments, and a free nail trim on their 10th visit.

She didn’t do anything differently day-to-day. The automation ran in the background. After six months, her VIP segment had grown to 67 customers, and their average spend increased by 22% because they felt recognized.

### Yelp + Google Business Profile

Yelp and Google don’t integrate directly with ActiveCampaign. But you can still use them to grow your email list. One trick that works consistently: Create a landing page in ActiveCampaign offering “The Pet Grooming Checklist” — a simple PDF with tips for at-home maintenance between visits. Put that link in your Yelp bio and Google Business Profile description. When someone clicks, they enter their email, download the PDF, and automatically enter a welcome sequence.

A groomer in San Diego did this two years ago. She drives about 80-100 downloads per month from Yelp alone. That’s roughly 1,000 new email subscribers per year, most of whom have never visited her shop. She converts about 8-12% of those into first-time bookings through a three-email welcome sequence.

## How to Set Up Your First Automation in Under 30 Minutes

If you’re reading this thinking “this sounds good but I have dogs to wash,” I understand. You don’t have an hour to sit and configure software. Here’s exactly what to do in 30 minutes or less.

### What you need before you start

- An ActiveCampaign account (Lite plan is fine — $49/month)
- A simple PDF called “At-Home Grooming Tips” (10 minutes in Canva)
- A link to your booking page (Booksy, Vagaro, or wherever you take appointments)

### Step 1: Create a signup form (5 minutes)

In ActiveCampaign, go to Forms. Create a new inline form. Fields needed: First Name, Email, Pet Name. That’s it. No phone number. No breed. No address. The shorter the form, the more people fill it out.

Publish it as a standalone page. Copy the URL.

### Step 2: Set up the automation (10 minutes)

Go to Automations. Create a new one from scratch. Name it “New Subscriber Welcome.”

Add these actions in order:
1. **Tag:** Add tag “Downloaded Grooming Guide”
2. **Send email:** Send the PDF link with a short welcome note. (Write: “Hey [First Name], thanks for signing up. Here’s your grooming checklist. And when you’re ready to book, here’s a link to my calendar — [link]. — [Shop Name]”)
3. **Wait:** 3 days
4. **Send email:** “How’s [Pet Name] doing? I have openings next week if you need a wash or trim.” (Attach the booking link again)
5. **Wait:** 5 days
6. **Send email:** “Last chance — here’s 20% off your first full groom. This link expires in 48 hours.” (Use a coupon code or a link to a discounted service in your booking system)

That’s three emails. Takes 15 minutes to write and set up.

### Step 3: Put the form link everywhere (15 minutes)

- Paste the URL in your Instagram bio
- Add it to your email signature
- Put it in your Google Business Profile (Q&A section or posts)
- Hand a card with the URL to every client after their appointment

If you get 20 new signups per week (a reasonable number if you have a steady flow of clients), that’s 80 new contacts per month. Three months from now, you’ll have 240 people in your system who have given you permission to email them. With a conservative 5% booking rate from that list, that’s 12 new appointments per month. At $65 per appointment, that’s $780/month in revenue from 30 minutes of setup time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I only have 200 clients. Is ActiveCampaign worth it for a small shop?**

If you charge $60 per groom and one client books twice a month, that’s $1,440/year from that single client. ActiveCampaign costs $588/year on the Lite plan. You need one additional client every six months to break even. In practice, most small shops see a return within 2-3 months because the automation recovers appointments that would otherwise be lost to forgetfulness or no-shows. The tool pays for itself before you finish setting it up.

**Q: Can I keep using Mailchimp instead?**

You can. But Mailchimp’s automation features are limited on their lower-tier plans, and their pricing increases sharply when your list grows past 500 contacts. ActiveCampaign gives you more automation logic at a similar price point. The real question is whether you want to set up “if this, then that” sequences (ActiveCampaign) or just send broadcast emails (Mailchimp). If you only want to send monthly newsletters, Mailchimp is fine. If you want triggered sequences based on customer behavior, ActiveCampaign is the better option.

**Q: What if my customers don’t open emails? Should I still bother?**

Yes, but only if you also have a text message backup. Email open rates for small businesses average around 20-25%. Text message open rates are 98%. Set up your automations to send an email first, then a fallback text message if the email isn’t opened within 24 hours. ActiveCampaign’s SMS feature costs $0.01 per message. Sending 100 texts costs $1.00. Compared to the $65 you lose from a no-show or the $120 in lifetime value from a lapsed customer, that $1.00 is the best marketing spend you’ll ever make.

**Q: How do I get people to sign up for my email list without being annoying?**

Don’t ask them to “subscribe to your newsletter.” Nobody wants another newsletter. Instead, offer something useful. Examples: “Text me your pet’s name and I’ll send you a free grooming checklist” or “Enter your email to get priority booking for holiday slots” or “Sign up for a reminder system — we’ll text you when your pet is due for a trim.” Frame the signup as a service, not a marketing ask.

**Q: I already use Booksy for reminders. Why do I need ActiveCampaign too?**

Booksy sends standard appointment reminders — time, date, location. That’s it. ActiveCampaign allows you to build a sequence around that appointment. Example flow: Day -3 (email with prep tips). Day -1 (text confirmation request). Day 0 (post-appointment thank-you). Day 3 (review request). Day 21 (rebook reminder). Booksy handles one step. ActiveCampaign handles the entire customer lifecycle.

**Q: What’s the one thing I should automate first if I have zero time?**

The post-visit follow-up. Set up an automation that triggers 24 hours after an appointment: “Thanks for bringing [Pet Name] in. Here’s a link to leave a Google review. And here’s a 10% off code for your next booking, valid for 14 days.” That one automation will generate reviews and rebookings with zero ongoing effort. Everything else can wait.
