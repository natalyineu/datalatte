## 1. Optimize Your Google Business Profile (GBP) for Visibility

Your GBP is the #1 tool for local pet groomers. 87% of pet owners check this profile before booking. Start by claiming your listing, then do this:

- Add **high-res photos** of your salon and happy dogs
- Include **"dog grooming near me"** in your business description
- Post updates about **last-minute cancellations** or **holiday hours**
- Collect **1-2 reviews weekly** (ask clients via text after their appointment)

## 3. Get Listed in Local Directories (Yelp, Google Maps, etc.)

Pet groomers in Seattle, WA, report 22% more calls after optimizing 5+ local listings. Focus on:

- **Yelp**: Add your business for free. 30% of pet owners use it to avoid "overpriced groomers"
- **Google Maps**: Claim your listing and add "services" like "deshedding" or "pet-friendly salon"
- **Petco/PetSmart Local**: They offer free vet referrals to businesses with verified listings

<Callout type="example">"After adding our mobile grooming van to Rover.com, we filled 14 weekend slots in 3 days," says Sarah from Portland’s Paws On Wheels.</Callout>

## 4. Launch a Referral Program with Real Incentives

Word of mouth brings 50% of new customers to pet groomers—but only if you make it easy. Try:

- **$10 credit** for both parties when a friend books
- "Refer 3 clients, get a **free bath**" for loyal customers
- Track referrals via unique promo codes (use Bitly or Google Forms)

---
## 5. Use Email Marketing to Retain Clients (and Reduce Churn)

Only 12% of pet groomers use email automation. Those who do see 35% fewer no-shows. Set up:

- A **welcome email** with "free first-time consultation"
- A **3-day reminder** with "last chance to book before holiday rush"
- A **post-appointment email** asking for a Google review

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much should I spend on dog grooming ads?**  
Start with $25–$50/month. Solo groomers in Austin see best results with $15/day Google Ads.

**Are Google Ads worth it for a small groomer?**  
Yes, if you target [Your City] + "rescue dog grooming" or "senior dog haircut." Avoid broad terms like "pet services."

**Can I advertise my dog grooming business on a tight budget?**  
Use free tools: Post on Facebook Marketplace, get listed on Yelp, and ask clients to share your Instagram stories.

**How do I track the success of my ads?**  
Use Google Analytics UTM parameters. Track bookings from "grooming" vs. "bath" services separately.

**What’s the best time to post on social media for pet groomers?**  
Post at 7:30 AM and 6:30 PM on weekdays—when dog owners check their phones during their walk.

**Should I partner with local vets for ads?**  
Yes! 68% of pet owners trust vet recommendations. Offer vets a $10 credit for every referral.

**How do I get more 5-star reviews?**  
Send a 2-minute follow-up text: "Your pup’s fur looks amazing! Can we ask for a quick review on Google?" Include a link.
