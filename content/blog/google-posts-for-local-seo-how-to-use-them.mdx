## What Are Google Posts & Why They Matter for Local SEO

Google Posts are the rich snippets that appear in Google Search and Maps when you search for a business. For a coffee shop in Austin, they might show your new latte menu, a yoga studio in London might promote a sale, or a pet groomer in Toronto could share their holiday hours.

These posts show up **above paid ads** in Google Maps and **rank in organic search** for keywords like “best coffee near me.” The catch? Most businesses treat them like Instagram posts. They don’t optimize for local search intent or use location keywords.

<Callout type="tip">Start with 3–5 posts per month. Google rewards consistency, but spamming turns users off. I’ve seen salons in Chicago double their walk-ins by posting 2x/week with clear CTAs like “Book Now” or “Get Directions.”</Callout>

---

## How to Create a Google Post in 5 Steps

1. Open Google Business Profile > “Posts” tab > “Create Post”  
2. Choose a template: Offer, Event, Announcement, or General  
3. Add **location-specific keywords** in the title and body (e.g., “Spring Yoga Sale – Brooklyn Studio”)  
4. Pin 1–2 high-quality images (1080×1080 pixels recommended)  
5. Add a link to your website (use a UTM parameter like `utm_medium=google_posts`)

A fitness studio in Sydney increased their class sign-ups by 40% after adding a Google Post with a link to their booking page. They used a video of a class in progress and wrote “Join our sunset yoga session in Surry Hills – book now!”

## Optimize Your Posts for Local SEO

Think of Google Posts as mini landing pages. They need the same SEO care as your website. Here’s what to focus on:

- **Primary keyword placement**: Use your main service + location in the title (e.g., “Pet Grooming in Dallas – Spring Sale 20% Off”)  
- **Search intent matching**: If someone searches “emergency dog walker near me,” your post should say “24/7 pet sitting in Austin”  
- **Rich media usage**: Posts with images get **85% more engagement** than text-only ones

## Add Rich Media to Boost Engagement

Google favors posts with videos, carousels, and high-resolution images. For coffee shops, this could mean:

- A 15-second video of your barista making a latte art  
- A carousel of your seasonal menu items  
- A photo of your storefront with “452 Maple St” clearly visible  

A barbershop in Toronto added a time-lapse video of a fade haircut and included “Book your next cut in Mississauga” in the caption. Result? Their post drove **120+ website visits** in 48 hours.

<Callout type="warning">Avoid stock photos. Google detects generic images and reduces visibility. Use real photos of your team or customers (with permission). A coffee shop in Seattle lost 30% of post engagement after switching to stock images.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do Google Posts help with local SEO rankings?**  
Yes. Google uses your posts to understand your business better. Posts with location keywords improve your chances of ranking for “near me” searches.

**Can I track the ROI of Google Posts?**  
Add UTM links to your posts. Then track clicks and conversions in Google Analytics. A dog walker in Los Angeles saw a 25% drop-off rate from posts without links.

**Should I use emojis in my Google Posts?**  
Only if it feels natural. A yoga studio in Miami added 🧘 and 🌴 to their “Summer Flow Class” post and saw a 30% increase in engagement.

**How do I promote my Google Posts?**  
Share them on Instagram Stories and Facebook with a “See more on Google” CTA. It’s free cross-promotion.

**What happens if I post too often?**  
Google may hide your posts. Stick to 1–3 posts/week and focus on quality. A coffee shop in Boston learned this the hard way after posting daily for a month and getting penalized.
